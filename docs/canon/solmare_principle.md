# The Solmare Principle

The Solmare Cycle is not the story of a war.

It is the story of civilizations changing.

History creates civilizations.

Civilizations create doctrine.

Doctrine creates decisions.

Decisions create conflict.

The people living through those decisions become history.

---

History creates civilizations.

Civilizations inherit that history and transform it into doctrine.

Doctrine guides decisions.

Decisions create cooperation, conflict, progress, and failure.

The people who live through those decisions reshape history, beginning the cycle again.

The cycle never truly ends. It only changes.

---

## Meaning

The Solmare Principle describes a continuous historical loop, not a linear progression toward an endpoint.

Every civilization inherits the consequences of those that came before it.

Institutions are shaped by that inherited history.

Doctrine is the accumulated memory of institutional experience — history transformed into guidance.

Individuals rarely change history alone. Instead, they make decisions within institutions, and those decisions gradually reshape history for those who come next.

Wars are not the purpose of the story. They are one consequence among others — cooperation, conflict, progress, and failure — produced by doctrine, fear, misunderstanding, ambition, necessity, or survival.

The true story is how civilizations adapt after those moments, and how the people who live through them become the history the next cycle inherits.

The cycle does not conclude. It continues under new conditions.

---

## Narrative Philosophy

The Solmare Cycle follows civilizations across generations rather than heroes across adventures.

Characters matter because they are the people living through history—not because destiny has chosen them. Through their decisions, they become history.

No individual is indispensable.

Institutions outlive people.

Doctrine evolves.

History accumulates, then returns as inheritance.

Every victory creates new questions.

Every failure creates new lessons.

Neither victory nor failure ends the cycle. Each only changes the conditions under which it continues.

---

## Author Guidance

Every major story should naturally fit somewhere within the Solmare Principle.

When evaluating new plots, ask:

- What historical event created this situation?
- What institutional doctrine resulted?
- What decisions are being made because of that doctrine?
- What conflict — or cooperation, progress, or failure — naturally follows?
- How do the people living through it become history for those who come next?

If a story cannot be traced through this chain, reconsider whether it belongs in The Solmare Cycle.
