## CANON PHILOSOPHY

**Locked canon.** Full text: `docs/canon/canon_philosophy.md`

This philosophy governs all future writing, editing, revisions, and worldbuilding decisions.

When creative decisions conflict, apply these rules in order:

1. Character wins over lore.
2. Story wins over worldbuilding.
3. Simplicity wins over complexity.
4. Consistency wins over novelty.
5. Mystery is preferable to premature explanation.

### Character wins over lore

Readers become invested in people, not encyclopedias.

If preserving a piece of lore weakens a character, revise the lore.

### Story wins over worldbuilding

Worldbuilding exists to support compelling stories.

Avoid creating information that never affects characters or plot.

### Simplicity wins over complexity

Prefer elegant solutions over increasingly complicated systems.

Complexity should emerge naturally rather than being designed for its own sake.

### Consistency wins over novelty

A surprising idea is valuable only if it remains internally consistent.

Protect established rules once they become canon.

Future stories should build upon previous foundations rather than replacing them.

### Mystery is preferable to premature explanation

Not every question requires an immediate answer.

Allow readers to wonder.

Reveal information only when it improves the story.

Preserve unanswered questions when they create intrigue rather than confusion.

### Apply to future development

Every future revision, outline, chapter, and worldbuilding decision should be evaluated against this Canon Philosophy before becoming permanent canon.

The goal is not to create the largest fictional universe.

The goal is to create a believable one whose characters, stories, and history remain internally consistent across the entire Solmare Cycle.

## Core commitments

- **People first** — character, world, and story before technical detail.
- **Neither civilization is the villain** — Fleet Authority and the Dominion are rational; history drives the collision.
- **Cultural recursion** — touchstones recur; never explain them on first introduction.
- **Civilization scene test** — every chapter deepens understanding of a civilization (family, tradition, duty, food, history) — not technology alone.
- **Unique character names** — when introducing a new character, both given name and surname must be unique across the Cycle unless the characters are directly related (search cast before naming). See `Writing_System/Style_Guide/global_writing_principles.md` → *Unique names*.
- **Book One Ch. 5–8** — Incident arc is locked; do not regenerate without explicit author request.

## THE SOLMARE PRINCIPLE

**Locked canon.** Full text: `docs/canon/solmare_principle.md`

History creates civilizations.

Civilizations create doctrine.

Doctrine creates decisions.

Decisions create conflict.

The people living through those decisions become history.

Never write a battle before understanding the history that made it inevitable.
