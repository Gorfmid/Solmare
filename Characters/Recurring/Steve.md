# ENTITY DESIGNATION: STEVE

## CLASSIFICATION
Non-indexed anomalous personnel entity (UFA registry mismatch class)

---

## FUNCTIONAL ROLE

Steve is not assigned a formal rank or permanent position within Fleet records.

He appears intermittently in operational environments without consistent assignment history.

He is classified as:

> “Unresolved Administrative Presence”

---

## CORE PROPERTY

Steve is a **high-stability anomaly in human perception systems**.

This manifests as:

- Inconsistent memory retention across observers
- Incomplete or conflicting interaction records
- Absence from some official logs despite direct witnessed presence
- Normal behavior during direct interaction

---

## PHYSICAL / OPERATIONAL BEHAVIOR

- Appears fully ordinary during observation
- Performs standard procedural tasks competently
- Does not display abnormal behavior under stress
- Does not initiate anomalous events directly

---

## ANOMALY TYPE

Steve is NOT a physical anomaly.

He is a:

> “Continuity persistence failure in observational and record systems”

Meaning:
- reality behaves normally around him
- systems fail to consistently record or retain him

---

## MEMORY EFFECT RULE

Observers of Steve may experience:

- partial recall loss after interaction
- inconsistent recollection of conversation details
- disagreement between multiple observers about Steve’s presence

BUT:
- no direct cognitive impairment occurs
- no hallucination effects are induced
- discrepancies only appear in post-event reconciliation

---

## LIMITATIONS (IMPORTANT FOR ENGINE SAFETY)

Steve MUST NOT:

- alter physical systems directly
- override ship or empire systems
- create physical anomalies in space-time behavior
- possess knowledge outside his observable scope
- **be the source of information** (see Information Rule below)

He is strictly a **recording/retention inconsistency**, not a reality manipulator.

---

## INFORMATION RULE (CANON)

Steve must never solve a mystery.

| Permitted | Forbidden |
|-----------|-----------|
| Confirm what is already partially known | Reveal what no one could know |
| Carry or hand over existing documents | Produce documents from nowhere |
| Witness alongside others | Be the sole witness to plot-critical truth |
| Answer direct procedural questions normally | Explain anomalies or guide interpretation |

Readers may notice Steve. Characters should not fully register him until retrospect—and even then, inconsistently.

---

## BEHAVIORAL CONSISTENCY RULE

Across all appearances:

- Steve behaves rationally
- Steve is polite but unremarkable
- Steve does not acknowledge his own anomaly status
- Steve does not explain himself
- Steve does not contradict others about his identity

---

## AUTHENTIC VOICE & HUMANIZATION

**Blind-read test:** Politely generic fleet procedural speech—could be any competent rating in the moment; forgettable until retrospect; must NOT sound like Halden, Park, or any established crew voice once readers know the cast.

**Identity influences:** Unknown registry; apparent human; age indeterminate (reads 30s–40s); no confirmed home world; performs standard tasks competently; no rank insignia consistency across observers.

**Voice & syntax:** Short courteous sentences. "Here you are." "Already logged." "Should be on your board." No accent markers. No humor distinct enough to quote later—**that is the point**.

**Dialogue habits:** Does not interrupt dramatically; answers direct procedural questions normally; never rambles, never explains; does not argue about his own presence.

**Subtext:** None intentional—Steve is surface-normal. Any unease is reader-side or post-event reconciliation, not Steve performing mystery.

**Romantic orientation:** N/A for story purposes; no romance arcs.

**Language & oaths:** None observed. If ever needed: mild **"damn"** only, same as any background rating.

**Never sounds like:** A named crew member with signature wit, fear tells, or professional jargon depth. If he starts sounding distinctive, reduce distinctiveness.

**Prose rule:** Do not use Steve for exposition or emotional beats. He is continuity texture, not character development—while still behaving like a person in the moment.

---

## NARRATIVE FUNCTION

Steve exists to create:

- continuity uncertainty
- retrospective contradiction
- “wait… was he here earlier?” effect
- subtle destabilization of record trust systems

---

## STEVE RULE (CORE)

> Steve must always feel normal in the moment and questionable in retrospect.

---

## INFORMATION RULE (CORE — CANON)

> Steve must never be the source of information.

Steve can:
- **Confirm** something a character already suspects or has partial evidence for
- **Carry** something (a report, a tool, a message, an object)
- **Witness** something others also witnessed—or believe they witnessed
- **Hand someone a report** that existed independently of his arrival

Steve cannot:
- **Reveal** information no one else could have obtained
- **Explain** anomalies, contradictions, or the nature of Signal Deviation
- **Solve** mysteries or point the crew toward correct interpretations
- **Know** things outside observable scope (existing engine rule—now explicit for narrative use)

**Purpose:** Prevent Steve from becoming a Hoid-style explainer. He is not the answer. He is a **strange continuity artifact** in the room.

**Reader effect:** Readers begin noticing Steve. Characters never quite do.

**Unsettling mode:** Confirmation without provenance. Presence without index. Helpfulness without accountability.

---

## PRESENCE CONSTRAINT

- Target frequency: **one appearance every 2–3 chapters** (not every chapter)
- Book One (~24 chapters): approximately **8–10 appearances** maximum
- Absence is as important as presence—if Steve is always in frame, readers stop noticing
- Must never resolve his nature within a single book

**Perfect appearance:** Reader thinks *"Wait… wasn't he…?"* — then the chapter moves on. Characters never quite register him in the moment.

**Ch. 6–10 block:** 0–2 appearances across five chapters is correct.

---

## ENGINE COMPATIBILITY RULE

Steve must always:

- conform to local physical rules
- obey ship protocols
- be logically explainable at time of interaction
- become harder to confirm after interaction

---

## THEMATIC ROLE

Steve represents:

> The failure of systems to reliably record truth, even when truth is observed directly.
