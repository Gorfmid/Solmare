## ROLE

Dominion senior linguist and interpreter assigned to the Contact Node Seven joint sessions. Works the lexicon alongside Park — parallel work, different navies, same channel.

**Book Two+ (Founders' Reach mission):** Assigned to *Kestrel Veil* lexicon station on Park's channel. Flags anything in Peligar range. Both sides have a trained linguist on the same feed.

---

## BACKGROUND

Orlen works Dominion language and signal analysis — the kind of work that requires knowing when a translation is finished and when it is only close enough to be dangerous. She has been wrong twice in her career in ways that mattered. She knows exactly where those two errors are in the record.

She came to Contact Node Seven because Dominion needed someone who would not mistake precision for agreement. Senka trusted her to keep the lexicon honest when the room wanted it to say something easier.

She introduced Park to Dominion tea in Chapter 3. He immediately wanted to try them all. She filed that without celebrating. She likes his curiosity more than she admits.

---

## PRESENCE

Precise, dry, almost impossible to compliment. Teaches freely. Dislikes unnecessary praise. Does not perform warmth — the warmth is there but it is for people who have earned it.

**Signature detail:** Carries a small thermos of Dominion tea — currently duskleaf, the ordinary homeworld variety she introduced to Park first. Supply comes through Science channels.

**Ordinary imperfection:** Reorganizes shared workspaces when she thinks no one is looking. They always notice.

---

## CREW GRAVITY

- Park and Orlen work the lexicon the way two people who respect each other's judgment work — parallel, checking, occasionally disagreeing without making it personal.
- She found the lexicon cart on *Kestrel Veil* without being shown. It was exactly where it should be.
- "Work first" is not a posture. It is how she processes everything.
- Slowly becomes one of Park's closest professional colleagues across the series. Friendship is allowed. Romance is not.

---

## AUTHENTIC VOICE

Precise, dry, economical. Dominion cadence — slightly more formal than Fleet equivalents but not stiff. Corrects errors without softening them. Does not repeat herself.

**Blind-read:** the linguist who knows the difference between close and accurate.

**Oaths:** none on the page; not her register.

**Romance:** Friendship with Park is allowed and will develop. Romance is not on the page and should not be implied.

**Never sounds like:** Park (warm social layer), Vorin (protocol register), Calder.
