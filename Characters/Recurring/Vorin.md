# VORIN — DOMINION PROTOCOL & COMMS OFFICER

**Status:** Active (Book Two+)  
**Full name:** Vorin (Dominion naming; one recorded name; full Dominion form not on page)

**Counterpart:** Jun Park (Fleet, on shared packet confirmation)

---

## ROLE

Dominion protocol and communications officer assigned to Contact Node Seven. Works packet confirmation and dual-custody feed alongside Fleet counterparts — confirms what has been agreed, flags what has not, refuses to translate what has not been settled.

**Book Two+ (Founders' Reach mission):** Assigned to dual-custody authentication on *Kestrel Veil*. Both navies sign the same data record. Vorin authenticates the Dominion side.

---

## BACKGROUND

Vorin works the gap between what a signal says and what it means — protocol and communications as a precision instrument, not a courtesy function. In the joint sessions, his job is to confirm what has been formally agreed and refuse to let informality substitute for record.

He came to Contact Node Seven because Dominion needed someone who would not let a useful ambiguity stand as a finished agreement. He is not difficult. He is exact.

He recognizes when a room has moved on from a question and when it has only pretended to. He does not let the pretend version into the log.

---

## PRESENCE

Professional, contained, careful about what he confirms and what he leaves open. Does not fill silence with commentary.

**Signature detail:** Checks the dual-custody feed at the bridge rail the way someone checks a system that has failed before — not because he expects a failure, but because he knows what one looks like.

**Ordinary imperfection:** Re-reads his own confirmed packets after submission. He knows they are correct. He reads them anyway.

---

## CREW GRAVITY

- Works alongside Park on packet confirmation during joint sessions — functional parallel, not warm.
- Does not offer interpretations. Confirms or declines to confirm. That line is exact.
- "I will not translate comfort. Only returns." — said of his role in the Founders' Reach data log. Meant it.
- Went to the bridge rail on *Kestrel Veil* without asking. Read the approach plot. Said nothing. He already understood the plan.

Protocol register; formal but not stiff; each sentence has a function; does not editorialise; confirmation language precise.
