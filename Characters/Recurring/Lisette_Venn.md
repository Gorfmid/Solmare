# LISETTE VENN — CALDER'S AUNT; PROPRIETOR, THE CARDAMOM OVEN (BOOK ONE)

**Status:** Active  
**Role:** Calder Venn's aunt; proprietor, **The Cardamom Oven**  
**Location:** Lower Meridian District, Helion Prime

---

## STORY FUNCTION

Calder's family anchor off-ship. Represents ordinary Empire life—bread on credit, relay messages, the room that feeds people when credits freeze.

**Reader should feel:** warmth without sentimentality; stubborn kindness; the Empire as millions of people doing their jobs.

---

## THE CARDAMOM OVEN

Family bakery, two generations in the Venn line—first a freight-worker lunch counter, later a cardamom roll institution.

**Sign:** Painted cardamom bun in profile above the door; name lettered in copper and deep blue (Lower Meridian district colors); paint worn soft at the edges from weather and foundry grit.

**Known locally as:** *the Oven*

**Signature:** Cardamom rolls; cream-filled pastries with cardamom glaze. Packaging stamped with the bun logo and *The Cardamom Oven* in copper script.

**Customs:** Credit when credits freeze; unsold rolls donated to night freight shift during Founders' Week. Old mixer still runs on engine parts accepted as payment during a Founders' Week storm twenty years ago.

**Assistant:** Priya Sharma (teenager; district archive photographer during festival week). **Not** Sana Mehta (*Veil* life-support) or Amara Lin (VI-Gamma watchpost).

---

## BOOK ONE CANON

**Family:** Calder's aunt (maternal line). Calder's mother was her younger sister; both grew up behind their grandmother's bakery counter. Raised Calder after parents' industrial transit death (classified systems error, corrected posthumously).
