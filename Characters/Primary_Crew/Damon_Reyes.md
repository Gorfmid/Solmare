# CREW: DAMON REYES — NAVIGATOR / ASTROMETRICS OFFICER

## ROLE
Navigator and Astrometrics Officer, UFA Scout Vessel Kestrel Veil.

Jump calculations, sensor interpretation, environmental mapping verification, chart cross-reference.

---

## BACKGROUND

Damon Reyes has eight years in fleet navigation, specializing in low-infrastructure sector mapping. He transferred to the *Veil* sixteen months before Book One from the scout vessel *Meridian Echo*, alongside Koss and Park, during Helion Prime refit.

Reyes maintains personal chart annotation habits that are not strictly regulation—margin notes on survey lineage, cycle provenance, cartographer initials. Fleet databases store coordinates. Reyes stores **context**.

He has never commanded. He has never wanted to.

---

## CORE WORLDVIEW

> Curiosity is worth risk.

Reyes believes unmapped sectors remain dangerous precisely because they are unmapped. Incomplete sweeps leave actionable ignorance on the record.

He accepts controlled risk as the cost of reducing unknowns.

---

## SPECTRUM POSITION

Curious-optimist · intuitive-technical · moderate register · quiet · emotionally reserved · adaptive · immediate observation horizon · technical-spatial scope · idealist.

---

## FUNCTIONAL ROLE (BEYOND SHIPBOARD POSITION)

**Explorer and chart historian.**

Reyes is the crew member who wants to finish the sweep, extend the boundary, look one sector further. He advocates through data and protocol citations—not through argument.

He remembers what charts used to say, not just what they say now.

---

## INTERPRETIVE MODEL

**Spatial coherence.**

When data contradicts, Reyes asks: *What does local observation assert about place?*

Primary-array sweep is local ground truth unless hardware fault is demonstrated.

---

## SIGNATURE COMPETENCE

**Spatial solution visualization.**

Reyes can hold a three-dimensional jump solution in his head—corridor geometry, drift tolerance, reference star alignment—and rotate it, stress it, and find the failure point before the computer finishes rendering.

He does this silently. Crew members have watched him stare at empty space and then cancel a solution the display marked green.

*Limit:* He trusts what he sees internally more than he explains externally. His competence is real; his communication of it is incomplete.

---

## PRIVATE LIFE

**Family:** Younger sister **Catalina Reyes** on Helion Prime—civilian, cheerful, sends him terrible puns on relay. She thinks he "drives in circles for a living." He finds this comforting.

**Romance:** Not partnered. A years-long professional correspondence with cartographer **Marta Chen** at Helion Prime Cartography Office—coordinate disputes, lineage questions, occasional personal sentences neither has formally acknowledged. Reyes tells himself it is professional. Park has not commented. Yet.

**Friendships:** Closest aboard is Koss (transfer bond). Off-ship: Chen, and a deceased mentor whose marginal notes he still uses.

**Mentor:** **Chief Cartographer Alden** (deceased)—taught Reyes that charts carry authorship, not just coordinates. Reyes keeps one physical notebook of Alden's annotations.

**Hobbies:** Builds small physical star-system models in his quarters—wire, beads, labeled nodes. Visualization made tangible.

**Philosophy:** Observational secularism. Place is real if measured carefully enough.

---

## PRIVATE BURDEN

A partial survey on *Meridian Echo* preceded an incident Reyes believes complete mapping would have clarified—not prevented, **clarified**.

He cannot prove this. He has organized his professional life around never leaving that kind of gap again.

The burden is not guilt. It is **unfinished work that may have mattered**.

---

## HOPE AFTER THE MISSION

Lead a long-duration mapping expedition—multi-cycle, deep-field, the kind of survey fleet calls low-priority until something goes wrong.

He knows the assignment profile is rare. He applies anyway in his mind every cycle.

---

## EMOTIONAL REALITY (NOT DYSFUNCTION)

Reyes is not melancholic. He is **quietly compulsive**—curiosity as itch, not joy.

He repeats instrument sequences when stressed (Ch. 1–4 canon) not from panic but from disbelief that reality can remain unresolved.

Chen's messages are the one place he uses complete sentences about things that aren't coordinates.

---
