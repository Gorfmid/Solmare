# KESTREL VEIL — CREW GRAVITY

Habits that prove these people have worked together for years.

**Not lore entries.** Not plot devices. Not dialogue tags.

These are physical behaviors to **establish in prose** once, then let recur without explanation. Readers infer history. Characters do not comment on the habits unless something breaks them.

---

## How to Use

- Introduce one or two habits per chapter during ordinary moments—not during crisis peaks.
- Never explain the origin in narration.
- When a habit **fails** (Brenner absent, Calder notices the packets aren't straightened), that absence carries weight without comment.
- Support crew habits matter as much as senior officer habits.

---

## Senior Officer Gravity

### Koss ↔ Brenner
- Brenner hands Koss the correct wrench before she asks.
- Koss does not thank him. Brenner does not expect thanks.
- When Koss appears in a passageway with a diagnostic tablet, Brenner is already pulling the access panel.

### Reyes ↔ Tanaka
- Tanaka starts reading Reyes's unfinished sentences during jump prep.
- Reyes does not repeat himself. He waits for her to finish, confirms or corrects one word.
- Tanaka's color-coded checklist is already open to the current sequence when Reyes turns around.

### Halden ↔ Park
- Park refills Halden's coffee without asking—knows how she takes it, knows when she stopped joking.
- Halden leaves mug space open on the secondary galley table. Park fills it.
- When Halden's humor stops, Park stops refilling. Nobody remarks on this.

### Calder ↔ Thessaly
- Thessaly straightens Calder's mission packet stack while talking to him about something else.
- Calder does not notice she did it. Thessaly does not mention it.
- When packets are disordered and Thessaly hasn't touched them, something is wrong with her day.

### Reyes ↔ Ortega
- Ortega unconsciously waits for Reyes's tiny nod before speaking in briefings.
- Reyes gives the nod without looking up from the display.
- Ortega talks too fast until the nod; after the nod, fleet-appropriate speed.

### Koss ↔ Sharma
- Sharma appears with the correct certification tablet already open to the section Koss is about to ask for.
- Koss calls her "Sharma" never "Priya" on-watch. Sharma does not mind.

### Park ↔ Cole
- Cole "borrows" Park's mugs. Park finds them on the secondary relay station without looking.
- Cole identifies relay congestion by sound; Park glances at her instead of the display first.

### Halden ↔ Hale
- Hale is already at the tactical systems panel when Halden's voice changes register.
- Hale does not ask if there's a problem. He runs the checklist.

## Cross-Layer Gravity

### Thessaly ↔ Walsh
- Walsh's personal watch notebook matches Thessaly's adjustments before she sends them.
- Thessaly never admitted she borrowed his notebook as reference. Walsh knows.

### Okwelu ↔ everyone
- Okwelu appears in passageways during long watches without being summoned.
- Crew members sit down when she tells them to. Even Calder. Eventually.

### Brenner ↔ the stuck door
- Environmental door 7-C (Corridor C junction) always sticks unless Brenner kicks it.
- Senior crew step aside for Brenner at that door without breaking stride.
- Calder still tries the handle first every time.

### Calder ↔ Park (learning)
- Park remembers names Calder forgets. Calder is beginning to glance at Park before addressing support crew.
- Not yet habit. Becoming one.

---

## Calder Gaps (Intentional)

Calder is new. He breaks or misses gravity constantly.

Useful prose signals:
- Reaches for a wrench Brenner already handed Koss.
- Finishes his own sentence because Tanaka hasn't learned his rhythms yet—she has, with Reyes.
- Straightens his own packets after Thessaly leaves.
- Tries door 7-C without the kick. Waits. Brenner or someone else kicks it behind him.

His integration arc can be tracked through gravity he eventually stops disrupting.

---

## What Gravity Is Not

- Not inside jokes (those are in `crew_culture.md`)
- Not signature competence (those are individual skills)
- Not relationship matrix entries (those are trust and history)
- Not plot foreshadowing

Gravity is **muscle memory between people**.

---

## Prose Introduction Priority

When revising or writing forward:

1. Brenner/wrench + door 7-C kick (engineering texture)
2. Thessaly/packets + Tanaka/Reyes sentences (command deck)
3. Park/Halden coffee (off-watch or margin of crisis)
4. Ortega/nod (briefing scene)
5. Walsh/Thessaly notebook (background)

One per scene maximum early. Density increases naturally as reader trust grows.
