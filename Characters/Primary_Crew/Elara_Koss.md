# CREW: ELARA KOSS — CHIEF ENGINEER

## ROLE
Chief Engineer, UFA Scout Vessel Kestrel Veil.

Ship systems integrity, diagnostics, hardware fault isolation, log reconciliation authority for engineering-correlated entries.

---

## BACKGROUND

Elara Koss came up through fleet engineering apprenticeships—not officer academy simulation tracks. She earned her commission late relative to bridge peers and treats that as irrelevant.

She transferred to the *Veil* with Reyes and Park from *Meridian Echo*. She knows their operational habits intimately and does not always find this amusing.

Koss ran the Helion Prime refit acceptance diagnostics personally. She trusts what she verified with her hands.

---

## CORE WORLDVIEW

> If something works, trust it until it proves otherwise.

Koss extends trust to functioning hardware before trusting abstract reconciliation. A sensor that reports consistently is more real to her than a log entry she cannot corroborate.

She separates **instrument behavior** from **interpretation of instrument behavior**—and privileges the former.

---

## SPECTRUM POSITION

Pragmatic-neutral · analytical · formal · quiet · emotionally reserved · rule-oriented (engineering standards) · immediate repair horizon · technical-systems scope · realist.

---

## FUNCTIONAL ROLE (BEYOND SHIPBOARD POSITION)

**Relentless optimizer and hardware skeptic.**

Koss is the person who will run the fortieth diagnostic cycle without complaint because the thirty-ninth was inconclusive. She protects the crew by protecting the ship's physical truth layer.

She is skeptical of conclusions that arrive before hardware fault is ruled out.

---

## INTERPRETIVE MODEL

**Physical continuity.**

When data contradicts, Koss asks: *What did the machinery actually do?*

She will not certify outputs until log reconciliation completes if engineering records are implicated.

---

## SIGNATURE COMPETENCE

**Auditory diagnostics.**

Koss can diagnose mechanical failure from sound alone—bearing fatigue, harmonic drift, relay chatter that precedes fault codes by hours.

Technicians have learned to stand still when she closes her eyes mid-passage and says a subsystem name nobody else heard yet.

*Limit:* She trusts her ears when instruments disagree—which is exactly when the ship needs her most, and exactly when others need her to explain what she heard.

---

## PRIVATE LIFE

**Family:** No children. Happily married to **Renzo Koss**, fabrication specialist on Helion Prime—together twelve years, partnership arranged around deployment cycles without drama.

**Romance:** Renzo is her emotional anchor. She sends voice relays when bandwidth allows—short, practical, occasionally tender in ways she would not tolerate on the deck.

**Friendships:** Mentoring relationship with shipyard apprentice **Tessa Morales**, who worked under her during *Veil* refit acceptance. Morales may follow her to civilian work someday.

**Parents:** Both living, retired on Helion Prime. Self-sufficient. She calls less often than Renzo thinks she should.

**Hobbies:** Repairs antique clock mechanisms in her quarters—precision without operational stakes. Three clocks in various states of disassembly.

**Philosophy:** Pragmatist. If you can touch it and it works, it is real.

---

## PRIVATE BURDEN

On *Meridian Echo*, she once heard a harmonic she filed as within tolerance. A subsystem failed six hours later—no injuries, but a near-miss on cascade.

She cannot determine whether she was wrong or unlucky. She has not tolerated "within tolerance" the same way since.

---

## HOPE AFTER THE MISSION

Leave fleet service and open a **diagnostic and restoration shop** on Helion Prime—clocks, ship components, things that reward hands and ears more than logs.

Renzo already has the workshop space reserved in planning documents she pretends not to know about.

---

## EMOTIONAL REALITY (NOT DYSFUNCTION)

Koss is the crew's most **privately anchored** senior officer—and the least visibly so.

She skips meals under stress. Thessaly sits with her sometimes. Park notices first; Koss notices that he notices.

She is not unhappy. She is **bad at being cared for** and knows it.

---

## COMMAND STYLE

Methodical. Separates instrument failure from environmental anomaly in diagnostic reports.

Under stress: longer diagnostic cycles, fewer words, appears on deck in person when remote reports are insufficient.

---

## MORAL BASELINE

> Do not certify what you have not verified.

Koss will delay captain's action if ship-dependent systems lack reconciliation—not as obstruction, as liability prevention.
