# CREW: JUN PARK — COMMUNICATIONS / RELAY OFFICER

## ROLE
Communications and Relay Officer, UFA Scout Vessel Kestrel Veil.

Fleet relay contact, transmission buffering, signal integrity monitoring, timestamp and packet-order logging.

---

## BACKGROUND

Jun Park commissioned through fleet communications specialty track. He transferred to the *Veil* with Reyes and Koss from *Meridian Echo*, sixteen months before Book One.

Park is the only senior officer who regularly speaks to support technicians by first name on-watch—a habit Thessaly tolerates because relay subsystems depend on technician responsiveness.

He logs everything. He also **watches everyone**.

---

## CORE WORLDVIEW

> People reveal more than instruments.

Park trusts latency in conversation more than latency in signal. He notices who stops eating, who stops joking, who starts agreeing too quickly.

His job is transmission integrity. His instinct is interpersonal integrity.

---

## SPECTRUM POSITION

People-optimist · intuitive-social · informal off-watch · talkative off-watch · moderately expressive · adaptive · relational long-term horizon · interpersonal scope · idealist.

---

## FUNCTIONAL ROLE (BEYOND SHIPBOARD POSITION)

**Confidant and unofficial morale sensor.**

Park is who people tell things to when they are not ready for the log. He does not gossip. He routes concerns— to Thessaly, to Calder, or to no one, depending on severity.

Halden talks to him. Koss sometimes does. Reyes rarely does, and Park respects that.

---

## INTERPRETIVE MODEL

**Transmission integrity.**

When data contradicts, Park asks: *What did the channel do to the message?*

He cannot always separate content variance from channel variance—and refuses to pretend otherwise.

---

## SIGNATURE COMPETENCE

**Conversational recall.**

Park remembers every conversation he has ever had—not word-perfect, but **structurally exact**: who said what, in what order, with what hesitation, on what date.

He can reconstruct a three-week-old exchange between two technicians that neither of them remembers having.

This makes him invaluable for relay disputes, crew mediation, and knowing when someone's story has changed.

*Limit:* He cannot forget. Slights, kindnesses, and confidences accumulate. He carries more than he reports.

---

## PRIVATE LIFE

**Family:** Supports his mother **Hye-Jin Park** on Helion Prime—comfortable retirement funded largely by his fleet pay. Calls every cycle-end. She tells him he works too hard. He tells her the latency is fine.

**Romance:** Single by choice. Says he "already tracks enough people." Not entirely joking.

**Friendships:** Academy relay poker circle—five friends, one still in fleet, four civilian. Plays badly on purpose when bandwidth allows because winning would end the conversation. **Halden** is his closest friend aboard.

**Extended family:** Younger cousin **Min Park**, whom he helped place into fleet technician training—Min is not on *Veil*; Jun checks in anyway.

**Hobbies:** Cooks adjustments to standard galley output ("L-3 seasoning"). Keeps a private log of crew food preferences he will never show anyone.

**Philosophy:** Observant tradition from home—attention as practice. People are signals worth decoding.

---

## PRIVATE BURDEN

He once delayed elevating a crew conflict he had already memorized in detail—waiting for "confirmation" that was really avoidance.

It became formal before he spoke. No one was injured. He has never been unsure of the line since, which means he is always watching himself.

---

## HOPE AFTER THE MISSION

Run a **quiet mid-rim relay station**—connecting people who are far apart, without being in the middle of what they are saying.

He wants to be infrastructure, not witness.

---

## EMOTIONAL REALITY (NOT DYSFUNCTION)

Park is warm, not naive. His memory makes him **socially over-capitalized**—he absorbs moods he cannot unhear.

He disappears into the relay station under stress (Ch. 3–5 aligned) because the buffers are honest in ways people are not.

He is the senior officer most likely to cry in private and least likely to let anyone see it.

---

## ORDINARY IMPERFECTIONS

Nothing to do with the plot. Exists when the camera is off.

- Always remembers names. Never remembers where he left his mug.
- Three mugs in circulation at any time; technicians return them like lost pets.
- Burns rice when distracted. "L-3 seasoning" does not fix rice.
- Keeps a bad hand at poker on purpose and is still too competitive to enjoy losing cleanly.

---

- Buffer discipline without premature merge (Ch. 1–4)
- Latency, echo, and reorder detection
- Local logging when outbound contact is withheld
- Crew mood detection before cohesion metrics move
- Relay handshake reliability under congestion

---

## WEAKNESS PROFILE

- Can over-weight interpersonal signal under instrument ambiguity
- Talkative register must be suppressed on-watch—occasionally slips
- Reluctant to report unverified crew concerns through official channels
- Less confident in spatial or engineering arguments—holds position anyway when channel evidence supports it

---

## COMMAND STYLE

Precise on-watch. Logs latency, packet loss, and reordering events by timestamp without speculation.

Off-watch: warmer, longer sentences, more questions than statements.

Under stress: on-watch precision increases; off-watch disappears into relay station unless pulled out.

---

## MORAL BASELINE

> Do not merge what you cannot account for.

Applies to signals and to secrets. Park will log Calder's withheld transmissions locally (Ch. 3–5 behavior aligned).

---

## DIALOGUE VOICE

**Vocabulary:** Latency metrics, buffer states, handshake terminology on-watch; plain language off-watch.

**Sentence rhythm:** Crisp reports on-watch. Longer, conversational off-watch. Asks questions others avoid.

**Humor:** Warm, understated. Often redirects tension. Only Halden matches him off-watch.

**Emotional expression:** The most visibly expressive senior officer—still restrained by fleet norms. Tone shifts before words do.

**Body language:** Looks up from relay station when speaking to captain. Off-watch, sits sideways on chairs. Notices exits and entrances.

---

## NARRATIVE ARC — SERIES

**Book One:** Transmission integrity model; relay strength decline reporter (Ch. 4).

**Long-term:** Learning when to speak for the crew and when speaking becomes its own distortion.

**Unresolved flaw:** Once delayed reporting a crew conflict until it became formal. Still unsure where the line is.

---

## FUNCTION IN STORY SYSTEM

- Transmission interpretive model
- Cohesion early-warning system (feeds Thessaly/Ch. 5 arc)
- Human counterweight to instrument-only arguments

---

## KEY THEMATIC ROLE

Park represents:

> The information that never makes it into the log—but still changes outcomes.
