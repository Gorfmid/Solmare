# Protected Recurring Cast

**Status:** Locked series canon  
**Scope:** Solmare Cycle (Book Two onward; applies retroactively as editorial rule)  
**Linked from:** Revision Pass 1 / Pass 4 · router report  

Personnel roster for *Kestrel Veil* / Interface: [`KESTREL_VEIL_CREW_REORGANIZATION.md`](KESTREL_VEIL_CREW_REORGANIZATION.md)

---

## Rule

Characters established as recurring supporting cast should **not silently disappear** because their original scene function is no longer needed.

### Preferred outcomes

- promotion  
- reassignment  
- transfer  
- temporary deployment  
- project assignment  
- command duty  
- station duty  
- council or liaison work  
- academy or training assignment  
- medical leave  
- retirement  
- an explicit personal choice  
- an explicit death or loss that matters to the story  

### Avoid

- silent disappearance  
- replacing a known character with a functionally identical new character  
- introducing a new specialist when an established character could naturally perform the role  
- keeping a character nominally present while never allowing them meaningful work, dialogue, or progression  

Before introducing a new recurring officer, specialist, engineer, diplomat, security officer, doctor, or pilot, check whether an existing character can fill the role.

Career movement should make Fleet and Dominion feel like living institutions.

**Characters may leave a ship without leaving the series.**

A reassigned character should remain available for natural later appearances through:

- Interface Station  
- Fleet operations  
- Dominion joint projects  
- Council meetings  
- engineering work  
- military deployments  
- relief missions  
- investigations  
- personal communications  
- later commands  

Protected cast do **not** require equal page time. They **do** require continuity.

When they appear, they should have at least one real contribution:

- a decision  
- a professional judgment  
- a disagreement  
- a useful action  
- a relationship beat  
- a consequence  
- a visible change in career or responsibility  

---

## Career progression check (every Revision Pass)

Ask:

1. Has an established character lost their former narrative function?  
2. Can that loss become a promotion, transfer, reassignment, or expanded duty?  
3. Has a new character been introduced to do work an existing character could do?  
4. Does personnel movement reflect the events of prior books?  
5. Are senior characters developing junior officers?  
6. Are supporting characters gaining responsibility rather than remaining frozen?  

**Report item:** *Protected cast / career progression:* recurring characters retained, reassigned, promoted, or intentionally retired; no unnecessary replacement characters introduced.

---

## Protect against over-correction

These rules guide revision; they do **not** create mandatory scenes.

- Do not insert a cameo for every protected character into every chapter.  
- Do not make every professional relationship romantic.  
- Do not turn career progression into roster exposition.  
- Do not pause scenes to explain where every former crew member works.  

Use personnel naturally when the story reaches their area of responsibility.

**One meaningful appearance is better than several roll-call mentions.**

The goal is continuity, not attendance.
