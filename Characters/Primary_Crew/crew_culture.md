# KESTREL VEIL — CREW CULTURE

Culture aboard UFA Scout Vessel *Kestrel Veil* ("The Veil"). Professional military-scientific recon crew—not a family, not a fraternity, but a **home with rules**.

---

## Identity

**Formal:** Kestrel Veil (fleet logs, external transmission, mission packets)

**Internal:** The Veil (intra-crew speech, casual reference, morale context)

Using "The Veil" in external comms is a mild taboo—Park has corrected technicians twice.

Ship motto (internal, not official): **"Survive long enough to report accurately."**

---

## Traditions

### Post-Jump Stability Call
After every FTL exit, Reyes calls "Exit stable" or states deviation. Thessaly logs transit nominal or flags anomaly. The sequence is **ritual**—even when numbers are wrong, the call happens.

Crew treats a skipped stability call as more alarming than bad numbers.

### First Meal After Departure
First full crew meal after leaving port (not ration bars at stations): Thessaly attends; Calder attends if he remembers; Park usually cooks adjustment to standard galley output ("L-3 seasoning"—slightly more salt since *Meridian Echo* transfer).

Not ceremonial. Just done.

### Chart Provenance Toast (Reyes, Private)
When a sector map updates successfully, Reyes drinks coffee alone and mutters the cartographer cycle ID. Halden found out once. Has not told anyone.

### Dennett's Empty Mug Hook
Hook on command deck bulkhead where previous Captain Dennett hung his mug. No one uses it. No one removed it. Calder noticed. Has not asked.

## Rituals

### Watch Change
Two-hour blocks under extended anomaly (Thessaly standard). Handoff is status-only—no narrative, no gossip on the deck.

### Pre-Sweep Brief
Reyes states sector, array lead, duration. Koss confirms hardware window. Halden states evasive baseline. Calder authorizes. Takes ninety seconds when done right.

### Relay Window
Park announces inbound fleet windows. Crew not on watch may gather for non-urgent inbound—morale tether to fleet.

### Segregated Buffer Acknowledgment
When logs or data cannot merge, Koss or Park says **"Segregated, not lost."** Meaning: we are not ignoring contradiction; we are refusing false unity.

## Holiday / Cycle Customs

Fleet cycle-end (roughly quarterly): abbreviated watch if mission permits; Park plays something from home system on relay speakers—badly, intentionally.

Captain's cycle sign-off: Dennett read a paragraph from old fleet recon memoirs. Calder has not established a replacement. Crew waiting without pressure.

## Meal Habits

**Galley:** Small scout galley; four-seat primary table, two-seat secondary nook.

**Usual seating:**
- Park and Halden: secondary nook off-watch (talking table)
- Reyes: eats at station or alone, fast
- Koss: eats while standing if at all; Thessaly sometimes sits with her to enforce pause
- Calder: irregular; coffee at command console counts as meal error Thessaly tracks

**Food as signal:** When Park stops adjusting seasoning, crew notices.

---

## After Successful Missions

No celebration. Quiet acknowledgment in log. Park sends a brief personal relay to fleet contacts. Reyes updates chart annotations. Halden deletes worst-case scenario notes she wrote but did not file.

Dennett bought round at port. Calder has not yet learned this.

---

## Reaction to Loss

Scout culture assumes **information loss before personnel loss**—but personnel loss is the line.

If crew lost: mission stops; return to relay range; no speeches; Thessaly handles notifications; Park stays on comms; Halden makes coffee no one drinks.

If data lost but crew safe: Koss treats as engineering failure; Reyes as professional wound; crew works recovery before grief.

---

## Unwritten Rules

1. **Do not merge incompatible truth.** Segregate. Report. Decide.
2. **Do not perform confidence for the captain.** Report what is verified.
3. **Off-watch arguments stay off-watch** unless they affect certification or safety.
4. **The XO's rest-cycle orders are medical, not personal.**
5. **Halden's silence is louder than Thessaly's briefing.**
6. **Park's mood read is admissible evidence in informal council.**
7. **New captain gets courtesy, not automatic intimacy**—Calder is still earning "The Veil" as home, not just assignment.

## Calder Integration (Book One Status)

The crew culture predates Calder. He is competent but not yet **embedded**.

Signs of integration when it comes:
- Uses "The Veil" naturally in intra-crew speech
- Attends first-meal without reminder
- Establishes cycle-end custom (or explicitly adopts Dennett's)
- Someone tells him a joke before he overhears it

Signs of strain (current, Ch. 5):
- Cohesion strained; not broken
- Park and Thessaly monitoring morale
- Calder's withhold decisions respected but privately logged
- Crew functions professionally; warmth is cautious
