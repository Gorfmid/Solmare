# FELIX ORTEGA — SCIENCE / SENSOR TECHNICIAN, KESTREL VEIL

**Status:** Active (Book One → Book Two+)  
**Rank:** Petty Officer Second Class  
**Age:** 26  
**Reports to:** Senior Navigator Reyes  
**See also:** `Characters/Support_Crew/support_crew.md`

---

## ROLE

Sensor array operation, sweep data ingestion, spectral analysis prep, archive cross-reference execution. Reyes interprets; Ortega runs the routines and flags raw anomalies first.

**Book Two+ rule:** Still aboard. Not elevated to a protected department head. Use when sensor work needs a second voice — do not let him vanish without a line of continuity.

---

## BACKGROUND

Ortega transferred to *Kestrel Veil* through the science detachment pool — the kind of billet that goes to people who are good at noticing. He is good at noticing. He talks too fast when excited, which is often. Reyes slows him with a look. He appreciates the look.

He keeps a personal log he calls "beautiful wrong numbers" — sensor readings that turned out to be instrument errors, atmospheric anomalies, misidentified mass. The entries are precise. The titles are not.

---

## PRESENCE

Youngest recurring crew member. Enthusiastic in a Fleet-appropriate way. On the command deck during sweeps; otherwise in the sensor bay running routines.

**Signature detail:** Personal log of "beautiful wrong numbers" — anomalies that resolved into nothing, kept because they were interesting anyway.

**Ordinary imperfection:** Talks too fast when excited. Reyes slows him with a look. Halden privately called him "the optimist." Ortega does not know this is not entirely a compliment.

---

## CREW GRAVITY

- Reyes interprets; Ortega flags. The boundary is clean.
- Enthusiasm contained by Fleet context — not suppressed, just directed.
- Halden's "the optimist" note lives in the unofficial crew lore. Ortega would probably agree with it.
