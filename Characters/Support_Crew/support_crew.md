# KESTREL VEIL — SUPPORT CREW

Recurring personnel aboard UFA Scout Vessel *Kestrel Veil*.

**Total complement (Book One):** fourteen personnel — six senior officers plus eight support crew listed below.

These are not deep-arc characters. They are **inhabitants**—people who appear repeatedly, have names, habits, and ordinary imperfections, and make the ship feel lived-in.

Senior officers know them by name. Park definitely knows them by name.

---

## Department Heads (Support)

### Dr. Sera Okwelu — Chief Medical Officer

**Rank:** Lieutenant (Medical Corps)  
**Age:** 38  
**Reports to:** Captain (medical authority); day-to-day coordination with XO on crew rest cycles

**Role:** Primary medical officer for a crew of fourteen. Handles routine care, fitness certification, sleep-deprivation monitoring, and emergency stabilization until relay evacuation. On scout vessels, medical is one person—Okwelu is it.

**Presence:** Calm, unhurried, impossible to fluster on the outside. Appears in passageways more than on the command deck. Thessaly consults her before enforcing rest cycles. Calder forgets she exists until someone needs her.

**Signature detail:** Keeps a paper notebook despite ship tablets—"latency doesn't apply to pen and paper."

**Ordinary imperfection:** Perpetually loses one earpiece from her diagnostic headset.

**Running note:** Has told Calder three times his sleep fragmentation is medically unsustainable. He acknowledged all three. He changed nothing.

---

### Tomas "Tom" Brenner — Senior Maintenance Technician

**Rank:** Chief Petty Officer  
**Age:** 44  
**Reports to:** Chief Engineer Koss

**Role:** Leads the maintenance technician team (two junior techs). Propulsion subsystems, life-support hardware, physical repairs Koss certifies remotely but Brenner executes.

**Presence:** Koss's hands when she is on the bridge. Gruff, competent, loyal to the engineer not the rank. Has been on scout ships longer than most officers aboard.

**Signature detail:** Can reproduce any ship sound Brenner has heard before—not Koss-level diagnostic genius, but uncanny mimicry used to mess with junior techs.

**Ordinary imperfection:** Leaves tools in exactly the wrong places on purpose (he calls it "optimizing retrieval paths"; Koss calls it something unprintable).

**Running note:** Park knows Brenner's birthday. Calder does not.

---

### Yuki Tanaka — Flight Operations Chief

**Rank:** Petty Officer First Class  
**Age:** 31  
**Reports to:** Navigator Reyes

**Role:** Jump sequencing support, transit checklist execution, secondary navigation monitoring, corridor data ingestion. Reyes calculates; Tanaka runs the sequence and catches the details Reyes's head-visualization skips in documentation.

**Presence:** On the command deck during jump prep and exit more often than not. Speaks when spoken to on-watch; precise when she does speak.

**Signature detail:** Color-codes personal jump checklists in a system only she and Reyes understand.

**Ordinary imperfection:** Chronic lateness to off-watch meals by exactly the amount of time it takes to recheck one more thing.

**Running note:** Was on *Meridian Echo* with Reyes, Koss, and Park. Quiet transfer bond—doesn't push, doesn't need to.

---

### Felix Ortega — Science Technician

**Rank:** Petty Officer Second Class  
**Age:** 26  
**Reports to:** Navigator Reyes (astrometrics support)

**Role:** Sensor array operation, sweep data ingestion, spectral analysis prep, archive cross-reference execution. Reyes interprets; Ortega runs the routines and flags raw anomalies first.

**Presence:** Youngest recurring crew member. Enthusiastic in a fleet-appropriate way. More visible during mapping operations (Ch. 3–4 territory).

**Signature detail:** Keeps a personal log of "beautiful wrong numbers"—contradictions he finds aesthetically interesting before he finds them alarming.

**Ordinary imperfection:** Talks too fast when excited; Reyes slows him with a look.

**Running note:** Halden calls him "the optimist" privately. Ortega doesn't know this is not entirely a compliment.

## Recurring Enlisted Personnel

### Nadia Cole — Relay Technician

**Rank:** Petty Officer Third Class  
**Age:** 29  
**Reports to:** Communications Officer Park

**Role:** Relay subsystem maintenance, buffer management support, handshake protocol execution during high-traffic windows.

**Presence:** Park's most frequent collaborator. Often visible at secondary relay station. Knows every technician by name because Park insists.

**Signature detail:** Can identify relay congestion by sound before the display flags it—learned from Park, not trained.

**Ordinary imperfection:** Collects novelty mugs from every port. Park's lost-mug problem is partly Cole's fault (she "borrows").

---

### Marcus Hale — Defensive Systems Technician

**Rank:** Petty Officer Second Class  
**Age:** 33  
**Reports to:** Tactical Systems Officer Halden

**Role:** Defensive pulse emitters, evasive thruster maintenance, tactical display calibration. Halden's hands for hardware.

**Presence:** Appears when evasive posture increases. Dry humor in Halden's orbit—neither initiates comedy on the command deck.

**Signature detail:** Named his favorite emitter array. Nobody else uses the name.

**Ordinary imperfection:** Falls asleep in any chair angled more than fifteen degrees from vertical.

---

### Priya Sharma — Life-Support Technician

**Rank:** Crewman First Class  
**Age:** 22  
**Reports to:** Senior Maintenance Technician Brenner

**Role:** Atmosphere processing, water reclamation, berth environmental systems. Junior but reliable.

**Presence:** The crew member most likely to be seen carrying a toolkit in a passageway. Koss knows her certification status by heart.

**Signature detail:** Sends weekly relay messages to her grandmother on Helion Prime without fail.

**Ordinary imperfection:** Apologizes when nothing is her fault. Park has stopped trying to fix this.

---

### Devon Walsh — General Support / Secondary Monitoring

**Rank:** Crewman Second Class  
**Age:** 24  
**Reports to:** Executive Officer Thessaly (watch assignments rotate)

**Role:** Fills secondary monitoring stations, cargo bay checks, general shipkeeping, overflow tasks. The flexible slot—competent at many things, specialist in none.

**Presence:** Thessaly's utility player. Shows up wherever the schedule has a gap. Easy to overlook until absent.

**Signature detail:** Keeps a perfect personal watch notebook Thessaly once borrowed as a scheduling reference without telling him.

**Ordinary imperfection:** Cannot whistle despite trying constantly in maintenance corridors.

## Where They Sit / Gather

**Galley primary table:** Rotating enlisted; Ortega when excited, Sharma when Brenner sends her to eat.

**Galley secondary nook:** Park, Halden (senior); Cole sometimes intrudes with mug commentary.

**Command deck:** Tanaka during jump prep; Ortega during sweeps; Cole at secondary relay; Hale when defensive posture elevated.

**Passageways:** Okwelu (ambient medical presence); Brenner (tools); Walsh (everywhere).

---

## Dialogue / Presence Rules

- Support crew speak on the command deck when reporting or when addressed. They do not offer unsolicited strategic opinions.
- They have names, habits, and ordinary imperfections—not arcs.
- Park and Thessaly know all eight by name. Calder is learning. Reyes knows Tanaka and Ortega. Koss knows Brenner and Sharma. Halden knows Hale.
- In crisis: same functional brevity as senior officers. Personality compresses, does not disappear.

---

## Introduction Strategy (Prose)

Do not introduce all eight in one scene.

**Natural entry points:**
- Ch. 1 readiness report: Brenner, Tanaka, Ortega by role reference
- Medical: Okwelu when sleep fragmentation or crew rest becomes plot-relevant
- Relay congestion: Cole beside Park
- Sweep operations: Ortega and Tanaka
- Evasive prep: Hale with Halden
- Passageway texture: Sharma, Walsh, Brenner

One name per scene is enough early. Recurrence builds recognition.
