## THE SOLMARE PRINCIPLE

History creates civilizations. Civilizations create doctrine. Doctrine creates decisions. Decisions create conflict. The people living through those decisions are the story. Never write a battle before understanding the history that made it inevitable.

## CROSS-CUTTING REFERENCE: KNOWLEDGE ASYMMETRY AT Y+0

The following asymmetries are the structural foundation of the entire 50-year arc. All phase analysis depends on these.

| Topic | Fleet Authority (Y+0) | The Dominion (Y+0) |
|---|---|---|
| Name of the other civilization | Unknown — Fleet does not know "Dominion" | Known functionally: "Fleet Authority" is identifiable through traffic patterns and corridor records |
| Whether a peer civilization exists | Suspected — Thessaly's report has no category for it; no institutional confirmation | Known — Fleet expansion has been watched for decades |
| Leviathan / cloak trial | Unknown — *Kestrel* crew perceived unexplained field effect, not identified technology | Known — classified; partial failure means *Kestrel* is an uncontrolled intelligence risk |
| Neutral Zone character | Believed empty-ish; segment seven proves otherwise | Strategic depth — maintained corridors, inherited geometry |
| Breakaway founding history | Edited from public canon; not in Academy curriculum | Retained in Dominion institutional memory |
| *Kestrel* crew survival | Known internally (Rowan receives report) | Uncertain at Y+0; Stratimirov ordered passive search for surviving vessel |
| Strategic intent of the other side | Completely unknown | Partially modeled; Fleet expansion doctrine studied for decades |

# PHASE 1: Y+0 to Y+3 — THE IMMEDIATE AFTERMATH

*The report exists. Neither side knows yet what it means for the next fifty years.*

---

## FLEET AUTHORITY — Phase 1

**Y+0**
- Fleet Admiral Elias Rowan receives Thessaly's report at Fleet Command, Helion Prime. Reads it twice before calling anyone.
- Classification problem immediate: no existing header fits what was observed. "Anomalous Contact" was written for single-vessel encounters; "Inhabited Volume" requires confirmed settlement — segment seven shows infrastructure, traffic, and maintenance patterns, not a first settlement flag. "Unknown Vessel" applies to single objects.
- Rowan initiates a temporary classification: **FLASH RESTRICTED — Provisional Contact / Segment Seven-Alpha**. This header is invented by his staff; it has no formal precedent in Fleet doctrine. The header itself is evidence of the problem.
- The *Kestrel* crew is ordered to stand down pending full debrief. Calder Venn and Thessaly are confined to Helion Prime Station. Neither is accused of anything. There is simply no protocol for sending them back to routine duty while a report of this character is unresolved.

**Y+0 to Y+1**
- Calder's debrief lasts fourteen weeks. Thessaly's formal interview runs separately, eight weeks, with Fleet Intelligence Bureau (FIB) analysts. The questions are good; the categories behind the questions are not adequate to what they are asking.
- Rowan briefs three senior Fleet officers under strict compartmentalization: Vice Admiral Tomas Grewe (Strategic Planning), Rear Admiral Solen Harwick (Scout Command), and the FIB civilian liaison, Maris Chen — the last of whom has no formal authority to escalate without command approval.
- **The Edition 143 problem:** Rowan requests a formal review of whether the current Fleet Doctrine Manual (Edition 143) contains any classification protocol adequate to segment seven. Fleet Academy senior instructor Commander Aldric Wasse delivers the answer within two months: it does not. Wasse privately tells Rowan the terminology infrastructure is simply too young. The Academy was built for first contact with primitive or absent intelligences, not peer civilizations.
- Rowan does not brief civilian political authority at this stage. His reasoning: there is no frame with which to brief them. A report that cannot be categorized cannot be escalated through a structure that requires categories.

**Y+1 to Y+2**
- Thessaly begins writing what will become "Limitations of Fleet Terminology in Frontier Contact Assessment." She frames it as a systems paper, not a report on segment seven. The paper is technically unclassified — she is careful to discuss the terminology problem in structural terms without referencing the *Kestrel* mission directly. The document circulates informally among FIB analysts and Academy doctrine staff by Y+2.
- Calder is assigned to a non-classified advisory role at Scout Command. Not a punishment. Not a promotion. A holding pattern while Rowan decides what to do with a captain whose most important observation falls outside every available institutional category.
- **Calder's frustration:** He did not disobey orders, did not take unnecessary risk, and produced an observation record that the institution appears incapable of using. This becomes a defining professional experience — not bitterness, but a sharpened understanding of how institutions fail their own information.
- The *Kestrel* crew: Most return to standard assignments. Engineer Doss Kellar is assigned to a systems review post. Several junior crew rotate through normal assignments unaware of the report's classification weight. All carry standing non-disclosure requirements.
- **The Segment Seven Archive:** Calder's mission documentation, sensor logs, and Thessaly's preliminary field notes are locked in a FLASH RESTRICTED vault at Fleet Intelligence Bureau. No index number. Access requires Rowan's direct authorization.

**Y+2 to Y+3**
- Rowan begins a quiet political effort — not a briefing, but a series of conversations with civilian Fleet Council members he trusts. The topic is never stated directly: he asks about "survey authorization flexibility near the Neutral Zone." He is preparing the ground without naming what he needs the ground for.
- Edition 143 instructors at Fleet Academy begin hearing about Thessaly's paper through informal channels. Initial reaction is resistant. The paper is read as a critique of the doctrine manual they teach, not as an honest structural assessment. Commander Wasse is one of the few instructors who reads it carefully and does not dismiss it.
- **Scout Command's assessment (Y+2):** Rear Admiral Harwick concludes that segment seven cannot be treated as a closed observation. The volume is active. Fleet has no current doctrine for observing a maintained, inhabited volume without either retreating entirely or making contact. Harwick recommends authorization for expanded survey near the zone boundary — not penetration, but authorized approach. The recommendation sits on Rowan's desk for four months.

## THE DOMINION — Phase 1

**Y+0**
- Director-General Stratimirov reviews Commander Vex's debrief a second time, alone, after his staff has left for the evening. He removes his gloves. The question is not what the cloak trial revealed about the technology — it is what the *Kestrel*'s retreat reveals about the Fleet captain who saw it.
- His immediate conclusion: the captain retreated. This is consistent with Fleet's established doctrine for scout-class vessels under uncertain contact — do not engage, preserve the observation record, return. This is not evidence of weakness. It is evidence that Fleet's doctrine is working exactly as designed. That makes the intelligence risk harder to assess, not easier.
- Stratimirov's orders stand from Ch. 9 Book One: continue mobilization; accelerate cloak refinement; locate surviving patrol vessel; recover intelligence; passive preferred.

**Y+0 to Y+1**
- **Vex's reassignment:** Commander Vex is formally removed from field command. Classified. The official record shows a medical reassignment — plausible in duration, consistent with a long-term cloaking field test's health monitoring requirements. Vex is reassigned to a joint Science Directorate / SSD Technical Assessment posting. Title: Senior Field Evaluation Consultant. No formal disgrace. Vex understands that "assigned where useful" means that the Leviathan trial's partial failure is now his problem to fix from the inside.
- Stratimirov does not regard Vex as incompetent. He regards Vex as the only serving officer with direct experience operating a Leviathan-class hull in proximity to active Fleet sensor contact. That experience is a resource, not a liability — provided the intelligence risk is managed.
- **Kovat's data:** Senior Warrant Officer Daria Kovat's K-17 passive contact records — filed without adjectives, without escalation, exactly as trained — become one of three independent observation threads that SSD analyst Petrov incorporates into a pattern scatter assessment. Stratimirov reviews Petrov's first clustering at Y+0+22 days: three observations, insufficient for action. *Come back when you have thirty.*
- The passive search for the *Kestrel* yields nothing by Y+1. Fleet scout vessels do not maintain persistent signatures at long range. The *Kestrel* is simply not findable through passive means once it is beyond K-17 spine range. Stratimirov accepts this. Passive preferred remains doctrine. He will not send an active signal to locate an intelligence risk and thereby create a second intelligence risk.

**Y+1 to Y+2**
- **Leviathan cloak program post-failure analysis:** The partial failure during the field trial is documented in an internal SSD/Science Directorate review — **Technical Assessment Report 44-3: Field Cloaking Anomaly Under Passive Contact Conditions**. The finding: the cloak's interference layer reacts to certain passive scan profiles in ways the model did not predict. Vex's report is the primary source. The Science Directorate begins a model rebuild.
- **Petrov's accumulation:** By Y+1+8 months, Petrov has collected twenty-one independent observations pointing toward the same conclusion Kovat's operational data suggested — the Fleet vessel retreated, preserved its records, and reported. Stratimirov's "thirty observations" threshold is a teaching tool, not a bureaucratic obstruction. Petrov understands this and keeps collecting.
- Border watch: K-17 spine passive array extended north by two nodes. No public announcement. Transportation Directorate planners (including Volkova's sector) note the cargo routing implications but do not ask questions above their clearance level.
- **Volkova's corridor geometry flag:** The 0.7–0.8% route balance variance on K-17 spine — which Volkova noticed in Ch. 12 — is now understood internally (at SSD level, not logistics level) as a consequence of the cloak trial's post-event logistics surge. Volkova files the variance flag exactly as trained. It is absorbed into SSD's pattern analysis without her being informed.

**Y+2 to Y+3**
- Stratimirov reviews the full thirty-observation threshold at Y+2+6 months. Petrov presents pattern deviation data showing no pursuit, no escalation, no follow-on survey from Fleet. Stratimirov's observation: *"Nothing. That is what changed."* Fleet's response to the segment seven observation appears to be institutional paralysis, not strategic silence. These are different things, and the difference matters.
- The SSD produces its first formal post-incident assessment: **Revised Border Geometry Assessment 1 (RBGA-1)**. Primary finding: the surviving Fleet scout's report almost certainly reached command level. Fleet's failure to act within the first two years suggests either: (a) the report was classified to a level that paralyzed institutional response, or (b) Fleet's categorization systems cannot process what the *Kestrel* crew observed. Both interpretations favor continued preparation over escalation. Neither interpretation suggests the incident will be forgotten.
- Mobilization acceleration continues. The Kessik fuel reserve expansion — already authorized before the cloak trial — is accelerated. Stratimirov does not need to order additional preparation. The existing preparation doctrine was already at elevated tempo. He simply removes scheduling friction.

# PHASE 2: Y+3 to Y+10 — THE DOCTRINE PROBLEM

*Fleet cannot name what it knows. The Dominion watches Fleet's silence and draws its own conclusions.*

---

## FLEET AUTHORITY — Phase 2

**Y+3 to Y+5**
- Thessaly's "Limitations of Fleet Terminology in Frontier Contact Assessment" is formally published through FIB's internal academic channel — technically unclassified, practically obscure. Distribution is narrow: senior doctrine staff, Academy instructors, and three civilian Council advisors who happen to have FIB research access. It goes largely unread in year three.
- Rowan's political groundwork bears its first result: civilian Council approval for **Survey Epoch Seven-Alpha** — an expanded survey authorization approaching the former Neutral Zone boundary. This is not a penetration of the zone. It is a formal authorization to survey the outer edge of the volume that the *Kestrel* observed. Harwick's recommendation is accepted — two years late, but accepted.
- The framing of Seven-Alpha for Council purposes: "Frontier boundary assessment." Not contact. Not investigation of inhabited volume. The word "contact" does not appear in the authorization document.
- **Calder's role in Seven-Alpha:** Calder is appointed senior advisor to the Survey Epoch Seven-Alpha planning committee. This is not a field command. It is the closest thing Rowan can offer that uses Calder's knowledge without revealing the Segment Seven Archive publicly. Calder accepts. He understands the offer.

**Y+5 to Y+7**
- Survey Epoch Seven-Alpha launches with three modified survey vessels — heavier sensor arrays than standard, reduced armament, explicit non-engagement protocols. They do not penetrate deep into former Neutral Zone territory. They approach the outer approach corridors and observe.
- **What Seven-Alpha finds:** Consistent with Thessaly's report. Evidence of maintained infrastructure at ranges consistent with a large, established civilization. No direct contact. No communication attempt authorized under Seven-Alpha protocols.
- Rowan receives Seven-Alpha's preliminary data. He now has confirmation from two independent sources (Thessaly/Calder's original report and the Seven-Alpha surveys) that the volume beyond Fleet's frontier is inhabited by a peer civilization.
- **The categorization war begins:** Seven-Alpha data triggers a formal request from Scout Command to create a new contact classification category in Edition 143. The Academy doctrine committee receives the request and forms a working group. Commander Wasse chairs it. The working group's first meeting is attended by twelve instructors; by the third meeting, it has twenty-three — every senior instructor in the building has an opinion about whether this is a contact scenario or an inhabited volume scenario or something else entirely.
- Thessaly is invited to address the working group. Her presentation does not go smoothly. She is correct. The instructors defending Edition 143 are also correct, within their framework. Neither side can prove the other wrong from within the existing category system. This is precisely what her paper argued.

**Y+7 to Y+10**
- The Edition 144 Committee is formally constituted at Fleet Academy — the working group elevated into an official revision process. It is expected to take two years. It takes seven.
- **Political pressure on Rowan:** By Y+8, civilian Fleet Council members who received early informal briefings are asking for formal responses. The language they use is carefully neutral: "frontier survey results," "new contact protocols," "Epoch Seven-Alpha outcomes." None of them use the words peer civilization. All of them know that is what they are not saying.
- Rowan's navigation: he is managing three competing pressures simultaneously — military command pushing for defined doctrine before contact authorization, political authority pushing for a defined diplomatic frame before public disclosure, and his own intelligence picture which grows clearer every year while remaining politically unusable.
- **Calder's career at Y+7:** Promoted to Fleet Captain, assigned to command of a second-tier survey cruiser. Not a punishment — a genuinely respected appointment. But it is also a position that keeps him away from the classified Segment Seven Archive debate without placing him in a command that might encounter the frontier.
- **Thessaly's trajectory:** By Y+9, "Limitations of Fleet Terminology" has been cited in four internal doctrine papers and two Academy thesis documents. She has become a known quantity in doctrine circles — not celebrated, but impossible to ignore. Her next paper begins to circulate in draft: "Provisional Classification Framework for Inhabited Frontier Volumes" — an attempt to build the new category structure from first principles.

## THE DOMINION — Phase 2

**Y+3 to Y+5**
- Petrov presents his full pattern analysis to Stratimirov at Y+3+2 months: thirty-two independent observations. Stratimirov authorizes **Expanded Passive Observation — K-17 to K-31 Spine Extension**. This adds eight passive sensor nodes along the border approach geometry. No active interrogation. Passive preferred.
- The SSD's institutional read on Fleet's response: **RBGA-1** conclusion confirmed. Fleet's institutional paralysis is real. It is not strategic silence — it is genuine category failure. The Dominion has watched Fleet Authority long enough to know the difference. Fleet innovates quickly and categorizes slowly. The absence of a follow-on response in three years means the *Kestrel*'s report was received but not processed.
- This matters strategically. If Fleet cannot process the report, it cannot escalate the report. That gives the Dominion time. The Leviathan program's cloak refinement continues without interference.

**Y+5 to Y+7**
- **Leviathan Mark II prototype:** Science Directorate and Vex's technical assessment team achieve a functional second-generation cloaking field model at Y+6+4 months. The interference profile that caused the partial failure during the original trial has been identified and partially suppressed. "Partially" is the word that bothers the Science Directorate lead; "suppressed" is the word that satisfies the SSD. The two directorates' debate about what "solved" means will continue for years.
- Vex's position in the program has expanded beyond his original consultation mandate. He is now effectively the institutional memory of what happens when the cloak meets a real Fleet sensor environment. No one else in the Dominion has that experience. He cannot be replaced. He is not formally restored to field command, but his advisory rank carries operational weight.
- **Kovat at Y+5:** Promoted to Watch Supervisor, K-17. The expanded passive array falls under her operational responsibility. Her daughter Nika is now old enough to help stake the garden for district festival visitors. Kovat is eleven years into border work and shows no interest in leaving. Her supervisors have stopped suggesting she apply for a posting closer to the capital. They have accepted that she is exactly where she should be.
- **Volkova's corridor geometry program:** By Y+6, the route balance variance on K-17 spine that Volkova flagged has resolved itself — the logistics surge from the cloak trial acceleration has worked through the network. But in resolving it, Volkova noticed a structural inefficiency in how the northern approach corridors handle competing military and commercial priority windows. She files a routing improvement proposal. It is approved. She does not know that the approval comes with a note from SSD's logistics liaison: *this improvement is also useful for sustained military throughput.*

**Y+7 to Y+10**
- **Stratimirov's retirement:** At Y+8, Lev Stratimirov steps down as Director-General of the Strategic Security Directorate. He is seventy-one years old. His five-decade career has encompassed the entire period during which the Dominion elevated preparation doctrine from institutional habit to strategic framework. His retirement ceremony is private. There is no public announcement; Strategic Security Directorate leadership transitions are not public events.
- His successor: **Director-General Yara Petrov** — the same Petrov who presented the pattern scatter analysis. She is fifty-two, precision-minded, and carries forward the thirty-observations methodology as institutional doctrine. Stratimirov's closing guidance to her, delivered in his office over tea: *The preparation honors those who come after us. Your job is to ensure those who come after us inherit something worth preserving.*
- The Leviathan program records are transferred under Petrov's authority. She reviews the *Kestrel* incident file in full for the first time. Her assessment matches Stratimirov's but is stated differently: Fleet is going to find the words eventually. When they do, they will act. The question is whether we are ready before they find the words.
- **Internal Dominion debates:** The Science Directorate pushes for a formal strategic assessment study on what Fleet contact protocols might look like — not because they advocate for contact, but because understanding Fleet's likely approach is operationally useful. Petrov authorizes **Containment Study 44-7** — an internal analysis of Fleet's historical first-contact behavior patterns and what they predict for eventual response to the segment seven data. The Science Directorate researchers have no access to the *Kestrel* archive (that remains Fleet's). They work from decades of passive observation and Fleet's own publicly observable institutional behavior.
- **Civilian life at Y+10:** Ordinary Dominion citizens in border regions have noticed a gradual tightening of certain freight priority windows near the harvest festival season — some commercial shipping is rerouted around military supply needs. Workers in the Kessik fuel network have seen overtime normalize. Shipyard families talk about accelerated hull programs. None of this is named. None of it is secret. It is simply the background texture of a civilization that has always prepared.

# PHASE 3: Y+10 to Y+20 — THE BUILD-UP

*Fleet begins to move. The Dominion watches Fleet learn to move. Both are building. Neither is ready to acknowledge the other directly.*

---

## FLEET AUTHORITY — Phase 3

**Y+10 to Y+13**
- Survey Epoch Seven-Alpha's fourth mission launches with authorization for the first **authorized approach** rather than boundary observation — vessels may enter the outer corridor geometry of the former Neutral Zone, but not the deep interior. The distinction is documented in three separate Council authorization documents to ensure legal clarity. No one is confident the distinction will remain clear under field conditions.
- **The political figures emerge:** The inhabited-volume question has been poorly kept. Within civilian Fleet Council circles, a genuine political debate is forming around two positions — not war vs. peace, but contact vs. sustained observation. The leading contact voice is Delegate **Maren Solis** (representing the Verdant Line territories), who argues that forty years of expansion into unknown space and the absence of any organized opposition is evidence that contact is safe and necessary for Fleet's economic future. The leading observation voice is Admiral **Gregor Vane**, a military council appointee who argues that the volume's scale and maintenance evidence suggests a civilization with capabilities that warrant extended strategic observation before any acknowledgment. Both are making rational arguments.
- Calder, now a senior Fleet Captain commanding the survey cruiser *ISV Halvard*, is assigned to coordinate the first deep-corridor approach missions. He is not the public face of the Seven-Alpha effort. He is the person Rowan trusts to bring back accurate data.

**Y+13 to Y+17**
- **Edition 144 published at Y+15** — five years behind schedule, still incomplete by Thessaly's standard. The Edition 144 debate produces an imperfect compromise: a new appendix category called **Frontier Contact Reserve (FCR)**, which acknowledges that a scouted volume may contain inhabited structures requiring contact classification protocols different from standard first-contact scenarios. The text is written to be technically correct while avoiding the specific claim that a peer civilization has been identified. Edition 143 instructors at the Academy teach the appendix; they do not always teach it with enthusiasm.
- Thessaly, now a Fleet Doctrine Consultant attached to the Academy's Strategic Studies division, considers Edition 144 an inadequate half-measure. She is correct. She does not say so publicly. She begins work on what will become "Inhabited Volume Protocol — A Structural Proposal" — a document she does not expect to see implemented in her career, but which she writes anyway.
- **Calder's rank:** Promoted to Rear Admiral at Y+15. He is now senior enough to attend strategic planning sessions. Not senior enough to control them. He is present at the first classified Council briefing in which Delegate Solis and Admiral Vane argue directly in front of Rowan about whether to authorize contact signals.
- **Scout Command's position:** Recommend a five-year observation posture before any contact authorization — the inhabited volume is too large, too well-maintained, and too close to the Neutral Zone boundary for uncontrolled contact. Harwick's successor, Rear Admiral Elara Doss (no relation to *Kestrel* crew member Kellar Doss), makes this recommendation formally at Y+14. It is accepted for a three-year period. Both sides know three years will run short.

**Y+17 to Y+20**
- The observation/contact debate intensifies at the political level. Delegate Solis gains two additional Council allies. Admiral Vane's coalition remains dominant in military command but loses two civilian supporters. Rowan navigates this by staying out of the public debate and controlling information flow — the Segment Seven Archive remains his leverage. What the political figures are arguing about is, in institutional terms, a report they have never fully read.
- **Thessaly's institutional influence:** By Y+18, her "Inhabited Volume Protocol" draft is circulating among Edition 144 committee members, some of whom are now treating it as the conceptual foundation for a future Edition 145. She is forty-six years old and has spent fifteen years correcting a doctrine system that was not built for what she saw.
- **The Kestrel crew by Y+20:** Calder is Rear Admiral, attached to Strategic Planning. Thessaly is Fleet Doctrine Consultant, influence growing. Most of the *Kestrel*'s junior crew have passed through normal career arcs — some promoted, some retired, some on station duty. The ship itself, *Kestrel* (ISV-44), has been decommissioned. The name will not be reused. [Series question: Who authorizes the next vessel to carry that name, and when?]
- **Rowan at Y+20:** Fleet Admiral Rowan, now in his seventies, is navigating a twenty-year holding pattern that he himself built. He has kept the segment seven finding contained, escalated it carefully, and is watching the political conditions for contact authorization finally mature — while simultaneously aging out of the position in which he can control the process. He begins to think about succession.

## THE DOMINION — Phase 3

**Y+10 to Y+15**
- **The Deepline Installation Program** begins at Y+10: a systematic upgrade of border stations from K-17 through K-47, including hardened observation nodes, expanded logistics staging capability, and personnel billet increases. The program is civilian-coded (Transportation Directorate and Industrial Directorate joint administration) and military-funded (SSD line item). Irina Volkova's sector coordination role in the program earns her a commendation at Y+12 for reducing the freight priority conflict rate during the construction phase.
- **Containment Study 44-7 findings** (Science Directorate, Y+11): Fleet's historical first-contact behavior pattern suggests a three-phase institutional arc — observation paralysis, doctrine revision, contact authorization. The Science Directorate estimates Fleet is currently in the middle of phase one transitioning to phase two. The study recommends that Dominion preparation posture be configured to complete the current build cycle before Fleet reaches contact authorization. Timeline estimate: ten to twenty years, depending on Fleet's internal political dynamics.
- Petrov receives the study. Her margin note on the timeline estimate is characteristically brief: *The keel goes down while the weather is still kind.*
- **Kovat at Y+12:** Senior Watch Supervisor. The K-17 border station's plaque wall has gained two new entries since she arrived — officers who completed long service at the station. She has helped write both. Her daughter Nika is in secondary school, studying geology. Kovat does not encourage her toward border service. She does not discourage it either.

**Y+15 to Y+20**
- **Stratimirov's legacy:** At Y+16, the SSD formally names its annual strategic review cycle **the Stratimirov Assessment** — not a monument, not a public award, but an internal institutional acknowledgment. The name appears on classified briefing covers. Petrov did not request this. The staff proposed it. She approved it without comment.
- **Leviathan program expansion:** Science Directorate begins scoping **Leviathan Mark III** at Y+15 — not a new hull, but a full cloaking field system rebuild incorporating every lesson from the Y+6 Mark II development. The Mark III program scope is classified above everything except the Stratimirov Assessment itself. Vex is formally named Program Advisor, Mark III. He is now in his late fifties and has spent fifteen years making the mistake that happened on the day of the cloak trial into something that cannot happen again.
- **Internal Dominion political pressure:** A minority faction within Civil Government — representing the industrial worlds of the Kessik corridor, whose freight disruption has been ongoing for a decade — begins asking formally whether the sustained military priority window that has been affecting commercial shipping since Y+1 has an end date. Petrov's SSD does not answer in writing. A senior Civil Government liaison meets with Volkova's sector director to discuss "long-term planning horizons." The question is answered by implication: there is no end date yet defined.
- **The Long Watch Directive (Y+17):** Petrov issues **SSD Standing Order 77 — Sustained Observation Posture (Border Geometry)**, colloquially referred to within SSD as the **Long Watch Directive**. It formalizes the passive observation structure, establishes the K-spine node network as permanent infrastructure, and codifies border station command authority for elevated-confidence contact scenarios. It is, among other things, Kovat's work raised to doctrine.
- **Civilian life at Y+18-20:** Dominion citizens living near the transit corridors have adapted to preparation as background condition. Harvest festival seasons now routinely include mention in schoolroom lessons of the "long service" families have given to border maintenance — not war preparation, which is never named, but service as such. Teachers use the established proverbs. Grandmothers use the established proverbs. The proverbs are not changing. That is the point.

# PHASE 4: Y+20 to Y+30 — FIRST CONTACT PROTOCOLS

*The category problem is solved badly but well enough. Both civilizations finally speak to the same fact. Neither is ready for what follows.*

---

## FLEET AUTHORITY — Phase 4

**Y+20 to Y+23**
- Rowan authorizes the drafting of **Provisional Contact Doctrine 6 (PCD-6)** — the first Fleet document in institutional history to acknowledge, in classified text, that an inhabited peer-civilization-scale volume has been confirmed beyond Fleet's frontier. The document is technically a protocol draft. Politically it is an admission that the eighteen years since the *Kestrel*'s return have produced a known fact.
- Delegate Solis secures Council approval for contact signal authorization at Y+21 — barely. The vote is eleven to nine. The dissenting nine vote against not because they deny the inhabited volume's existence, but because they believe Fleet is not prepared for the response. They are not wrong. They lose anyway.
- **The first intentional signal at Y+22:** A coded, long-range, passive broadcast in the direction of the former Neutral Zone. Not a hail. A structured information packet — navigational data, an acknowledgment of the volume's existence, a request for reciprocal communication on an agreed frequency. Fleet Intelligence Bureau's civilian chief describes it privately as "the most important letter in Fleet's history sent on a frequency we have no certainty anyone is monitoring." [Note: From the Dominion's perspective, they have been monitoring.]

**Y+23 to Y+27**
- **The response arrives at Y+23+11 months** — structured, geometrically coherent, identifiable as a reciprocal communication. Not a translation. Not a spoken language. A mathematical acknowledgment that tells Fleet: the signal was received; the sender is capable of structured response; the sender is not announcing hostility. Fleet Intelligence Bureau spends eight months analyzing the response before concluding it is genuine.
- **The name:** Embedded in the response signal's metadata structure is a term that FIB linguists identify as a self-referent — the civilization's name for itself. It takes three additional months to extract the phonetic rendering: **Dominion**. Fleet knows the name for the first time at Y+24+7 months.
- The institutional reaction to the name is peculiar. Rowan had prepared himself for the discovery that there was someone out there. He had not prepared himself for discovering that the name felt — to FIB historians who pulled the deep archive — like a term that should have already been familiar. The Neutral Zone records contain the faintest etymological echo. It is not conclusive. It is deeply unsettling. [Author decision pending: how directly does the etymological echo connect to the breakaway founding history, and in which book does Fleet formally rediscover it?]
- **Calder at Y+25:** Rear Admiral Venn is the senior officer most associated with the original *Kestrel* observation. When the contact confirmation becomes known at classified levels, he is brought to briefings as — in the language of one political aide's memo — "the witness who first understood the scale of what was there." Calder does not consider himself a witness-authority. He considers himself a scout captain whose report took twenty-five years to be processed. He says this, carefully, in classified settings. It does not produce the institutional self-reflection he intends.
- **The Aldor Commission (Y+26):** Fleet Council constitutes a formal contact review body — **The Contact Assessment Commission**, chaired by Vice Admiral Sera Aldor (Strategic Planning), charged with producing a formal diplomatic engagement framework. The commission has twelve months and produces a 400-page preliminary framework in fourteen months. Thessaly is one of three doctrine consultants attached to the commission. She considers the framework adequate in structure and inadequate in cultural preparation. She writes a dissenting annex. The annex is included.

**Y+27 to Y+30**
- **Partial public disclosure at Y+27:** Fleet Council authorizes a controlled public statement acknowledging that survey operations near the Neutral Zone have confirmed the presence of an established neighboring civilization and that preliminary communication protocols have been established. The statement uses the term "neighboring civilization" — a deliberate choice by Aldor Commission linguists who argued that "alien" carried conquest-era connotations while "neighbor" implied a claim the Dominion had not made. The statement does not name the Dominion publicly. [Author decision pending: in which book does "Dominion" become public knowledge in Fleet territories?]
- **The *Kestrel* archive declassified in part (Y+27+9 months):** Rowan, near retirement, authorizes partial declassification of the Segment Seven Archive — the mission structure, the observation framework, and Thessaly's original category-failure analysis. Calder's personal debriefs and the Leviathan cloak field effect documentation remain classified. The archive enters the Fleet Historical Office as document reference **HO-7A: Initial Inhabited Volume Observation, ISV Kestrel.**
- **The Meridian Hearings (Y+28):** Fleet civilian Council holds its first public sessions on "frontier expansion policy in light of confirmed inhabited volumes." Calder testifies in open session. His testimony is carefully controlled — he describes what segment seven looked like, not what intelligence analysis concluded. He is asked whether he was afraid. He says: *I was uncertain. Those are different things.* The phrase is quoted in Fleet media for years. It is correct. It also becomes a myth-fragment that misrepresents what the incident was, in ways Calder spends the next twenty years quietly correcting.
- **The Provisional Coexistence Framework (Y+28-Y+29):** Negotiated through relay channel — not an embassy, not a meeting, but an exchange of structured documents establishing that both parties acknowledge the other's existence and agree to avoid active hostility in shared frontier corridors while formal diplomatic contact is prepared. This is the first formal arrangement between Fleet Authority and the Dominion in centuries. Neither party calls it a treaty. Neither party has authority to call it binding. It exists, and both parties observe it for different reasons.

## THE DOMINION — Phase 4

**Y+20 to Y+23**
- Petrov's SSD has anticipated Fleet's contact authorization for roughly three years — Containment Study 44-7 predicted the timeline correctly. When Fleet's intentional signal arrives in Y+22, the SSD's response protocol is already drafted. It was drafted at Y+19.
- **The response decision (Y+22):** This is one of the genuine political divisions of the period. Civil Government argues for immediate formal diplomatic engagement — the Dominion is well-prepared; contact on Dominion's terms is better than contact on Fleet's terms; the strategic advantage of appearing stable and confident is real. The SSD argues for a minimal, structured acknowledgment that does not reveal Dominion's institutional awareness of Fleet — maintaining intelligence asymmetry as long as possible. The Science Directorate argues, on different grounds, that the response should be designed to learn more than it reveals.
- The compromise: the response that is sent at Y+23+11 months from Fleet's perspective is an acknowledgment without context, technically honest (it confirms the sender's existence and rational capacity), strategically opaque (it reveals nothing about Dominion governance, military doctrine, or preparation status), and designed to be ambiguous about how long the Dominion has been watching. The civilian population is not told a contact signal was sent. The harvest festival season passes without public announcement.

**Y+23 to Y+27**
- **Internal debate acceleration:** Having made first contact, the Dominion must now manage what follows. The SSD and Civil Government produce competing policy memos at Y+24. The SSD position: structured engagement through relay channel only; no embassy; no face-to-face diplomacy; maintain intelligence advantage. Civil Government position: structured engagement through relay channel, with a parallel track toward eventual formal diplomatic presence. The debate is genuinely unresolved for three years. Both tracks proceed simultaneously at half-speed.
- **What the civilian population is told (Y+25):** A brief Civil Government public communication — not a broadcast, but a managed disclosure through official institutional channels — confirms that the "neighboring corridor presence" observed at the border periphery has been formally acknowledged. Citizens who have lived near border regions have known something was being prepared for decades. The announcement is received with less shock than Civil Government anticipated. Several veterans' café conversations that evening recycle the same proverb: *Preparation honors those who come after us.* They mean it as acknowledgment, not gloating.
- **Vex at Y+25:** Now 62 years old. Has spent twenty-five years at the intersection of the Leviathan program and SSD intelligence. His name appears in almost no public records. Within SSD and Science Directorate circles, he is known as the officer who first met a Fleet vessel under real conditions. His assessments of Fleet tactical behavior — based on that one encounter and twenty-five years of analysis — are considered the best in the Dominion. He remains assigned where useful. He has never returned to field command. [Series question: Does Vex return to field command, and what triggers it?]
- **Diplomatic channel opened (Y+26):** The relay-channel exchange that follows the Provisional Coexistence Framework negotiations involves, on the Dominion side, a diplomatic team drawn from Civil Government's Frontier Affairs division. Petrov's SSD provides classified support (intelligence product sanitized to appear as normal diplomatic briefing material) without being visible in the channel. The Dominion's diplomatic posture is calibrated to appear cooperative without being transparent. Every offer of information about Dominion governance, geography, or capability is accurate at the level provided and incomplete at the level not asked.

**Y+27 to Y+30**
- **The Provisional Coexistence Framework — Dominion reading:** The Dominion signs the Framework through its Civil Government apparatus. Civil Government calls it a stability instrument. SSD calls it a preparation window. Military command calls it a period of managed strategic patience. All three are correct. None of the three interpretations is shared with the other party.
- **Domestic reception at Y+28-29:** Dominion citizens in the K-spine border regions — the ones whose harvest festivals have included freight delays and border station expansion for twenty-eight years — receive the Framework's existence as confirmation of what many had assumed: the corridor neighbors are real, acknowledged, and not currently hostile. The relief is not celebration. It is more like the satisfaction of a long-held professional expectation being formally met. Workers at the Kessik corridor ask, not for the first time, whether the sustained military freight priority will now ease. The answer, internally, is no. Publicly, it is: the situation is being monitored.
- **Petrov's strategic assessment at Y+29:** The SSD's annual Stratimirov Assessment concludes that the accommodation period — however long it lasts — is the best preparation time the Dominion will have. Fleet is currently absorbing the institutional shock of confirmed contact. Fleet's political leadership is managing public disclosure. Fleet's doctrine infrastructure is in the middle of the transition from Edition 144 to what will eventually become something more comprehensive. This is not the moment for Dominion escalation. This is the moment for the Deepline program's second construction phase, Leviathan Mark III's final development cycle, and the Transit Spine Seven expansion — all of which can proceed under the diplomatic cover of normal infrastructure operation.

# PHASE 5: Y+30 to Y+40 — INSTITUTIONAL ACCOMMODATION & PRESSURE

*Both civilizations are adjusting to the existence of the other. Adjustment is not resolution. The gap between accommodation and preparation grows each year both sides choose not to name it.*

## FLEET AUTHORITY — Phase 5

**Y+30 to Y+35**
- Rowan retires at Y+31. His successor as Fleet Admiral is **Elara Doss** — the same Rear Admiral who gave Scout Command's five-year observation recommendation at Y+14. Doss is 58 years old, operationally experienced, and carries the full intelligence picture that Rowan spent three decades building. She does not share Rowan's particular talent for political navigation, but she is a better strategist. This will matter.
- **Calder's retirement (Y+31):** Fleet Admiral Venn — promoted to full admiral in a ceremony notable for its brevity — retires from active Fleet service at Y+31. His retirement statement is three paragraphs. He makes one public remark that the media records: *Segment seven was the beginning of an understanding that will outlast everyone in this room. I hope we deserve it.* He is sixty-one years old. The remark is quoted; the mission remains partially classified; most Fleet citizens do not know what "segment seven" means.
- **The *Kestrel* Archive becomes a teaching document:** Fleet Historical Office begins incorporating HO-7A as assigned reading in Fleet Academy's Strategic Studies advanced seminar — not the full classified record, but the observation framework and the category-failure analysis. Thessaly's "Limitations of Fleet Terminology" paper is, for the first time, required reading in an official Academy course. She teaches it herself twice, as a visiting instructor, before her health declines.
- **New ship classes (Y+32-35):** Fleet constitutes the **Observation-Cruiser class** — survey-capable vessels with heavier sensor arrays, extended range, independent diplomatic communication systems, and reduced combat profile. The class is formally designated for "inhabited volume interface operations." Twelve hulls are authorized. Three are commissioned by Y+35. The class represents the institutionalization of what the *Kestrel* did informally — observing without engaging.
- **The economic relationship:** Limited exchange through relay channel begins at Y+33 — not trade in any meaningful sense, but technical data exchange agreements covering navigational hazards in shared frontier corridors. This is the minimum that can occur given that both civilizations are operating vessels in overlapping space. It is also the foundation from which more substantial exchange could grow. Whether it does depends on political decisions that have not been made.

**Y+35 to Y+40**
- **The colony expansion debate:** Fleet's expansion ideology has not changed. The inhabited volume's confirmation has, however, produced a direct confrontation between the forward-expansion logic and the reality that the frontier volume adjacent to the Dominion is not available for colonization on Fleet's standard terms. Delegate Solis's faction — now ascendant in the Council — argues for a negotiated colony buffer framework that would exchange Fleet's recognition of Dominion frontier geometry for Dominion's formal recognition of Fleet's colonization right in mid-rim territories. Admiral Doss's military command argues this would be giving away strategic depth Fleet does not yet know it needs.
- **Thessaly's death (Y+37):** Fleet Doctrine Consultant Mira Thessaly dies at sixty-five, following an illness that had reduced her teaching to correspondence work for the final two years. Fleet Academy holds a public memorial — one of the few times an institutional doctrine figure receives a public ceremony. Her dissenting annex to the Aldor Commission report is read at the memorial. Edition 145 of the Fleet Doctrine Manual, then in early draft, is dedicated to her in a footnote that takes the committee eighteen months to agree on.
- **Calder's role in the memorial:** He speaks for six minutes. He says she wrote the paper the institution needed before the institution knew it needed it. This is the most direct public statement either of them has ever made about what happened in segment seven. The room understands. The archive classification means most people in the room only partially understand. [Author decision pending: When is the *full* Segment Seven Archive declassified, and what does it change?]

## THE DOMINION — Phase 5

**Y+30 to Y+35**
- Petrov is 76 years old at Y+30 and showing signs of the fatigue that comes from fifteen years at the head of the SSD. She has no intention of retiring before the Leviathan Mark III program is complete. She did not say this aloud. Everyone who works with her knows it.
- **Leviathan Mark III commissioning (Y+35):** The first full Mark III-specification Leviathan-class hull with operational cloaking field capability is commissioned in a ceremony that is, by Dominion custom, classified above SECRET and attended only by SSD, Science Directorate leadership, and the hull's command staff. The hull's name is not published in any accessible record. Vex attends. He removes his gloves before signing the commissioning documentation. He is 71 years old. This is the thing he has spent thirty years making possible.
- The Mark III's cloaking field performance under passive Fleet-profile sensor simulation achieves a contact suppression rate that the Science Directorate describes as operationally sufficient. The SSD describes it as the minimum acceptable threshold. Neither description is wrong.

**Y+35 to Y+40**
- **Petrov's retirement (Y+37):** Yara Petrov retires after fifteen years as Director-General. Her retirement ceremony is private. Her successor is appointed from within SSD's border intelligence division — **Director-General Cassian Holt**, 54 years old, who came up through the K-spine passive observation program. He studied Kovat's original filing methodology. He met Kovat once, at a border command review. He regards her as one of the genuine practitioners of what SSD calls sustained watch discipline.
- **The civilian accommodation:** By Y+38, the harvest festival seasons have fully normalized the existence of "corridor neighbors" in Dominion public culture. School history lessons cover the acknowledgment period (Y+22-28) as a chapter in the ongoing story of frontier management — not a crisis, not a breakthrough, but a managed development in Dominion's long relationship with its strategic geometry. Children who grew up during the accommodation period treat the neighbors' existence as background fact. They do not share the older generation's certainty that the background fact is also a strategic threat. This generational difference produces its first institutional friction at Y+38-39, when several junior Civil Government appointees propose a more open diplomatic exchange framework than the SSD will accept.
- **The cost question:** Thirty-eight years of sustained military freight priority, Deepline expansion, Leviathan program funding, and passive array maintenance have produced measurable strain on the commercial freight network. Volkova's successor at Sector Nine Transportation — she retired at Y+33 after completing the Corridor Redundancy Program — wrote a formal report noting that commercial shipping capacity in the K-17 to K-47 corridor region has declined 12% relative to pre-Y+0 baseline due to sustained military priority windows. The SSD's response: the capacity shortfall is acceptable. The SSD does not use the word *acceptable* in writing. They use the phrase *within operational parameters*.
- **Diplomatic channels as intelligence channels:** The relay-channel diplomatic exchange that has been running since Y+26 is, from Dominion perspective, also one of the most productive intelligence collection operations in SSD history. Fleet's diplomatic communications reveal institutional priorities, capability gaps, political debate positions, and cultural self-understanding in ways that passive border observation never could. The Dominion has not told Fleet this. It is not the sort of thing one tells.

# PHASE 6: Y+40 to Y+50 — THE SLOW PRESSURE

*Forty years of contact without resolution. The accommodation holds because neither side is ready to end it. The accommodation strains because neither side believes it will last.*

---

## FLEET AUTHORITY — Phase 6

**Y+40 to Y+45**
- **Leadership generational shift:** Fleet Admiral Doss retires at Y+41. Her successor, **Fleet Admiral Renen Holt** (no relation to Dominion's Cassian Holt), is the first Fleet Admiral who was not an officer during the original *Kestrel* period. He was a junior lieutenant when partial disclosure of the inhabited volume occurred. For him, the Dominion's existence is institutional background, not revelation. This changes how he approaches doctrine in ways the older generation finds pragmatic and the pre-contact generation finds unsettling.
- **Calder at Y+42:** Seventy-two years old. Retired eleven years. He is occasionally consulted by Fleet historians and, when the political situation requires credibility, by Council staff. He has become, against his preference, a symbol — "the first Fleet officer to see them." His actual contribution — the observation methodology, the report structure, the category-failure he identified in real time — has been partially obscured by the myth. He gives an annual lecture at Fleet Academy, which he has been doing since Y+33. Enrollment in the lecture is consistently one of the highest in the advanced seminar program. He always opens it the same way: *The observation was ordinary. What it meant took twenty years to categorize. The next observation will be ordinary too. Your job is not to miss it.*
- **The "segment seven generation" retires:** The officers who came of age during the *Kestrel* period — who debated Edition 144, ran the Seven-Alpha surveys, staffed the Aldor Commission — have largely retired or transitioned to civilian roles by Y+43. Their institutional memory retires with them in ways that archives cannot fully capture. The Fleet Historical Office holds HO-7A. The documents are accurate. The texture of what it felt like to not have categories — that is harder to preserve.
- **Public debate intensifies (Y+43-45):** Fleet civilian populations in frontier territories — particularly the worlds nearest the former Neutral Zone boundary — have lived for twenty years with the diplomatic framework's promise that the corridor neighbors are acknowledged and non-hostile. Those worlds also notice the Dominion infrastructure that is visible through commercial transit observation: maintained border stations, expanded logistics nodes, freight corridor geometry that looks, to experienced transit operators, like a network designed for sustained high-throughput operations. The words "what are they building" begin appearing in frontier world media at Y+44. It is not the first time the question has been asked. It is the first time it has been asked publicly in a way that reaches central Fleet civilian media.

**Y+45 to Y+50**
- **The military readiness debate (Y+46-48):** Fleet military command has been watching Dominion infrastructure expansion through observation-cruiser operations and diplomatic channel inference for twenty years. Admiral Holt's Y+47 strategic review — the most serious since Rowan's original classified escalation — concludes that Fleet's current military posture is inadequate relative to what the Dominion appears to be building. This conclusion is classified. It leaks.
- **The Council floor debate (Y+48):** The first open Fleet Council debate on Dominion military capacity in the history of the Provisional Coexistence Framework. It is not a war debate. It is a readiness debate. But the language of a readiness debate and the language of a war debate look similar to populations watching it from frontier worlds. The diplomatic channel to the Dominion is not formally interrupted. Relations are described, officially, as "stable and under active monitoring."
- **The *Kestrel* archive question:** At Y+49, a Fleet Council motion proposes full declassification of the Segment Seven Archive — including the Leviathan cloak field effect data that has been classified since Y+0. The motion fails, seven to thirteen. The classified field effect data is the only evidence in Fleet's possession that the Dominion possessed cloaking technology fifty years ago. Fleet military command does not want that evidence in the public domain before understanding whether it is still current. [Series question: Is it current? What did the Leviathan program become across fifty years?]
- **Y+50 state:** No war. The accommodation holds in formal terms. The Provisional Coexistence Framework has been renewed three times without substantial renegotiation. Frontier world populations are asking questions that the diplomatic framework was not designed to answer. Fleet military command believes Fleet is behind. Fleet political authority believes engagement continues to be preferable to confrontation. Both assessments are correct. They are also incompatible.
- [Series question: What is the specific trigger — political, military, or incident — that ends the accommodation period? The archive suggests it will be something ordinary misread through inadequate categories. That has happened before.]

## THE DOMINION — Phase 6

**Y+40 to Y+45**
- **Cassian Holt's SSD at Y+40-44:** Holt inherited from Petrov an SSD that is the most thoroughly prepared intelligence organization in Dominion history. The K-spine passive arrays now cover the full frontier geometry. The diplomatic channel intelligence product runs continuously. The Leviathan Mark III operational capability is confirmed. Holt's question, which he asks at every annual Stratimirov Assessment, is not whether the Dominion is ready. It is whether readiness maintained at this level can be sustained without an endpoint.
- **The Leviathan Mark IV program (Y+43):** Authorized at Y+43. Not a cloaking field improvement — the Mark III field performance is considered operationally adequate. Mark IV is a hull generation program: increased endurance, improved command redundancy, and integrated cloaking field as a standard system rather than a classified add-on. The program means the Dominion is no longer developing stealth as exceptional capability. It is normalizing it. [Series question: Does Fleet discover the Mark IV program, and if so, through which channel?]
- **Vex at Y+43:** Eighty years old. He has retired from formal SSD advisory roles. He lives near one of the historic shipyard districts — a neighborhood where families have worked ship construction for generations. He still plays chess. He occasionally speaks to the Science Directorate's Leviathan program staff when they seek historical context for decisions that have no documentation because the original trial was classified above the current staff's clearance level. He is, in the truest sense, irreplaceable institutional memory. He is also eighty years old.

**Y+45 to Y+50**
- **The 50-year cost question becomes public (Y+47):** A Civil Government transparency commission — staffed by the same junior-generation appointees who proposed more open diplomacy at Y+38 — publishes a freight capacity analysis that reaches essentially the same conclusion Volkova's successor reached at Y+37, but with fifty years of data instead of thirty-eight. Commercial shipping capacity in the K-17 to K-47 corridor region has declined 18% relative to pre-Y+0 baseline. The report does not mention military preparation. The report does not need to. Citizens who have grown up near the freight corridors understand the implication.
- **The internal debate:** This produces the most visible Dominion internal political disagreement in the 50-year arc. Civil Government's frontier-region representatives argue that fifty years of sustained preparation has a legitimate cost question — not whether preparation is right, but whether its benefits are being distributed fairly relative to its costs. SSD's institutional position: the preparation is the benefit. These are not reconcilable arguments. They will not be resolved by the Y+50 mark.
- **What the Dominion has built in fifty years:** The accounting is real, even if the purpose remains unannounced. Deepline border station network: complete. K-spine passive observation array: operational K-17 through K-47. Kessik and Transit Spine fuel and logistics infrastructure: at sustained-operations capacity. Leviathan-class hull inventory: classified. Cloaking field capability: operational, normalized in newest class. Scientific and industrial capacity: elevated and maintained. Reserve mobilization record: fifty-one active recalls at Y+0, now significantly higher at Y+50 — [Series question: exact count is an author decision that implies commitment to a specific engagement scale].
- **What ordinary Dominion citizens believe at Y+50:** Most citizens over fifty have lived their entire adult lives under preparation posture. They understand, as background fact, that they live in a civilization that has been getting ready for something. They do not know exactly what. Most do not expect to know. The proverb that teachers have been using since before they were born covers the uncertainty: *Preparation honors those who come after us.* Younger citizens — those under thirty, who grew up with "corridor neighbors" as background fact rather than revelation — are less certain the posture is still necessary. This is the political tension Holt's SSD watches most carefully. Not Fleet. The generation that has never known preparation as anything other than permanent background. [Series question: Is this generational tension a subplot in Book Two, or a later arc?]
- **Holt's Y+50 Stratimirov Assessment:** The annual review's conclusion, delivered by Holt to an SSD leadership council that includes representatives from Science, Industrial, and Military directorates: the Dominion is prepared. The strategic question is no longer whether the preparation is adequate. It is what the preparation is for, and whether the moment to use it can be chosen rather than forced. Those are different questions. The assessment ends there. *The next encounter cannot be accidental.* Stratimirov said it fifty years ago. It is still true. The difference is that fifty years ago, the Dominion was making it true. At Y+50, the Dominion has made it true — and is now waiting to understand what truth, exactly, it has prepared itself for.
