# 1. Core Principle of Solmare Combat

Fleet combat is NOT based on:

- raw firepower alone
- ship size alone
- technology superiority alone

Fleet combat is determined by:

## Three factors

1. Role advantage (ship class matchups)
2. Fleet composition (combined arms structure)
3. Positioning & initiative (who controls engagement range and vector)

---

# 2. Role-Based Combat System

Each ship class has defined strengths and counters:

---

## Light Warfare Layer

- Fighters / Ion Fighters
- Corvettes

**Role:**

- harassment
- interception
- screening
- swarm pressure

**Dominance:**

- can overwhelm large ships in coordinated groups
- highly vulnerable individually

---

## Line Warfare Layer

- Frigates
- Cruisers

**Role:**

- primary engagement line
- anti-capital pressure (frigates)
- shielded tactical command (cruisers)

**Key dynamic:**

- Frigates counter capital ships through missiles
- Cruisers counter frigates through shields + plasma systems

---

## Capital Warfare Layer

- Heavy Cruisers
- Battleships

**Role:**

- fleet anchors
- sustained engagement dominance
- anti-capital suppression

**Key dynamic:**

- Heavy Cruisers outperform Battleships in flexibility and survivability vs mixed fleets
- Battleships dominate structured capital engagements but struggle against fighters without support

---

## Strategic Warfare Layer

- Dreadnaughts
- Titans
- Leviathans
- Fleet Carriers

**Role:**

- fleet control
- battlefield shaping
- system-level dominance

**Key dynamic:**

- These ships define engagement zones, not just damage output
- Smaller fleets must react to them rather than engage directly unless conditions are favorable

---

# 3. Weapon System Logic

Weapon types follow strategic purpose:

---

## Lasers

- Fighter / Corvette standard
- High precision
- Low sustained capital damage

---

## Missiles

- Frigate primary weapon
- Effective vs capital ships
- Requires targeting solutions (can be disrupted)

---

## Plasma

- Cruiser / Heavy Cruiser systems
- Balanced damage + shield interaction

---

## Ion Weapons

- Battleship primary system
- Designed for structural disruption and capital ship disablement

---

## Photon Weapons

- Dreadnaught / Leviathan systems
- High-energy destruction
- Area dominance capability

---

## Disruptor Systems

- Titan-class weaponry
- Causes systemic failure, sensor collapse, or structural destabilization

---

# 4. Combat Behavior Rules

---

## Rule A — Ships Fight Roles, Not Individual Matchups

A ship does not win because it is "bigger."
It wins because it is deployed in the correct role against the correct target.

---

## Rule B — Support Is Critical

No capital ship operates effectively without:

- fighter screen
- sensor support
- fleet coordination

---

## Rule C — Fighters Are Force Multipliers

- Fighters do NOT win wars alone
- Fighters DO determine capital survivability
- Ion fighters in particular become decisive in late-stage engagements

---

## Rule D — Sensor Information Is a Weapon

- fleets that control targeting data control outcomes
- misinformation or delayed sensor data can reverse tactical superiority

---

## Rule E — Engagement Range Matters

- Corvettes and fighters dominate close-range chaos
- Cruisers dominate mid-range structured combat
- Dreadnaughts+ dominate long-range strategic engagement zones

---

# 5. Battle Outcome Logic

Battle results must be determined by:

1. Composition advantage
2. Tactical positioning
3. Information superiority
4. Coordination quality
5. Attrition over time

**NOT:**

- single ship superiority
- raw tonnage comparisons
- simplistic "who is stronger" logic

---

# 6. Narrative Integration Rule

All battle scenes must:

- reflect correct ship role behavior
- avoid unrealistic solo dominance of capital ships in mixed engagements
- show fleet coordination effects
- emphasize command decisions and information flow

---

# 7. Kestrel Veil Special Case (Important)

The Kestrel Veil (Scout-Class vessel):

- is NOT combat dominant
- does NOT win through firepower
- survives through:
  - speed
  - evasion
  - environmental exploitation
  - crew ingenuity
  - enemy miscalculation

The ship is NOT a battleship in disguise.

Its survival is a result of:

> doctrine-breaking outcomes under stress conditions, not superior capability.

This distinction must remain consistent in all future chapters.
