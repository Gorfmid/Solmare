# 1. Fleet Vessel Class System (Canon)

All ship classes follow this hierarchy:

---

## Scout (mission designation — not a combat rung)

- Reconnaissance / observation hull
- Typical crew: **14**
- **Smaller than corvette**
- **Fastest hull class in Fleet** (faster than corvettes and all heavier classes)
- Weapon: small laser / light defensive emitter only — **no offensive battery**
- Role: observe, report, withdraw; Segment and frontier quiet-system work; deep recon when tasked
- Survive by speed and evasion, not firepower
- Detail: `Universe/Technology/scout_class.md`

---

## Fighters

- Small strike craft
- Single pilot
- Weapon: Lasers
- Role: Dogfighting, escort, harassment

---

## Ion Fighters

- Small strike craft
- Single pilot
- Weapon: Ion cannons
- Role:
  - Excellent vs battleships in sustained fire
  - Dangerous in large swarms against titans and leviathans

---

## Corvettes

- Light warship
- Faster than frigates; **slower than scout-class**
- Typical crew: 40
- Weapon: Laser systems
- Role:
  - Anti-frigate combat
  - Fast response interceptor

---

## Frigates

- Medium-light warship
- Slower than corvettes
- Weapon: Missile systems
- Role:
  - Anti-cruiser / anti-capital harassment
  - Fleet support and screening

---

## Cruisers

- Medium capital ship
- First class to use shields
- Weapon: Plasma systems
- Carries: ~4 fighters
- Role:
  - Anti-battleship combat
  - Fleet command ships in small engagements

---

## Heavy Cruisers

- Larger and slower than cruisers
- Stronger shields and weapons
- Weapon: Plasma systems (enhanced output)
- Carries: ~8 fighters
- Role:
  - Anti-battleship and anti-dreadnaught
  - Can resist fighter attacks effectively

---

## Battleships

- Large capital ship
- Slow but heavily armed
- Weapon: Ion systems
- Carries: ~40 fighters
- Role:
  - Anti-dreadnaught and anti-titan warfare
  - Weak vs fighters unless supported

---

## Dreadnaughts

- Fortress-class warships
- Heavy base defense role
- Weapon: Photon systems
- Role:
  - Destroy frigate fleets and fighter swarms
  - Strong defensive platform

---

## Titans

- Massive warships
- Significantly larger than dreadnaughts
- Weapon: Disruptor systems
- Carries: ~200 fighters
- Role:
  - Anti-capital dominance platform
  - Can eliminate cruisers and smaller fleets easily
  - Strong fighter defense capability

---

## Leviathans

- Super-capital warships
- Extremely large strategic assets
- Weapon: Photon systems
- Carries: ~4000 fighters
- Role:
  - Fleet annihilation platforms
  - Superior vs cruisers, frigates, fighters
  - Strategic dominance units

---

## Fleet Carriers

- Roughly battleship-sized hull
- Weapon systems minimal / defensive
- Carries: ~400 fighters
- Role:
  - Fighter deployment and air superiority control

---

# 2. Design Philosophy Rules

- Smaller ships = faster, less armored, tactical roles
- Larger ships = slower, more armored, strategic dominance
- Plasma = cruiser-tier energy weapons
- Ion = anti-capital / system-disruption weapons
- Photon = heavy destructive/defensive capital weapons
- Disruptor = titan-level system-breaking weaponry

---

# 3. Narrative Usage Rules

- Ship classes should be used consistently in tactical descriptions
- Do NOT blur class roles unless explicitly describing experimental or damaged vessels
- Fleet battles must respect class hierarchy logic
- Larger ships are not automatically "better" — they are specialized
- Engagement outcomes must reflect:
  - role advantage
  - fleet composition
  - positioning
  - not raw size alone
