# 1. Core Principle

Fleet ships are NOT open environments.

They are:

- compartmentalized military structures
- layered access-control systems
- self-contained survival ecosystems

Boarding a ship is NOT entering a building.

It is entering a controlled, multi-layer defensive environment.

---

# 2. Internal Ship Structure Model

All ships share a hierarchical internal layout:

---

## Layer 1 — External Hull Zone

- armor plating
- maintenance access points
- repair corridors
- docking interfaces

**Function:**

- first breach zone
- heavily defended on capital ships
- minimal access on scouts/frigates

---

## Layer 2 — Transition / Buffer Zone

- airlocks
- decompression chambers
- cargo locks
- maintenance tunnels

**Function:**

- slows boarding forces
- enables compartment isolation
- primary containment zone during breaches

---

## Layer 3 — Functional Core Zones

Includes:

- engineering
- propulsion systems
- power distribution
- environmental control
- sensor arrays

**Function:**

- ship survival systems
- highest priority defense zones

---

## Layer 4 — Command & Operation Zones

Includes:

- bridge
- tactical control rooms
- navigation hubs
- communications arrays

**Function:**

- command hierarchy nodes
- coordination of ship-wide systems
- highest-value targets in combat

---

## Layer 5 — Habitation & Crew Zones

Includes:

- crew quarters
- mess halls
- medical bays
- recreation areas

**Function:**

- human survivability layer
- morale and endurance support
- secondary tactical importance unless compromised

---

# 3. Boarding Combat Logic

Boarding actions follow structured progression:

---

## Phase 1 — Breach

- hull penetration
- airlock compromise
- localized system failure

---

## Phase 2 — Containment Struggle

- defenders isolate sections
- bulkheads seal automatically
- corridors become choke points

---

## Phase 3 — System Warfare

- engineering sabotage
- life support control
- power redistribution battles

---

## Phase 4 — Command Hunt

- attackers attempt to locate command nodes
- defenders relocate or isolate command functions
- misinformation and internal routing confusion common

---

## Phase 5 — Control or Evacuation

- attackers seize ship control systems **OR**
- defenders purge, vent, or abandon sections

---

# 4. Size-Based Boarding Difficulty

---

## Scout–Frigate Class

- 1–3 boarding zones
- rapid containment possible
- ship can be fully cleared quickly

---

## Cruiser–Heavy Cruiser

- multiple independent compartments
- boarding becomes corridor warfare
- full clearance requires coordinated teams

---

## Battleship

- multi-deck internal warfare
- defenders can isolate entire sections
- boarding becomes prolonged siege event

---

## Dreadnaught

- internal geography behaves like small city districts
- multiple simultaneous engagements required
- full control rarely achieved quickly

---

## Titan

- layered internal command ecosystems
- boarding becomes multi-front campaign
- attackers often fight for objectives, not full control

---

## Leviathan

- internal geography treated as strategic theater
- full boarding is effectively impossible during combat operations
- engagements focus on subsystem capture or sabotage, not total control

---

# 5. Compartmentalization Rule (Critical)

All ships are designed so that:

- damage can be isolated
- sections can be sealed independently
- crew survival does not depend on single-point failure systems

This enables:

- survival after catastrophic damage
- partial ship operation during internal breaches
- realistic long-duration boarding conflicts

---

# 6. Communication Breakdown Rule

During boarding actions:

- internal comms degrade by section
- command hierarchy fragments physically
- misinformation is common due to sensor desync and compartment isolation

Higher-tier ships suffer more complex breakdown patterns.

---

# 7. Tactical Narrative Rule

All boarding scenes must:

- respect compartment boundaries
- avoid "instant ship-wide control"
- show progression through layers
- reflect increasing difficulty with ship size
- emphasize confusion, isolation, and fragmented control

---

# 8. Kestrel Veil Special Case

On scout-class vessels like the Kestrel Veil:

- limited compartments
- fast containment cycles
- crew knows entire ship layout intimately
- boarding events are short, intense, and personal

Survival depends on:

- speed of response
- familiarity with quirks
- improvisation under pressure

The ship is not defensible by scale—it is defensible by knowledge and crew cohesion.
