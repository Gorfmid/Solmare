## Ideology

Order through controlled expansion. Stability over autonomy.

**Strategic worldview (series canon):**

- Expansion creates stability; exploration creates opportunity
- New colonies create prosperity; open trade creates peace
- Unclaimed space should be explored; borders move outward naturally
- *The unknown exists to be explored.*

Fleet Authority is **young in self-mythology** — Founders' Week, Relay Charter, prospective expansion — even though the polity is ~**800 years** old. Pre-contact frontier is **uncharted / empty-on-the-chart** space, not a named Neutral Zone. After peer discovery, the Neutral Zone becomes an institutional buffer label. Dominion institutional memory retains the **breakaway founding**; Fleet public memory does not. See `fleet_dominion_frontier_history.md`.

**Cultural memory:** Fleet characters think **prospectively** — exploration, recent discoveries, new colonies, current missions, living pioneers. Dominion characters think **historically**. Show the difference; never explain it. See `Writing_System/Series_Principles/cultural_identity_and_philosophical_foundation.md`.

**Cultural touchstones (series canon):** `Universe/cultural_touchstones.md` — Founders' Week, blessing procession, Second Sunrise, academy customs, *"Someone has to go first."*, and related Fleet texture.

**Dominion proverbs (series canon):** *"Preparation honors those who come after us."* · *"Conquest is what other people call it when sacrifice succeeds."* — `Universe/The_Dominion/dominion_worldview.md`.

## Military Doctrine
- Hierarchical command structure
- Risk-averse engagement policy for scouts
- Information centralization is mandatory

## Strengths
- Largest coordinated fleet network
- Superior logistics and resupply chains

## Weaknesses
- Slow decision cycles
- Overreliance on data integrity assumptions

## Hidden Tension
Internal fragmentation between scientific exploration division and strategic command.

## Governance note

The UFA is not a single centralized command. Political authority, military command, and field operational command are intentionally separated — and conflict under crisis. See full governance document.
