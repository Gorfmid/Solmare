## Continuum

Training does not end at the sword ceremony. Fleet expects continuation courses, qualification renewals, and scenario refreshers throughout a career. The Academy is the confidence engine; the fleet is the proof.

| Phase | Owner | Notes |
|-------|-------|-------|
| Pre-commission | Academy system | Simulation primacy; specialization tracks |
| First-tour qualification | Ship / depot | Station quals; local standing orders |
| Specialty schools | Shore commands | Intel, logistics, tactical, medical, etc. |
| Command pipeline | Fleet Command / Academy liaison | XO/CO prep; CQB-4 package |
| Doctrine refresh | Doctrine Bureau | Edition deltas; ROE updates |

Coffee is allowed in most classrooms. Bluffing on a watch board is not.

## Simulation vs hull

*"Bridges teach command before hulls do."* Field training then teaches what simulation cannot: vibration, crew fatigue, cache lies, and paperwork that arrives late.

Failure in sim is data. Refusal to decide is not. The same ethic applies to tabletop ROE drills ashore and to damage-control evolutions that smell like real smoke.

Notable Academy scenarios that reappear in fleet refreshers: The Fourteen Hours · Contact Protocol Seven · The Relay Dark · The Vellor Pair · The Long March. See [Fleet Academy](Fleet_Academy.md) · [Historical Incidents](Historical_Incidents.md)

## Required currencies (typical)

Ship captains set local drill calendars; shore audits exist. Typical recurring currencies:

- Weapons qualification (annual where applicable)  
- Damage-control drills and compartment seal practice  
- Manual navigation under failure (especially scout / frontier billets)  
- Emission discipline / passive listen refreshers for scout-track  
- Custody and reporting standards brief before extended deployments  
- Watch qualification renewals for dual-hatted small-hull crews  

Scout hulls (crew ~20) dual-hat extensively. Qualification boards are oral and practical. See [Ships](Ships.md) · [Scout Operations](Scout_Operations.md)

## Officer continuation paths

**Command Qualification Board (CQB-4):** written doctrine examination, simulation command package, and sponsor assessment from current CO. Scout and Exploration Corps commands weigh extended-patrol decision records heavily. Form index: [Forms](Forms.md)

**Academy return rotations:** Instructors typically serve 2–4 year postings; career resident faculty are tracked to prevent detachment from fleet service.

**Direct commission / OID-12:** abbreviated indoctrination for specialized civil professionals. Rank is real; mess culture still has to be learned the hard way. See [Personnel](Personnel.md)

## Enlisted & warrant professional education

Enlisted advancement is inseparable from watch qualification. A rating is not fully “made” until the department chief signs the watch bill. Technical schools and shipboard apprenticeship feed rate families (operations, engineering, communications/relay, logistics, medical, security).

Warrant officers hold technical authority between senior enlisted and commissioned department heads. On scout hulls, warrant billets are rare — cross-training substitutes. See [Ranks](Ranks.md) · [Departments](Departments.md)

---

## Logistics officers (special note)

Academy logistics instruction is “correct but soft” per FLC culture. New logistics officers often spend ~18 months at Tier 1/2 depots before FLC assignment — cultural education in what *approximately stocked* means at 0300. See [Logistics](Logistics.md)

## When doctrine edges move

If Edition language changes or restricted incidents force new sims (Contact Protocol variants, provisional designation cases), Training and Academy curriculum committees lag field reality by months. File after-actions that feed the lag honestly. Prefer witness framing over hero framing when events exceed curriculum.

Public training materials will not teach classified Neutral Zone conclusions. Use provisional / unconfirmed / pending classification language until public release. See [Known Civilizations](Known_Civilizations.md) · [Reporting Standards](Reporting_Standards.md) · [Doctrine](Doctrine.md)
