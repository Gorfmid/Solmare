## Combat hierarchy (smallest → largest)

| Class | Primary weapon | Role notes |
|-------|----------------|------------|
| Fighters | Lasers | Dogfight, escort, harassment |
| Ion Fighters | Ion cannons | Excellent vs battleships in sustained fire; swarm threat to titans/leviathans |
| Corvettes | Lasers | Anti-frigate; fast intercept |
| Frigates | Missiles | Anti-cruiser harassment; screening |
| Cruisers | Plasma | First shield class; anti-battleship; ~4 fighters |
| Heavy Cruisers | Plasma (enhanced) | Anti-battleship/dreadnaught; ~8 fighters |
| Battleships | Ion | Anti-dreadnaught/titan; weak vs fighters alone; ~40 fighters |
| Dreadnaughts | Photon | Fortress defense; anti-frigate/fighter swarms |
| Titans | Disruptors | Anti-capital dominance; ~200 fighters |
| Leviathans | Photon | Strategic annihilation platforms; ~4000 fighters |
| Fleet Carriers | Defensive | ~400 fighters; air superiority control |

**Destroyer** appears in crew-scale tables between corvette and frigate. Full combat-class entry may be expanded separately; treat as light combat hull for complement planning.

---

## Design philosophy (do not blur)

- Smaller = faster, less armored, tactical roles  
- Larger = slower, more armored, strategic dominance  
- Plasma = cruiser-tier energy  
- Ion = anti-capital / system disruption  
- Photon = heavy capital destructive/defensive  
- Disruptor = titan-level system-breaking  

Outcomes depend on **role advantage**, **composition**, and **positioning** — not tonnage alone.

## Warfare layers (field briefing)

| Layer | Classes | Function |
|-------|---------|----------|
| Light | Fighters, Ion Fighters, Corvettes | Harassment, screening, swarm |
| Line | Frigates, Cruisers | Engagement line |
| Capital | Heavy Cruisers, Battleships | Fleet anchors |
| Strategic | Dreadnaughts, Titans, Leviathans, Carriers | Zone control |

Capital ships require fighter screen, sensors, and coordination. Fighters multiply force; they do not win wars alone. Targeting data is a weapon.

## Mission designation: Scout

**Scout-class is not a combat rung.** It is a reconnaissance / observation designation. Scouts survive by speed, evasion, environment, crew ingenuity, and enemy miscalculation — not by pretending to be battleships.

See [Scout Operations](Scout_Operations.md) · `Universe/Technology/scout_class.md`

## Logistics by class (one glance)

Fuel Mass Equivalent (FME) and depot requirements scale sharply. Scouts: FME-1, forward cache capable. Leviathans: FME-200+, Helion Prime-grade yards. Full tables: [Logistics](Logistics.md) and `Universe/Fleet/Fleet_Authority/Fleet_Logistics_Manual.md`
