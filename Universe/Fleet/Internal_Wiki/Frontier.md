## What "frontier" means in Fleet speech

Frontier is prospective language: edge of survey, edge of reliable resupply, edge of relay confidence. It is where Founders' Week mythology points and where Logistics footnotes get honest.

Core vs frontier contrast (logistics view):

| Factor | Core | Frontier edge |
|--------|------|---------------|
| Depot density | High | Low to none |
| Cache currency | Quarterly | Annual / best effort |
| Maintenance | Tier 2 within days | Often none in range |
| Comms | Near-real-time | Multi-day latency; gaps |
| Supply paths | Redundant | Single-path routine |
| FLC picture | High | Projection, not ground truth |

## Neutral Zone corridor approaches (public)

Public products treat the Neutral Zone as buffer / provisional space with corridor approach waypoints, sparse Fleet infrastructure, and ROE annexes in Edition materials.

From Logistics: a **supply black box**. From Exploration: opportunity surface. From Doctrine: observation-before-contact rules.

**Public wiki rule:** Do not assert confirmed peer civilization status for Neutral Zone volumes on open pages. Use *provisional*, *unconfirmed*, *structured observation pending classification*, *anomaly*.

## Cultural note

Officers say *Someone has to go first* on the frontier. Historical Office prefers beginnings. If the edge turns out occupied, both cultures will argue in coffee lines. File the observation first.
