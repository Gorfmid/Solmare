## Mission designation

Scout operations are **reconnaissance and observation**, not line combat. Scout hulls are eyes. They gather survey data, corridor traffic patterns, and anomaly observations for Fleet Command and Cartography.

Prestige among cadets is high. Reality includes cold caches, OW flags, and months of coffee that tastes like the pot.

## Operational profile (Kestrel-class teaching example)

| Parameter | Typical |
|-----------|---------|
| Crew | 20 |
| Fuel | FME-1; lowest class consumption |
| Standard patrol to planned resupply | 60–90 days |
| Extended Deployment Protocol (EDP) | 120–180 days with forward cache sequence |
| Beyond EDP | Mission abort mandatory |

Single-path supply is routine at frontier edge. Form [LC-7](Forms.md) authorizes EDP. Captains who drop below 20% FME without confirmed cache violate EDP minimums.

## Observation before announcement

Standing contact culture for scouts:

- Observation precedes hailing  
- Edition 143 baseline: three full observation passes before active communication attempt  
- Provisional chart notation means **unconfirmed**, not empty  
- Preserve contradictions in sensor states — do not force tidy conclusions  

Anomaly stages (Stage 0–4): `Universe/Observation_Doctrine/anomaly_stage_definition.md` · digest in [Glossary](Glossary.md)

## Emission discipline

Scouts often operate under passive listen / emitter discipline near corridor approaches. Active illumination is a command event, not a habit. Communications Command cares about custody of what you heard; Doctrine Bureau cares about what you call it.

See [Communications](Communications.md) · [Doctrine](Doctrine.md)

---

## Data courier problem

If the hull cannot transmit, the data travels with the crew. If the crew is incapacitated, custody follows Form [ERP-3](Forms.md). A scout that dies with its logs is a strategic loss.

Emergency return: [Logistics](Logistics.md) ERP sequence — fuel, medical, debrief intake, data priority.

## Neutral Zone corridor approaches (public language)

Public ops language: *provisional volumes*, *corridor approaches*, *unconfirmed structured observations*, *anomaly pending classification*.

Do not file public after-actions that assert confirmed peer civilization status for Neutral Zone findings.

> **[CLASSIFIED — post-authorization]**  
> Segment-level corridor observations and industrial-intelligence translations are restricted. Route through Intelligence / FLC Industrial Intelligence channels after authorization.

---

## Culture check

*Someone has to go first* is Scout Track oxygen. When observation suggests Fleet may have arrived **late**, report the observation. Do not edit the proverb into the log.

---

## Watchbill realities

Twenty people means dual-hats. Navigation shares chart fights with Communications. Engineering briefs fuel into Logistics language. Medical sits on psych checks. The ship still runs three watches somehow.

## Success criteria

A successful scout mission returns: crew alive, hull recoverable, data with custody, and observations that refuse to become fiction. Illumination glory is optional and often forbidden.

## After EDP

Debrief through ERP if damaged; otherwise standard AAR + chart amendments + Intelligence routing. Do not brief the mess before custody seals.
