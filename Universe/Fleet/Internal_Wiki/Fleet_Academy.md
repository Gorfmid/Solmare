## Purpose

Commission officers capable of executing Fleet doctrine across exploration, patrol, logistics, intelligence, diplomatic escort, and frontier contact. The name "Administrative" is founding-era charter language — ignore civilian corrections.

The Academy is Fleet's **confidence engine**: shared curriculum, shared scenarios, shared oath. Satellite houses graduate into the same Fleet with identical legal commission weight.

## Cadet years (digest)

1. **Candidate** — vocabulary dump; seat hours as simulation observers; highest washout  
2. **Midshipman** — BPQ; first field rotation; grading fully active  
3. **Ensign-Candidate** — command-entry sims; Proving Rotation (36-hour continuous)  
4. **Senior Cadet** — captain-position sims; posting ceremony; commissioning  

Specialization tracks (Scout, XO, Intel, Logistics, Tactical) deepen in Years 3–4. See [Personnel](Personnel.md).

## Doctrine taught here

- Solmare Principle as reasoning framework  
- Exploration Ethics case library (Sovan Contact, Relay Seven Notation, Wellhaven Refusal, etc.)  
- First contact as curriculum event — *Protocol takes you to the edge. At the edge, you are the protocol.*  
- Edition 143 primer access restricted to Year 3+ by tradition  

Edition debate and provisional designation pressure: see [Doctrine](Doctrine.md). Public wiki does not teach classified Neutral Zone conclusions.

## Graduation culture

- Cohorts named by **Founders' Week** cycle (Major-Cycle every seven years)  
- Sword ceremony (dulled blade, true balance) + Commissioning Oath  
- Archivist presses Founders' Week program for first mess wall  
- Copper-and-blue bunting; fortune rolls at formal dinner  

Unofficial line *Someone has to go first* is cadet culture, not primer text.

## Simulation culture (one rule)

Failure in simulation is data. Refusal to decide is not. Notable scenarios: The Fourteen Hours, Contact Protocol Seven, The Relay Dark, The Vellor Pair, The Long March.

## Commissioning Oath (compressed)

Service to Fleet Authority and member worlds; doctrine and law as foundation; faithful execution where orders arrive; judgment in their spirit where they do not; protect civilian life and authority; carry and report observational truth; accept authority and weight.

Commandant response: *Fleet Authority receives your service.*
