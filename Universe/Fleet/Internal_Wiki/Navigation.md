## Function

Navigation keeps the ship where the chart says it is — and tells Command when the chart is lying by omission. Manual navigation under system failure remains a required qualification even though computational navigation is primary in fleet ops.

Academy Navigation Lab still exists for a reason. See [Fleet Academy](Fleet_Academy.md).

---

## Chart notation (officer essentials)

| Notation | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| Confirmed | Surveyed and accepted into corridor routing |
| Provisional | Volume **not confirmed** — not a claim of emptiness |
| Uncharted | Outside active survey products |
| Restricted / ROE annex | Corridor-specific engagement and transit rules |

Edition 143 tightened provisional designation: active survey confirmation required, not mere absence-of-survey. See [Doctrine](Doctrine.md).

**Teaching case:** Relay Seven Notation — provisional vs confirmed dispute in active corridor ([Historical Incidents](Historical_Incidents.md)).

---

## Observation before routing

Do not route commercial or fleet corridors through provisional volumes as if they were empty parking. Provisional means work remaining.

Cartography distinguishes **observed** vs **inferred**. Navigation officers who blur them create after-actions that Intelligence cannot trust.

---

## Neutral Zone corridor approaches

Public navigation products show buffer / provisional / approach corridor language. Boundary notation conventions live in Edition appendices.

Public wiki rule: do not relabel provisional Neutral Zone volumes as confirmed civilizational space on open pages.

## Degraded navigation

The Relay Dark Academy scenario exists because complete communication loss happens. Dead reckoning, manual charts, and reasonable course plots are assessed on reasonableness, not magical arrival.

Field: if relays are dark, local navigation truth outweighs delayed corridor advisories. Log the basis.
