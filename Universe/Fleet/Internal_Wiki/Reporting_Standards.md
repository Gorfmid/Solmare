## One rule

**Carry the truth of what you observe, and report it honestly.**  
(Commissioning Oath — fifth commitment)

Do not improve the story. Do not delete inconvenient contradictions. Do not invent headers that make Doctrine Bureau happy if the sensors disagree. Timestamps before adjectives.

Coffee may appear as setting in a deck log. Cooling cups are not a clock — use stamps, watch changes, and form versions.

---

## Observation language

Prefer:

- *Observed* / *inferred* (label which)  
- Sensor states: `confirmed` · `probable` · `degraded` · `corrupted` · `contradictory`  
- Anomaly stage if applicable (0–4) — keep distinct from sensor states  
- Timestamps, custody chain, emitter state  

Avoid premature:

- *Enemy* / *hostile civilization* / named unconfirmed polities on public or general channels  
- Collapsing multi-system disagreement into a single tidy cause  
- Treating provisional chart ink as empty *or* as confirmed neighbors  

Public Neutral Zone phrasing: *provisional*, *unconfirmed*, *structured observation pending classification*, *anomaly*. See [Known Civilizations](Known_Civilizations.md) · [Frontier](Frontier.md)

## Minimum reconnaissance discipline

When filing a scout / recon summary, include mission ID, chart epochs with conflicting overlays, timestamped observed facts, labeled inferences with confidence, retained contradictions, emissions log, custody chain, classification recommendation (including “insufficient taxonomy”), and sustainment / hull state in Logistics language.

Officers who force hostile Edition 143 headers onto observation products that refuse them create Intelligence and Doctrine debt. Say the taxonomy is insufficient. That is a valid product.

See [Scout Operations](Scout_Operations.md)

## Transmission practice

Duplicate under uncertain integrity. Buffer via relays. Archival Tier 4 is authoritative in peacetime and fragile in crisis. Never assume real-time strategic guidance on frontier scout ops.

Intelligence value depends on delivery. File early; amend later. A perfect report that arrives after Operations has already chosen from rumor is a historical document, not a decision tool.

See [Communications](Communications.md) · [Operations](Operations.md)

## Classification

Match classification to content. Provisional Neutral Zone observations often require restricted handling even when public language remains soft. When unsure, ask Communications custody desk — do not self-publish to the wiki or to Founders’ Week exhibit text.

> **[CLASSIFIED — post-authorization]**  
> Restricted report series and segment-level survey packages are distributed on authorized lists only. Do not paste them into public Historical Incidents or Known Civilizations entries.

## Common failure modes

1. Confident conclusions from ambiguous evidence  
2. Mixing observed and inferred without labels  
3. Broken custody  
4. Late filing that orphans Intelligence  
5. Hero framing that buries procedure  
6. Using coffee, atmosphere, or proverb as elapsed-time proof  

Prefer **witness** framing when events exceed curriculum. The institution updates its grammar later; your job is to carry the observation intact.

Briefing slides: short declarative bullets, attachable numbers, explicit uncertainty. See [Templates](Templates.md)
