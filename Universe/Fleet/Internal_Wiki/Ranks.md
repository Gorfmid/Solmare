## Commissioned officer grades (working table)

Fleet uses a conventional naval-style ladder. Exact pay codes live in Personnel systems; this table is for wardroom literacy.

| Grade | Typical authority surface |
|-------|---------------------------|
| Ensign | Watch / junior division |
| Lieutenant Junior Grade | Watch officer; specialist lead |
| Lieutenant | Division officer; depot watch; staff |
| Lieutenant Commander | Department head (smaller ships); regional logistics; staff lead |
| Commander | XO (many hulls); shore ops chief; Academy posting instructor |
| Captain | Ship command; directorate lead; senior Academy faculty |
| Commodore | Task group / regional command; Academy Commandant (Helion); intel director-level |
| Rear Admiral | Deputy command roles; senior shore deputies |
| Vice Admiral | Major command deputies (e.g. Logistics Ops) |
| Admiral | Command-level (Logistics Commander, etc.) |
| Fleet Admiral | Fleet Operations apex (example: Commander, Fleet Operations) |

Warrant officer grades exist for technical mastery tracks (engineering, communications, navigation specialties). Treat warrants as authority in their domain; do not outrank them with confident ignorance.

## Authority is not only rank

Under the Information Authority Rule, a junior officer with current local sensors may hold more *valid* command weight than a delayed flag order. Rank still matters for peacetime administration. In degraded conditions, **verified currency** matters more than stripe count.

See [Operational Procedures](Operational_Procedures.md) · governance doc.

## Addressing norms

- Use rank + surname in formal channels.
- Bridge watch may shorten for clarity under time pressure; after-action reports restore formal address.
- Do not invent courtesy titles for classified contacts on public forms.
