## Function

Engineering owns propulsion, power, life support, structural integrity, and the gap between **what the board says** and **what the hull does**.

Command officers are taught systems at command depth, not repair depth. Engineers who confuse understanding with expertise cause incidents; officers who confuse certification with readiness cause worse ones.

---

## Three-tier maintenance (officer digest)

| Tier | Where | Scope | Classification |
|------|-------|-------|----------------|
| 1 Field | Anywhere / forward bay | Patch, reset, consumables | Operational Workaround (OW) — **deferred**, not solved |
| 2 Regional | Relay depots / regional yards | Component replacement, matrix work | Maintenance Certified (MC) |
| 3 Full overhaul | Helion Prime / full-grade yards | Hull, drive, full cert renewal | Yard Certified (YC) |

Forms: [OW-1](Forms.md), [RC-12](Forms.md). Full doctrine: Logistics Manual.

---

## Patched, not repaired

Institutional phrase for unresolved OW debt on a technically certified hull. Vibration, thermal drift, and recurring breakers are data. Log them.

> *"Certification tells you what was signed. The vibration tells you what's true."*  
> — Engineering culture (attributed across scout yards)

FLC Readiness tracks aggregate OW flag counts as systemic health. Captains who treat OW as paperwork only inherit cascading failure under stress.

## Fuel state partnership

Engineering and Logistics share fuel truth. Scouts at extended range live on FME discipline. Emergency Fuel Protocol ([EFP-1](Forms.md)) is a command declaration, not an engineering suggestion — but Engineering calculates the divert math.

## Watch culture

Coffee in engineering spaces is survival, not ceremony. Night watches document deltas in the log, not in chat. Hand over with numbers: FME remaining, OW open count, systems at amber.
