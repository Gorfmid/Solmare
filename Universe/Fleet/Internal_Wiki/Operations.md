## Function

Fleet Command turns orders into movement: deployments, mission amendments, ROE issuance, crisis routing through relay chains. It balances **verified loss** against **assumed contact**. It signs what Intelligence and Doctrine recommend — and owns the tempo of escalation.

Cultural notes: calm under incomplete information; people before systems on live calls; margin thinking; short declarative sentences.

---

## Chain of command (working)

**Normal:** Civilian political authority → Military Command → Task Forces → Captains  

**Degraded comms / combat:** Captains may override delayed, contradictory, or ambiguous higher orders. Field survival supersedes hierarchical delay.

**Information Authority Rule:** Command valid only if current, consistent with local sensors, and not contradicted by independent systems — else local ship command.

See [Operational Procedures](Operational_Procedures.md) · [Communications](Communications.md)

## What Operations does not own

- Intelligence analysis product (Intelligence owns; Operations signs flags)  
- Doctrine text (Bureau owns editions)  
- Fuel physics (Logistics owns sustainment windows)  

Friction among these desks is normal. Document disagreements; do not erase them in the summary slide.

## Crisis fragmentation (expect it)

In Fleet-scale conflict: political authority becomes delayed/symbolic; military command partially blind; field command becomes primary. Multiple "truth states" and conflicting legitimate orders are a **known condition**, not a personal failure.

Ship-level resilience (scout example): interpret locally; survive; return data. See [Ships](Ships.md).

## Public ops language for frontier anomalies

Use provisional / unconfirmed / pending classification. Do not drive public ROE changes from rumor.
