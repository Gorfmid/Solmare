# Awards

---

## Purpose

Fleet decorations recognize service, valor, meritorious achievement, and campaign participation. Historical Office maintains ribbon precedence charts; Personnel processes packages.

This page lists **common categories** officers encounter — not a complete medals encyclopedia.

---

## Common categories

| Category | Typical basis |
|----------|---------------|
| Campaign / service ribbons | Authorized operational periods / theaters |
| Meritorious service | Sustained excellence in billet |
| Commendation | Specific action short of highest valor awards |
| Unit citations | Ship / task group collective performance |
| Lifesaving / medical | Crew rescue under hazard |
| Exploration / survey devices | Extended survey achievement (Corps-linked) |
| Academy devices | Marksmanship, simulation distinctions (pre-commission) |

Exact names and metals vary by issuance era. Cite Personnel catalog codes on packages.

---

## Culture notes

- Private praise and public funerals are not a Fleet mirror of foreign custom — do not import foreign award cultures into Fleet packages.  
- Scout survival is not automatically hero narrative. Witness discipline may matter more than medals.  
- Logistics officers rarely get parade attention; preventable disasters that never happen are still the job.

---

## Founders' Week & commissioning

Sword presentation is **commission symbol**, not an award. Dulled blade, true balance. Program copies for mess walls are archival tradition, not decorations.

See [Fleet Academy](Fleet_Academy.md)

---

## Package hygiene

Awards packages require verified logs and custody. Inflated claims die in review. Coffee-stained drafts are fine; fabricated timestamps are not.

---

## Precedence & wear

Ribbon racks follow Historical Office precedence charts. When in doubt, ask the archivist before the parade. Incorrect wear during Founders' Week ceremonies generates more tickets than incorrect coffee orders.

## Unit citations vs individual awards

Unit citations recognize collective performance — logistics saves, relay recoveries, survey completions. Individual awards require named acts with verified logs. Scout crews sometimes prefer unit recognition when observation discipline was collective.

## Declining theater

Officers may request quiet presentation. Fleet culture permits it. Public hero framing of anomalous contact survivors is a Doctrine/Historical Office problem, not an awards loophole.
