## Shipboard departments (standard)

On vessels frigate-class and above, departments are distinct. On scout–corvette hulls, officers dual-hat and the same functions still apply.

| Department | Core function | Wiki deep-dive |
|------------|---------------|----------------|
| Command | Captain / XO decision loop; mission authority | [Operations](Operations.md) |
| Navigation | Course, charts, provisional notation | [Navigation](Navigation.md) |
| Communications | Relays, custody, emission discipline | [Communications](Communications.md) |
| Engineering | Propulsion, life support, hull truth | [Engineering](Engineering.md) |
| Tactical / Weapons | Engagement systems, ROE execution | [Doctrine](Doctrine.md) |
| Sensors / Listen | Observation, anomaly staging | [Scout Operations](Scout_Operations.md) |
| Medical | Crew health, casualty, quarantine | [Medical](Medical.md) |
| Logistics / Supply | Stores, fuel state, OW flags | [Logistics](Logistics.md) |
| Security | Internal access control, boarding defense | [Ships](Ships.md) |

## Shore departments vs ship departments

Shore commands (Fleet Command, Intelligence, Doctrine Bureau, Logistics Command, etc.) are **institutions**. Ship departments are **working teams**. Do not confuse a shore "desk" with a shipboard department head when routing reports.

Institutional map: [Fleet](Fleet.md)

---

## Department head culture

- Department heads own watch integrity for their domain.
- Cross-department disagreement is expected; unresolved disagreement escalates to XO, then Captain.
- On scouts, the same argument happens faster and with fewer witnesses.
- Coffee service in Doctrine Annex and Map Room hours is not a department — stop filing it as one.

## Dual-hat reality (scout–frigate)

Expect Navigation to share chart custody with Communications. Expect Engineering to brief fuel state into Logistics language. Expect Medical to sit in on extended-deployment psych checks. Small ships do not get smaller problems — they get fewer people to hold them.
