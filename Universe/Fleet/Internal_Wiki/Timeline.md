## How to read this page

This is Fleet’s **public institutional chronology** — what officers are expected to recognize in briefings, Academy sidebars, and Founders’ Week exhibits. It is curated memory, not a complete archaeology of every corridor.

Historical Office prefers beginnings: first relays, first colonies, pioneer achievement framed as expansion success. Frontier campaigns that other archives retain under different names appear here as dated sidebars without opposing institutional perspective. Selection is curation, not fabrication.

Coffee arguments in the annex do not amend this page. Classification working groups do.

## Relay Charter era (public summary)

Before the Charter, officer practice was regional and oral. Contact protocols could diverge across sectors until a diplomatic or operational incident forced comparison. Public curriculum treats the Charter as prosperity theology: connection enables trade; trade enables peace; peace justifies further relays.

Exhibits emphasize first successful mid-rim handshakes and living pioneers who “went first.” Deeper corridor archaeology — lanes and maintenance cultures predating Fleet survey epochs — is not the exhibit’s job unless Doctrine and Cartography force a revision cycle.

See [Communications](Communications.md) · [Navigation](Navigation.md)
