## Mission in one paragraph

Fleet Authority exists to keep passage open, colonies supplied, and the frontier surveyed under shared law. Expansion is treated as stability work, not adventure hobby. Officers are formed to move toward the unknown with procedure in hand — and judgment when procedure ends.

Deeper overview: `Universe/Fleet/unified_fleet_authority.md`

---

## What Fleet is (and is not)

Fleet is **not** a single centralized command. Political authority, military command, and ship-level field command are intentionally separated. In peacetime that separation looks like bureaucracy. In crisis it looks like competing legitimate orders.

Authoritative governance model: `Universe/Fleet/fleet_authority_governance_and_command_hierarchy.md`

| Layer | Owns | Weakness |
|-------|------|----------|
| Civilian political authority | Trade, diplomacy, funding, interstellar law | Slow; poor real-time military response |
| Fleet Military Command | Deployments, doctrine enforcement, threat authorization | Depends on information integrity |
| Operational field command | Survival, tactics, local crisis | Lowest external visibility; highest responsiveness |

**Normal chain:** Civilian → Military Command → Task Forces → Ship Captains  
**Degraded / combat:** Field survival authority can supersede delayed or contradictory higher orders. See [Operational Procedures](Operational_Procedures.md)#information-authority.

## Ideology officers actually live

- Expansion creates stability; exploration creates opportunity
- New colonies create prosperity; open trade creates peace
- Unclaimed space should be explored; borders move outward naturally
- *The unknown exists to be explored*

Fleet is centuries old as a polity and **young in self-mythology**. Founders' Week, the Relay Charter, and living pioneers dominate public memory. Officers think **prospectively** — current missions, recent discoveries, what comes next.

Cultural texture (coffee, Founders' Week, chapel plaque language): `Universe/cultural_touchstones.md`

## Strengths and known weaknesses

**Strengths**
- Largest coordinated fleet network in known space
- Superior logistics and resupply chains (see [Logistics](Logistics.md))
- Shared officer grammar via [Fleet Academy](Fleet_Academy.md)

**Weaknesses**
- Slow decision cycles across political–military layers
- Overreliance on data integrity assumptions
- Internal friction between scientific exploration culture and strategic command caution

## Everyday institutional texture

Officers recognize Fleet before anyone says the name: coffee between watches, Founders' Week copper-and-blue, chapel plaque language schoolchildren memorize, mess walls layered with graduation programs, prospective talk about the next corridor rather than the last century.

Speech habit: Fleet values innovation. Officers almost never quote academy instructors or grandparents the way some foreign cultures do. Dialogue tends toward mission language, procedure, peer banter, and forward solutions.

See `Universe/cultural_touchstones.md`.

## Hidden tension (acknowledged)

Scientific exploration culture and strategic command caution pull opposite directions. Both are Fleet. Neither cancels the other on a form. Route disagreements through [Operations](Operations.md) and [Doctrine](Doctrine.md); document both positions.
