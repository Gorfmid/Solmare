## Function

Preserve Fleet's story of itself: Founders' Week archives, Relay Charter displays, campaign ribbons, academy dioramas, commemorative plaques. This is **institutional memory**, not battlefield intelligence.

Historians curate narrative continuity — why Founders' Week matters, how the Relay Charter became prosperity theology, what Fleet chooses to remember about expansion.

---

## Prospective memory

Fleet historical culture emphasizes **beginnings**: first relays, first colonies, living pioneers. Deep-time corridor archaeology is less comfortable territory. Selection is curation — professional, not cartoon propaganda, but still selection.

Contrast: other polities weight ancestors and old campaigns. Fleet officers think forward. Show it; do not lecture it. Touchstones: `Universe/cultural_touchstones.md`

- Campaign ribbon eligibility research  
- Teaching-case packets for Academy liaison

## Tension with frontier findings

When survey products imply that volumes Fleet called empty already carried someone else's history, Historical Office faces revision pressure: update public displays, sequester findings, or delay.

Public wiki stance: maintain Founders' Week and Relay Charter as origin narrative; route contested frontier chronology through classification until Doctrine and Cartography issue public language.

## Culture

Ceremonial precision. Dates, editions, parade orders, glass cases. Coffee in sorting rooms. Archivist presses remain sacred during graduation week — do not "borrow" the press for wardroom jokes.
