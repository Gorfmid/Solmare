## Working definition

A Fleet ship is a **military ecosystem**, not a city. Even Leviathans are fleet-defining warships with controlled complements — not population habitats. Narrative and administrative focus tracks command teams and department heads, not every billet at scale.

## Command models by size

**Scout–Frigate:** Small centralized command. Captain + XO primary loop. Officers know most of the crew. Dual-hatting is normal.

**Cruiser–Heavy Cruiser:** Distinct departments. Watch officers and department heads are the working surface.

**Battleship and above:** Structured hierarchy. Bridge / CIC / key departments carry operational narrative. Boarding and internal security still resolve at human scale — see boarding doctrine outside this wiki.

## Internal geography (officer summary)

Ships are compartmentalized layers, not open buildings:

1. External hull  
2. Transition / buffer (airlocks, cargo locks)  
3. Functional core (engineering, propulsion, life support)  
4. Command / operations (bridge, CIC, comms)  
5. Habitation (quarters, mess, medical)

Boarding or internal crisis follows phase logic: breach → containment → system warfare → command hunt → control or evacuation. Full rules: `Universe/Fleet/boarding_combat_and_internal_ship_geography.md`

## Certification vs truth

Every vessel carries a maintenance certification board. Logistics officers read it at port. Engineers live with what it omits.

Institutional phrase: **"patched, not repaired"** — technically certified, operationally fragile. See [Engineering](Engineering.md) and [Logistics](Logistics.md).

Readiness codes (GF / GL / AL / AD / RD / BE) live in logistics reporting. Captains who ignore AMBER flags own the outcome.

## Mess walls and living culture

First mess walls accumulate Founders' Week graduation programs pressed by Academy archivists — faded copies from every officer who passed through. Reading a mess wall is a quick history of who Fleet produced and where Fleet sent them.

Coffee is default institutional drink shipboard. Wardrooms, engineering nooks, and night-watch kiosks keep pots going. Do not use "coffee cooling" as a time metric in after-action prose; use watch change, stamps, and log times. See [Reporting Standards](Reporting_Standards.md).

## Scout hulls as data couriers

Scout-class vessels are **mission designations** (reconnaissance / observation), not a rung on the combat hierarchy. **14 billets**, smaller than corvette, **fastest hull in Fleet**, small laser only. They consume little fuel and operate farthest from Grade-A infrastructure. A scout that cannot return is a data-delivery failure as much as a life-safety failure.

See [Scout Operations](Scout_Operations.md) and [Ship Classes](Ship_Classes.md).
