## Core principle

**Information is a battlefield.** Engagements are simultaneous physical and information conflicts. Communications Command decides what counts as *heard*.

---

## Communication tiers

1. **Local ship networks** — fastest; vulnerable to internal sabotage / compartment loss  
2. **Task group relays** — tactical; jamming under combat  
3. **Long-range Fleet network** — strategic; light-speed delay; outdated in active combat  
4. **Official archival channels** — slowest; authoritative in peacetime; fragile in crisis  

---

## Sensor / information states

Never treat a single stream as absolute. Standard states:

`confirmed` · `probable` · `degraded` · `corrupted` · `contradictory`

Preserve contradictions as operational signal. Collapsing them early is a professional failure mode.

Anomaly stages: Stage 0–4 — see [Glossary](Glossary.md) · `Universe/Observation_Doctrine/anomaly_stage_definition.md`

---

## Chain of custody

Who touched the packet matters. Separate custody lanes exist for a reason. Buffer integrity, timestamps, and handshake logs beat adjectives.

Field rule: if you cannot prove custody, you do not have a conclusion — you have a rumor with good formatting.

---

## Command under uncertainty

Aligns with governance Information Authority Rule:

- No command is valid unless verifiably current, consistent with local sensors, and not contradicted by independent systems  
- Otherwise authority defaults to local ship command  

See [Operational Procedures](Operational_Procedures.md) · [Operations](Operations.md)

## Failure modes (expect)

Partial loss · temporal desync · selective silence · false confirmation loops · jamming · deception · lag exploitation · data poisoning  

Captains never have full situational awareness in uncertainty. That is doctrine, not personal inadequacy.

## Fringe awareness

Relay nodes at the hinge of frontier coverage are not headquarters. Treat fringe windows as scarce. Buffer early; argue later.
