## Usage

Use this archive when a chapter, briefing, Academy seminar, mess-wall plaque, or civilian holiday needs a **named Fleet past** that feels inherited rather than invented on the page.

**Rules**

1. **Prospective memory.** Fleet remembers beginnings, first installs, living pioneers, and successful passage. It does not teach millennia-scale corridor archaeology as civic identity.
2. **Asymmetric archives.** Dominion institutional memory retains breakaway founding, campaigns, and Fleet expansion as strategic pressure. Fleet public canon does **not**. Do not grant Fleet POV the name *Dominion*, breakaway founding detail, or peer-civilization certainty before authorized beats.
3. **Book One lock.** Do not contradict locked Book One events, Edition 143→144 causal chain, or First Doctrine adoption (Ch. 21; Edition 144 teaches). Prefer silence over speculative overwrite.
4. **Expansion tags.** Entries marked `[Archive expansion — review]` are reusable texture pending author confirmation. Prefer them for color; do not treat as immovable lock until reviewed.
5. **Show, don't dump.** On page, one plaque, one ribbon, one mess-wall story. This file is the deep drawer.

**Related governance / doctrine:** `fleet_authority_governance_and_command_hierarchy.md` · `Fleet_Authority/Fleet_Doctrine_Manual.md` · `Fleet_Authority/Fleet_Academy_Reference.md`

## How Fleet Dates Itself

Fleet Historical Office prefers **Founders' Week cycle markers**, Relay Charter editions, survey epoch numbers, and campaign-ribbon years over continuous deep chronology. Academy cohorts are named by Founders' Week cycle. Cartography uses **survey epoch** bands. Doctrine Manual editions are teaching layers, not calendars.

Author shorthand in this file:

| Marker | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **FW** | Founders' Week cycle / civic origin texture |
| **RC** | Relay Charter era (communications unification) |
| **Epoch** | Cartography survey epoch |
| **Ed.###** | Doctrine Manual edition context |
| **Book One / Y+0** | Anchor for post–Book One series timeline only |

Exact absolute years are often **institutionally soft**. Prefer relative phrasing (*two generations after Relay Charter*, *the Mid-Rim Consolidation decade*) unless a locked document supplies a date.

# I. ERA FRAMEWORK

Reusable period labels for plaques, curricula, and briefing slides.

### ERA-01 — Breakaway Depth (Pre-Public Canon)

**Date / era marker:** Deep pre-Founders' Week institutional past (not public curriculum)  
**Summary:** Series seed: Fleet Authority originated as a breakaway founding polity that left Dominion civilization and built a new mid-rim identity. Cartographic and legal vocabulary (including *Neutral Zone* notation) survived; public memory of shared inheritance was scrubbed or reframed.  
**Institutional significance:** Explains why Fleet feels young while institutions are centuries old; why Neutral Zone language exists without peer-civilization model.  
**Narrative reuse notes:** Reveal through Dominion POV, Historical Office friction, Act IV+ revision pressure — never Act I exposition. Fleet characters do not "discover the breakaway" casually.  
**Canon:** `fleet_dominion_frontier_history.md`  
**Tag:** Series depth — not Fleet Historical Office exhibit language.

---

### ERA-02 — Founders' Week Origin Texture

**Date / era marker:** Founding generation / first synchronized Founders' Week cycles  
**Summary:** Civic origin mythology: people who chose passage, shared law, and outward expansion. Six-day synchronized celebration across member worlds; local foods substituted; same story in a hundred accents. Blessing processions, fortune rolls, copper-and-blue bunting, prosperity practice (sharing food with night shifts).  
**Institutional significance:** Primary emotional calendar of Fleet identity. Academy graduations lock to Founders' Week. Historical Office glass cases prioritize this era.  
**Narrative reuse notes:** Recognition before naming. Do not lecture. Use sensory markers (bunting, fortune rolls, neighbor potlucks) rather than founding speeches. Saying *"Someone has to go first."* is earned sparingly.  
**Canon:** `cultural_touchstones.md`

---

### ERA-03 — Relay Charter Unification

**Date / era marker:** Early consolidation generations after founding  
**Summary:** Relay Charter stabilized fleet communications across the mid-rim; prosperity theology attaches to connected passage ("many worlds, one law of passage"). Chapel plaques (Meridian Gate and equivalents) memorize the patience clause. Pre-Academy officer culture was informal; Relay Charter made inconsistent regional doctrine politically expensive.  
**Institutional significance:** Birth of coordinated Fleet network culture; names Relay Hall at the Academy; grounds Communications Command legitimacy; ties Founders' Week to living infrastructure rather than only parade.  
**Narrative reuse notes:** Characters treat relays as weather and inheritance. Charter "editions" can appear as archive labels. Political arguments about tariff and passage often invoke Charter language without quoting it.  
**Tag:** `[Archive expansion — review]` for specific Charter article numbers and named signatories.

### ERA-04 — Academy Common Grammar

**Date / era marker:** Third generation of Founders' Week history (Academy charter)  
**Summary:** Fleet Administrative Academy chartered during a Founders' Week legislative session as "the common grammar of Fleet expectation." Inception Cohort enters the following year. Prospective memory encoding becomes policy: exploration and frontier doctrine outweigh historical campaign depth.  
**Institutional significance:** Officers become interchangeable across sectors; honor code and Contact Protocol Seven become shared language; Founders' Week cohort identity becomes permanent social binder.  
**Narrative reuse notes:** Mess-wall program copies; Major-Cycle cohorts; dulled commencement swords; Relay Hall's confusing floors as informal orientation test. Calder Venn = Major-Cycle Founders' Week graduate (locked texture).  
**Canon:** `Fleet_Authority/Fleet_Academy_Reference.md`

### ERA-05 — Mid-Rim Consolidation

**Date / era marker:** Post-Academy expansion decades  
**Summary:** Colonies, survey epochs, relay installs, industrial supply chains (Helion plate, Kestran alloy, Verdant Line harvest, Selene cultural export). Meridian Gate rises as three-corridor commercial junction. Neutral Zone remains buffer language on charts.  
**Institutional significance:** Creates the prosperity citizens assume is permanent; produces Cartography epoch pride; seeds core–mid-rim tariff politics.  
**Narrative reuse notes:** Ordinary life windows — queue, dispatch, Second Sunrise foundry aurora, coffee between watches. Expansion feels natural, not conquest.
