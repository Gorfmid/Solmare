# 1. Core Principle

In the Solmare universe:

> **INFORMATION IS A BATTLEFIELD.**

Fleet engagements are not only physical conflicts.

They are simultaneous struggles over:

- sensor accuracy
- message integrity
- timing of information
- interpretation of incomplete data

A ship that controls information controls combat outcomes.

---

# 2. Communication Tiers

Fleet communication is layered:

---

## Tier 1 — Local Ship Networks

- internal ship systems
- fastest communication layer
- used for command execution and systems coordination

**Characteristics:**

- high speed
- high reliability
- vulnerable to internal sabotage or compartment loss

---

## Tier 2 — Task Group Relays

- ship-to-ship communication within fleet formations
- tactical coordination layer

**Characteristics:**

- moderate delay under combat conditions
- subject to jamming and signal degradation
- redundancy-dependent

---

## Tier 3 — Long-Range Fleet Network

- strategic command communication
- Fleet Authority oversight layer

**Characteristics:**

- delayed by light-speed constraints
- often outdated during active combat
- used for strategic rather than tactical control

---

## Tier 4 — Official Archival Channels

- intelligence reporting systems
- historical record integration
- administrative Fleet memory

**Characteristics:**

- slowest
- most authoritative in peacetime
- most vulnerable to corruption, delay, or suppression in crisis conditions

---

# 3. Sensor Reliability Model

Sensor data is NEVER absolute.

All sensor input must be treated as:

## Possibility states

- confirmed
- probable
- degraded
- corrupted
- contradictory

Multiple sensor systems may legitimately produce conflicting "true" readings.

This is a known and accepted condition of Fleet operations.

---

# 4. Information Warfare Mechanics

---

## Jamming

- disrupts signal transmission between ships
- increases delay and packet loss
- forces reliance on local decision-making

---

## Deception

- falsified sensor echoes
- mirrored fleet signatures
- ghost fleet projections

---

## Signal Lag Exploitation

- attackers exploit delay between:
  - observation
  - transmission
  - command execution

---

## Data Poisoning

- corrupted intelligence injected into Fleet systems
- may appear valid at first verification layer

---

# 5. Command Under Uncertainty Rule

Fleet captains must operate under the assumption that:

- no single sensor source is fully reliable
- no communication stream is guaranteed current
- no external command is guaranteed accurate

Therefore:

> Local command authority supersedes delayed external command during active uncertainty events.

---

# 6. Contradiction Handling Rule

When multiple valid sensor or intelligence reports conflict:

Fleet doctrine requires:

- preserve all versions of data
- do NOT discard contradictions
- treat contradiction as operational signal, not error

Contradictory data may indicate:

- jamming
- time displacement effects
- sensor deception
- or non-standard environmental physics

See also: `Universe/Observation_Doctrine/anomaly_stage_definition.md` for anomaly-stage escalation.

---

# 7. Communication Failure Modes

Fleet systems anticipate:

---

## Partial Loss

- segments of fleet lose contact
- information becomes regionalized

---

## Temporal Desync

- reports arrive out of chronological order
- causes operational confusion in command chains

---

## Selective Silence

- certain systems stop transmitting without clear failure cause

---

## False Confirmation Loop

- multiple systems incorrectly confirm same false data

---

# 8. Strategic Consequences

Information warfare results in:

- delayed reinforcements
- misaligned fleet positioning
- contradictory engagement orders
- fragmented battlefields
- localized "truth zones" where different fleets operate under different realities

---

# 9. Narrative Rule

All communication in story scenes must reflect:

- uncertainty
- delay
- partial knowledge
- conflicting interpretations

Captains do NOT have full situational awareness.

Readers should often know:

> less than what feels comfortable for characters

This is intentional tension design.

Aligns with `Writing_System/Generation_Rules/continuity_rules.md` — Character Knowledge Rule.

# 10. Kestrel Veil Special Case

The Kestrel Veil is uniquely resilient in information warfare scenarios due to:

- older, less complex sensor systems
- fewer integrated dependency layers
- faster local decision loops
- reduced exposure to Fleet-wide data corruption

However:

- it is NOT immune to deception
- it relies heavily on crew interpretation
- it survives through judgment, not system superiority

The ship's strength is:

> interpretive clarity under degraded information conditions
