### Sports and Physical Culture

Physical readiness is already Academy doctrine (baseline year one; annual weapons qualification; The Ranges). What is missing is **recreational and competitive sport** as cadet culture rather than assessment.

Fleet Academy does not run a civilian-style athletics league. Organized sport exists as **optional readiness clubs** under the Deputy Commandant for Operations: unit runs, obstacle circuits on The Ranges, zero-g orientation pools at satellite houses that have them, and informal pickup contests on the Yard after evening formation. Winning is not recorded on transcripts. Attendance is noted only if a cadet’s physical scores are borderline and the club is clearly helping.

Satellite flavor matters more than Helion uniformity:

- **Calder Station** treats endurance marches and vacuum-suit drills as the local “sport.”
- **Tessa’s Range** folds physical contests into its gaming culture — short timed obstacle relays between simulation blocks.
- **Verdant Line** runs seasonal field-day games tied to host-community harvest weekends.
- **Wellhaven** favors logistics-themed team challenges (timed load-out races) that Helion cadets mock and then quietly copy.

Formal inter-academy athletic championships do not exist. Alumni who want glory talk simulation rankings, not trophies.

### Clubs and Cadet Organizations

There is no student-government bureaucracy and no open-ended club charter system. Cadet organizations that persist do so because an instructor sponsors them and Operations tolerates the room reservation:

| Informal society | What it actually is |
|------------------|---------------------|
| **Chart Society** | Navigation hobbyists; manual-plot nights in the Navigation Lab |
| **Ethics Round** | Voluntary extra case nights; often louder than graded seminars |
| **First Mess Choir** | Founders’ Week music only; dissolves the week after graduation |
| **Scenario Guild** | Cadet-run scenario design group feeding ideas to Tactical Studies (rarely accepted; always attempted) |
| **Relay Correspondence Circle** | Letter/packet exchange with satellite academies; social, not doctrinal |

Honor-code adjudication and cohort housing already provide the social structure civilian clubs would otherwise supply. Cadets who need a club usually invent a recurring Yard table rather than file paperwork.

### Competitions (Beyond Simulation)

The Reference already covers simulation culture and Tessa’s Range adversarial gaming. Academy-wide **public competitions** are limited to three recurring events:

1. **Seat-Hours Observation Prize (Year 1)** — best written scenario identification from observer sessions; bragging rights and a coffee voucher at the Doctrine Annex.
2. **Bridge Position Cup (Year 2)** — optional multi-station BPQ showcase; track departments watch quietly for talent.
3. **Colloquium Challenge (annual)** — Strategic Studies Colloquium case argued before visiting officers; the “win” is surviving cross-examination without collapsing into Edition 143 recitation.

No parade-ground drill championship, no formal debate league, no fleet-wide cadet olympiad. Competitive energy is supposed to drain into simulation decision quality. When it drains into status games instead, instructors treat that as assessment data.
