# Fleet Logistics Command
## Supply, Readiness, and Industrial Reach — Unified Fleet Authority

---

## 1. Function

Fleet Logistics Command keeps the network moving— fuel, parts, yard schedules, resupply chains, deployment sustainability.

Strengths per unified Fleet canon: **largest coordinated logistics network** in the quadrant.

Logistics translates strategic decisions into **timelines**:

- How fast Fleet can ready a response
- What a damaged scout actually needs vs. what pickup can provide
- Whether industrial observations from segment seven imply **peer-scale** sustainment

---

## 2. Cultural Personality

- **Quantitative.** Throughput, tonnage, slip counts, readiness curves.
- **Practical optimism.** Believes logistics wins wars Fleet hasn't fought yet.
- **Impatient with philosophy.** Wants Thessaly's report in numbers— lanes, depots, duty cycles.
- **Assumes Fleet industrial base is the reference class** for "major capacity."

---

## 3. Act IV Relevance

Segment seven observed **foreign** yard throughput, fuel spine depth, reserve baselines— Logistics must answer:

- What does this imply for Fleet readiness margins?
- Is opposing industrial depth **mobilization** or **continuous preparation**?
- How fast could a peer sustain operations without Fleet seeing a spike?

Logistics pressure collides with Rowan's restraint— readiness timelines vs. panic procurement.

---

## 4. Likely Assumptions

- Fleet supply chains are the strategic baseline others should be measured against.
- Enemy/industrial capacity is inferred from **visible hull counts**, not lane duty cycles.
- Passive throughput data is secondary to confirmed hull classification.
- Neutral Zone operations do not require logistics re-tiering yet.

---

## 5. Likely Friction

- Misreading long-horizon preparation as immediate surge threat— demands crash programs.
- Dismissing civilian traffic density as economically irrelevant.
- Undervaluing Halden/Park correlation appendices (*lags*, not drama).
- Treating Calder's departure as leaving questions unanswered **before** logistics gets numbers.
