## Core model (series canon)

Fleet Authority and the Dominion are **not** strangers who met at a treaty line.

They are civilizations whose **histories diverged**, whose **maps overlap**, and whose **institutional memory is asymmetric**.

| Civilization | Age (institutional) | Memory posture |
|--------------|---------------------|----------------|
| **The Dominion** | Ancient — centuries of institutions, border watch, campaign memory | Historical — campaigns, plaques, strategic depth |
| **Fleet Authority** | Young in **self-mythology** — Founders' Week, Relay Charter, prospective expansion | Prospective — beginnings celebrated; deep frontier past edited or forgotten |

**Book One restraint:** Fleet characters do **not** know the name *Dominion* until authorized discovery beats. Dominion POV may know Fleet Authority by function and habit, not necessarily by Fleet's self-names on-page.

## Origins (deep history — series seed)

Fleet Authority originated centuries ago as a **breakaway founding polity** — people who left Dominion civilization and built a new institutional identity toward the mid-rim and beyond.

The founding generation carried **cartographic and legal vocabulary** (including *Neutral Zone* notation) but **scrubbed or reframed** Dominion history in public memory:

- Founders' Week celebrates a **fresh beginning**, not shared inheritance.
- Fleet Historical Office curates **prospective** origin stories.
- Frontier campaigns that Dominion schools still teach were **edited down** in Fleet curricula to buffer language, footnotes, or silence.

**Dominion institutional memory** retains the breakaway, the campaigns, and Fleet's expansion as **ongoing strategic pressure** — not as a mystery civilization.

Neither civilization is lying in full. Each inherited a **different archive**.

## The Neutral Zone

The **Neutral Zone** is a **surviving institutional term** — corridor notation, mission orders, cartographic labels — not proof that Fleet understands who occupies the volume.

### Fleet understanding (pre–segment seven)

- The Neutral Zone **exists on charts** — named corridors (e.g. seven-alpha), ROE language, border advisories, published boundaries.
- Doctrine treats it as **buffer / provisional volume** — pause between ambitions, low-occupancy confidence, exploration edge.
- Fleet knows **something** lies beyond routine patrol space; it does **not** maintain a living model of Dominion civilization.
- No formal **border treaties** in current canon — contact history is geographic collision and institutional erasure, not diplomatic parity.

### Dominion understanding

- The same geometry is **strategic depth** — maintained lanes, border nodes (K-17 pattern), decades of passive watch.
- Dominion maps treat overlapping volume as **inherited space**, not empty buffer.
- Dominion is **aware of Fleet Authority** — traffic habits, Founders' Week patterns, corridor watch, expansion pressure.

### Book One discovery beat

Segment seven reveals that Fleet's **category survived** while the **referent was hollowed out** — infrastructure, traffic, and maintenance layering prove the volume was never empty; Fleet doctrine was.

## Expansion collision (current era)

- **Fleet Authority** continues expanding outward — colonies, survey epochs, relay installs, mid-rim consolidation.
- **The Dominion** holds and maintains border geometry — preparation doctrine, not reactive surprise.
- The collision is **geographic and historical** — two expansion logics meeting in the same corridors — not a first meeting at a negotiated line.

## Narrative rules

1. **Do not** reintroduce border treaties or diplomatic parity unless a later book explicitly authorizes it.
2. **Do not** grant Fleet the name *Dominion* before authorized beats.
3. Fleet may reference the **Neutral Zone** in early chapters — that is **institutional vocabulary**, not full awareness.
4. Dominion POV may contrast Fleet's **edited memory** with Dominion **campaign memory** — show; do not lecture.
5. The exodus origin is **series depth** — reveal through Dominion scenes, Historical Office friction, and Act IV revision; not Act I exposition dumps.
