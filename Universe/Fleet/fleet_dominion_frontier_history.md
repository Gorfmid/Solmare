## Core model (series canon)

Fleet Authority and the Dominion are **not** strangers who met at a treaty line.

They are civilizations whose **histories diverged**, whose **maps eventually overlap**, and whose **institutional memory is asymmetric**.

| Civilization | Age (institutional) | Memory posture |
|--------------|---------------------|----------------|
| **The Dominion** | Ancient — substantially older than Fleet; deep institutions, campaign memory | Historical — campaigns, plaques, preparation doctrine |
| **Fleet Authority** | ~**800 years** since breakaway seed; still young in **self-mythology** | Prospective — Founders' Week, Relay Charter, expansion celebrated; origin scrubbed |

**Book One restraint:** Fleet characters do **not** know the name *Dominion* until authorized discovery beats. Dominion POV may know Fleet Authority by function and habit, not necessarily by Fleet's self-names on-page.

## Origins (deep history — series seed)

Fleet Authority originated ~**800 years** before the present story era as a **breakaway founding polity** — people who left Dominion civilization into far uncatalogued space and built a new institutional identity.

### Multi-wave founding

Founding was **not** a single convoy myth (even if public memory prefers one clean story).

- Multiple **survey / colony convoys** left Dominion charted space over an extended founding period (Book Two Prelude shows one autumn cohort; registrar records already imply others on the same destination notation).
- Long convoy transit (order of **generations** at colony pace — e.g. ~**142 years** for the Prelude wave) created substantial geographic separation from Dominion space and from a harvest pressure the founding cohorts would not fully name on forms.
- Early settlements remained independent or loosely networked at first.
- As Fleet institutions matured, they **absorbed** colony worlds and station-states into membership — growth by incorporation as much as by new planting. This supports dense multi-world texture over ~800 years without requiring every world to be planted from Helion.

### Memory split

The founding generations carried practical skills and some cartographic habits, but **scrubbed or reframed** Dominion history in public memory:

- Founders' Week celebrates a **fresh beginning**, not shared inheritance.
- Fleet Historical Office curates **prospective** origin stories.
- Dominion institutional memory retains the breakaway as historical fact and later watches Fleet expansion as pressure — not as a mystery civilization that appeared from nowhere without precedent.

Neither civilization is lying in full. Each inherited a **different archive**.

## Pre-contact frontier (no Neutral Zone yet)

**Authoritative for Book Two+ production.**

Before peer discovery, the **sector** between Fleet survey edge and Dominion survey edge is **uncharted / empty-on-the-chart space** — not a named buffer polity zone.

### Fleet understanding (pre-contact)

- Charts end in **uncatalogued / beyond survey / provisional empty** language.
- Expansion doctrine treats the unknown as space to explore.
- Fleet does **not** maintain a living model of a peer civilization beyond the edge.
- Fleet does **not** use **Neutral Zone** as an inherited founding term.

### Dominion understanding (pre-contact)

- The same far **sector** reads as **empty / unoccupied depth** relative to Dominion cataloguing — until Fleet traffic and contact prove otherwise.
- Dominion may hold border watch and preparation posture along its own fringe without naming a shared Neutral Zone.
- Once Fleet expansion becomes visible, Dominion interprets it as strategic pressure on depth — still without requiring the later diplomatic label.

### Distance logic

~800 years of Fleet growth + multi-decade founding leaps + Dominion's greater age create enough separation that the civilizations can expand for centuries before hard peer contact. Contact is **geographic collision after long mutual ignorance of the other's full nature**, not a meeting at a pre-negotiated line.

## The Neutral Zone (post-contact institutional product)

The **Neutral Zone** is **created after peer discovery** — a negotiated / institutional label for contested or shared buffer geometry between Fleet Authority and the Dominion.

It is **not**:

- Surviving founding vocabulary carried from the breakaway
- Proof that either side always understood who occupied the sector
- A Book One–era requirement for series continuity going forward

It **is**:

- Post-contact chart language, ROE framing, and later instruments (warning corridors, joint observation, Helion Accord–era products in Book Two)
- A political map object both sides can argue about while still disagreeing on history

### Book One published text

Book One is **locked and published**. Do **not** reopen chapter prose for Neutral Zone terminology cleanup unless the author explicitly unlocks an errata pass. Production for Book Two+ follows **this** document. Do not spotlight Book One wording conflicts on-page or in reader-facing materials.

## Expansion collision (current era)

- **Fleet Authority** expands outward — colonies, survey epochs, relay installs, absorption of earlier colony worlds into membership.
- **The Dominion** holds and maintains its own fringe geometry — preparation doctrine, not reactive surprise once Fleet is known.
- The collision is **geographic and historical** — two expansion logics meeting — not a first meeting at a long-standing Neutral Zone treaty line.

## Narrative rules

1. **Do not** treat Neutral Zone as pre-contact inherited vocabulary in new prose or production docs.
2. **Do not** grant Fleet the name *Dominion* before authorized beats.
3. Pre-contact / early-contact frontier speech: **uncharted**, **beyond survey**, **empty on the chart**, corridor/approach names as local survey products — not Neutral Zone doctrine lectures.
4. Introduce or lean on **Neutral Zone** as a **post-discovery institutional product** when the story has earned peer contact and buffer politics.
5. Dominion POV may contrast Fleet's **edited memory** with Dominion **campaign memory** — show; do not lecture.
6. The exodus origin is **series depth** — multi-wave, ~800-year Fleet age, absorption growth — reveal through Dominion scenes, Historical Office friction, and earned discovery; not Act I exposition dumps.
7. Prefer **sector** (or corridor / approach / charted space) for geographic regions. Do **not** use **volume** for regions of space — it confuses.
