# 2. Themes of the Series + Solmare Principle

## THE SOLMARE PRINCIPLE

> History creates civilizations.  
> Civilizations create doctrine.  
> Doctrine creates decisions.  
> Decisions create conflict.  
> The people living through those decisions are the story.  
>  
> **Never write a battle before understanding the history that made it inevitable.**

## Series thesis (production framing)

The Solmare Cycle is **not** good versus evil.

It is **good people serving civilizations whose histories have placed them on an unavoidable collision course**.

Readers should frequently think: *I understand exactly why they made that decision* — even when that decision moves civilizations closer to war.

**Success metric:** Empathy with **both** Fleet Authority and the Dominion simultaneously. Operational allegiance may still favor Fleet.

## Core thematic pillars

| Pillar | Production meaning |
|--------|-------------------|
| **History as antagonist** | Neither Fleet nor Dominion is the series villain |
| **Asymmetric memory** | Same geography; different archives; different commemorations |
| **Institutional realism** | Meetings, headers, editions, freight windows, classification failure |
| **Observation before judgment** | Book One First Doctrine lineage; later books stress-test it |
| **Wonder before explanation** | Especially for alien third civilization and deep cosmology |
| **Trust is harder than war** | Book Two working theme (`Book2_Story_Bible.md`) |
| **People > ships > technology** | Emotional priority in craft doctrine |

## Philosophical conflict (Fleet ↔ Dominion)

| Fleet Authority believes | The Dominion believes |
|--------------------------|----------------------|
| Expansion creates stability | Strategic depth creates security |
| Exploration creates opportunity | Buffer space prevents wars |
| Colonies / trade create prosperity and peace | Fleet colonies near the border reduce Dominion security |
| Unclaimed space should be explored | Empty space is protection |

**Same events, different readings:** survey = science vs intelligence; colony ship = hope vs expansion; beacon = progress vs future military infrastructure. **Neither side is lying.**

Full doctrine: `Universe/The_Dominion/dominion_worldview.md` · `Writing_System/Series_Principles/cultural_identity_and_philosophical_foundation.md`

# 3. Canon Rules + Things Future Books Should Never Violate

## Hard locks (do not violate)

1. **Neither civilization is the series antagonist. History is.**  
2. **Ch. 5–8 Kestrel Veil Incident = classified Dominion Leviathan cloak field trial** aboard *Iron Harrow* — **not** an unprovoked attack. Commander Vex followed doctrine; cloak partially failed under sustained passive Fleet scout detection.  
3. **No border treaties** between Fleet and Dominion in current deep-past / Book One framing — collision is expansion meeting maintained strategic depth, not diplomatic parity. (Later provisional frameworks appear only if timeline docs are adopted and book outlines authorize.)  
4. **Fleet does not know the name *Dominion*** until authorized discovery beats. Dominion POV may know Fleet by function/habit.  
5. **Asymmetric memory:** Dominion retains breakaway / campaign memory; Fleet public myth scrubbed or reframed it (Founders' Week forward-facing memory).  
6. **Cultural naming:** Fleet = **Founders' Week** + **coffee**; Dominion = **harvest festival** / **district festival** + **tea**. No casual cross-bleed.  
7. **Cultural memory habits:** Fleet thinks **prospectively**; Dominion thinks **historically**. Dominion quotes inheritance; Fleet almost never does. Show; never lecture.  
8. **Vessel class hierarchy** in `Universe/canon_and_world_rules.md` — do not blur roles without in-story justification.  
9. **Scout-class** = mission designation, not a combat class in the fighter→leviathan hierarchy.  
10. **Kestrel Veil** crew complement standard: **20**; narrative foregrounds principal personnel (see crew canon).  
11. **Peligar** = Dominion exonym already known in Dominion industrial/civilization comparison (Book One Ch. 13). Self-name unknown. Do not invent a full Peligar encyclopedia in production docs without outline need.  
12. **Epistemological rule:** Do not explain everything. Operational clarity + structural ambiguity.  
13. **Romantic interests:** Principal/significant characters are straight (series craft rule).  
14. **Language:** No swearing stronger than *hell* / *damn* / *bastard*; invent mild in-universe oaths as needed.

## Soft locks (expand carefully)

| Topic | Rule |
|-------|------|
| True Dominion strategic objective | Ambiguous in Book One; multiple in-character beliefs; do not confirm sole author truth early |
| Breakaway founding details | Series depth; reveal through archives / Dominion memory / Historical Office friction — not Act I dumps |

# 5. Fleet History

## Summary

Fleet Authority (formal: **Unified Fleet Authority / UFA**; colloquial: **the Empire**) is a layered administrative-military federation practicing **order through controlled expansion**.

- **Self-mythology:** young, prospective, Founders' Week, Relay Charter, pioneering ethos (*Someone has to go first.*)

# 6. Dominion History

## Summary

The Dominion is an **ancient interstellar civilization** — not a dictatorship, not a democracy, not an evil empire. Power is shared among competing institutions (Civil Government, Strategic Security Directorate, Dominion Fleet, Science / Industrial / Intelligence Directorates). National loyalty is high; unanimity is not.

- **Age:** centuries of institutions; border installations older than Fleet Authority itself  
- **Strategic philosophy:** preparation, strategic depth, surprise, logistics; glory irrelevant  
- **Memory:** historical — campaigns, plaques, ancestors, sacrifice stories  
- **Relationship to Fleet:** breakaway remembered; Fleet expansion watched for decades; Neutral Zone = maintained depth  
- **Book One posture:** preparing for major conflict for years — **show evidence, never announce “we are preparing for war”**  
- **Leviathan Program:** one instrument of long-term preparation (stealth / surprise / technical edge)

## Dominion historical beats (index)

| Beat | Production note |
|------|-----------------|
| Deep institutional age | Ancient academies; historic shipyards; memorials to forgotten wars |
| Breakaway remembered | Campaign memory; loss of depth; edited Fleet forgetting as strategic fact |
| Border watch centuries | K-node pattern; plaques honoring prior generations |
| Decades of Fleet observation | Traffic habits, Founders' Week patterns, expansion pressure modeled |
| Preparation elevation | Fuel depots, shipyard tempo, reserve recalls — ordinary texture that aggregates |
| Iron Harrow cloak trial | Book One Incident; Vex accountability; intelligence risk |
| Post-trial (if adopted) | Mark II–IV; Long Watch; Deepline; Stratimirov Assessment tradition |
| Accommodation cost politics | Freight capacity decline vs preparation benefit — Civil Government vs SSD |

## Evidence-of-preparation palette (show, do not announce)

Expanding shipyards · reserve mobilization · fuel depots · industrial expansion · exercises · procurement · scientific acceleration · transport congestion · fortified borders · classified planning conferences · surveillance upgrades · infrastructure modernization.

None alone proves war. Together they reveal pattern.
