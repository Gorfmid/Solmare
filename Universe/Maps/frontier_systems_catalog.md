## Kestran Spiral Sector, Neutral Zone & Beyond
### Fleet Cartography Service — Supplement to Edition 143
### Classification: RESTRICTED — Survey Authority Distribution Only

---

## EDITORIAL NOTE

This catalog covers the stellar systems of the frontier arc: the Kestran Spiral Sector, the Neutral Zone buffer volume, and the strategic geography beyond Fleet's current survey authority. Zone A entries draw from Edition 143 charted data. Zone B entries are marked by status uncertainty, provisional survey confidence, and in several cases active discrepancy between relay geometry and epoch baseline shading. Zone C and Zone D entries represent author-side canon — what exists beyond Fleet's current visibility, organized for series continuity.

**Asymmetric naming note:** Several systems carry dual designations — Fleet and non-Fleet. Neither civilization's name for a given system is acknowledged by the other in the current era.

**On boring entries:** Not every system in the frontier arc is interesting. A catalog that contained only strategically significant or narratively rich systems would have already made editorial decisions the scout in the field cannot afford to make. Some entries are included because they are there.

## Kestran Prime — Kestran I

**Star type:** G-type main sequence
**Position:** Fleet side — Segment 6 interior
**Fleet status:** Charted
**Dominion presence:** None known
**Strategic value:** High — industrial anchor for the Kestran Spiral Sector; alloy production and sector supply hub
**Notable features:**
- Source of Kestran alloy, referenced in mid-rim trade networks and Fleet construction supply chains
- Administrative seat for Kestran Spiral Sector patrol and survey operations
- Moderate civilian population; established colony with multi-generational service tradition
- Relay infrastructure connecting to outer Kestran loop via secondary travel lanes

**Historical notes:** Kestran Prime was among the first systems charted in the sector now bearing its name. Its alloy output has supported Fleet construction for generations; the Kestran industrial belt is named for this system and its adjacent extraction networks. Survey epoch data from Kestran Prime forms the calibration anchor for much of the Segment 6 outer arc. Its administrative records office manages personnel files for the entire sector.

**Narrative potential:** A world people leave to become scouts and return to in stories about promotions or funerals. Industrial, proud, undersupplied relative to what it produces. The kind of place that sends soldiers and welders and waits for both.

## Kestran VI — Watchpost Station VI-Gamma

**Star type:** Binary — K-type primary, M-dwarf secondary
**Position:** Fleet side — Segment 7 approach
**Fleet status:** Charted
**Dominion presence:** None known
**Strategic value:** High — listening post, fringe relay berthing, border watch command node for sector
**Notable features:**
- Kestran VI-Gamma: watchpost with grafted berthing collar; infrastructure described as "stressed but operational"
- Relay routing classification layer: VI-Gamma filters and routes all fringe traffic before Fleet Operations
- Current occupants: Watchpost Commander Holt, Operator Lin
- Fringe contact range; six-hour comms windows with Fleet operational centers
- Berthing capacity: collar grafted for repair of wounded scouts; *Kestrel Veil* repaired here (Book One, Act II)

**Historical notes:** VI-Gamma began as a passive listening post and grew into the sector's primary fringe relay node through forty years of incremental expansion rather than designed intent — each successive epoch adding one more collar, one more antenna array, one more desk in a room not built for it. Maintenance logs describe the current structure as functional. This is accurate. Holt annotates outgoing traffic before it enters Fleet Operations. What she annotates shapes the intelligence picture.

**Narrative potential:** The gatehouse. Every report from segment seven passes through VI-Gamma. What Holt and Lin forward — and how they annotate it — is the first editorial filter between field reality and institutional response.

**Historical notes:** Sable Point has the quiet reputation of a place where odd things happen and nobody writes the report.
