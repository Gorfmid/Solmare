# 1. United Fleet Authority (UFA) Overview

## Names in use

| Name | Usage |
|------|--------|
| **Unified Fleet Authority (UFA)** | Formal institutional name — fleet paperwork, survey manuals, archive headers |
| **Fleet Authority** | Primary narrative and professional name |
| **the Empire** | Colloquial citizen-facing name — festivals, pride, everyday speech |

## What the UFA is (and is not)

The UFA is best understood as a **layered administrative-military federation**:

- **Not** a single centralized government (political, military, and field command are separated by design).
- **Not** a pure military alliance (member worlds, trade law, and civilian oversight exist).
- **Is** an interstellar authority that combines **civilian political governance**, **Fleet military command**, and **exploration/survey institutions** under one institutional identity citizens call *the Empire*.

**Ambiguity (intentional):** Founding date in standard calendar years is **not fixed in manuscript canon**. Public memory emphasizes **Founders' Week**, **Relay Charter**, and **prospective expansion** — the polity is **centuries old** but **young in self-mythology** (`Universe/Fleet/fleet_dominion_frontier_history.md`).

## Purpose

- Maintain passage law and corridor stability across member worlds  
- Expand charted space through survey epochs and relay installation  
- Defend member worlds and Fleet assets  
- Regulate trade and interworld arbitration (often slowly)  
- Centralize exploration metadata, navigation baselines, and doctrine  

## Political structure

Three parallel systems (`Universe/Fleet/fleet_authority_governance_and_command_hierarchy.md`):

1. **Civilian political authority** — Central Assembly, Senate, trade accords, resource allocation, Fleet funding oversight. Consensus-driven; poor real-time crisis response.  
2. **Fleet military command** — Deployments, strategic defense, ROE, threat response. Hierarchical; dependent on information integrity.  
3. **Operational field command** — Ship and station captains; survival authority when comms degrade or orders contradict local sensor reality.

**Normal chain:** Civilian Authority → Military Command → Task Forces → Ship Captains  
**Under uncertainty:** Field command may override delayed or contradictory higher orders.

## Member worlds and civilian governments

- Hundreds of member worlds and station-states connected by corridors (`book1_core_premise.md`).  
- Local governments retain cultural and economic identity; Fleet holds **passage law**, defense obligations, and survey authority at the frontier.  
- **Core houses** and regional blocs (e.g. **Helion Industrial States**) compete and cooperate with Fleet — allies in name, competitors in practice (Ch. 1).

## Fleet command structure (summary)

| Layer | Examples (Book One) |
|-------|---------------------|
| Strategic / operations | Fleet Command (Rowan), Fleet Operations |
| Intelligence | Commodore Sato; Fleet Intelligence Bureau interface |
| Doctrine | Fleet Doctrine Bureau — Edition 143 → 144 transition |
| Survey / charts | Fleet Cartography Service; Fleet Survey Authority (Edition 143 manual) |
| Communications | Fleet Communications Command; relay routing (VI-Gamma layer) |
| Field | Scout captains, watchpost commanders (Holt), sector patrol assets |

## Exploration authority

- **Fleet Survey Authority** — survey epochs, segment boundaries, Edition 143 assumptions (*Maps describe what we know. Not necessarily what exists.* — prologue margin culture).  
- **Fleet Cartography Service** — reconciliation, baseline divergence, routing overlays.  
- **Fleet Exploration Corps** — scout missions, frontier verification (see `scout_class.md`).

Scouts **verify and report**; they do not independently expand borders or engage offensively.

## Defense responsibilities

- Corridor patrol, picket traffic, escort where tasked  
- Scout risk-averse engagement policy — withdrawal over confrontation  
- Capital assets exist in class hierarchy (`canon_and_world_rules.md`) — Book One focuses on scout operations  

## Trade responsibilities

- Senate and Central Assembly accords; tariff bands; industrial supply chains (Helion hull plate, Selene textiles, Kestran alloy, Verdant Line harvest — Ch. 1 traffic summaries).  
- Meridian Gate as major commercial junction (three major jump corridors).

## Research responsibilities

- Cartography reconciliation and epoch review  
- Passive detection doctrine (FCC reviews)  
- Sensor verification at frontier — often underfunded until contradictions force review

# 2. UFA Space Segment Structure

Fleet Cartography divides **charted UFA space** into **seven survey segments** on the official navigation chart. Segments are **administrative-survey regions**, not equal population zones. Boundaries revise with epoch updates — poorly, when metadata outruns reconciliation (Segment Seven).

## Segment One — Core Systems

**Official designation:** UFA Segment 1 — Core Systems  
**General purpose:** Oldest, densest governed territory; political and institutional heartland.  
**Development level:** Maximum infrastructure density; established law and relay mesh.  
**Population density:** Highest in charted UFA space.

**Travel lane importance:** Hub for all primary lanes; every secondary route eventually ties back.

# 3. Travel Network

## Core principle

Control is **node-based**, not continuous territory (`galaxy.md`). Empires hold **corridors between systems**, not empty volume.

## Primary travel lanes

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Purpose** | Core ↔ mid-rim freight; military deployment; passenger transit |
| **Traffic volume** | Highest in Segments 1–3; Meridian Gate junction |
| **Military importance** | Strategic movement; convoy escort routes |
| **Travel time** | **Not fixed numerically in canon** — mission loops stated in hours/days (e.g. ~46-hour patrol loop Ch. 4) |

## Secondary travel lanes

| Type | Examples |
|------|----------|
| Colony routes | Expansion Corridor settlements |
| Resource routes | Helion plate, Kestran alloy, Verdant harvest |
| Commercial routes | Selene textiles; ocean-world perishables |

## Explorer lanes

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Purpose** | Survey verification; scout patrol; frontier calibration |
| **Infrastructure** | Limited relay; buffer-dependent comms |
| **Navigation risks** | Degraded jump precision in outer segments; probabilistic jumps in Deep Void (`galaxy.md`) |

## Interstellar travel rules (canon)

| Topic | Rule |
|-------|------|
| **FTL** | Jump-drive using **fixed calibration nodes**; accuracy degrades in Outer Rim / Segment 6–7 |
| **Navigation** | Fleet charts + margin annotations; epoch baselines; scout officers attest local read |
| **Relays** | Long-range comms and traffic routing require relay infrastructure; outside coverage → buffer and delay |
| **Communications** | Tiered model — local ship → task group → Fleet network → archival (`fleet_communication_sensor_and_information_warfare_doctrine.md`); **information is never absolute** |
| **Travel times** | Light-speed constraints on strategic comms; tactical execution local |
| **Deep Void** | Unstable; probabilistic jumps — not routine Fleet traffic |

# 10. Canon Rules (summary)

1. This document **supports** the story; it does **not** rewrite it.  
2. **Locked manuscript** and authoritative `Universe/` docs **win** on conflict.  
3. **Unknown regions remain unknown** — no premature civilization fills.  
4. **Not every contact is an enemy** — structured presence ≠ hostility (Book One).  
5. **Institutional realism** — Fleet professionals operate on incomplete information.  
6. **Expansion is slow, expensive, bureaucratic** — reconciliation queues, epoch review, Founders' Week burying priority drafts.  
7. **The map is a Fleet instrument** — segments, provisional shading, margin notes, commanding officer attestation.  
8. **Map-only names** (Accord Prime, Harmony, Founders' Bastion, etc.) — do not invent prose backstory until promoted to manuscript canon.  
9. **Dominion** — placeholder section only until scene authorization; use `dominion_worldview.md` when writing authorized Dominion material.  
10. **Asymmetric history** — Fleet prospective memory vs Dominion historical memory; never flatten into single official truth.
