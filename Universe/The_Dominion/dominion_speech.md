## Core Principle

**Dominion culture values continuity.**

**Fleet culture values innovation.**

Readers should **feel** this difference in how characters speak — **without explanation**.

---

## Dominion Speech Habit

Dominion characters **frequently quote** (or echo without attribution):

- teachers
- parents
- grandparents
- academy instructors
- old commanders
- historical sayings

Not every line. Not every scene. But often enough that dialogue carries **inherited authority** — wisdom passed down, not invented in the moment.

Examples in spirit (never explain on page):

- *"My grandmother used to say—"*
- *"Instructor Vek at the academy—"*
- *"Old Commander Petran would have called that rounding error wearing a uniform."*
- A proverb dropped mid-argument as if everyone in the room already knew the source
- Kovat repeating her mother's line to Nika; Stratimirov hearing an instructor's voice in his own policy

**Established series proverbs** (*"Preparation honors those who come after us."* · *"Conquest is what other people call it when sacrifice succeeds."*) may appear through **any** of these voices — grandmother, instructor, planner, veteran.

**Do not explain** who said it first or why it matters. Context and repetition teach the reader.

---

## Fleet Speech Contrast

Fleet characters **almost never** quote teachers, parents, grandparents, academy instructors, or old commanders in the same way.

Fleet dialogue tends toward:

- current mission language
- procedure and checklist
- forward-looking solutions (*someone has to go first*)
- innovation, adaptation, what works **now**
- peer banter and professional shorthand

Fleet officers may reference **Founders' Week** ritual or academy graduation — but as **shared culture**, not inherited quotation. They do not habitually speak through ancestors.

| Dominion (continuity) | Fleet (innovation) |
|----------------------|-------------------|
| Quotes grandmother, instructor, old commander | Quotes regulation, peer, current doctrine |
| *"Preparation honors those who come after us."* | *"Someone has to go first."* |
| History as living voice in the room | Future as open problem to solve |
| Sacrifice preserved in sayings | Opportunity in forward motion |

**Show the contrast.** Never have characters comment on it.

---

## Writing Rules

1. **One inherited quote per scene** is often enough — do not stack proverbs.
2. **Earned voices** — the same saying may come from different speakers across the series (grandmother, instructor, border officer).
3. **No glossaries** — readers learn who Dominion people sound like by hearing them.
4. **Ordinary speech first** — parents at school rehearsal, planners in meetings, welders on gantries — not only directors.
5. **Fleet POV stays Fleet** — Calder's crew should not start quoting Dominion instructors. Dominion Thread should not sound like Fleet banter.
6. **Recognition before naming** — Dominion dialogue patterns should help readers know whose scene they are in before the header.

## Book One Seeds (Ch. 9–12)

- Stratimirov: instructor memory (*strategic depth*); policy without speechmaking
- Volkova: grandmother taught gloves courtesy; Jasko/Dusan proverb exchange
- Kovat: grandmother to Nika (*conquest is what other people call it when sacrifice succeeds*); Teacher Marin at rehearsal
- Jasko: *preparation honors those who come after us* / Fleet counterpart in planning room

**Continue the pattern** — Dominion voices carry history; Fleet voices carry the next watch.

---

## Do Not

- Explain that Dominion culture values continuity
- Make every Dominion line a quotation
- Give Fleet characters inherited-quote habits without in-story reason (e.g. Dominion upbringing)
- Use quotations as exposition dumps
- Label speakers *as the Dominion* in narration — let speech do the work
