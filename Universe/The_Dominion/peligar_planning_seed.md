## Civilization triangle

| Civilization | Exists to preserve… |
|--------------|---------------------|
| **Fleet Authority** | Life |
| **The Dominion** | Order |
| **Peligar** | **Continuity** |

Not conquest. Not glory. **Continuity of the whole.**

Everything they do is about ensuring continuation of the collective system. Stopping expansion equals decline. Decline equals extinction.

## What they are not

- Not space locusts / cartoon eaters
- Not sadists; not haters of humanity
- Not a single-mineral MacGuffin civilization
- Not negotiable-by-default (negotiability unknown — withhold)

They are **industrial ecologists** with a survival doctrine that does not encode other civilizations as moral subjects.

---

## Industrial ecology (core model)

When they find a system, they do not see cities, people, or nations.

They see:

- metals
- volatiles
- carbon
- fusion fuels
- orbital mechanics
- energy gradients

A star system is a **resource graph**.

They are not "stealing worlds." They are **processing** them.

They dismantle, over time:

- asteroids, moons, atmospheres, oceans, orbital infrastructure
- eventually entire planets — because planets contain useful matter

**People are obstacles, not objectives.**

### Ellis beat (series-ready)

> We keep asking why they invaded.  
> They didn't.  
> They began extraction.  
> They killed twelve billion people.  
> They removed twelve billion obstacles.

That distinction is the horror. Timing: seed in Book Two; land fully Book Three/Four if needed. Book Two may show the *behavior*; Ellis names the frame when earned.

## The expanding border

The border expands because expansion is how the civilization survives.

Metaphor (author only — do not lecture on page): a shark does not hate fish; it keeps swimming.

- Stopping = decline
- Decline = extinction
- Aggression is the wrong frame; **necessity-without-hatred** is the right one

Fleet/Dominion maps show a frontier that **moves**. Controllers argue whether it is war, migration, or industry. It is continuity doctrine made geographic.

---

## Speech

Peligar communication combines:

- sound
- infrared
- polarization
- harmonic vibration

Humans hear only a fraction.

**To human ears:** loud, dissonant, multi-vocal — layered screaming. First intercepts are often misfiled as distress, pain, or medical emergency.

**Iconic recognition beat:** Park (or equivalent) realizes — *They're talking.*

**Inverse:** To Peligar, human speech may register as a single-note hum — inadequate channel, not hostility.

**Book Two lock:** Full translation remains **impossible**. Partial pattern recognition, latency maps, and "this is language" are allowed. Meaning exchange with Peligar is not.

## Appearance (when shown)

Lean into **radial biology**:

- Six limbs; radial symmetry
- No obvious face, eyes, or shoulders
- Infrared perception; polarized skin signaling
- Multiple vocal organs
- Every direction can be "forward"; they may rotate while moving
- Observers cannot tell whether they are "looking" at you

Use sparingly. Recognition through behavior and sensor returns first; anatomy later if at all in Book Two.

---

## Identity

Peligar culture does not center individual identity.

Fleet may ask a captured or contacted unit for a name and receive something equivalent to:

> This processor belongs to Harvest Group Nine.

Not refusal — **category error**. The question does not parse.

Do not overuse. One earned beat is enough for Book Two.

Fleet: *They're monsters.*
