## Design posture

The Dominion is a **centuries-old interstellar civilization**. Fleet Authority is comparatively young in institutional self-mythology. Every section below should reinforce that age: layered offices, inherited procedures, archives older than Fleet colonies, Directorates that argue in forms their grandparents already used.

The Dominion is **not** a dictatorship and **not** a democracy. It is a **preparedness civilization** — duty, service, family, discipline, education, competence, history, sacrifice — whose people genuinely believe hardship today prevents catastrophe tomorrow.

**Neither civilization is the series antagonist. History is.**

Daily life texture (cafés, shift culture, grandparents, memorials, food, festivals) lives in detail in `dominion_civilian_life.md`. This document covers the **civilization as institutional architecture**. Where civilian texture is needed, cite that file rather than restating it.

## 1. Government

### Constitutional character

The Dominion has no single sovereign person and no electoral mandate in the Fleet sense. Authority is **distributed among enduring institutions** that compete for resources while sharing one loyalty: preservation of the Dominion.

The constitutional feeling of the state is **inheritance**. Offices occupy buildings older than living memory. Procedures cite precedents by campaign name and century. A new Director-General does not reinvent Strategic Security; they inherit a table, a plaque wall, and a standard for what failure looks like on paper. Civil Government does not “found” districts each generation; it administers districts whose names already carry commanders and settlement histories.

| Layer | Function | Character |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| **Civil Government** | District administration, education logistics, public health, memorials, permits, civic ceremony | The face most citizens meet daily |
| **Directorates** | Specialized civil-military-technical authorities (see §2) | Career institutions; centuries of procedure |
| **Dominion Fleet** | Armed force, frontier garrison, academy pipeline | Significant authority; not sole power |
| **Political Liaison** | Civil–military interface, oversight packets, institutional grievance channels | Oversight without day-to-day command of operations |

**Military leaders possess significant authority. Political leadership exercises oversight. Neither fully trusts the other.** Scientists, industry, intelligence, logistics, and the military compete for influence. Political disagreement exists. National loyalty does not.

### Age of government

Evidence of governmental age should appear constantly in institutional settings:

| Marker | Example |
|--------|---------|
| Building tenure | SSD basalt-and-steel complex occupied for three centuries (Book One) |
| Script layers | Memorial stones with archaic script in oldest name bands |
| Procedure archaeology | Routing mathematics older than current frontier revision codes |
| Career memory | Officers and administrators who quote instructors still living in anecdote |
| Plaque walls | Campaign names everyone already knows; anterooms as compressed history |

Fleet Authority headquarters may feel purposeful and recent. Dominion government spaces feel **revised for centuries without being replaced**.

### Civil Government structure

```
Civil Government (central)
│
├── District Administration (member worlds / stations)
│   ├── District Administrator
│   ├── Permit & Leave Offices
│   ├── Memorial Coordination
│   ├── Festival Logistics (harvest / district festival support)
│   └── Local Transit Coordination (with Transportation Directorate)
│
├── Public Health Administration (see § Healthcare)
├── Civic Records & Attestation
│   ├── Family / service record registries (civil copies)
│   ├── Passage Day / Civic Attestation certification
│   └── Inscription certification for memorial additions
└── Education Logistics (facilities, calendars; curriculum with Education Directorate)
```

**District Administrators** are the connective tissue between civilian life and institutional function. They are known by name throughout their districts. They process harvest festival leave, school calendar approvals, public memorial logistics, and the quiet paperwork that makes freight windows and funerals both possible. During filing seasons and festival surge, they are among the first to arrive and last to leave.

Most ordinary citizens experience the Dominion through **local faces**: teachers, district administrators, transit officials, market notice boards — not through Strategic Security or Intelligence. See `dominion_civilian_life.md` §1, §6, §10.

### How the state is experienced

| Channel | What citizens feel |
|---------|-------------------|
| Education system | Continuity narrative; sacrifice history; civic choice at Passage Day |
| Directorates | Employers, regulators, freight calendars, research grants |
| Military | Deployed relatives, garrisons, **public funerals** |
| Harvest festival | Annual convergence of civilian and institutional life |
| District offices | Permits, leave, memorials, school calendars |

The relationship of citizen to state is closer to **household continuity** than to subject-vs-ruler or rights-vs-authority. Citizens believe previous generations sacrificed so the Dominion would continue — and that their own contributions are the same form under different circumstances. Full texture: `dominion_civilian_life.md` §1–2.

### Legitimacy model

Legitimacy rests on **demonstrated continuity**:

1. Schools open; tea is hot; harvest festival arrives.  
2. Memorials are maintained; funerals are public; promotions stay private.  
3. Freight mostly moves; clinics mostly function; Directorates argue without dissolving.  
4. When the state asks for more (shifts, taxes, postings), history suggests there was usually a reason — even if unclassified.

This is not blind faith. It is pattern recognition across generations. Where institutions fail locally (unsafe shifts, under-resourced frontiers), complaint is real — and still usually framed as making the household function, not replacing the household.

## 2. Directorates (full set)

Directorates are the Dominion’s primary specialized institutions. They are **not cabinets that change with personalities**. They are centuries-old bodies with buildings, archives, career ladders, and internal cultures. Advancement within a Directorate confers recognized status without requiring military rank.

|-------------|------------------|---------------------|--------------------------|

| **Science Directorate** | Research institutes, applied programs, academy pipeline, sealed technical channels | High prestige | Cloak bench metrics; grants; sealed crates in freight |
| **Industrial Directorate** | Production schedules, yards, safety regulation, military procurement interface | High in industrial worlds | Helikon plate stock; Slip schedules; eighteen-hour rotations |

### Supporting offices (not Directorates)

These appear in institutional speech as **named offices**. They nest under Directorates or Civil Government.

| Office / Division | Typical parent | Function |
|-------------------|----------------|----------|
| **Procurement Office** | Industrial / Fleet logistics interface | Forecasts, requisitions, depot timelines |
| **Planning Office** | Transportation / SSD as context requires | Corridor models, congestion overlays |
| **Transit Control** | Transportation Directorate | Live routing, holds, window execution |
| **Customs Office** | Border Administration / Transportation | Manifests, restricted-zone clearance |

**Naming rule (series canon):** Use full institutional names on first reference in a scene (*Education Directorate*, *Regional Transportation Directorate*). Characters may shorten after establishment. Do not use bare abstract nouns as org names. Full table: `Characters/Antagonists/The_Dominion.md`.

### Directorate detail — functions and cultures

#### Strategic Security Directorate

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Mandate** | Preserve strategic depth; coordinate long-horizon preparation; oversee classified programs that affect civilization risk |
| **Method** | Packets, investigation of failure, documentation, career consequence without theatrical threat |
| **Culture** | Calm procedural gravity; tea; gloves removed before serious work; campaign plaques in anterooms; family photographs on desks |
| **Relationship to Fleet** | Oversight and strategic framing; does not replace ship command |
| **Relationship to Science** | Needs Science metrics; distrusts Science framing failure as “performance” rather than outcome |
| **Civilian face** | Classification decisions, border travel advisories, research restrictions |

Failure is investigated, documented, assigned, and remembered. Glory is irrelevant. Preparation is the highest virtue.

#### Intelligence Directorate

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Mandate** | Know more about expansion pressure and external civilizations than any single operational command |
| **Method** | Passive preference where doctrine allows; pattern accumulation; clearance walls |
| **Culture** | Sparse speech; inherited quotation rarer in operational rooms than in civilian life; still historically minded |
| **Civilian face** | Almost none — appropriate division of labor |

#### Science Directorate

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Mandate** | Durable knowledge; applied results that support preparation; documentation across generations |
| **Method** | Institutes, sealed channels, bench criteria, grant competition, citation of three-generation-old findings |
| **Culture** | Intellectual pride; internal argument about basic vs. applied research is centuries old; tea in quantity |
| **Prestige** | Research leads outrank most civilian administrators socially — broadly accepted |
| **Tension** | Frequently argues military doctrine overvalues tactical preparation vs. long-horizon research (resource battles, not public revolt) |

Expanded institutional section: § Science Directorate below.

#### Industrial Directorate

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Mandate** | Sustained production capacity above current-demand minimums; yard continuity; safety and schedule |
| **Method** | Quotas framed as good management; coordination with Procurement Office and military berthing |
| **Culture** | Shift identity; inherited tools; yard proverbs (*The keel goes down while the weather is still kind.*) |
| **Civilian face** | Factory employment, safety reviews, shipyard districts (Korrast, Kessik-Outer pattern) |

Old ships are revered rather than retired. Yards layer new berths on old anchor blocks. Continuity is visible as weld scars under paint.

#### Transportation Directorate

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Mandate** | Civilization as scheduling problem solved in advance — freight, passengers, military priority, maintenance, festival surge |
| **Method** | Corridor geometry; freight windows; Transit Control; Regional Directorates for member-world clusters |
| **Culture** | Map-and-schedule native language; strong esprit de corps; mobile careers |
| **Most public annual function** | Harvest festival freight-window management |

**Regional Transportation Directorate** is the operational face in Book One (Volkova). Planning mathematics are older than current frontier revision codes.

#### Education Directorate

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Mandate** | Curriculum continuity; sacrifice-narrative history; logistics literacy; academy/university feed |
| **Method** | Central standards with regional application variance; Review Assembly; Passage Day; Civic Attestation |
| **Civilian face** | Highest-trust ordinary profession interface (teachers); school calendars; festival pageants; transit grievances for excursions |

Detail: § Education, § Universities; daily texture in `dominion_civilian_life.md` §4, §17.

#### Agricultural Directorate

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Mandate** | Food-system stability; harvest timing; agricultural world standards; coordination with Transportation for seasonal mass flow |
| **Method** | Crop-cycle calendars synchronized to institutional harvest festival dates; reserve margins |
| **Civilian face** | Highest on agricultural worlds (Ostavar-pattern); seasonal everywhere via markets and freight |

#### Communications Directorate

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Mandate** | Institutional notices, memorial broadcasts, public information framing, courier and notice-board networks |
| **Method** | Preparation framing over alarm; logistical language for military-adjacent announcements |
| **Civilian face** | District notice boards, feeds, funeral notices, festival calendars |

Not a propaganda ministry in the crude sense. Framing reflects preparedness doctrine embedded throughout culture. See `dominion_civilian_life.md` §14 and § Media below.

### Career architecture (all Directorates)

| Stage | Typical path |
|-------|----------------|
| Feed | Preparatory civic track → technical college / apprenticeship; or university → Directorate entry |
| Early career | Single-world or single-region posting; heavy procedure literacy |
| Mid career | Cross-world mobility for Administrative and Transportation tracks; local depth for Industrial floor and Agricultural |
| Senior | Liaison desks, Regional Director roles, institute leads, Political Liaison exposure |
| Prestige markers | Science grants; SSD/Fleet sealed clearances; multi-generational family service (social, not legal privilege) |

Directorate identity often outranks local origin for administrative and technical-professional classes. Service-class identity remains more district-rooted. Both are Dominion.

### Inter-Directorate liaison pattern (Book One texture)

Operational rooms routinely seat **liaison officers** from peer Directorates (e.g. Industrial liaison at Regional Transportation morning planning). Liaisons do not command the host Directorate. They certify constraints (*Industrial Directorate certified. We execute.*) and carry grievances home. This is how competition stays procedural rather than theatrical.

### Resource competition (normal politics)

| Dispute type | Typical parties | Outcome style |
|--------------|-----------------|---------------|
| Sealed research channels | Science vs. SSD / Political Liaison | Classification wins; Science files complaint |
| Berth plate vs. passenger windows | Industrial vs. Transportation vs. Education | Split routes, delayed grievances |
| Applied vs. basic research funding | Science internal + Fleet/SSD pressure | Applied bias under preparation doctrine |
| Frontier under-resourcing | Border Admin / local Civil vs. core worlds | Persistent grievance; loyalty intact |
| Shift length / safety | Industrial floor vs. Industrial Directorate | District channels; not cross-Directorate revolt |
| Festival freight vs. military windows | Agricultural / Transportation / Fleet priority | Public overlays; private priority penalties |

**The Dominion is unified. It is not unanimous.**

## 3. Political structure

### Power-sharing model

From `dominion_worldview.md` — power is shared among:

1. Civil Government  
2. Strategic Security Directorate  
3. Dominion Fleet  
4. Science Directorate  
5. Industrial Directorate  
6. Intelligence Directorate  

Transportation, Education, Agricultural, and Communications Directorates are full institutional peers in civilizational function; Strategic Security and Fleet carry disproportionate weight on **frontier and classified** matters. Industrial and Science carry disproportionate weight on **capacity and programs**. Civil Government carries the **everyday legitimacy** of memorials, schools, and festivals.

### What “politics” looks like inside the Dominion

Politics is **resource allocation under shared loyalty**. It looks like:

- Packet fights over sealed channels  
- Liaison desks arguing berth plate vs. school excursions  
- Science institutes citing prior directors against current Fleet demand curves  
- Frontier districts filing under-resource grievances that core worlds treat as perennial noise  
- Sacrifice-rhetoric skeptics saying *we are always the ones sacrificing* — while still attending funerals  

It does **not** look like multi-party elections, free press crusades, or military coups as default plot engines. Careers end; Directorates do not.

### Decision pathways (simplified)

```
Routine civil matter
  → District Administrator → Civil Government channel → (Directorate if specialized)

Corridor / freight / festival logistics
  → Regional Transportation Directorate → Transit Control → (Industrial / Education / Agricultural inputs)

Classified strategic matter
  → Intelligence assessment → SSD packet → Fleet operational tasking (as authorized)
  → Science sealed channel (technical) parallel, not political liaison

Resource conflict
  → Directorate liaisons → Political Liaison oversight packet → recorded compromise
```

### Oversight without paralysis

Political Liaison and Civil Government can slow, query, and document. They do not micromanage corridor geometry or ship command. Field and Directorate professionals execute within doctrine; failure is investigated afterward.

Colonel-grade Political Liaison readers may appear in SSD morning packets (Book One pattern) — translating Transportation summaries into political language (*normalization reads like holiday congestion models*) without owning Transit Control.

### Strategic posture politics (author canon — show, do not announce)

Institutions disagree about **tempo and tools**, not about whether the Dominion should continue:

| Belief cluster (in-character, ambiguous) | Typical institutional flavor |
|------------------------------------------|------------------------------|
| Strategic depth / buffer | SSD, Fleet frontier, Education civic studies |
| Fear of Fleet expansion | Intelligence assessments; border watch |
| Economic / industrial necessity | Industrial, Agricultural, Transportation |
| Scientific durable advantage | Science Directorate |
| Historical trauma / campaign memory | Academies, memorial culture, veterans |

**Do not confirm a single strategic motivation in Book One.** Multiple beliefs coexist. True strategic objective remains incompletely revealed.

### What politics is not

| Not | Instead |
|-----|---------|
| Personality cult | Institutional continuity |
| Party democracy | Career institutions + district legitimacy |
| Military junta | Military authority inside a multi-pillar system |
| Free-speech absolutism | Information as preparation labor division |
| Diplomatic peer treaties (current canon) | Geographic collision + asymmetric memory |

Citizens may criticize shift length, freight priority, and sacrifice rhetoric from below — within the framework. They still attend public funerals and tell the proverbs. See `dominion_civilian_life.md` §16.

## 4. Education

*Canonical daily and ceremonial detail: `dominion_civilian_life.md` §4, §17. Summary and institutional expansion below.*

### Three-phase progression

| Phase | Ages | Function |
|-------|------|----------|
| **Primary School** | 5–13 | Universal; sacrifice history; math; language/rhetoric; physical training; introductory logistics; civic studies (*strategic depth* by age ten as civic concept) |
| **Preparatory School** | 13–18 | Preparatory track → academies/universities; civic track → Directorate apprenticeships / technical colleges; mandatory history + logistics |
| **Academy / University** | 18–24 (variable) | Military, Science, Administrative, Technical colleges — heavy historical case study |

### Pedagogical character

- History taught as **preserved things**, not taken things (“the Vereth Corridor families gave three generations before the border held”).
- Military history (prep onward) is analytic: *“What did they not have enough of, and when did they know?”*
- Assessment privileges historical case study — what others chose and why consequences followed.
- Regional variance tolerated when core narrative (sacrifice, preparation, continuity) is not contested.
- Frontier schools may overweight campaign history; agricultural schools may integrate harvest logistics into mathematics — without contesting core narrative.

### Education Directorate vs. Civil Government

| Body | Owns |
|------|------|
| **Education Directorate** | Curriculum standards, academy liaison, grievances (e.g. excursion cuts), pageant season guidance |
| **Civil Government** | Facilities, local calendars, district school staffing logistics, Passage Day / Attestation certification support |

Teachers remain the most trusted ordinary profession even when Directorate and Civil Government paperwork conflict over a passenger hold.

### Civic ceremonies (education)

| Ceremony | When | Character |
|----------|------|-----------|
| **Review Assembly** | Twice yearly, primary | Student recites personal historical account (letter, inscription, testimony) |
| **Passage Day** | End of primary | Academic record read; track selected publicly; tea and simple food |
| **Civic Attestation** | End of preparatory (~18) | Pledge of civic contribution by profession — not a military oath |

Military academy graduates take a **separate military oath** alongside Civic Attestation.

### Teachers

Most socially trusted ordinary profession. Transmit proverbs before Academy and grandparents deepen cost. Family-known at market and café. Stay late for harvest pageant rehearsal. Full texture: `dominion_civilian_life.md` §3–4.

## 5. Universities

### Institutional types

| Institution type | Parent / affiliation | Emphasis |
|------------------|----------------------|----------|
| **Military academies** | Dominion Fleet (+ SSD liaison) | Logistics, history, strategic theory, tactics; frontier duty expectation |
| **Science academies / institutes** | Science Directorate | Applied research, documentation, cross-discipline collaboration |
| **Administrative colleges** | Civil Government / Education Directorate | Law, civil management, Directorate procedure |
| **Technical colleges** | Industrial / Transportation / Agricultural | Apprenticeship-credentialed specialists |
| **University faculties (member worlds)** | Education Directorate charter | Broader scholarship under same historical-case culture |

### Shared teaching characteristic

All higher institutions evaluate students on **historical consequence**, not only proposed action. Academy manuals are often multi-generation annotated objects — new editions supplement rather than erase (Book One academy texture).

### Prestige and mobility

| Path | Social signal |
|------|---------------|
| Science Directorate institute lead | High — rivals admirals in respect |
| Military academy + frontier service | High — family service-record pride |
| Administrative college → district / Directorate | Solid institutional standing |
| Technical college → yard / freight / agri | Competence prestige; local identity |

Scientists are respected almost as much as admirals (`dominion_worldview.md`).

### University life texture

- Tea culture; chess in waiting rooms and commons  
- Inherited quotation of instructors (see `dominion_speech.md`)  
- Research communities geographically dense on scientific worlds — professional and domestic life inseparable (`civilian_life` §12)  
- Harvest festival observed but smaller share of calendar than on agri/industrial worlds

## 6. Military

### Doctrine (authoritative)

From `dominion_worldview.md`:

- Wars are not won by courage; they are won **years before the first shot**
- **Preparation** highest virtue; **surprise** second
- Victory = preserving the Dominion; glory irrelevant
- Losses acceptable; **failure** not
- Logistics decide campaigns; technology changes battles; discipline preserves civilizations
- Initiative expected within doctrine
- Failure investigated; repeated failure ends careers

**Strategic motto (in spirit):** *A border is only secure until someone believes it is empty.*

### Institutional place

```
Dominion Fleet
├── Capital / theater commands
├── Frontier / Border Surveillance nodes (K-17 pattern)
├── Training command / academies
├── Logistics & depot interface (Procurement, Transportation, Industrial)
└── Classified program tasking (e.g. Leviathan Program — SSD + Science sealed channels)
```

### Academy and officer formation

Military academies weight **logistics and history** as heavily as tactics. Cadets inherit annotated manuals across generations. Instructors teach corridor questions in halls the way Transportation planners teach overlays. Suspension from a trial command does not erase teaching reputation if the name on the roster still carries expectation (Book One academy texture).

Frontier duty is normative. Officers who never serve the border are statistically unusual and socially legible as incomplete formation — not always career-ending, but noticed.

### Professional ritual

| Ritual | Rule |
|--------|------|
| **Gloves** | Removed before serious discussion |
| **Tea** | Traditional drink in government and military meetings |
| **Chess** | Professional pastime — commons, veterans’ halls, waiting rooms |
| **Promotions** | **Private** |
| **Funerals** | **Public** |
| **Frontier duty** | Most officers serve at least once |
| **Old ships** | Revered, not casually discarded |
| **Family service records** | Pride without formal caste privilege |
| **Border plaques** | Stations display plaques honoring previous generations (K-17 tradition) |

### Force posture (civilizational, not order of battle)

This reference does not lock a full class hierarchy mirror of Fleet’s published combat tree. Dominion force design emphasizes:

| Priority | Expression |
|----------|------------|
| Depth | Depots, fuel, munitions refresh, corridor maintenance |
| Surprise | Classified programs (cloak / Leviathan pattern) |
| Continuity of hulls | Old ships kept; yards expand around inherited anchors |
| Watch | Passive border surveillance before illumination |
| Logistics literacy | Officers who cannot think in tonnage and windows are incomplete |

Fleet-facing readers should feel **preparation and geometry**, not parade-ground spectacle.

### Leviathan Program (Book One)

One component of long-term preparation — stealth, strategic surprise, technological superiority. The Kestrel Veil Incident was a **classified field test** of a prototype cloaking field aboard a Leviathan-class command hull (*Iron Harrow*), not an unprovoked attack. Commander Vex followed doctrine; cloak partially failed against passive scout detection; surviving scout = uncontrolled intelligence risk (Dominion perspective).

**Trial terminology lock:** *Iron Harrow trial* / *cloak field trial* = test of the hull; *trial hull* = *Iron Harrow*; *Iron Harrow trial encounter* = unexpected scout contact during the window. Never *trial scout* / *trial contact* as if Kestrel were the subject. See `Iron_Harrow.md` · `dominion_worldview.md`.

### Naming tone (warships)

Dominion warship names tend toward **material and consequence** (*Iron Harrow* — depth, pressure, furrow; iron — yard inheritance), not hero epithets. Use as texture when naming later hulls.

### Civilian relationship

Supportive without martial romanticism. Necessity, cost, honor — in that order. Veterans re-enter cafés and workforce without formal ceremony; service is known, not constantly performed. Full treatment: `dominion_civilian_life.md` §10.

### Rank and consequence culture

Authority is calm. Consequence is bureaucratic and remembered. Officers may stop appearing in official photographs. Usefulness is survival metric. No requirement for raised voices. SSD dismissals can read as: *you are still useful, therefore you are still alive* — not cruelty as sport, but civilization as accountability.

## 7. Transportation

### Mandate

Civilization understood as **corridor geometry and scheduling**. Freight, passengers, military priority, maintenance closures, and seasonal leave surges are one problem set. Routing mathematics are old — older than current frontier revision codes, older than many planners’ careers — built by people who believed civilization was a scheduling problem solved in advance.

### Structure

```
Transportation Directorate (central standards)
│
├── Regional Transportation Directorates (member-world clusters)
│   ├── Planning Office (congestion models, festival overlays)
│   ├── Transit Control (live execution)
│   └── Liaison desks (Industrial, Education, Agricultural, Fleet priority)
│
├── Interstellar freight corridors
├── Passenger transit networks
└── Maintenance Division interface (tugs, corridor works)
```

### Planning inputs and outputs (institutional model)

| Inputs | Outputs |
|--------|---------|
| Mass flows | Predicted congestion |
| Tug capacity | Predicted delay |
| Passenger windows | Predicted fuel burn |
| Military priority penalties | Reroute recommendations |
| Maintenance closures | Leave restriction advisories |
| Seasonal leave surges / harvest overlays | Festival pressure maps |

Half the items on a morning planning slate may never appear in a citizen’s news feed. Fuel moves because depots need depth. Plate stock feeds slips. Reserve transport requests look like paperwork until counted as bodies and tons. Science Directorate sealed crates travel the same lanes as school excursions — not treated as equal, only as both needing routes.

### Harvest festival logistics

Freight windows — scheduled periods when civilian agricultural cargo has priority — are announced weeks ahead. Transportation staff leave is restricted during surge. Corridor maps show festival pressure as congestion models. Ordinary civilians see public schedule adjustments and understand, at some level, that personal routes share infrastructure with harvest and military freight. Detail: `dominion_civilian_life.md` §9.

**Naming in packets:** harvest festival overlays; district festival passenger holds (school pageants); bare *festival* only in personal voice when context is clear.

### Professional culture

- Highly mobile between worlds; district apartment as home base  
- Practical, essential, not glamorous  
- Chess and tea in planning rooms  
- Corridor / depth / geometry vocabulary (Dominion Thread; do not overuse)  
- Strong esprit de corps — they know their work underpins everything else  
- Execute priority even when personally disagreeing (*Military gets priority because the Directorate gets priority because the border gets priority*)  

### Urban transit habit

Dominion cities developed transit infrastructure early, before private transport was widely available; the habit is deep. Commutes, notice boards, and shift changes are civilizational texture — see `civilian_life` §3.

### Contrast with Fleet

Fleet thinks in patrol, mission, survey, frontier. Dominion Transportation thinks in **maintained lanes, depth, and overlays**. Same geometry may appear on Fleet charts as Neutral Zone buffer language while Dominion treats it as inherited strategic depth (`fleet_dominion_frontier_history.md`). Fleet traffic analysis of border holiday congestion should say *local holiday*, not *harvest festival*.

## 8. Industry

### Character

Concentrated on dedicated industrial worlds and orbital platforms. Factory work is the largest single civilian employment category. Industrial workers in shipyard districts work alongside military construction timelines without formally being military employees — legal distinction dissolves in practice.

### Industrial Directorate role

| Function | Expression |
|----------|------------|
| Production schedules | Quotas; berth expansion; shift rotations |
| Safety regulation | Monthly reviews; annual component inspection days (ritual weight) |
| Military procurement interface | Plate stock, cranes, slips; Procurement Office forecasts |
| Capacity doctrine | Run above current-demand capacity — slack = vulnerability |

### Yard culture (civilizational marker)

- Machinery warm from previous crew  
- Personal tools marked with initials / service notches  
- Anchor blocks reused across generations  
- New composite over limestone; weld scars under paint  
- Proverbs from yard families (*keel / weather*)  
- Family photographs on safety boards  
- Long shifts authorized as notation, not celebration  
- Outer rings / expansion collars growing around inner inherited slips  

Book One texture: Kessik-Outer / Helikon / outer-belt yards; Sorokin-pattern labor pressure; eighteen-hour rotations as Industrial Directorate notation only.

### Industrial world social texture

On worlds like Korrast, factory culture dominates. Shift identity is strong; district identity is working-class in character. Science-and-academy prestige hierarchy is present but distant. Harvest festival evening gatherings run later and louder; district orchestras more prominent. Detail: `civilian_life` §12.

### Preparedness without announcement

Shipyard expansion, fuel depots, reserve recalls, and munitions refresh appear as **economic development** and **standard rotation language**. Individually ordinary; together, pattern. Never state “preparing for war” in institutional voice — show ordinary institutional scenes (`dominion_worldview.md`).

When a factory worker learns a new wing will produce heavy-cruiser components, the response is quiet recognition that the state is doing what it always does — preparing — not martial excitement.

## 17. Economy

### Sector map

| Sector | Directorate / authority | Notes |
|--------|-------------------------|-------|
| Industrial production | Industrial Directorate | Largest employment; yards; platforms |
| Agriculture | Agricultural Directorate | Food security; festival logistics driver |
| Transportation / logistics | Transportation Directorate | Underpins all other sectors |

### Preparedness embedded in “good management”

| Practice | Stated as | Functions as |
|----------|-----------|--------------|
| Above-demand factory capacity | Efficiency / resilience | Mobilization slack |
| Above-use freight lane capacity | Reliability | Surge capacity |
| Applied research funding bias | Useful science | Preparation support |
| Depot expansion ahead of forecast | Infrastructure | Depth |

### Labor and complaint

Shift culture (eight shifts across eleven days, three rest — local day-length adjusted). Shift pairs develop café tables, arguments, and commemorative habits at transitions. Complaints via district channels: safety, shift length, freight priority, institutional procurement crowding small vendors. Disloyalty is not the default endpoint of complaint. See `civilian_life` §3, §11, §16.

### Markets

Market vendors open before transit begins; know customers across generations; keep district notice boards near displays. During harvest season, stalls extend (goldcrust, cider, crafts). Markets are economic and civic — not separate from memorial culture nearby.

### Currency of status

Profession, family/personal service record, education level, geography (core vs. frontier). Not a formal caste. Military Family Class overlaps all economic levels. Core proximity carries status; frontier postings are respected as preparedness evidence and sometimes resented for implying core comfort is a luxury.

## 18. Border administration

### Strategic meaning

Borders are **maintained strategic depth**, not empty buffer. Fleet charts may label overlapping volume Neutral Zone; Dominion maps treat the same geometry as inherited space requiring watch (`fleet_dominion_frontier_history.md`).

**No border treaties** in current series canon — collision is expansion meeting maintained depth, not diplomatic parity.

Dominion institutional memory retains breakaway founding pressure, campaigns, and Fleet expansion as **ongoing strategic concern**. Fleet public memory scrubbed shared history while keeping cartographic vocabulary. Neither side is lying in full; each inherited a different archive.

### Structure

```
Border Administration (composite)
├── Dominion Fleet — frontier garrisons & Border Surveillance nodes
├── Strategic Security Directorate — advisories, classification, strategic framing
├── Transportation Directorate — corridor maintenance, military priority windows
├── Customs Office — manifests, restricted-zone clearance
├── Civil Government — station districts, schools, memorial boards, harvest observance
└── Intelligence Directorate — anomaly assessment (clearance-walled)
```

There is no single “Border Directorate” that replaces these. Border administration is a **composite function** — which is why sleepy stations still sit at the intersection of Fleet, SSD, Transportation, and Civil Government paperwork.

### Node culture (K-17 pattern)

| Feature | Function |
|---------|----------|
| Plaques / memorial boards | Previous generations of assignment — civilian and military |
| Sleepy assignment reputation | Routine, procedurally heavy — existence is the point |
| Civilian–military social mix | Shared cafés, schools, festival; narrowest civic-military divide |
| Passive watch preference | Doctrine-aligned observation before illumination |
| Harvest festival | Observed; freight overlays still apply |
| Persistence logging | Low-confidence returns filed and not retracted without cause |

Civilians at the frontier live preparedness as geography. Children encounter *strategic depth* as why they live where they live. Veterans who return to core worlds and later take civilian jobs at border stations often describe the return as “feeling right.” Full texture: `dominion_civilian_life.md` §13.

### Asymmetric knowledge (Book One lock)

| Topic | Dominion institutional | Fleet institutional |
|-------|------------------------|---------------------|
| Name *Dominion* | Yes | No (until discovery beat) |
| Peer civilization beyond border | Known as civilization | Buffer / empty doctrine |
| Neutral Zone term | Strategic depth framing | Buffer framing |
| Cloak / Leviathan | Yes (cleared) | No |

### External civilizational awareness (institutional)

Dominion institutions watch Fleet Authority as expansion pressure — traffic habits, Founders’ Week / holiday congestion patterns, corridor behavior — better than Fleet understands the Dominion.

**Peligar** — Dominion exonym for a known external civilizational/industrial comparison class (yard/mass-curve reference in professional speech). **Series seed only** in this reference: do not expand into encyclopedia here. Self-name, interior politics, and full motivations remain withheld pending series outlines. Book One on-page use: professional exclusionary comparison (*not Dominion yard make; not Peligar either*).

## 19. Science Directorate

### Mandate and prestige

Durable knowledge in service of civilization continuity. Applied results weighted under preparation doctrine; pure research persists and argues for itself across generations. A Science Directorate grant is among the most sought-after civilian recognitions. Scientists are respected almost as much as admirals.

### Internal organization (reference model)

```
Science Directorate
├── Central Administration (funding, classification liaison, academy standards)
├── Research Institutes (member worlds / stations)
│   ├── Applied programs (including sealed military-technical channels)
│   ├── Basic research faculties
│   └── Documentation / archives (multi-generation citation culture)
├── Science academies (feeder)
└── Medical research institutes (liaison Public Health)
```

### Culture

| Trait | Expression |
|-------|------------|
| Documentation | Meticulous; cite findings from three generations ago |
| Tea | Large quantities; pots reheated without ceremony |
| Argument | Resource allocation, methodology, basic vs. applied — ancient disputes |
| Pride | Institute directors referenced like old commanders |
| Skepticism | Often skeptical of ceremonial sacrifice rhetoric while personally committed to work as service |
| Tension with military | Durable security via research vs. tactical preparation — fought in budgets |
| Hours | Later starts, irregular; documentation never irregular |

### Scientific worlds

Small, dense communities; high prestige; high internal debate. Harvest festival observed but smaller calendar share — research schedules do not pause easily. Contradiction between institutional skepticism and personal service ethic is lived, not resolved (`civilian_life` §12).

### Book One interface

Cloak bench metrics, sealed equipment manifests in freight, “promising” language in packets that SSD treats as inadequate against outcomes. Science measures performance; Strategic Security measures outcomes. Sealed channels bypass Political Liaison when SSD orders it; Science complains; classification holds.

Field trial authorization waits on bench satisfaction. Science leads may disappear from visible programs when failure investigation requires it — career gravity, not melodrama.

### Visual / speech alignment

Institute corridors: layered architecture, inherited instruments, chess in break rooms, inherited quotation of prior directors and instructors (`dominion_visual_language.md` · `dominion_speech.md`).

## 24. Culture

### Core values

Duty · Service · Family · Discipline · Education · Competence · History · Sacrifice

Citizens believe the military preserves peace through **overwhelming preparedness**. Hardship today prevents catastrophe tomorrow. This is inherited cultural framework, continuously reinforced — not one-generation propaganda success.

### Cultural memory

Dominion characters think **historically** — ancestors, campaigns, old institutions, famous commanders, events everyone already knows. Fleet characters think **prospectively**. Neither is superior. Show the difference; never explain it.

Military memory organizes around campaigns, commands, strategic outcomes. Civilian memory organizes around names, families, what was preserved. Both reinforce sacrifice, preparation, continuity through different frameworks (`civilian_life` §15).

| Dominion | Fleet Authority |
|----------|-----------------|
| Depth, buffer, endurance | Exploration, opportunity, forward motion |
| Sacrifice as preservation | Pioneering as progress |
| Prepare so those who follow may live | Go first so those who follow may know |
| Tea; harvest festival; gold thread | Coffee; Founders’ Week; copper and blue |
| Continuity speech (inherited quotation) | Innovation speech (mission, peer, now) |

### Recurring vocabulary (Dominion Thread)

corridor · depth · geometry · continuity · preservation · strategic horizon · containment · preparation  

**Do not overuse.** One or two earned words per scene.

### Age markers (constant evidence)

- Centuries-old navigation corridors  
- Historic shipyards and ancient academies  
- Memorials to forgotten wars  
- Border installations older than Fleet Authority  
- Generational military families  
- Layered architecture; archaic script on oldest memorial stones  
- SSD complexes occupied for centuries  

**Feel:** Dominion = established. Fleet = comparatively young and energetic.

### Social structure (spectrum, not sharp civilian/military divide)

Administrative · Technical-Professional · Service · Military Family (overlap) · Frontier classes — full definitions in `dominion_civilian_life.md` §2.

The divide exists on paper and dissolves in practice. A factory worker producing crane components understands, at some level, that cranes exist because yards exist because the fleet must be maintained — whether or not they name that chain on shift.

### Regional variation

Unifying: harvest festival, primary curriculum core, tea, chess, canonical proverbs, public funerals, private promotions.  
Varying: industrial shift intensity, agricultural rootedness, scientific debate culture, frontier strategic awareness.  
Detail: `civilian_life` §12–13.

### Children’s culture (civilizational outcome)

By end of primary school, a Dominion child has typically memorized proverbs, recited at Review Assembly, visited memorials, performed in a harvest pageant, heard family service history, and been told the Dominion continues because people before them made sure it would. Play favors strategy and coordination. Chess arrives informally from grandparents before formal prep instruction. Full: `civilian_life` §17.

### True antagonist reminder

Readers should understand Dominion decisions without being required to approve them. Empathize with both civilizations simultaneously. History — not malice — drives collision with Fleet Authority.

**Civilization scene test:** What aspect of Dominion culture (family, tradition, logistics, duty, food, history — never technology alone) will the reader understand better after this scene?
