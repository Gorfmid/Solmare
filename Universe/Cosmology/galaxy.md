# Galaxy Overview

## Type

Spiral galaxy with fragmented outer arms and several uncharted void regions.

## Structure

Galactic geography as Fleet cartography models it — **node-based control**, not continuous territory.

- **Core Worlds** — densely governed Fleet Authority heartland (Central Assembly, Senate, major yards)
- **Mid-Rim Expansion Zones** — active colonization, contested claims, patrol corridors
- **Outer Rim** — low infrastructure, high autonomy, scout routes
- **Deep Void** — unstable navigation, rare contact, probabilistic jumps

The **Dominion** occupies separate strategic geography beyond Fleet survey assumptions. Fleet maps describe UFA-held and UFA-claimed volumes — not Dominion internal zoning. Do not conflate galactic core geography with the Dominion civilization.

## FTL Travel Model

- Jump-drive traversal using fixed calibration nodes
- Accuracy degrades in Outer Rim
- Deep Void jumps are probabilistic, not deterministic

## Strategic Reality

Control is not continuous; it is node-based. Empires do not hold space — they hold corridors between systems.
