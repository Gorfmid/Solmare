# Prelude — The Founding Exodus

**Act:** Prelude (reader-knowledge seed)  
**Theme pressure:** Trust is Harder Than War — trust will later require shared history; this chapter shows the split before either civilization can name it  
**Chronology:** Centuries before Book Two Act I (T−∞). Cut to present day without character recognition.  
**Canon locks:** Voluntary departure; harvest-festival–season texture (era-appropriate); Founders' Week seed without modern Fleet branding; no Peligar; no modern character descendants named on-page

---

## Working Title

The Founding Exodus

---

## Purpose

Give the **reader** the departure truth centuries before most characters can accept it: a group of colonists **voluntarily broke off from the Dominion** and founded what will become **Fleet Authority**. This happened **during harvest festival** — which is why, centuries later, **Founders' Week falls at the same time of year** as Dominion harvest festival. Neither civilization remembers the event accurately. Seed Book Two's Ellis arc. End so Act I can open in the present without lecture.

---

## POV Characters

- **Primary:** Composite / rotating close third among departure-era colonists and Dominion officials (unnamed or lightly named period figures — not Book Two principals)
- **Secondary texture:** A Dominion continuity official (or local district authority) who experiences departure as wound, relief, or both
- **Forbidden:** Full modern Fleet/Dominion institutional POV; any Peligar presence; naming Calder, Thessaly, Vex, Ellis, etc. as descendants

## Plot Objectives / Plot movement

1. Establish a harvest-season festival atmosphere in Dominion continuity culture — food, inheritance ritual, seasonal rhythm — without using modern Founders' Week exhibit language.
2. Show a convoy of colonists assembling and **voluntarily** leaving Dominion territory seeking a different future (freedom to expand, doctrinal disagreement, hope — rational, not cartoon rebellion). This breakaway **founds Fleet Authority** (as seed — not modern branding).
3. **Calendar lock (reader-only):** the departure is timed to harvest festival season. That single fact is why later Fleet Founders' Week and Dominion harvest festival occupy the **same seasonal window** — Fleet invents an origin holiday on the anniversary of leaving; Dominion keeps celebrating continuity on the season of loss. Characters will not connect these until Ellis.
4. Imply cartographic / political vocabulary that will survive as Neutral Zone / breakaway seeds without resolving modern border treaties.
5. Deliver a last shared meal / ritual beat that can mutate into two commemorations.
6. End on the beginning of **divergent memory** — the same departure already being filed differently by those who leave and those who stay.
7. Hard cut to Book Two present (~18 months after Book One) without any character recognizing the Prelude truth.

- Voluntary colonial departure as lived choice, not legal brief
- Seeds of asymmetric memory (who records what; what gets edited by hope vs duty)

- Dual holiday root without naming modern Fleet Founders' Week as already invented

## Scenes

### Scene 1 — Festival Before Departure

**Goal:** Establish harvest-season festival texture and the emotional normality of Dominion continuity culture on the eve of change.  
**Conflict:** Joy and ritual collide with private packing, quiet arguments, and officials who cannot decide whether departure is wound or relief.  
**Outcome:** Reader feels a living civilization about to lose people — not a villain regime expelling rebels. Festival language stays era-true (continuity, inheritance, seasonal duty).

### Scene 2 — Reasons That Feel Rational

**Goal:** Make the decision to leave legible as human disagreement about the future — expansion freedom, doctrinal friction, hope — without cartoon tyranny.  
**Conflict:** Families and neighbors split; some stay for order and depth; some leave for prospective possibility. Dominion official voice frames departure in institutional language that will mutate in archives.  
**Outcome:** Reader understands both staying and leaving as coherent. No modern politics lecture in future tense.

### Scene 3 — Convoy Assembly

**Goal:** Show physical scale of departure — hulls, manifests (impressionistic, not Ellis-proof catalogs), last cargo, children, tools, seeds.  
**Conflict:** Logistics and emotion compete; last permissions; last shared rituals; cartographic vocabulary of "beyond" that will fossilize into frontier language.  
**Outcome:** Convoy is real and voluntary. Seeds of Neutral Zone / breakaway geography are felt, not explained.

### Scene 4 — Last Shared Meal / Ritual Beat

**Goal:** Plant the commemorative root that will become two holidays **on the same seasonal clock**.  
**Conflict:** Those leaving and those remaining perform the same harvest-season ritual with different futures already in their mouths — continuity language vs pioneering hope — without either side inventing modern holiday names.  
**Outcome:** Reader understands: later Founders' Week and harvest festival commemorate **the same departure at the same time of year**. Characters in Act I will not.

### Scene 5 — Memory Begins to Diverge

**Goal:** End on asymmetric recording: what the leavers will teach as beginning; what the remainers will file as loss of depth / campaign aftermath seed.  
**Conflict:** Already, accounts disagree on motive, blessing, and blame — gently, humanly, bureaucratically.  
**Outcome:** Reader holds the truth. Cut to present-day Act I. Characters do not know.

---

## Ending Hook

The convoy crosses into darkness / open chart. Behind them, Dominion continuity continues. Ahead, a civilization that will invent Founders' Week as if no one was left behind. Reader alone carries the bridge into Act I's uneasy neighbors — who still believe they are strangers.

## Notes for Cursor

### Procedural realism
- Show departure as logistics + ritual + paperwork texture, not mythic pageant.
- Officials use institutional language; colonists use personal language; neither is omniscient about "history."

### Institutions
- Dominion continuity / district authority present as real governance, not cartoon empire.
- Do not invent modern Fleet Council, Helion, or Founders' Week museum copy.
- Archives begin lying by omission and emphasis immediately — seed for Ellis, not lecture.

### Theme
- Trust is harder than war because shared origin will not automatically produce trust when rediscovered.
- Prelude earns dual empathy: leavers and remainers are both right enough to hurt each other later.

### Hard locks
- Spelling **Peligar** irrelevant here — **do not show Peligar**.
- No full Peligar POV anywhere in Book Two; Prelude especially must stay human-era only.
- Reader knows; characters in subsequent chapters do not.
- No prose fiction drafting from this file — outline / planning only.
- **Calendar / holiday lock:** Departure during harvest festival → Founders' Week and harvest festival share seasonal timing. Show; never lecture the coincidence in later chapters until Ellis earns it.
- Holiday naming in Prelude: era festival texture only; modern Founders' Week branding forbidden on-page.
