Formalize Fleet–Dominion contact under political radioactivity. Make communication a first-class plot engine: headers imperfect, meetings costly, mistranslation an operational hazard. ## Scenes

### Scene 1 — Venue and Headers

**Goal:** Open the contact channel as institutional product — who may speak, who records, who can walk out.  
**Conflict:** Fleet Council/Command fear legitimizing a rival; Dominion SSD fear contamination and overshare.  
**Outcome:** A brittle protocol exists. Courtesy is armor. No friendship declared.

### Scene 2 — Park Meets the Linguists

**Goal:** Human–human language work begins; establish Dominion linguist personalities and taboos.  
**Conflict:** False friends in vocabulary; military terms that map wrong; beverage/ritual friction as civilization texture (coffee/tea) without glossary.  
**Outcome:** Small usable shared glossary for *human* speech. Trust remains thin. Park earns professional respect, not affection.

### Scene 3 — The Distress Misfile Corrected

**Goal:** Revisit Ch.1 scream recordings with Dominion input and Fleet sensor science.  
**Conflict:** Fleet medical/distress filing vs Dominion insistence this is known (or known-of) Peligar channel behavior — without full explanation. Park's recognition: **they're talking**.  
**Outcome:** Classification updated to language-without-meaning. Horror upgrades: not victims screaming — a civilization speaking on channels humans barely hear.

### Scene 4 — Mistranslation as Hazard

**Goal:** Show a near-miss caused by protocol/software or human mistranslation on a joint alert or status exchange.  
**Conflict:** Blame markets open immediately (sabotage vs incompetence vs doctrine clash). Thessaly/Calder contain the incident procedurally.  
**Outcome:** Language work proven mission-critical. Mutual suspicion reinforced rationally.

### Scene 5 — What May Be Shared

**Goal:** Close on institutional negotiation of the next disclosure layer — maps, warnings, Peligar name usage.  
**Conflict:** Dominion will not explain everything; Fleet will not accept unexplained fear as orders.  
**Outcome:** Enough shared words exist to schedule the map conversation (Ch.3). Productive contact ≠ trust.

## Ending Hook

A corrected classification header sits on Fleet systems: not distress — speech. Meaning: none. On the Dominion side, linguists pour tea and do not celebrate. Tomorrow someone will have to put lines on a map and ask for help without sounding like a petitioner — or a trap.

- Park's breakthrough is epistemological ("language") not semantic ("dictionary").

- Human–human progress must not be mistaken for Peligar progress.

- Spelling **Peligar**.
- No full Peligar POV.
- Suspicion remains rational throughout.
- Outline only — no prose fiction.
