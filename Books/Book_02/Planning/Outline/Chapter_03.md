## Scenes

### Scene 1 — The Map That Moves

**Goal:** Show border expansion through institutional artifacts — charts, missing check-ins, Dominion watch products sanitized for Fleet eyes.  
**Conflict:** Fleet demands proof standards First Doctrine recognizes; Dominion refuses to hand full depth maps.  
**Outcome:** Reader and Calder see a pattern that will not sit still. Categories still incomplete.

### Scene 2 — Continuity, Not Glory

**Goal:** Seed industrial-ecology / continuity framing via Dominion operational language (processing, harvest groups as texture only if careful) without encyclopedia.  
**Conflict:** Fleet officers reach for war vocabulary (invasion, aggression); Dominion speakers resist without winning a moral-superiority monologue.  
**Outcome:** Conceptual gap established for Ellis later. Act I still prefers debate over proof.

### Scene 3 — The Help Request

**Goal:** Dominion formally asks for help — scoped: warning share, observational cooperation, Fleet buffer posture — not vassalage.  
**Conflict:** Who has authority to ask / accept? SSD sabotage-by-delay vs Civil Government necessity; Rowan vs Council fear.  
**Outcome:** The ask is on the record. Pride paid. Trust not purchased.

### Scene 4 — Calder and Vex as Counterparts

**Goal:** Private or semi-private professional exchange: each predicts the other's move; neither softens.  
**Conflict:** Calder's certainty addiction vs Vex's preparation doctrine; mutual recognition without liking.  
**Outcome:** Operational mirror seated for Acts III–V. Respect ≠ friendship. Theme embodied.

### Scene 5 — Provisional Instruments

**Goal:** Thessaly/Rowan convert the meeting into next actionable instruments (data lanes, liaison schedules, no-alliance clarifications).  
**Conflict:** Every clause is a veto opportunity; language of "cooperation" triggers panic on both sides.  
**Outcome:** Act I can proceed to "productive exchanges" in Ch.4 — while the map already contains a colony that will not answer.

### Scene 6 (Optional) — Quiet Label on the Chart

**Goal:** Brief Fleet-side beat: Founders' Reach as ordinary frontier colony on a status board (patrol due, school petition trivia) — lived civilization, low strategic value.  
**Conflict:** None overt; dread is dramatic irony for the reader who holds Prelude + expanding border.  
**Outcome:** Setup Ch.4 consumption without telegraphing disaster-porn. Do not wink at the name.

## Ending Hook

The help request is filed. Provisional channels open. Calder and Vex can predict each other and still cannot trust each other's civilizations. On a Fleet chart, Founders' Reach is a quiet label — agricultural trials, shipping-container school, eight-week patrol rhythm — already inside a resource graph nobody on the Fleet side has agreed to believe in.

## Notes for Cursor

### Procedural realism
- Maps, headers, liaison schedules, veto clauses — not summit speeches.
- Authority questions mandatory: who can agree, who can delay legally.
- Logistics of sharing (sanitization, lag) matter as much as the ask.

### Institutions
- Fleet Council / Rowan / Doctrine: contact legitimizes rival + third threat.
- Dominion SSD / Civil Government / Military: share vs depth philosophy.
- Keep Dominion from "we told you so" as moral victory.

### Theme
- Asking for help from a feared peer is a trust problem, not a tonnage problem.
- Professional respect (Calder–Vex) is the only trust available — and it is not enough.
