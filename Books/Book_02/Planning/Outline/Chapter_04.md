## Plot Objectives / Plot movement

1. Show communication becoming productive: joint status, corrected headers, usable shared vocabulary for *human* coordination, provisional warning products moving.
2. Intercut or parallel the ordinary life / administrative rhythm of **Founders' Reach** (Segment 6 frontier colony; low strategic value; self-sufficient; school petition; patrol cycle) — lived civilization, not a MacGuffin planet.
3. Depict consumption/processing as **industrial ecology**: resource-graph behavior — atmospheres, orbitals, infrastructure dismantled over tempo; people as obstacles, not objectives. Avoid locust/sadist framing.
4. Fleet loses contact; sensors show processing signature vs "attack" language — debate begins even as proof lands.
5. Dominion does not gloat; Vex/liaison register necessity and dread, not victory.
6. Act I ending condition: Fleet can no longer treat Dominion warnings as theater. **Trust has not begun — necessity has.**

## Scenes

### Scene 1 — Productive Exchange

**Goal:** Show Fleet–Dominion contact working: a real shared product (alert format, lane discipline note, corrected signal classification, or joint status board) that professionals can use.  
**Conflict:** Even success is radioactive — every useful share looks like overshare to SSD / FIB.  
**Outcome:** Communication is no longer theater. Calder/Thessaly/Park have something that functions. Theme irony armed.

### Scene 2 — Founders' Reach, Ordinary

**Goal:** Establish the colony as lived place — agricultural trials, school container, relay officer boredom, pride of self-sufficiency — through traffic and procedure, not travelogue.  
**Conflict:** Undersupply and administrative neglect as Fleet frontier texture (petition in Senate queue) — ordinary injustice, not foreshadowing wink.  
**Outcome:** Reader cares that people exist there. Strategic value "low" is an institutional category about to become morally obscene.

### Scene 3 — The Pattern Reaches Fleet Soil

**Goal:** Loss of contact / anomalous returns / processing signatures appear on Fleet sensors and Dominion warning feeds nearly together.  
**Conflict:** First Doctrine and war vocabulary fight the data; is it attack, accident, Dominion false flag, or the thing on the map?  
**Outcome:** Denial becomes expensive. Productive channels light up with the wrong kind of confirmation.

### Scene 4 — Processing, Not a Battle

**Goal:** Depict consumption with industrial ecology framing — dismantling, gradients, infrastructure as feedstock; multi-vocal signal may appear as work-speech, still untranslatable.  
**Conflict:** Human witnesses (remote) reach for invasion language; sensors refuse a clean battle narrative. People register as obstacles in the system's behavior.  
**Outcome:** Proof without understanding. Horror is categorical, not gore-forward. No Peligar interiority.

### Scene 5 — Institutional Proof

**Goal:** Rowan / Council / Dominion liaison absorb the fact: a Fleet colony is gone or going; Dominion warnings were genuine.  
**Conflict:** Blame, panic, classification, and the urge to reject cooperation *because* it would admit dependence — all fire at once.  
**Outcome:** Act I ending condition met. Necessity replaces debate. Trust still absent.

### Scene 6 (Optional) — Silence After Shared Words

**Goal:** Final beat: the productive exchange room goes quiet; tea/coffee unfinished; Calder and Vex (same channel or parallel) have nothing useful to say.  
**Conflict:** The first successful shared words cannot save the dead; cannot yet bind the living.  
**Outcome:** Cut to Act II. Diplomacy-alone time is over.

---

## Ending Hook

Founders' Reach stops answering. The first exchanges between Fleet and Dominion finally mean something — and what they mean is that warnings were never theater. Nobody trusts anybody more. Everybody needs everybody more. Act II will survey the glass and argue about names for what was done. Act I ends on proof.
