# Chapter 07

## Working Title

No Survivors

## Purpose

Deliver the **casualty reckoning** for Founders' Reach (and fringe losses in view). Force Fleet institutions to mourn at civilizational scale while fighting over what the event *means*. **Ellis begins resisting the "invasion" frame**, seeding extraction / obstacle language without completing the series reveal. Mega-figures remain author-gated.

## POV

Primary: **Soraya Ellis** (Historical Office / narrative hygiene under massacre tempo).  
Secondary: **Calder** or **Thessaly** (operational grief; refusal of easy war myths).  
Institutional beats: **Rowan**/Council receiving casualty boards; optional Dominion funeral/plaque texture for dual empathy (not "we told you so").

## Estimated Word Count

6,500–8,000

## Plot Objectives

1. Make "no survivors" institutional fact for Founders' Reach — empty manifests, sealed rescue windows, memorial logistics.
2. Stage the language war: invasion / genocide / natural disaster / industrial accident / "Peligar attack" — none fully fit.
3. Ellis begins formal resistance to invasion framing: extraction/processing logic enters archival/analysis notes (seed, not dump).
4. Keep demography honest: prefer **named colony / system scale** grounded in frontier catalog; flag mega-figures as pending.
5. Advance political pressure toward cooperation without yet authorizing it (handoff to Ch. 08).

## Scene 1 — The Empty Manifest

### Goal
Institutional confirmation: survivor searches close; Founders' Reach population accounting ends in silence.

### Conflict
Hope protocols vs forensic finality. Families/crew demand stories that sensors cannot give.

### Outcome
"No survivors" becomes procedural status, not a slogan. Grief shifts from search to reckoning.

## Scene 2 — Naming the Dead / Misnaming the Act

### Goal
Casualty boards, memorial drafts, Council talking points attempt to label the event.

### Conflict
War language mobilizes; accurate language paralyzes. FIB headers and Historical Office captions collide.

### Outcome
Public-facing "attack/invasion" draft advances; Ellis's private notes refuse it.

## Scene 3 — Ellis's Frame Resistance

### Goal
Ellis compares recon products (Ch. 05) to Dominion pattern language and Fleet war rhetoric; articulates the seed distinction: extraction vs invasion; people as obstacles not objectives.

### Conflict
Institutional pressure to bury category-breaking analysis (morale, Founders' Week hygiene, Council unity). Personal cost of being "the historian who won't say invaded."

### Outcome
Seed lands for reader and for Calder/Rowan channels — **not** full series thesis dump. Park-adjacent signal notes may support "language exists elsewhere" without Peligar translation.

## Scene 4 — Dual Mourning (Fleet / Dominion)

### Goal
Show grief on both sides of the frontier: Fleet memorial procedure; Dominion tea/plaque/funeral discipline for their own earlier losses or for the shared fringe.

### Conflict
Fleet characters misread Dominion composure as coldness; Dominion misreads Fleet speech as performance.

### Outcome
Dual empathy without reconciliation. Theme: shared mourning is a trust ingredient — and still insufficient for alliance.

## Scene 5 (Optional) — The Number That Must Not Lock

### Goal
Staff debate whether to publish a mega-casualty estimate for mobilization politics.

### Conflict
Inflated numbers create panic markets; suppressed numbers look like cover-up.

### Outcome
Chapter preserves **`[Author decision pending]`** — characters may argue ranges; manuscript must not treat a mega-figure as canon until author locks A4.

## Ending Hook

Rowan's office receives the political bill for grief: either Fleet authorizes a new posture toward Dominion military cooperation, or institutions will invent a simpler war against a simpler enemy. Necessity is about to write terms — Ch. 08.
