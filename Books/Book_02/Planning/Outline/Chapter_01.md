## Scenes

### Scene 1 — *Iron Harrow* Under Fire

**Goal:** Put reader inside Dominion command under Peligar engagement — damage, sectors, incomplete returns.  
**Conflict:** Vex must fight and decide with sensor states that will not stabilize into a familiar enemy profile; crew expects doctrine answers she cannot give.  
**Outcome:** *Iron Harrow* survives or disengages at cost; Peligar remain opaque; Vex retains professional control without sentimentalizing the enemy.

### Scene 2 — Incomplete Categories

**Goal:** Show Dominion institutional knowledge asymmetry — they know *of* Peligar as comparison class / threat continuity — without dumping encyclopedia.  
**Conflict:** SSD / military / Science language collide in short operational exchanges; share-with-Fleet question already toxic.  
**Outcome:** Reader feels Dominion knows more than Fleet and still does not know enough. Help request is not yet formal (Ch.3).

### Scene 3 — Fleet Intercept: The Scream

**Goal:** *Kestrel*/Fleet sensors capture multi-vocal screaming; Park/Calder/Thessaly process it through human distress protocols.  
**Conflict:** First Doctrine / observation categories fight medical-distress filing; contradictory readings; lag to higher authority.  
**Outcome:** Misread locked as operational fact: scream = distress (wrong). Wonder and horror preserved. No translation.

### Scene 4 — No Bin for the Attacker

**Goal:** Fleet attempt to classify the attacker fails (mass curves, emissions, behavior vs known classes).  
**Conflict:** Council/Command headers demand labels; field officers refuse false certainty; Rowan-adjacent pressure if present.  
**Outcome:** Institutional bafflement established. Calder's mediator future is foreshadowed by refusal to invent a clean enemy story.

### Scene 5 — After-Action Silence

**Goal:** Parallel after-action: Dominion tea/brief competence; Fleet coffee/debrief unease; both sides staring at the same gap.  
**Conflict:** Mutual suspicion — is this a Dominion trick, a Fleet misread, a third thing?  
**Outcome:** Ending posture for Ch.2: contact must happen, and nobody wants it.

## Ending Hook

The scream is still on the recording — filed under distress. Somewhere beyond Fleet charts, a border that moves continues to move. Vex knows the name **Peligar** and will not yet spend it as a gift. Calder knows only that observation has met something that does not care whether Fleet has a category.
