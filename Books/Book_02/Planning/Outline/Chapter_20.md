# Chapter 20

## Working Title
Revelations

## Purpose
Advance Ellis’s **shared-origin evidence** to politically live proof-pressure without completing full series history or automatic reconciliation. Helion’s Founders’ Week narrative cracks; Dominion campaign/archive memory partially confirms what the Prelude already showed the reader. Extraction thesis and origin thesis collide: the same civilizations arguing about Peligar categories discover they are arguing about themselves. Revelation destabilizes — it does not heal.

## POV Characters
- **Soraya Ellis** (primary) — archival breakthrough as lived discovery
- **Calder Venn** — mediator forced to carry historical shock into wartime rooms
- Dominion archive / SSD / historian counterpart (recognition uneven — not uniform civilian knowledge)
- **Rowan** (institutional response) — classify, delay, weaponize, or release?
- Light **Thessaly** — people impact of myth fracture

## Estimated Word Count
7,000–7,800

## Plot Objectives
1. Deliver Ellis’s breakthrough via **objects, edition comparisons, exhibit politics, cartographic fossils** — not lecture.
2. Establish for characters: Fleet and Dominion share a **departure root** (partial confirmation of Prelude truth; archives still partly wrong/incomplete).
3. Keep Founders’ Week ↔ harvest festival **recognition pressure rising** — full dual-holiday lock can land in Ch. 21, but Ch. 20 must make origin evidence undeniable enough to fight over.
4. Show institutions weaponizing *and* burying the discovery.
5. Deepen extraction frame’s moral vertigo: if we are split inheritance, what does “enemy” mean — and why does that not save anyone from Peligar processing?
6. Withhold complete true history / cosmology; withhold Peligar negotiability.

## Character Development
- **Ellis:** Historian becomes critical path; love of Fleet beginnings vs evidence; duty to record vs bury pressure peaks.
- **Calder:** Certainty addiction meets historical uncertainty; mediation now includes myth management.
- **Rowan:** Disclosure vs panic; refuses both naive transparency and total erasure.
- **Dominion counterpart:** Partial institutional memory surfaces — recognition without slogan; depth philosophy threatened by kinship implications.
- **Thessaly:** Tracks which crews/councilors will treat kinship as bridge vs betrayal.

## Worldbuilding Introduced
- Archival object(s): convoy log fragment / dual commemorative liturgy / cartographic fossil / exhibit edition mismatch — choose concrete props at draft; outline allows set.
- Helion Historical Office politics: Founders’ Week as civic religion under wartime.
- Dominion campaign memory / harvest-festival continuity language as uneven knowledge (not everyone knows; not everyone agrees).
- Classification headers applied to *history* as information warfare.
- Coffee/tea scenes as human pauses inside archival dread.

## Scene 1 — The Object
### Goal
Ellis correlates Helion exhibit language with restricted materials and Dominion-shared fragment; the departure ceases to be metaphor.
### Conflict
Handlers interrupt; access windows close; a colleague tries to reframe as coincidence or forgery.
### Outcome
Ellis achieves private certainty. She cannot yet force public certainty. Reader feels Prelude echo without characters naming the Prelude.

## Scene 2 — Partial Confirmation
### Goal
Dominion institutional response: some recognition (campaign archive / SSD historical cell), some denial, some strategic calculation about what Fleet knowing costs.
### Conflict
Share enough to stabilize joint talks vs retain asymmetry; kinship language threatens preparation identity and Long Watch suspicion.
### Outcome
Partial confirmation enters joint classified channels. Not a press release. Not reconciliation.

## Scene 3 — Weaponize / Bury
### Goal
Helion factions fight over the discovery: use kinship to force Accord; bury to protect Founders’ Week morale; leak selectively to destroy rivals.
### Conflict
Rowan must choose a containment strategy that is ethically ugly and politically necessary; Calder argues for controlled truth; Ellis refuses silent archive.
### Outcome
Provisional classification with scheduled review — unstable. Seeds Ch. 21 holiday recognition crisis. Extraction thesis cited by all sides for incompatible ends.

## Scene 4 — Human Fallout
### Goal
Thessaly / crew-level beat: rumor reaches people who need myths to mourn; kinship does not feel like comfort under funeral logistics.
### Conflict
Some see Dominion as cousins; some see traitors twice over; joint crews fracture socially without operational mutiny.
### Outcome
Micro-trust among professionals holds unevenly. Civilizational trust worsens. Calder understands Ch. 21’s holiday season will be a detonator.

## Ending Hook
Ellis stands before a Founders’ Week display that still tells the wrong story to the public. She has the correction in a classified folder. Outside Helion, the frontier keeps moving. History will not wait for the Council’s review date.

## Notes for Cursor
- Theme: Trust is Harder Than War — shared blood/history does not create trust.
- Reader already knows departure from Prelude; characters **earn** partial rediscovery.
- Do not dump complete breakaway legal brief or series cosmology.
- Do not make revelation automatic friendship comedy.
- Holiday names: pressure toward dual recognition; Ch. 21 owns the Founders’ Week ↔ harvest festival lock beat.
- Peligar remains external pressure; origin reveal must not steal focus into bilateral therapy.
