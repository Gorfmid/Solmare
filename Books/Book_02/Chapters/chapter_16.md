***HELION — FLEET COUNCIL OPEN GALLERY / SEALED ANNEX***
*GRIEF MARKETS / WARTIME MEASURES*

Fleet Admiral Elias Rowan entered the open gallery the way he entered every room that wanted certainty more than it wanted survivors: without ceremony, without apology, and without the comfort of believing the hour belonged to him.

Coffee sat on the sideboard as work presence. Nobody used it as a clock. People argued.

Provisional nodes matching intervals. Extraction frame live under higher seal. Joint instruments under sunset and veto. Protest notes traveling like jewelry nobody could remove.

Rowan had seen that posture before, on other clerks, in other rooms, every time institutions asked civilians to carry grief as logistics and then blamed them for wanting a simpler story. "Contested language is what the sensors allow," Rowan said. "False certainty is how institutions lose the next week after they calm this hour. New Meridian is authorized for memorial logistics under civilian affairs. Names will come through proper custody when forensics and census windows close. I will not give this gallery a false precision number to make the banners feel useful."

His leave was cancelled into readiness the week before Founders' Reach went quiet. "Which is why I will not spend your husband's absence as proof for either abandonment theater or strike theater. Civilian Affairs will keep household recovery moving. Field command will keep warning corridors moving. Neither will restore what is finished. Both remain necessary."

He looked like a man who had been asked to mediate public grief with incomplete maps and had already priced that the maps would not finish in time. Thessaly watched faces instead of slogans. Rowan looked at the open gallery and the sealed annex as if they were two watches on the same hull that refused the same clock. Outside, a Founders' Week committee clerk argued whether children's pageant props could keep the old first-departure mark if commemorative editions stayed contested. Inside, three factions hardened around cooperate, isolate, and preemptive strike, each rational under a history that had taught different lessons about neighbors and endings. "Limited wartime measures," he said. "Elevated mobilization authorities under sunset. Leave cancellation into readiness and mourning under Segment hardship codes. No preemptive strike language in public briefs. Extraction frame remains sealed as contested analysis. Public product: catastrophic loss, contested fringe pressure, warning-network priority elevated. The vote passed the way wartime votes often passed: ugly, incomplete, and already being spent by people who had not read the sealed strip.

A junior council clerk posted the gallery summary while Founders' Week cloth still argued copper in the corridor. Limited wartime measures live. Joint framework damaged and necessary. Calder's mediation role became a public target before the hour finished. Thessaly's people billets became the place where policy turned into cancelled leave and freight windows that would not wait for better nouns.

In the sealed annex, Denara updated the memorial logistics strip without looking at the open gallery. Names still arrived slower than petitions. Household recovery still needed clerks who could carry paper without inventing cosmology. Rowan watched her work and felt the people-before-systems question press against funeral logistics the way lag pressed against field command: unavoidable, unglamorous, and impossible to answer cleanly for every household at once.

"Which systems get warning priority," a logistics captain asked from the wall, not as theater, as form. "Segment boards cannot hold every fringe label at once. Observation support is a ration. Rations imply choosing."

"Calder and Thessaly hold the ration under incomplete product," Rowan said. "Helion does not invent a prettier schedule from this gallery. If we withhold intelligence to protect depth philosophy or panic management, we say so in sealed rooms and we live with the cost in public ones. Do not pretend purity is free."

Rowan signed the order packet with coffee present as work and no comfort attached.

"Get me the logistics twin from Civil Government," he said to Neve. "If Helion is going to spend funerals as argument, I want the other side's freight truth on the same table before anyone baptizes purity."

Neve nodded once. "Already queued. They are exhausted and they are still arguing. Same as us."

Outside, Helion kept moving. Memorial kits moved under civilian affairs custody. Freight schedules still posted. Founders' Week committees still fought cloth. A dockhand on a New Meridian lag side screen argued a cancelled leave code with a supervisor who kept pointing at hardship language as if language could restore a berth. Inside, necessity held and legitimacy bled.

Rowan did not ask the board for comfort.

He asked it for the next honest header that would still cost people.

***CIVIL GOVERNMENT DEPTH — LOGISTICS BEAT / SSD TWIN***

*FLEET PARTNERSHIP IS CONTAMINATION WITH BETTER MANNERS. SHARPER SUNSETS AUTHORIZED. ANY DEPTH FISHING FROM HELION UNDER FUNERAL PRESSURE IS A CLOSING CONDITION.*

Limited share continues. Complete model withheld. Hatred would be easier because hatred can end. This does not. Keep the corridors honest for those who come after us, even when honesty looks like refusal."

Volkova put her gloves back on before she closed the packet. Habit. Courtesy to the task. The room read it the way logistics rooms always had: serious work had not finished.

Outside, shipyard lights kept their tempo. Inside, Dominion continued limited share with sharper sunsets and veto instincts that would matter later, when capitals tried to spend necessity as kinship and logistics still had to move the living.

No moral victory. No *we told you so* sustained.

Only freight truth, tea present as work, and a civilization still arguing whether preparation could survive neighbors who invented weather.
