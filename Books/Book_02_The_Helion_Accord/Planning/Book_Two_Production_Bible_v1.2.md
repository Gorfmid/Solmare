## 4. Prelude

### The Founding Exodus

**Purpose**  
Give the reader the departure truth centuries before most characters can accept it. Seed Founders’ Week / Harvest Season as one event remembered two ways. Create quiet historical irony.

**Beginning state**  
Dominion district life during harvest season. Leave/stay is a local, rational human choice — not a modern Fleet/Dominion politics scene.

**Ending state**  
Convoy departs into darkness. Memory already beginning to diverge. Hard cut to present. Characters of Book Two do not know what the reader now knows.

**Major turning point**  
The last shared ritual / grain-bundle farewell — departure becomes irreversible.

**Emotional goal**  
Tenderness, loss, and irony without lecture. The reader should feel the leave as human, not as a lore dump.

**Character progression**  
Period figures only (e.g., Yeva line). No Book Two principals.

**Questions raised**  
What will the leavers become? What will those who stay remember? Will anyone keep the true reason?

**Questions answered**  
A population left Dominion space during harvest season voluntarily (as they understand it). Records will diverge.

**Themes introduced**  
Asymmetric memory; commemorative drift; preparation vs departure; history creates civilizations.

**Themes resolved**  
None — Prelude opens the mystery; it does not close it.

## 5. Act I — Uneasy Neighbors

**Chapters:** 1–4

**Purpose**  
Build communication under fire. Seat Calder–Vex as professional counterparts. Deliver Dominion help request. End with proof on Fleet soil (Founders’ Reach consumed). Trust has not begun — necessity has.

**Beginning state**  
~18 months after Book One. Fragile awareness of a peer civilization. No shared wartime instruments. Peligar unknown/uncategorized to Fleet. Dominion already has the exonym and earlier awareness.

**Ending state**  
Contact channels exist and can produce useful shared work. Founders’ Reach is silent/processed. Fleet can no longer treat Dominion warnings as theater. Institutional shock opens Act II.

**Major turning point**  
Founders’ Reach consumption while First Exchanges are becoming productive (Ch.4).

**Emotional goal**  
Bafflement → brittle competence → moral vertigo. Necessity without comfort.

**Character progression**

| Character | Movement in Act I |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Calder | Observer under uncategorizable signal → seated as mediator |
| Thessaly | Makes contact work executable; guilt only seeded lightly if at all |
| Vex | Competence under fire; counterpart recognition |
| Park | Distress misfile → “they’re talking”; Holt ladder should begin |
| Ellis | Light presence only |
| Rowan | Institutional ceiling appears |

**Questions raised**  
What is attacking/processing? Why does the scream sound like distress? Can Fleet and Dominion speak productively? Why is the border moving?

**Questions answered**  
Fleet and Dominion can build brittle protocols. Dominion needs help against an expanding threat. A Fleet colony can be erased by that threat. “Peligar” enters as Dominion designation.

**Themes introduced**  
Trust vs necessity; observation under tempo; language without meaning; indifference vs hatred (felt, not named).

**Themes resolved**  
None fully. “Warnings were theater” is killed.

## 6. Act II — The Burning Fringe

**Chapters:** 5–8

**Purpose**  
Destroy the illusion that diplomacy has unlimited time. Institutional fallout of Act I proof. Move Fleet into uneasy military-cooperation posture — not alliance.

**Beginning state**  
Founders’ Reach finished. Capitals scrambling. Observation-alone still culturally dominant in Fleet.

**Ending state**  
Limited military-cooperation instruments authorized on paper (joint patrol eligibility, warning prototypes, ROE collision rules, dual vetoes, explicit non-alliance). First joint patrol ordered.

**Major turning point**  
Terms of Necessity (Ch.8): cooperation posture accepted without affection.

**Emotional goal**  
Shock, mourning, ugly pragmatism. Vindication without gloating on Dominion side.

**Character progression**

| Character | Movement in Act II |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Calder | Unwanted political mediator role hardens |
| Thessaly | Becomes schedule-of-joint-reality; guilt seeds if offered/refused texture appears |
| Ellis | Resists “invasion” frame; extraction language begins |
| Rowan | Ugly diplomacy under existential clock |
| Park/Holt | Warning/protocol work should deepen personally |
| Vex | Utility without liking |

**Questions raised**  
How many more systems? What do we call the dead? Can observation doctrine survive wartime? What will limited cooperation cost?

**Questions answered**  
The border is still moving. Casualties are real and political. Limited cooperation is necessary. Alliance is refused.

**Themes introduced**  
Extraction vs invasion; public vs classified panic; necessity arithmetic.

**Themes resolved**  
“We have unlimited time to debate” is resolved as false.

## 7. Act III — The First Accord

**Chapters:** 9–13

**Purpose**  
Reluctant cooperation becomes lived joint work. Cultures collide. Operational trust among professionals rises ahead of civilizational trust.

**Beginning state**  
Instruments on paper. Crews expect cheating. No shared combat rhythm.

**Ending state**  
First joint patrols and actions survived. Calder–Vex can predict each other. Ellis’s founding question is ticking. Vex’s “no hatred” observation earned. Map of Peligar pressure is widening into Act IV scale.

**Major turning point**  
Operational Trust (Ch.10): mutual professional prediction under shared risk.

**Emotional goal**  
Fragile working trust; fatigue; respect without friendship; archival dread beginning for Ellis.

**Character progression**

| Character | Movement in Act III |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Calder | Certainty addiction punished on live routes; mediator in real time |
| Thessaly | Patches joint failure mid-mission; command without title |
| Vex | Peer-not-friend lock; teaches costly pattern language |
| Park/Holt | Protocol scars; human–human clarity vs alien opacity |
| Ellis | Shared-origin **seed** (questions, not courtroom proof) |

**Questions raised**  
Can joint ROE work? Is field trust contamination? Do Fleet and Dominion share older history? What does Peligar “no hatred” mean?

**Questions answered**  
Doctrine collision can nearly kill a patrol without betrayal. Professionals can trust procedures across the line. Peligar are not motivated by hatred (Dominion observation). Origin question is live but unproven.

**Themes introduced**  
Operational trust vs civilizational trust; commemorative rhyme; indifference as horror.

**Themes resolved**  
“Joint work is impossible” is disproved — “joint work is easy” is not.

## 8. Act IV — The Expanding Shadow

**Chapters:** 14–18

**Purpose**  
Reveal multi-system scale. Advance industrial-ecology thesis. Exact civilian/political cost. Break peacetime procedures. Deliver **Calder’s major hesitation failure**. Authorize costly Counterstroke and set cloak-share / deep-recon hinge.

**Beginning state**  
Joint instruments exist but are insufficient. Capitals still fight categories. Calder still believes more observation will save him from wrong action.

**Ending state**  
Scale undeniable. Calder’s delay has contributed to catastrophic loss; aftermath begins. Limited Counterstroke has proven costly and incomplete. Path to Helion and to deep recon is locked. Public/political trust damaged; operational trust strained but not dead.

**Major turning point**  
Calder’s major hesitation failure (intended center of gravity: **Ch.16**, with reinforcement through Ch.17–18) — waiting for confirmation closes an evacuation/intervention window.

**Emotional goal**  
Expanding dread; grief markets; the first true personal invoice for Calder; Thessaly’s people judgment outpacing him.

**Character progression**

| Character | Movement in Act IV |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Calder | Mediation across theaters → **failure** → doctrine doubt begins |
| Thessaly | Guilt arc should surface (avoidance, refusal); locks when he hesitates |
| Ellis | Extraction thesis hard enough to crack invasion grammar; archive under siege |
| Park/Holt | Language≠meaning under political contagion; personal stakes rise |
| Vex | Teaches what war requires; withholds applause; cloak as protected asset |

**Questions raised**  
Is this invasion or processing? Whose civilians are spent first? Can First Doctrine survive? What would a deeper look into Peligar space cost politically?

**Questions answered**  
Multiple systems are in a resource graph. Cities can be “in the way.” Peacetime observation procedures fail under consumption tempo. Limited kinetic action can delay but not defeat. Calder’s certainty hunger can cost lives.

**Themes introduced**  
Hunger/ecology; observation as ration; failure without villain cartoon.

**Themes resolved**  
“If we cooperate politely, the border will wait” is resolved as false.

## 9. Act V — The Cost of Trust

**Chapters:** 19–22

**Purpose**  
Capital politics and Accord pre-negotiation. **Deep cloaked reconnaissance** and processing revelation. Ellis origin/dual-holiday landing and post-recon Harvest reframing. Calder’s confidence collapse. Thessaly pull-back. Joint trust crisis under ash. Cooperation re-chosen — hope unauthorized.

**Beginning state**  
Helion becomes center of gravity. Calder carries unpaid failure. Cloak share is politically radioactive but possible. Historical exhibits are about to detonate identity.

**Ending state**  
Accord mandate live under ash. Calder continues after failure (not absolved). Thessaly’s command trajectory is clear. Shared-origin knowledge is politically live and destabilizing. Peligar processing nature is known to principals through recon. Formal alliance still refused.

**Major turning point**  
Deep recon revelation (intended **Ch.19–20 hinge**) — Calder sees processing/mobile-civilization scale and understands what his earlier delay cost.

**Emotional goal**  
Darkest discovery; confidence break; hard honesty between Calder and Thessaly; grief; austere re-choice of cooperation.

**Character progression**

| Character | Movement in Act V |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Calder | Recon → collapse across chapters → Thessaly refuses easy comfort → continues |
| Thessaly | Pulls Calder back; recognizes her own perfection demand; moves toward acceptance |
| Ellis | Origin + dual holiday; then Harvest = Peligar harvest / refugee reframing after recon |
| Park/Holt | Warning-net / Accord language labor; personal near-admission territory |
| Vex | Catastrophic loss texture; no-hatred if not fully landed earlier |
| Rowan | Corridor-keeper of non-alliance drafts |

**Questions raised**  
Does shared blood mean anything under processing? Can Calder still command? Will capitals abandon the framework? What is Harvest really?

**Questions answered**  
Fleet originated from Dominion departure. Founders’ Week and Harvest Season mark the same departure. Peligar process worlds; evacuation windows are short because processing is industrial. Calder’s delay mattered. Cooperation can be re-chosen after failure without becoming love.

**Themes introduced**  
Kinship without reconciliation; observation as incomplete responsibility; command after failure.

**Themes resolved**  
Calder’s certainty-as-virtue identity breaks. “We can wait for perfect proof” is personally finished for him (not for all institutions).

## 10. Act VI — The Helion Accord

**Chapters:** 23–25

**Purpose**  
Earn hope without false alliance. Lock limited instruments. Cross the frontier together under Accord. Close character arcs with restraint. Plant Book Three’s Alliance question.

**Beginning state**  
Mandate to negotiate exists. Ash and unfinished names remain. Trust is provisional and professional.

**Ending state**  
Helion Accord is real in the world. Fleet and Dominion can cross together under limited rules. Thessaly accepts command. Mysteries remain. Map still moves. Reader leaves hopeful and unfinished.

**Major turning point**  
The Accord signing (Ch.23) — instruments not vows; ceremony without parade.

**Emotional goal**  
Austere hope. Provisional belonging. Exhaustion with meaning.

**Character progression**

| Character | Movement in Act VI |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Calder | Mediator role imperfectly institutionalized; supports Thessaly |
| Thessaly | Accepts a real command position |
| Park/Holt | Communications foundations of Accord; personal weight on crossing/aftermath |
| Ellis | Dual-holiday / Harvest truth remains unsettled in institutions |
| Vex | Signs without surrendering strategic depth; respect as doctrine anecdote |
| Rowan | Keeps non-alliance language honest |

**Questions raised**  
If this is not alliance, what happens when necessity demands it become one? (Book Three)

**Questions answered**  
Limited cooperation instruments can exist. Crossing together is possible. Trust is still harder than war — and still unfinished.

**Themes introduced**  
Implementation vs declaration; unfinished partnership.

**Themes resolved**  
Book Two’s cooperation question is answered provisionally: yes, narrowly, under ash. Alliance question is explicitly deferred.
