# Chapter 05

## Working Title

Glass Horizon

## Purpose

Open Act II on **fallout**, not first discovery. Survey/recon the already-consumed Founders' Reach system. Force Fleet language to confront **extraction / processing signatures** versus instinctive **"attack / invasion"** framing. Destroy any remaining institutional hope that Act I's colony loss was anomalous, reversible, or misreported. Trust has not begun — necessity is still being priced.

## POV

Primary: **Calder** (recon command / mediation posture under grief).  
Secondary: **Thessaly** (mission procedure, crew discipline, people-judgment under shock).  
Brief institutional frame allowed: **Rowan** or FIB/Doctrine liaison receiving first sanitized recon products (not a full political chapter — that belongs to Ch. 06–08).

## Estimated Word Count

6,500–8,000

## Plot Objectives

1. Confirm Founders' Reach is not "under attack" in progress — it has been **processed**: infrastructure stripped, planetary/orbital matter rearranged along resource-graph logic.
2. Produce Fleet recon data that resists war-vocabulary headers (attack vector, aggressor intent, hostage logic) without yet completing Ellis's series-scale extraction thesis.
3. Establish operational contact with Dominion border/watch assets that already have vocabulary for this pattern — without Dominion gloating.
4. Keep Peligar **external**: sensor returns, silence where speech was expected, multi-channel noise if present — **no translation**, no interior POV.
5. End Act II's opening on institutional aftershock: the report will force mobilization debates (handoff to Ch. 06).

## Worldbuilding Introduced / Advanced

- **Consumed-system forensics:** missing volatiles/metals signatures; orbital debris that looks industrial rather than battle-scattered; atmospheres/oceans as feedstock language in Dominion briefs vs Fleet combat language.
- **Peligar as industrial ecology (behavior only):** processing tempo; people as obstacles implied by empty habitats, not sadism displayed.
- **Classification friction:** recon products that cannot be filed cleanly under existing FIB war-incident headers.
- **Spelling lock:** **Peligar** (Dominion exonym). Fleet may adopt the word here if not already locked in Act I — still not a self-name.
- Cultural texture: Fleet coffee in after-action silence; Dominion tea if joint brief occurs — courtesy without warmth.

## Scene 1 — Approach / Denial Window

### Goal
Enter the Founders' Reach approaches under wartime recon rules; establish what "survey" means when the colony already failed Act I's proof beat.

### Conflict
Crew and institutional expectation want a battlefield (wreckage of fight, survivors, enemy fleet). Sensors refuse that story: wrong debris geometry, wrong energy residues, wrong silence.

### Outcome
Mission reframes from "rescue / assess damage" to "document a finished process." First Doctrine tempo stress made concrete.

## Scene 2 — Surface / Orbital Forensics

### Goal
Walk the evidence: habitats emptied without classic massacre theater; infrastructure dismantled along usefulness; planetary/orbital matter moved as feedstock.

### Conflict
Language fight inside Fleet procedure — "attack," "raid," "genocide event," "natural catastrophe" all fail differently. Specialists argue over which header protects careers vs which protects truth.

### Outcome
Working recon product: **extraction / processing signature** as operational description. Invasion language survives in unofficial talk; official draft cracks.

## Scene 3 — Dominion Overlay Brief

### Goal
Compare Fleet recon to Dominion border-watch pattern library (sanitized). Establish that Dominion warnings were pattern-recognition, not theater.

### Conflict
Fleet pride / suspicion: accepting Dominion frame feels like political surrender. Dominion restraint: sharing enough to keep Fleet useful as buffer without dumping full strategic depth maps.

### Outcome
Partial shared vocabulary ("processing," "continuity frontier," "resource graph" as *Dominion* phrasing Fleet resists). No friendship. No Peligar translation breakthrough.

## Scene 4 — The Unfileable Report

### Goal
Calder/Thessaly finalize what can be transmitted up-chain without lying.

### Conflict
FIB/Doctrine/Command want actionable enemy intent. Evidence supports continuity industry, not hatred. Headers break.

### Outcome
Report ships with contradictions preserved (sensor doctrine rule). Institutional panic fuse lit for Ch. 06.

## Scene 5 (Optional) — Ordinary Life Fracture

### Goal
One short beat of human scale: leave cancelled, memorial logistics begin, Founders' Week commemorative kits already in cargo manifests for a colony that no longer exists.

### Conflict
Ceremony language vs forensic language.

### Outcome
Grief made institutional and personal without disaster-porn. Reader feels civilizational cost starting.

## Ending Hook

The recon report clears classification gates just as a second fringe alert arrives: the border is **still moving**. Glass horizon was not an endpoint — it was a finished node on an expanding graph. Cut toward Ch. 06 panic markets.
