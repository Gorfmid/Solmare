# Chapter 10

## Working Title

Operational Trust

## Purpose

Earn **Calder–Vex operational trust**: each correctly predicts the other's professional move under procedure and shared risk. Distinguish operational trust (scarce, professional, provisional) from civilizational trust (still absent). Advance Act III's grinding micro-trust without sentimentalizing Dominion–Fleet relations.

## POV

Primary split: **Calder** and **Vex** (paired operational chapters/beats).  
Secondary: **Thessaly** (designs the conditions where prediction can be tested); brief **Rowan**/SSD oversight as institutional ceiling.

## Estimated Word Count

6,500–8,000

## Plot Objectives

1. Design a joint evolution where success requires anticipating partner doctrine — not liking it.
2. Deliver the earned beat: Calder predicts Vex; Vex predicts Calder; both choose restraint that looks like trust but is professionalism.
3. Keep respect non-sentimental: gloves, tea/coffee, inherited quotation vs innovation speech — competence recognition only.
4. Advance joint recon doctrine drafts under wartime clock.
5. Leave civilizational suspicion intact (Council/SSD still hostile to deep sharing).

## Character Development

| Character | Movement |
|-----------|----------|
| **Calder** | Mediator identity hardens: acts before complete certainty because partner competence is now a known variable. |
| **Vex** | Hostility → competence recognition. Mirror of Calder. Never confesses friendship; shows trust as prediction and restraint. |
| **Thessaly** | People-reader: knows when to put the two in the same kill-zone of responsibility. |
| **Institutions** | Begin citing "field trust" while refusing political trust — dangerous split. |

- Joint ROE revision after Ch. 09 near-failure (editions, headers, watch bills).
- Dominion preparation proverbs meeting Fleet improvisation — shown through decisions, not lectures.
- Iron Harrow / Leviathan-lineage sensitivity: Vex shares only what war requires.
- Shared warning-net pilot improves slightly — still not Helion Accord.
- Peligar signal mapping continues without language (infrared/polarization/harmonic channels as science, not dictionary).

## Scene 1 — Conditions of Trust

### Goal
Thessaly/Rowan set a joint problem that cannot be solved by either navy alone (escort + watch + ambiguous Peligar-adjacent return).

### Conflict
Political minders want separation of forces; field reality demands interlaced responsibility.

### Outcome
Mission geometry forces Calder–Vex interdependence.

## Scene 2 — The Prediction Beat (Calder → Vex)

### Goal
Under time pressure, Calder correctly anticipates Vex's preparation-doctrine choice (archive-confirm, refuse pursuit, protect depth, etc.).

### Conflict
Fleet subordinates urge Calder to override / preempt Dominion "rigidity."

### Outcome
Calder aligns instead of dominating. Operational trust shown as *not forcing the partner to be Fleet*.

## Scene 3 — The Prediction Beat (Vex → Calder)

### Goal
Vex correctly anticipates Calder's observation/mediation move (hold fire, preserve contradictory sensors, refuse simple war story).

### Conflict
Dominion subordinates urge Vex to treat Fleet initiative as contamination/risk.

### Outcome
Vex allows the Fleet move within agreed lanes. Respect without warmth.

## Scene 4 — Shared Risk, Separate Myths

### Goal
After-action: both captains report upward in culturally true but mutually irritating language.

### Conflict
Institutions try to claim the success as proof of alliance readiness or as proof the other side nearly failed.

### Outcome
Field trust exceeds political trust — Act III ending condition advances. Hook toward intelligence sharing politics (Ch. 12) and Ellis pressure (Ch. 11).

## Scene 5 (Optional) — Quiet Professional Mirror

### Goal
Brief non-sentimental beat: tea/coffee, gloves off/on, silence that is not bonding montage.

### Conflict
Either man almost says too much; both refuse.

### Outcome
Mirror relationship locked as Book Two spine — still not friendship myth.

## Ending Hook

A sealed intelligence packet and an archival query arrive in the same duty cycle: shared intel politics (Ch. 12) and Ellis's historian question (Ch. 11) will test whether operational trust can survive truth and bureaucracy.
