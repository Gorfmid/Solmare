# Chapter 17

## Working Title
Broken Procedures

## Purpose
Prove that Book One’s observation-before-judgment inheritance **fails under wartime consumption tempo** when treated as delay rather than method. Joint procedures collide; headers disagree; local command supersedes outdated orders; First Doctrine wartime amendment becomes a fight, not a memo. The chapter’s violence is procedural failure — doctrine breaking in people’s hands — setting the costly Counterstroke of Ch. 18.

## POV Characters
- **Calder Venn** (primary) — doctrine doubt beginning; mediator trapped between observation ethics and abandonment math
- **Mira Thessaly** — converting political mess into executable procedure under contradictory authority
- **Commander Vex** — Dominion preparation doctrine vs Fleet innovation under shared failure
- Supporting: Doctrine Bureau / Academy voice; field captains under fragmented command

## Estimated Word Count
7,000–7,800

## Plot Objectives
1. Stage **doctrine failure under wartime tempo**: observation windows that equal abandoned populations; joint ROE that cannot agree on engagement thresholds for a non-hating processor civilization.
2. Show information warfare / sensor states (confirmed · probable · degraded · contradictory) breaking joint trust without requiring betrayal.
3. Force First Doctrine / wartime observation amendment into open institutional conflict (Doctrine Bureau / Academy / field command).
4. Escalate Calder’s certainty addiction into visible **doctrine doubt** (full crisis lands harder in Ch. 22).
5. End with a decision for limited joint military action (Ch. 18 Counterstroke) taken under broken procedures — necessity, not confidence.
6. Keep Peligar opaque: opacity causes failure; no negotiation plot; no self-name.

## Character Development
- **Calder:** Sees observation become excuse or paralysis in others’ hands — and fears his own. Mediator role requires acting before maps complete.
- **Thessaly:** Demonstrates command-scale people judgment under fragmented authority; schedules meaning when doctrine documents contradict.
- **Vex:** Professional respect for Fleet competence survives frustration; preparation quotations meet innovation speech in a room that has run out of clean procedures.
- **Institutions:** Shown as real — meetings, editions, headers — failing in character-consistent ways.

## Worldbuilding Introduced
- Wartime observation amendment drafts; Edition revision pressure (placeholders OK if timeline not locked).
- Joint ROE collision: Fleet prospective improvisation vs Dominion historical depth/preparation habits.
- Command fragmentation under lag: local survival superseding delayed capital orders (Fleet governance canon).
- Warning-network prototype stress: trust prosthesis that also creates false confirmation loops.
- Coffee/tea as tempo markers during endless procedure fights — not jokes.

## Scene 1 — Contradictory Truth States
### Goal
A joint theater receives incompatible sensor packages and delayed capital orders; captains must choose whose “confirmed” to obey.
### Conflict
Fleet field command vs outdated Helion guidance; Dominion depth-map partial share creates asymmetric awareness; Peligar processing advances during argument.
### Outcome
Local improvisation saves some capacity, loses some. Blame immediately politicized. Procedures exposed as peacetime artifacts.

## Scene 2 — Doctrine Room Fracture
### Goal
Doctrine Bureau / Academy / Rowan-line command fight over whether observation remains method or becomes abandonment.
### Conflict
Calder argues for amended observation that still refuses false certainty; hawks demand kill criteria that assume hatred/intent; Dominion refuses to encode Peligar as “enemy” in Fleet moral language while still requiring kinetic options.
### Outcome
No clean edition. Provisional wartime rules issued with sunsets and vetoes — unstable foundation for Ch. 18.

## Scene 3 — Thessaly Makes It Runnable
### Goal
Thessaly translates provisional mess into joint executable checklist: who speaks, who fires, who aborts, who reports.
### Conflict
Crews from both sides distrust the checklist; Calder hesitates on a call Thessaly would lock; Vex tests whether Fleet will honor abort criteria when scared.
### Outcome
Checklist holds in exercise / near-real pressure. Micro-trust among professionals spikes; civilizational trust does not. Calder notices Thessaly’s judgment outpacing his.

## Scene 4 — Authorization Under Failure
### Goal
Capitals authorize a **limited joint counterstroke** (Ch. 18) because procedures have already failed to protect lanes — not because anyone believes the plan is sound.
### Conflict
Political resistance from Ch. 16 resurfaces; Ellis’s extraction language is either stripped from orders or used as fig leaf; Rowan and Dominion counterparts accept ugly necessity.
### Outcome
Go order. Calder and Vex paired as professional mirrors for the action. Ending emotional register: bitter competence, not hope.

## Ending Hook
The provisional ROE document is still warm when the first Counterstroke clock starts. Calder understands the next chapter will not redeem doctrine — it will invoice it.
