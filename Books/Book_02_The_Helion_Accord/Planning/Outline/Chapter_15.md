# Chapter 15

## Working Title
The Shape of Hunger

## Purpose
Advance the **industrial-ecology / extraction thesis** hard enough that Fleet’s “invasion” grammar cracks — without dumping Book Four cosmology or completing the series reveal. Ellis names the frame characters can almost bear: systems as resource graphs; people as obstacles, not objectives. Political and doctrinal institutions resist the language even as the evidence mounts. This is Act IV’s intellectual hinge.

## POV Characters
- **Soraya Ellis** (primary) — archival / forensic synthesis; extraction frame earned
- **Calder Venn** — mediator forced to carry a category that breaks war language
- **Jun Park** (supporting) — signal science without translation; “language ≠ meaning” pressure
- Dominion science / SSD assessment beat (Vex or designated counterpart) — partial agreement without Fleet moral vocabulary

## Estimated Word Count
7,000–7,800

## Plot Objectives
1. Land Ellis’s extraction thesis as **lived discovery** (objects, editions, system forensics, dual readings of the same data) — not author lecture.
2. Distinguish **processing / extraction** from invasion cartoon language; show institutional preference for the wrong frame.
3. Advance multi-system pattern into a **resource-graph** reading (metals, volatiles, orbitals, energy gradients) without claiming full Peligar motive stack.
4. Keep Peligar speech opaque: pattern recognition allowed; translation still impossible.
5. Make the thesis politically radioactive — it threatens Founders’ Week morale language *and* simple wartime mobilization narratives.
6. Seed “something larger” only as pattern implication — no cosmology dump.

## Character Development
- **Ellis:** Moves from narrative hygiene to critical historian; horror is the distinction, not the body count alone. Seed the series-ready beat: *They didn’t invade. They began extraction.* Full series cosmology still withheld.
- **Calder:** Must decide whether observation doctrine can absorb ecology framing without becoming delay excuse — or war framing without becoming false certainty.
- **Park:** Deepens “they’re talking” lineage into channel mapping (IR / polarization / harmonic) without meaning exchange.
- **Dominion voice:** Closer to continuity logic than Fleet; still incomplete; resists becoming Fleet’s translator of alien morals.

## Worldbuilding Introduced
- System-consumption forensics: dismantling sequence (asteroids → moons → atmospheres/infrastructure → planetary matter) as **observable stages**, not encyclopedia.
- Resource-graph vocabulary entering joint briefings (contested; politically dangerous).
- Historical Office exhibit / edition politics colliding with wartime data needs.
- Peligar multi-channel speech science without dictionary.
- Dual-civilization beverage/texture under archival dread (Ellis’s coffee cooling; Dominion tea poured before ugly assessment).

## Scene 1 — Forensics Without Comfort
### Goal
Joint survey / intel package on a mid-process or newly silent system shows staged dismantling inconsistent with conquest objectives (no occupation logic; no demand; no hatred signature).
### Conflict
Fleet Command prefers “enemy offensive”; Doctrine Bureau fears ecology language will paralyze ROE; Dominion refuses to gift a complete model.
### Outcome
Data forces a temporary working label: **processing signature**. Calder protects the label long enough for Ellis to be brought in as synthesizer.

## Scene 2 — Ellis Names the Frame
### Goal
Ellis correlates forensics with Dominion-shared fragments and Fleet survey language; articulates extraction vs invasion.
### Conflict
Historical Office and Council handlers want war-usable myth; Ellis’s duty to record collides with institutional bury pressure; colleagues hear “excusing the enemy.”
### Outcome
Ellis states the horror aloud in a room that cannot unhear it: extraction; obstacles removed. Not full series cosmology — the behavioral frame only. Room splits.

## Scene 3 — Channels, Not Conversations
### Goal
Park + Dominion linguists / signal officers demonstrate multi-channel Peligar emission as structured language without recoverable meaning.
### Conflict
Politicians demand “talk to them”; scientists refuse false mastery; Vex (or counterpart) warns that hatred-ending logic does not apply.
### Outcome
Joint doctrine note: **translation impossible this book**; warning-network value rises because meaning-exchange will not save populations. Seeds Ch. 17–18 operational design.

## Scene 4 — Political Contagion
### Goal
The extraction frame leaks into Council / Dominion Civil Government debate as both tool and threat.
### Conflict
War-hawks claim ecology talk weakens will; isolationists claim cooperation with Dominion “imported” the wrong categories; Dominion factions fear Fleet will demand depth maps as payment for accepting the thesis.
### Outcome
Thesis becomes politically live. Capitals authorize limited deeper cooperation *and* begin resistance campaigns that Ch. 16 will make costly for civilians. Ending beat: another system’s resource graph updates in real time — hunger with a shape.

## Ending Hook
Ellis files a provisional note she knows will be classified: the Peligar are not coming for cities. Cities are in the way. Calder reads it and understands the next fight is not only on the frontier — it is over which language institutions are allowed to use while people die.
