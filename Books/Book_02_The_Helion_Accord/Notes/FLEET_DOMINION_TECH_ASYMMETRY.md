# Fleet / Dominion tech asymmetry (author lock)

**Status:** Author lock for Book Two+ — seed in scenes; do not dump as encyclopedia dialogue until characters have earned comparing notes.

## Split (working)

| Edge | Side | Why (history) |
|------|------|----------------|
| Communications listening / signal science | **Dominion** | ~1000 years of Peligar-facing contact; channel work is survival work |
| Warfighting systems (weapons, sustained combat doctrine vs Peligar pressure) | **Dominion** | Same long war; Fleet did not fight that war |
| Stealth (Fleet native methods — **not** counting borrowed/shared cloak) | **Fleet** | Scout / frontier culture; cloak is separate politics |
| Speed / drive performance for range and tempo | **Fleet** | Expansion and flight heritage |
| Colonization / expansion logistics | **Fleet** | Rebuild-as-expansion after departure; Dominion continuity culture did not prioritize the same |

## Historical reason (author)

Dominion stayed and fought / held depth against Peligar pressure.  
Fleet ancestors ran, rebuilt, and optimized for getting elsewhere fast and staying hard to pin — some war/comms depth likely lost or never developed the same way during flight and Founders rebuild.

## On-page discipline

- **Ch.2 seed (done lightly):** Orlen can state Dominion already used better *listening* gear on the Peligar channel than Fleet’s packet — more signal, still no meaning. Do **not** turn that into a full tech-scoreboard speech.
- Neither side needs to fully *realize* the whole split yet. Characters notice local edges (listening, guns, drives, yards) as scenes require.
- Never imply Dominion understands Peligar meaning because their instruments are better.
- Cloak remains separate: Dominion loan politics; not “Fleet is worse at stealth” if cloak is the comparison.
