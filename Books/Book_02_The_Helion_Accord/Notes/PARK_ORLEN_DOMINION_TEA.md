## Plant (Ch. 3)

Orlen makes Park try **duskleaf** from her thermos. He loves it. She names ~three ordinary examples; Park interrupts:

> "Stop." / "Why." / "Because I already want to try them all."

Humor belongs to Park's enthusiasm, not a tea catalog.

| Dominion name | Profile (Ch. 3) |
|---------------|-----------------|
| **Frostblossom** | floral, light |
| **Duskleaf** | Park's first — ordinary homeworld tea |
| **Kilnleaf** | roasted, savory |

Full variety count (hundreds) may be referenced later; do not dump lists on the page. Additional names (cellar resin, saltfen, embercut, etc.) remain available for later books when Park asks for the next tin.

She offers tins through Science channels. Start duskleaf, then kilnleaf. Park: **want to try them all** (Orlen files it; does not celebrate).

## Permanent rules (after Ch. 3)

1. Park no longer drinks Fleet coffee. He quietly abandons it.
2. He carries his own tea tin.
3. Fleet personnel automatically provide hot water.
4. Supply comes from **Orlen** or Dominion Science channels.
5. He gradually learns different Dominion teas across the series.
6. Never framed as politics — genuine enjoyment + cultural curiosity.
7. Humor understated (*Hot water is fine.* / *No coffee?* / *I've made better decisions.*)
8. Keep it light. One line or a thermos in the background is enough.
9. Friendship with Orlen is allowed; romance is not. Do not explain every time.
10. Separate from the Park/Calder **mug gag** — mug can hold tea; gag stays about the label.

## Orlen continuity (paired)

- Precise, dry, almost impossible to compliment.
- Teaches freely; dislikes unnecessary praise.
- Enjoys Park's curiosity more than she admits.
- Slowly becomes one of Park's closest professional colleagues.
