# Running gag — Park / Calder mugs

**Status:** Author lock for Book Two (*The Helion Accord*)  
**Tone:** Quiet. Dry. Never explained in dialogue. Funny and faintly irritating at once.

---

## Objects

| Mug | Owner (true) | Who uses it | Label |
|-----|--------------|-------------|-------|
| **A** | Calder’s missing mug | Park (custody by theft/joke) | **BEST CAPTAIN EVER** |
| **B** | Calder’s reply mug | Calder, after discovery | **WORLD'S BEST COMMS OFFICER** |

### Mug A — origin (locked)

- **Given by:** Lisette Venn (aunt; The Cardamom Oven)
- **Occasion:** Calder’s first command / *Kestrel Veil* posting
- **Tone of gift:** Tourist-tacky print; she meant it sincerely and also thought it was funny
- **Why he won’t file a loss report:** Too sentimental to put on a form. Six months of quiet looking is the character.

Fleet coffee culture on the mug gag. Separate running beat: Park switches to Dominion tea via Orlen after Ch. 3 — see `PARK_ORLEN_DOMINION_TEA.md`. Mug may hold tea; gag stays about the label.

---

## Rules

1. **Not every chapter.** Hit every few chapters when Park and Calder share a room with drinks.
2. **Early phase (pre-discovery):** Park deliberately turns/holds the mug so the label faces away from Calder. Calder notices. He does not ask.
3. **Discovery:** Calder finds out Park has his mug. Reaction = amused + irritated (both). No speechifying. No long confrontation.
4. **Reply phase:** Calder acquires mug B and uses it in front of Park. **Never says a word** about either mug. Park notices. Also says nothing.
5. Do not let the gag undercut crisis scenes. Prefer breathers, waits, protocol rooms, galley, after-action quiet.
