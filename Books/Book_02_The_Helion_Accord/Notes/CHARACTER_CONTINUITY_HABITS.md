# Book Two+ — Character Continuity Habits

**Status:** Locked series canon (2026-07-21)  
**Type:** Quiet recognizable behaviors — not gimmicks, not running jokes.

## General rules

- Behaviors emerge naturally when appropriate.
- Never force them into scenes.
- Never stop the story to remind readers.
- They may evolve across books.
- Goal: readers recognize the person before they speak.

---

## Park

After Orlen introduces him to Dominion tea (Ch. 3), he quietly abandons Fleet coffee.

- Carries his own tea tin.
- Fleet personnel automatically provide hot water.
- Tea comes from Orlen or through Dominion Science channels.
- Gradually learns different Dominion teas across the series.
- Never treated as political — he genuinely enjoys it.
- Curiosity about other cultures is the root; tea is one expression.
- Humor stays understated (*Hot water is fine.* / *No coffee?* / *I've made better decisions.*)

## Thessaly

- Calm competence; enormous responsibility without advertising it.
- Exceptionally dry humor; observations often sharper than anyone else's.
- Frequently understands what Calder is struggling with before he says it.
- Never rescues him by deciding for him — asks the question that lets him reach the correct decision.
- Series arc: increasingly Calder's mentor in command, not only his XO.

## Brenner

- Solves problems before meetings finish discussing them.
- Almost always arrives from actual work (grease, dust, weld burns, open maintenance logs).
- Thinks with his hands. Politics bore him. Engineering fascinates him.
- Humor from practicality — never from trying to be funny.

## Holt

- Quiet operational backbone connecting Fleet.
- Remembers people, small colonies, traffic.
- Quietly routes around bureaucracy when lives depend on it.
- Never calls attention to herself.
- Readers should eventually realize she is one of Fleet's most valuable officers.

## Walsh

- Quiet operational competence: notebook already open, already thinking, already solving.
- Rarely wastes words.
- Security is how she understands the world.
- Increasingly respected by Fleet and Dominion professionals — competence recognizes competence.

## Orlen

- Precise, dry, almost impossible to compliment.
- Teaches freely; dislikes unnecessary praise.
- Enjoys Park's curiosity far more than she admits.
- Slowly becomes one of Park's closest professional colleagues.

## Vex

- Evaluates competence before personality.
- Notices discipline, preparation, professional habits.
- Respects ability long before trust.
- **Never** evaluates someone by appearance — only behavior, actions, professionalism.
- Defining series trait (planted Ch. 3 with Walsh).
