# Chapter 17

## Echoes

The echo arrived forty-one minutes after the Library filed them.

Park knew the interval because he had logged it before he understood what he was logging—relay discipline first, interpretation second, panic never on-watch if he could help it. Cole sat beside him at secondary peel, headphones half-on, the congestion posture they had worn since chapter nine turned permanent: shoulders forward, one hand on the gain wheel, listening for the flat line that meant a lane had lied cleanly enough to kill someone.

"Sideband variance on buffer seven appendix coupling," Park said. "Not merge pressure. **Replay.**"

"Unknown source. Checksum partial—Library read tags present, timestamp **future** relative to ship clock by four hours twelve minutes."

On the command deck, Calder did not turn. He had stopped turning for every relay anomaly unless Thessaly or Park changed register—which meant Park had learned to change register only when the channel forced it.

The *Kestrel Veil* held whisper in segment eleven occluder shadow post-Library egress, the way Reyes had taught the volume to hold them: by refusing to agree with itself. Hull integrity seventy-nine percent per Koss's formal update—ring three micro-fractures propagated one segment during positional adjustment, not catastrophic, not imaginary. Primary sensor mast segment four at forty-one percent effective, corridor-biased, Brenner's patched path holding because ugly fixes were honest fixes. Tag coupling quadruple baseline port-aft, the Library's clerk-stamp still felt in Park's narrow-path monitors like a tooth that had been drilled and left sensitive.

Relay contact absent. Narrow path listen-only continued. Segregated buffers five through seven intact. Buffer seven carried Reyes's Library read—register CV-7-minor, do-not-merge headers copied three times because redundancy was love when merge was violence. Lines of Loyalty operational. Pursuit geometry tightened. KISC listener continuity confirmation inside shadow depth post-read, passive, not weapons hot.

Park had not slept in a cycle he would not name aloud. His mother Hye-Jin on Helion Prime expected a call at cycle-end. He had sent a deferred message with the latency excuse he always used when the truth was *I am afraid the channel will teach me something I cannot unhear.* She would forgive latency. She would not forgive silence.

The echo did not behave like sideband spoof.

Spoof wanted you to act. Spoof carried urgency headers and hostile merge invitations and the institutional violence of maps that pretended to be singular. This packet sat in appendix coupling like a book returned to the wrong shelf—metadata wrong, body present, auth layer **absent** in the way Library register fragments had been absent: not forged, **unowned.**

Cole rewound the capture window. "Voice fragment in the payload tail. Not ours. Not Fleet band."

Park's stomach tightened before his ears confirmed it.

The voice was his.

Not metaphor. Not synthesis. His own cadence on the relay test loop he used when calibrating peel boards—*latency nominal, handshake clean, do not merge headers holding*—except the words in the echo were not words he had spoken yet.

*—if the future lands in buffer seven, treat it like a person who hasn't entered the room. Log the door. Don't shake hands until you count exits.*

He had not said that. He might say it. The structural shape of the sentence matched how he thought when he was tired enough to stop performing precision for the log.

Cole looked at him. Park looked at the timestamp.

Future-relative. Four hours twelve minutes.

"Peel only," Park said. "No merge. No command relay. Cole—congestion profile on the carrier?"

"Clean where it shouldn't be. Library read window left harmonic bleed on local bus. This rides the bleed."

That was weather, not revelation. Weather could still drown you.

Through the bulkhead, Okwelu passed medical bay toward the relay alcove periphery—sleep enforcement patrol, concussion watch on Walsh continuing, Tanaka contusion functional, Brenner palm burn dressed. Okwelu paused long enough to read Park's face the way she read pupils.

"You look like someone heard a message addressed to them that hasn't been sent yet," she said.

"Working hypothesis," Park said.

"Get a second witness before you let it change your pulse."

She left. Park logged her sentence anyway because Okwelu was always data even when she pretended to be authority.

Reyes appeared at the relay station threshold without hurry—the navigator's walk when hurry had become something the ship spent on hull percentages.

"Heard you caught a future," Reyes said.

"Heard you filed us into a volume that answers questions with timestamps we haven't earned."

Reyes almost smiled. Almost did not count today.

"Show me layer tags."

Park showed him. Gravitational shelf grammar in the metadata tail—Sharma's vocabulary, not Sharma's transmission. Electromagnetic facet index pulse at interval seven—Ortega's window language. Primary-True null placeholder paired with redundant facet address—the Library's cruel politeness.

Reyes went still the way he went still when jump solutions rotated in his head.

"Not spoof," he said. "Not hallucination. **Register bleed.** Consequence registers. Okonkwo's store warned downstream effects without naming builders. We read one fragment. The volume may be… answering adjacent shelves."

"Adjacent shelves meaning futures?"

"Meaning records stored as relation. Future is a relation if observation method couples to read progress. Reyes does not like saying that aloud."

Park appreciated the honesty more than the theory.

Calder's voice from the rail, tired and clear: "Status in sentences the institution would hate."

Park: "Buffer seven appendix coupled an unprompted replay with future-relative timestamp and Library layer tags. Voice payload includes Comms Officer speech not yet spoken. Checksum partial, auth absent. Recommend segregated peel, no merge, no command action pending echo protocol."

Thessaly: "Define echo protocol."

Park met Reyes's eyes. "We don't have one yet."

Calder: "Then you and Reyes write one before the next packet arrives."

That was command on the *Kestrel Veil* now—sequence under maintained contradiction, doctrine written in the same hour the weather turned.

Park's private fear was not the anomaly. It was his memory. He remembered every conversation structurally—not word-perfect, but exact enough that an echo shaped like his future self felt less like instrument failure and more like **someone had edited his life ahead of him.**

He thought of Okonkwo in medical bay describing *Watcher's Mark*—maintenance tags dated months apart, ration stamps implying crew had stopped eating before they had stopped breathing, Lieutenant Reyes forward debris in testimony that disagreed with annex manifests. Staggered clocks. Not ghosts. **Records that had not agreed on when loss occurred.**

He thought of Calder at 1634 in chapter thirteen: *If we name suppression as adversary, we cut the last thread of institutional escalation.* Park had logged the sentence before Calder finished speaking because that was what he did when captains tried to be careful and the volume tried to be louder. Now an echo spoke his own calibration loop back at him with grammar he had not earned yet, and the cruelest part was how **competent** it sounded.

Halden appeared at the relay alcove doorway—the tactical officer's version of checking on a friend without calling it that.

"If it's your voice, I don't want it on my board," Halden said.

"It's not on your board. It's on mine."

"Good. My threat model already has enough futures."

She sat sideways on the spare chair the way Park sat sideways off-watch, because both of them noticed exits for a living. "Okonkwo said repetition versus request. I say **leverage.** If an echo hurts the right person, it moves the ship without firing a shot. You're the right person for half this crew's confidences. That makes you a target even when the channel is innocent."

Park: "I'm also the person who writes the rules now."

Halden's mouth twitched. "Then write them like someone who knows his memory is a weapon pointed inward."

She left before he could thank her. Gratitude was another thing Park logged and did not always speak.

The echo was not cheap hallucination. Cheap hallucinations did not carry Sharma curvature numbers in metadata tails. Cheap hallucinations did not make Cole whisper, "There's another one loading," with the flat tone he used when congestion was about to become casualty.

Park and Reyes convened at relay primary with Cole on peel, Walsh at perimeter pen ready, Thessaly on doctrine tags, Calder listening without merging authority.

The second echo loaded while Reyes spoke.

Okonkwo was in medical bay under Okwelu's authority—limited clearance, no command deck. Park had logged her alive status forty minutes ago.

Park opened medical channel. Okwelu answered on first ring.

Okonkwo arrived twenty minutes later—sitting, blanket on shoulders, eyes sharper than her body. Park played the echo once without merge, peel board isolating audio from command bus.

Reyes: "You're saying echo traffic matches Watcher's Mark stagger?"

"I'm saying KISU stores carried both field truth and archive fiction because my vault didn't merge. If Library grammar is older than KISU, it's the same disease at scale—**when** becomes negotiable if you enforce the wrong observation method."

Okonkwo looked at Park. "Your officer hears voices structurally. Good. Tell him the difference between an echo that **repeats** and an echo that **requests.** Repetition is record. Request is trap."

Park logged *repetition vs request* in buffer seven segregated appendix before the doctrine had a header.


Echoes did not affect the crew uniformly—which was how Park knew they were not cheap hallucination. Cheap hallucinations were democratic. These were **targeted by instrument and wound.**

Ortega reported facet-index flicker on passive sweep—not structure, **relation spikes** aligned to echo load windows. "Redundant array shows returns that aren't objects," he said. "They're **indices.** Like the Library shelved names next to coordinates."

Tanaka at helm bias held three colors with one missing since Library egress—the hue she had removed without comment. Now amber pulsed in rhythm with echo metadata, as if the board remembered a grammar spent.

Koss on engineering channel heard Brenner's voice in ring three junction telemetry—not audio echo, **stress prediction**: fracture propagation tick aligned to future-relative timestamps in echo tails. Koss refused to call it prophecy. "I call it **someone filing hull stress before the hull agrees.** If we act on it, we might **make** it true by sprinting repairs."

Tanaka reported helm bias tremor—not mechanical, **semantic**: the missing hue from Library egress pulsed when echo loads arrived, as if color grammar were a sense organ the ship had grown against regulation. The third echo was Halden's weather.

Cole: "Fourth load incoming. Congestion profile changing. Not clean bleed anymore—**structured.**"

Thessaly brought tea nobody drank and placed it where Park's mug would have been if he had remembered which of his three mugs was alive today. "Infrastructure," she said quietly—one word, no speech. Witness was what the volume demanded. The chapter between them was not resolved by tea.
