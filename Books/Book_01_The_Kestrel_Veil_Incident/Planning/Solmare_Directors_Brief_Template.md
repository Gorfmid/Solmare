# Solmare Director's Brief Template
## Chapter Planning — Author Input Format

**Status:** Author briefing template (not generation canon)  
**Usage:** Provide this structure when commissioning a chapter. The AI writing process treats it as the authoritative creative brief for that chapter.  
**Companion:** Root `MyraTempalte.md` (abbreviated checklist) · `Writing_System/Generation_Rules/chapter_generator_spec.md` (execution rules)

---

> **Rule:** Every chapter must permanently change something.  
> If the chapter does not change the reader, a character, a doctrine, or a civilization, it does not yet earn its place.

---

## 1. Chapter Identity

- **Title**
- **Act**
- **Target length** (Book One guideline: ~4,000–4,500 words; follow `chapter_length_principle.md` for narrative function)

---

## 2. Why This Chapter Exists

Not what happens. **Why it exists.**

What understanding, relationship, or pressure must advance?

---

## 3. Strategic Question

Exactly **one** question the chapter must answer (or deepen without closing).

---

## 4. Reader Emotional Objective

What should the reader **feel** by the end?

---

## 5. Reader Must Not Yet Know

Hard secrecy boundaries for this chapter. List what remains withheld.

---

## 6. Chapter Structure

Scene count and alternation pattern (e.g. six scenes; Dominion / Fleet / Dominion…).

---

## 7. Scene Objectives

Per scene:

- POV
- Purpose (not prose)
- Key story beats
- Character goal

---

## 8. Character Progression

Who changes how— cast list with one-line arc delta each.

---

## 9. Civilization Progression

What Fleet, Dominion, or other civilization layer advances?

---

## 10. Cultural Touchstones

Recurring civilization markers to reinforce (do not explain on page).  
Reference: `Universe/cultural_touchstones.md` · Dominion visual/speech docs.

---

## 11. Recurring Symbols

Images, objects, phrases that should recur in this chapter.

---

## 12. Worldbuilding Goals

What the reader should **see** without exposition lecture.

---

## 13. Continuity Notes

- Prior chapter state to honor
- Character availability
- Timeline / segment / location
- What must **not** contradict

---

## 14. What NOT To Do

Guardrails: tone traps, premature reveals, craft violations.

---

## 15. Solmare Principle

How this chapter links the chain:

> History creates civilizations. Civilizations create doctrine. Doctrine creates decisions. Decisions create conflict. The people living through those decisions are the story.

Which link does this chapter strengthen?

---

## 16. Ending Objective

What new truth or pressure should the reader carry into the next chapter?

---

## 17. State Changes

Bullet list of canonical updates after the chapter (ship, crew, institutions, reader knowledge).

---

## 18. Why This Matters Later

Connect this chapter to Act arc and series.

- What future chapter or act pays off here?
- What doctrine, relationship, or mystery does this chapter load?

If this section is empty, reconsider whether the chapter is necessary.

---

## Submission Checklist

Before sending the brief:

- [ ] One strategic question only
- [ ] Scene objectives are purposes, not draft prose
- [ ] State changes are concrete
- [ ] "Must not yet know" protects series surprises
- [ ] Section 18 explains payoff
- [ ] Something **permanently changes**
