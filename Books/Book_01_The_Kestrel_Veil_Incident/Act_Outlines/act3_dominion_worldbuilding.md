# ACT III — DOMINION WORLDBUILDING (CH. 10–16)

**Authority:** Apply to all Act III generation and revision from Ch. 10 forward.  
**Series foundation:** `Universe/The_Dominion/dominion_worldview.md` — **mandatory for all Dominion writing**  
**Ch. 9 status:** Locked — establishes Dominion, Stratimirov, Kovat, infrastructure discovery. **Do not rewrite Ch. 9** unless canon conflict discovered.

**Related:** `Universe/The_Dominion/dominion_worldview.md` · `Characters/Antagonists/The_Dominion.md` · `solmare_craft_doctrine.md`

**Terminology:** Scenes from the opposing civilization = **Dominion Thread** (not *enemy POV*). See `dominion_worldview.md` — recurring vocabulary.

---

## Core Objective

Expand the Dominion as a **living civilization** per `Universe/The_Dominion/dominion_worldview.md`.

Readers should **understand** why intelligent people on both sides believe they are protecting their civilization — while recognizing that **history**, not malice, has placed them on a collision course.

> *I understand these people.*  
> *I understand exactly why they made that decision.*

Neither civilization is the antagonist. Empathize with both simultaneously. Full doctrine: `Writing_System/Series_Principles/cultural_identity_and_philosophical_foundation.md`.

That tension is the emotional foundation of the conflict.

---

## 1. The Dominion Is a Civilization

Stop thinking of the Dominion primarily as "the enemy."

Think of it as **another superpower**.

Every Dominion Thread scene should reveal another piece of its society.

**Possible perspectives (rotate — do not repeat Stratimirov/Kovat every chapter):**

- military officers
- scientists
- engineers
- logistics personnel
- intelligence analysts
- border operators
- diplomats
- industrial workers
- shipyard supervisors
- civilian administrators
- transport captains
- merchants
- teachers
- veterans

Each Dominion scene must answer:

> **What does life look like inside the Dominion?**

---

## 2. The Dominion Is Old

**Fleet Authority is still expanding.**  
**The Dominion has existed for centuries.**

**Frontier history (series canon):** `Universe/Fleet/fleet_dominion_frontier_history.md` — breakaway founding, Neutral Zone as surviving term, asymmetric memory, no border treaties.

Readers should constantly encounter evidence of age:

- old military traditions
- infrastructure hundreds of years old (Ch. 9 infrastructure fits this)
- family military service spanning generations
- ancient border stations (K-17 plaque tradition — expand this pattern)
- monuments
- long-established trade routes
- historical references treated as common knowledge

**Feel:** Dominion = established. Fleet Authority = comparatively young.

---

## 3. People Are Different

Avoid making every Dominion officer sound identical.

Create personality variation while remaining professional:

- dry humor
- quiet sarcasm
- impatience
- optimism
- exhaustion
- ambition
- family concerns
- retirement approaching
- rivalry between departments
- respect earned through experience

**Professional ≠ emotionally identical.**

---

## 4. Internal Disagreement

The Dominion is **unified**. It is **not unanimous**.

Military · Scientists · Political leadership · Intelligence · Industry — each has different priorities.

| Voice | Example attitude |
|-------|------------------|
| Scientists | *The cloak nearly succeeded.* |
| Military | *It failed.* |
| Political oversight | *Failure is failure.* |
| Industry | *We can improve the design.* |

Nobody is openly rebellious. Everyone works **within the system**.

Show disagreement through procedure, subtext, and priority conflict — not treason plots.

---

## 5. Show Civilian Life

Throughout Act III, include **occasional short glimpses** of ordinary life:

Families · markets · schools · transit · shipyards · restaurants · apartment blocks · children · parks · veterans · holiday traditions

Readers should realize: **these are ordinary people living ordinary lives.**

Ch. 9 seeds: officers' commons apples, Stratimirov's family photograph, Kovat's crossword shift, **tea ritual**, **chess in commons**, **K-17 plaque**, **strategic depth** in Stratimirov memory — **continue this pattern**, do not front-load every scene with HQ.

**Cultural touchstones (series canon):** `Universe/cultural_touchstones.md` · `Writing_System/Series_Principles/cultural_identity_and_philosophical_foundation.md` — Dominion proverbs, gloves/tea/chess/plaques, sacrifice-based education, etc. Recur naturally; never explain on page; one or two per scene.

**Dominion visual language (series canon):** `Universe/The_Dominion/dominion_visual_language.md` — readers recognize Dominion space **before the chapter heading**. One or two motifs per scene; never checklist.

**Dominion speech (series canon):** `Universe/The_Dominion/dominion_speech.md` — Dominion characters quote teachers, parents, grandparents, instructors, old commanders, historical sayings; Fleet almost never does. Continuity vs. innovation — never explain on page.

**Civilization scene test:** Every Dominion chapter leaves the reader understanding one new aspect of Dominion culture — family, tradition, logistics, duty, food, history — never technology alone.

---

## 6. Plant the Coming War

**Never state:** *We are preparing for war.*

**Show evidence** — readers slowly realize preparation has been underway for years:

- new shipyards under construction
- fleet exercises increasing
- strategic resource stockpiles
- expanded fuel depots
- industrial production shifts
- reserve personnel recalled
- logistics convoys
- munitions manufacturing
- transportation congestion
- military procurement
- infrastructure expansion
- political meetings becoming more frequent
- emergency planning exercises
- scientific funding increasing

Ch. 9 seeds: Stratimirov's *continue mobilization* / *accelerate cloak refinement* — show downstream effects in Ch. 10+ POVs (shipyard, logistics, analyst).

---

## 7. Keep Motivation Ambiguous

**Do NOT reveal** the Dominion's true strategic objective in Book One.

Different people should believe different things. **No single explanation confirmed.**

Possible beliefs (in-character, not author canon):

- resource shortages
- population growth
- historical trauma
- strategic depth
- fear of Fleet expansion
- economic necessity
- national destiny

Let readers assemble partial theories. Withhold confirmation.

## 10. Tone

The Dominion is:

- **dangerous**
- **disciplined**
- **human**

Dominion Thread scenes: Tom Clancy procedural — competence creates tension; people solve problems; technology supports realism, never dominates.

Every Dominion scene: at least one meaningful human interaction that passes the **spaceship-disappeared test** (`solmare_craft_doctrine.md`).
