## Narrative Shift (Critical)

Chapters 1–5 established the anomaly.

Chapters 6–11: the **anomaly is the setting**. The **crew is the story**.

> How do good people continue doing their jobs when reality itself refuses to cooperate?

Readers should finish Chapter 10 wanting to turn the page—not for a shock, but because they **know and trust this crew**.

---

## The 70/30 Rule (Mandatory)

Load `Writing_System/Style_Guide/the_70_30_rule.md`.

**~85%** crew, mission, relationships, problem-solving, politics, exploration, action, professional life.  
**~15%** direct anomaly focus.

Anomaly = **weather**. Crew = **people operating in the rain**.

## Human Victory Pattern (Ch. 6–10)

Each chapter ends with a **human victory**—not an anomaly revelation.

| Chapter | Human Victory | Support Crew Spotlight |
|---------|---------------|------------------------|
| **6 — First Contact** | Crew reconstructs a corrupted survey log through collaboration | **Brenner** |
| **7 — Ghost Fleet** | Park proves one communications path is still trustworthy | **Okwelu** |
| **8 — Boarding Action** | Boarding team rescues something (data, a person, or both) | **Cole** |
| **9 — The Silent War** | Crew exposes Fleet information suppression as fact | **Walsh** |
| **10 — Running Dark** | Crew evades superior forces without engagement | **Sharma** |
| **11 — First Engagement** | *(No clean victory—fight becomes unavoidable)* | Rotate (e.g. **Hale**) |

**Spotlight rule:** One support crew member gets meaningful recurring presence per chapter—not a biography dump, not extra pages for their sake. By Chapter 20, readers know all eight almost without realizing it.

Senior officers remain ensemble; spotlight supplements, never replaces.

## Chapter 10 → Chapter 11 (Cinematic Transition)

**Chapter 10 ends in Running Dark—not battle.**

Final movement should feel **cinematic**:

- Emissions control engaged
- Lights dimmed
- No Fleet contact
- Passive sensors only
- Unknown contacts on the scope
- Crew whispering on the deck
- Reyes navigating by local observation
- Halden building threat models
- Park barely hearing intermittent traffic
- Calder making decisions almost blind

**Human victory for Ch. 10:** they **evade** superior forces. They are not caught. They are not safe—they are **uncaught**.

Chapter ends on held breath: shadowing, darkness, professional fear without panic.

---

**Chapter 11 opens:**

> **Weapons lock.**

That is the page-turn. First major engagement. Teamwork, preparation, and understanding—not superior firepower. Ch. 11 is not a victory chapter in the same sense; the crew **cannot avoid the fight anymore**.

Full engagement doctrine: multi-department coordination under sensor uncertainty. See `chapter_11_generation_directive.md` — **The First Engagement** (Act II climax).

**Ch. 10→11 continuity:** Single continuous sequence. Ch. 10 ends: Halden *"They're done watching."* / held breath broke. Ch. 11 opens: weapons lock, no time skip.

## Humor

Professional humor relieves tension without breaking tone. Halden dry, Park warm, Brenner/Koss engineering sarcasm. **Humor stops** when Halden stops joking.

Avoid Marvel quips, slapstick, everyone being funny.

## The Anomaly (Act II)

Deepen **consequences of existing rules**. Do not stack new anomaly types per chapter.

| Avoid | Prefer |
|-------|--------|
| "New anomaly!" each chapter | Same weather, harder conditions |
| Mystery as main character | Crew adaptation |

## Steve (Canon)

- **Never** source of information (`Steve.md`)
- **Target frequency:** one appearance every **2–3 chapters**—not every chapter
- **Perfect appearance:** reader thinks *"Wait… wasn't he…?"* then chapter moves on
- If always present, people stop noticing him
- Ch. 6–10 block: **0–2 Steve appearances total** across five chapters is correct

## Chapter-by-Chapter Roadmap

### Chapter 6 — First Contact

**Beat:** Abandoned survey installation; records say empty decades; physical evidence disagrees.

**Human victory:** Crew reconstructs corrupted survey log through multi-department collaboration (Reyes/Ortega lineage, Park buffer discipline, Koss hardware corroboration, Brenner physical access).

**Spotlight:** Brenner — hands, access panels, installation mechanics.

**End question:** Who studied here and why does Fleet say otherwise?

---

### Chapter 7 — Ghost Fleet

**Beat:** Prior Fleet expeditions compartmentalized; crew reconstructs missing history from fragments.

**Human victory:** Park proves **one communications path** remains trustworthy enough to act on—not full relay restoration.

**Spotlight:** Okwelu — crew fatigue, medical read on stress before history work breaks someone.

**End question:** Who ordered compartmentalization?

**Note:** Ghost Fleet contacts/auth anomalies support history work; no battle required.

---

### Chapter 8 — Boarding Action

**Beat:** Boarding mission; mystery becomes physical.

**Human victory:** Boarding team **rescues** something—survivor, critical data, or both. Extraction under contradictory readouts.

**Spotlight:** Cole — relay/buffer support for away-team comms; congestion by sound.

**End question:** What happened to everyone else?

**Every senior officer contributes** from ship or away team.

---

### Chapter 9 — The Silent War

**Beat:** Espionage; Fleet suppression evidence.

**Human victory:** Crew **exposes suppression as fact**—operational proof, not full conspiracy scope (`series_principles.md`).

**Spotlight:** Walsh — watch notebook, schedule intelligence, Thessaly's unofficial reference.

**End question:** Protection or cause?

---

### Chapter 10 — Running Dark

**Beat:** Shadowed. Emissions control. Evasion.

**Human victory:** Crew **evades superior forces** without engagement.

**Spotlight:** Sharma — life-support/emissions tradeoffs under Koss; berth environment during dark running.

**Ending:** Cinematic Running Dark sequence (see transition section above). **No weapons lock in this chapter.**

---

### Chapter 11 — The First Engagement (Opener)

**Beat:** Weapons lock. First major tactical confrontation.

**Not a human-victory chapter** in the Ch. 6–10 sense—crew cannot avoid fight.

**Opens with:** weapons lock, immediate tactical coordination.

**Emphasis:** sensor uncertainty, comm delays, Halden/Hale/Reyes/Koss/Park integration; victory through teamwork if any victory comes—not firepower dominance.

**Spotlight:** Hale (suggested) — defensive systems hands.
