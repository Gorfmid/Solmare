## Act III Shift

Act II proved the crew is **competent**.

Act III tests whether competence remains enough when:

- Maps no longer map
- Loyalty becomes ambiguous
- Political actors enter the field
- Fleet orders contradict lived reality
- Historical records become unstable
- The anomaly references past and future
- Action may worsen the catastrophe

**Not bigger spectacle. Deeper uncertainty.**

> The anomaly remains the environment.  
> The crew remains the story.

Deepen pressure through:
- Psychological, navigational, institutional, political pressure
- Crew fatigue, data mistrust, Fleet mistrust
- Hull tag consequences, damage that cannot be cleanly repaired
- Realization that return to Fleet may be impossible

## The 70/30 Rule

**~85%:** crew, mission, procedure, politics, relationships, problem-solving, damage control, survival, moral choices  

**~15%:** anomaly, mystery, reality distortion  

Every chapter: **Competent people make a costly decision in bad reality weather.**

Not: "new weird thing happens."

## Politics & Espionage

Introduce **gradually**. Factions understood by **what they want**, not exposition dumps.

Possible motives (all think they prevent something worse):
- Suppress anomaly data / prevent panic
- Exploit for strategic advantage
- Hide illegal research
- Rescue trapped personnel
- Destroy evidence
- Prevent Library from being read
- Force Veil to return or go deeper

Avoid simplistic good/bad labels.

## Anomaly Rules (Act III — May Understand / Must Withhold)

**May understand by end of Act III:**
- Anomaly responds to observation methods
- Contradiction can be preserved and used
- Ancient structures may store truth as incompatible states
- Human suppression was a *response*, not necessarily origin

**Do not fully reveal:**
- Who built the Library
- Why the anomaly exists / whether intelligent
- Whether future echoes are fixed

## Chapter-by-Chapter Plan

### Chapter 12 — Broken Maps (~5,000 words)

**Tone:** Quiet aftermath. Not sprinting into explosion.

**Primary goals:**
- Process battle damage; establish cannot go back
- Hull tag changes decisions
- Navigation breaks at larger scale — star systems in mutually exclusive locations by observation method
- Reyes's importance deepens

**Human victory:** First **living map** — conditional navigation model by observation method, not fixed chart.

**Cost:** Map only works while crew keeps multiple contradictory models alive.

**Ending question:** If maps depend on how you observe, who or what chooses which map becomes dangerous?

---

### Chapter 13 — Espionage (~5,000 words)

**Primary goals:**
- Connect Interior Mandate, KISC, KISU, suppression into operational structure
- Hidden Fleet research architecture; factions disagree
- Calder: suppression protection vs. cause (not resolved)
- Procedural espionage: hidden logs, false headers, dead-drops, corrupted auth, compartmentalized channels, disagreeing personnel lists

**Human victory:** Park, Walsh, Cole, Thessaly identify hidden research **lattice** without exposing position.

**Cost:** Lattice now knows the *Veil* is alive.

**Ending question:** Was Interior Mandate hiding truth from Fleet, or Fleet from the truth?

---

### Chapter 14 — Lines of Loyalty (~5,000 words)

**Primary goals:**
- Contradictory Fleet orders; external factions pursue *Veil*
- Fleet not monolithic; crew loyalty tested without soap opera
- Introduce factions gradually (Fleet Ops, Interior Mandate, Cartography, civilian oversight, colonial/corporate, Ghost Fleet/KISC remnant)

**Human victory:** Internal loyalty principle — preserve truth, crew, civilians before institutional obedience.

**Cost:** May make them fugitives depending on which Fleet layer survives contact.

**Ending question:** If multiple authorities claim legitimacy, who commands reality-breaking evidence?

---

### Chapter 15 — Siege of Korrath (~5,000 words)

**Major Act III battle** — bigger than Ch. 11, not just louder.

**Primary goals:**
- Multiple factions, same battlespace; relay data untrusted
- *Veil* as translator of broken information
- Ch. 11 damage constrains; Margaret partial rebuild; Hale matters
- Calder commands with more confidence, greater moral burden

**Victory by:** identifying locally useful data source; preventing friendly fire on false relay data; exploiting enemies who assume one map; broken maps as tactical terrain — **not firepower**.

**Human victory:** Save ships/civilians from acting on false relay data.

**Cost:** Intervention exposes *Veil* to all hunting factions.

**Ending question:** Prevented massacre, or revealed themselves to everyone capable of hunting them?

---

### Chapter 16 — The Library (~5,000 words)

**Major mythic/science reveal — grounded, not magical.**

**Primary goals:**
- Shift human conspiracy → pre-human scale
- Anomaly = consequence of something older (not weapon, not natural disaster)
- Do not fully explain

**Library framing:** ancient archival structure; non-human not mystical; records in gravitational/spatial/observational layers; requires contradictory observation methods; truth stored as **disagreement**.

**Human victory:** Read one small portion by preserving contradiction, not resolving it.

**Cost:** Reading may alter position, expose, or mark them further.

**Ending question:** If the Library stores contradictions on purpose, what was it built to remember—or contain?

---

### Chapter 17 — Echoes (~5,000 words)

**Primary goals:**
- Temporal/causal pressure; records referencing futures
- Echoes affect crew differently — not cheap hallucinations
- Ambiguity: records, predictions, alternates, or manipulated data?
- Connect Okonkwo/Watcher's Mark, KISU, crew hopes, possible futures

**Human victory:** Park and Reyes establish rules for usable vs. dangerous echoes.

**Cost:** One echo predicts/implies future loss crew cannot ignore.

**Ending question:** If the future appears as record — warning, memory, or trap?

---

### Chapter 18 — Into the Fold (~5,000 words)

**Act III climax.**

**Primary goals:**
- Calder **chooses** initiative over reaction — deliberate, not reckless
- Only rational move left; crew understands risk, commits professionally
- Uses all Act III discoveries; enters anomaly core mechanism

**Human victory:** Enter Fold **on their own terms**, not because enemies forced them.

**Cost:** May lose external reference entirely.

**Ending question:** Can they return from a place where reference itself no longer exists?

**Momentum:** Act IV (Ch. 19 — First Truth).
