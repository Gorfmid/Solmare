# Fleet Exploration Corps
## Frontier Missions, Survey Doctrine, and First-Arrival Culture — Unified Fleet Authority

---

## 1. Function

Fleet Exploration Corps missions scout-class hulls like the *Kestrel Veil*— reconnaissance mandates, spiral surveys, green readiness charts that are accurate if incomplete.

Exploration translates Fleet ideology into **motion**:

- The unknown exists to be explored
- Patrols gather data for expansion and safety
- Scouts are eyes, not battle line

Calder's assignment was routine reconnaissance— prestige expected elsewhere, folklore aboard an aging scout.

---

## 2. Cultural Personality

- **Optimistic professional.** Curiosity without melodrama.
- **First-arrival identity.** *Someone has to go first* — touchstone, not joke on serious decks.
- **Respects whisper burn and emitter discipline** when taught by survival.
- **Uncomfortable with passive-only doctrine** as primary product— wants to **see** things.
- **Young institution-within-institution.** Exploration champions Fleet's self-image.

---

## 3. Act IV Relevance

Exploration Corps must absorb segment seven's reversal:

- Calder did not arrive first— he arrived **late**
- Exploration data now includes **civilization-scale** observation, not rocks and lanes
- Corps culture must reconcile pride with **strategic humility**

Act IV may feature Exploration liaison demanding new scout ROE and training sims— doctrine shift hits Corps identity hardest.

---

## 4. Likely Assumptions

- Scouts illuminate when necessary; passive is temporary discipline.
- Frontier is opportunity surface.
- Reconnaissance products feed expansion planning.
- Unknown contact templates will apply when Fleet chooses contact.

---

## 5. Likely Friction

- Interpreting Calder's restraint as Corps cowardice.
- Demanding active return missions before Rowan approves.
- Resisting *observation-first* protocols as anti-exploration.
- Hero narrative pressure on Calder vs. witness truth.
