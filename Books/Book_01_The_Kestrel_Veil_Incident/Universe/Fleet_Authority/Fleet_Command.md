## 1. Function

Fleet Command is the operational heart of the Unified Fleet Authority— where orders become movement, margins become policy, and scout survival becomes strategic asset.

Responsibilities include:

- Fleet-wide operations authority (deployment, mission amendment, ROE issuance)
- Crisis routing through relay chains (VI-Gamma, Cartography nodes)
- Balance between **verified loss** and **assumed contact** (Rowan's professional ethic)
- Signing orders that send damaged hulls back toward risk without pretending risk is gone

Fleet Command does not own intelligence analysis (Sato) or doctrine text (Doctrine Bureau) but signs what both recommend.

---

## 2. Cultural Personality

- **Calm under incomplete information.** Speaks last in large rooms; ends briefings when the room has enough to decide.
- **People before systems** in live calls— asks who is on the deck before what the deck shows.
- **Short declarative sentences.** No rank-per-sentence; dry humor reserved for trusted staff.
- **Margin thinking.** *Six hours was not a gift. It was a margin.* — not cruelty, accounting.
- **Young confidence at institutional scale.** Fleet Command believes procedure can hold the frontier if adults in chairs do their jobs.

## 3. Act IV Relevance

Fleet Command receives the *Kestrel Veil* return and Thessaly's report first.

Rowan must decide:

- How much of segment seven to elevate immediately
- Whether to treat observation language as intelligence or delay for classification
- How fast to move ROE, pickup, medical, and strategic assessment without panic
- Whether to protect Calder's crew as witnesses or consume them in briefing cycles

Act IV opens Fleet Command's **doctrine crisis**— not a battle crisis.

---

## 4. Likely Assumptions

- The Incident was severe but **bounded**— one contact geometry, one survival miracle.
- Neutral Zone seven-alpha produced **data**, not necessarily **strategic revision**.
- Scout reports process through normal channels after debrief.
- *Patched* hulls can be tasked if orders require it.
- Fleet still **controls escalation tempo** if no one illuminates first.
- First contact remains a managed event Fleet can schedule after classification.

---

## 5. Likely Friction

- Pressure to rename *structured civilization* into *hostile contact* for political usability.
- Impatience with Thessaly's rejected headers (*enemy*, *unknown*, *frontier*).
- Underestimating passive-only intelligence as "soft" until Sato numbers it.
- Treating Calder's caution as command hesitation rather than witness discipline.
- Split between Rowan's restraint and subordinate appetite for readiness orders.
