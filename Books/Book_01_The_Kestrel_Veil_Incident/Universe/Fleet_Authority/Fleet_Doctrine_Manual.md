## 1. Edition 143 — Pre-Kestrel Doctrine

### Fleet Academy Strategic Studies — First-Year Cadet Primer (Edition 143)

> History creates civilizations.  
> Civilizations create doctrine.  
> Doctrine creates decisions.  
> Decisions create conflict.  
> The people living through those decisions are the story.

— Fleet Academy Strategic Studies, First-Year Cadet Primer  
(Edition 143)

*Note: Prologue epigraph uses these five lines. Series craft adds: "Never write a battle before understanding the history that made it inevitable." — author-facing only unless later edition adopts it.*

### Edition 143 posture (summary)

- Doctrine is **moral infrastructure**— not mere regulation.
- History is abstract civics— not operational corridor archaeology.
- Cadets learn **prospective** Fleet memory (Founders, relays, pioneers).

## 2. Pre-Incident Fleet Assumptions

Fleet doctrine (Edition 143 era) assumes:

| Assumption | Operational expression |
|------------|------------------------|
| Exploration is stabilizing | Patrol mandates; survey prioritization |
| Frontier is open | Expansion lanes; provisional notation |
| First contact is Fleet-initiated or Fleet-managed | Handshake protocols; UNKNOWN CONTACT templates |
| Unknown space is empty until surveyed | *Absence* on charts = absence in fact |
| Patrols gather data for expansion and safety | Reconnaissance → planning pipelines |
| Doctrine can classify most encounters | Taxonomy before testimony |

*Prologue and epilogue include Required Reading bookend lines.*

**Fleet Survey Manual (Edition 143), §7.2 — archived as `FSA-143-07`:**  
*Regions beyond established survey boundaries should be treated as unoccupied unless direct evidence indicates otherwise.*  
See `Books/Book_01_The_Kestrel_Veil_Incident/Archive/archive_01_fleet_survey_manual_edition_143.md`.

**Cultural corollary:** *Someone has to go first.*

## 3. Frontier Doctrine Language

Common terms (pre-Edition 144):

| Term | Typical use |
|------|-------------|
| **patrol** | Routine sovereign presence |
| **survey** | Charting provisional volume |
| **contact** | Detected vessel/platform/event |
| **frontier** | Outward-moving edge of Fleet concern |
| **neutral zone** | Buffer; pause between ambitions |
| **reconnaissance** | Scout mission class |
| **first contact** | Managed handshake phase |
| **exploratory authority** | Legal/mission permission to proceed |
| **rules of engagement** | Engagement thresholds by mission tier |

**Template header (First Contact Bureau):**  
*UNKNOWN CONTACT — INITIAL CLASSIFICATION*
