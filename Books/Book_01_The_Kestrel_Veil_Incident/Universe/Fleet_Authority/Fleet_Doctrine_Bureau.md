# Fleet Doctrine Bureau
## Doctrine Formulation — Unified Fleet Authority

**Companion:** `Fleet_Doctrine_Manual.md` (living excerpts)

---

## 1. Function

The Fleet Doctrine Bureau writes, maintains, and distributes the manuals Fleet lives by— ROE templates, first-contact protocols, patrol classifications, academy primers (Edition 143 and successors).

It translates institutional belief into **usable boxes**:

- UNKNOWN CONTACT — INITIAL CLASSIFICATION
- exploratory authority
- rules of engagement tiers
- frontier provisional notation standards

When reality no longer fits the boxes, the Bureau either revises editions or becomes the bottleneck.

---

## 2. Cultural Personality

- **Taxonomic.** Believes clarity prevents casualties.
- **Conservative.** Edition changes are political events; wording is negotiated.
- **Procedural over narrative.** Prefers tables to testimony.
- **Earnest.** Not cynical— genuinely believes doctrine saves lives when applied early.
- **Unprepared for peer civilizations.** Built for primitives, anomalies, and Fleet-managed contact.

---

## 3. Act IV Relevance

The Bureau faces Edition 143's failure in public.

Thessaly's report bypasses their headers. Act IV forces:

- Emergency working groups on *non-declared civilization contact*
- ROE debate (Ch. 18 roadmap)
- **First Doctrine adopted** (Ch. 21); Edition 144 primer **documents and teaches** the framework (epilogue)

---

## 4. Likely Assumptions

- First contact is a **phase** Fleet initiates with handshake protocols.
- Unknown space is **empty until surveyed**— provisional notation is temporary.
- Patrol data supports **expansion and safety**, not civilizational redefinition.
- Terms like *enemy*, *hostile*, *border* remain serviceable with minor revision.
- Passive intelligence supplements contact; it does not replace it.

---

## 5. Likely Friction

- Demanding headers Thessaly refused (*enemy*, *opposing force*).
- Misreading *first contact has not occurred* as crew error or cowardice.
- Treating *Limitations of Fleet Terminology* subsection as insubordination.
- Rushing Edition 144 language for political consumption before observation review.
- Colliding with Exploration Corps optimism (*someone has to go first*).
