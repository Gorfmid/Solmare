# Fleet Historical Office
## Institutional Memory — Unified Fleet Authority

---

## 1. Function

The Fleet Historical Office preserves Fleet's story of itself— Founders' Week archives, Relay Charter displays, campaign ribbons, academy dioramas, commemorative plaques.

It is **not** battlefield intelligence. It curates narrative continuity:

- Why Founders' Week matters
- How the Relay Charter became prosperity theology
- What Fleet chooses to remember about expansion

Historians serve political comfort and genuine inheritance alike.

---

## 2. Cultural Personality

- **Prospective memory.** Fleet history emphasizes **beginnings**— first relays, first colonies, living pioneers.
- **Ceremonial precision.** Dates, editions, parade orders, glass cases.
- **Less comfortable with deep time.** Prefers Founders' Week to millennia-scale corridor archaeology.
- **Professional archivists.** Not propagandists— but selection is curation.

Contrast Dominion Transportation archives (layered handwritten margins). Fleet Historical Office is brighter, newer, forward-facing.

---

## 3. Act IV Relevance

The *Kestrel* report implies **someone else's history was already in the volume Fleet called empty**.

Historical Office must reconcile:

- Frontier maps with lane antiquity predating Fleet surveys
- Founders' Week origin stories with *frontier was never empty*
- Whether to revise public historical displays or sequester segment seven findings

Act IV may use Historical Office as **institutional denial** or **slow honest revision**— not comic relief.

---

## 4. Likely Assumptions

- Fleet expansion is the primary civilizing force in surveyed space.
- Neutral Zone history is **buffer**, not shared inheritance.
- Old infrastructure beyond the border is **unclaimed** until Fleet dates it.
- Founders' Week is about Fleet origins, not maintenance schedules on foreign lanes.

---

## 5. Likely Friction

- Reframing *exploration* narratives as **late arrival**.
- Calder's *we found neighbors* vs. *someone has to go first* academy culture.
- Pressure to bury uncomfortable timeline evidence to protect Founders' Week morale.
- Treating Dominion-age layering as "anomaly" rather than precedence.
