# Fleet Academy
## Officer Formation — Unified Fleet Authority

**Doctrine primer:** Edition 143 (`Chapters/prologue.md`)  
**Living manual:** `Fleet_Doctrine_Manual.md`

---

## 1. Function

Fleet Administrative Academy (Helion Prime and satellite houses) commissions officers— simulation culture, Founders' Week graduation, sword ceremony, assignment posting.

Produces the **grammar** cadets carry to first command:

- First contact as curriculum event
- Exploration ethics case studies
- *Someone has to go first* (cultural touchstone)
- Strategic Studies primer — Solmare Principle (five lines)

Calder is Founders' Week cohort; Thessaly is executive-track competence; academy is Fleet's **confidence engine**.

---

## 2. Cultural Personality

- **Ceremonial + procedural.** Obligation first, meaning second, music third (Calder's graduation).
- **Optimistic frontier mindset.** Unknown space as opportunity surface.
- **Simulation-forward.** Bridges teach command before hulls do.
- **Respectful restraint symbolism.** Commencement swords— dulled edges, balance true.
- **Young.** Fleet itself is young; academy encodes that youth as virtue.

---

## 3. Act IV Relevance

Academy must eventually teach what Calder learned in segment seven:

- Civilizations may already exist where charts show *provisional*
- Listening may precede handshake
- *Neighbors* may be more accurate than *unknown contact*

Edition 143 primer is about to feel incomplete. Act IV may show academy instructors arguing over Edition 144 drafts while Rowan briefs.

## 4. Likely Assumptions

- Graduates are prepared for **first arrival** scenarios.
- First contact protocols apply to most frontier encounters.
- Founders' Week celebrates **origin**, not maintenance of foreign corridors.
- Scout-class is stepping stone, not destiny hull (pre-*Kestrel* Calder view).
