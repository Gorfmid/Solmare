# CHAPTER LENGTH PRINCIPLE (DYNAMIC MODEL)

**Authority:** Non-binding guidance for all Book One chapters. Overrides fixed word-count targets in older generation directives when they conflict.

**Companion docs:** `pacing_rules.md`, `revision_philosophy.md`, `solmare_craft_doctrine.md`, `chapter_generator_spec.md`

---

## Core rule

Chapters do **not** have fixed word-count targets.

**Guideline:** approximately **4,000 words** when material supports it (`solmare_craft_doctrine.md`).

**Typical band:** ~4,000–6,000 words when the material supports it.

Length follows **narrative function**, not quota.

---

## What each chapter must fully support

Each chapter expands naturally to complete:

1. **Setup and grounding** — environment and characters
2. **Progression** — core scene objective
3. **Resolution** — emotional or perceptual shift that closes the chapter's primary beat

---

## Guidelines

| Situation | Length |
|-----------|--------|
| No meaningful state change | May be **short** — do not pad |
| Escalation, discovery, or system transition | May **extend significantly** |
| High-intensity or contact-driven chapters | May exceed typical band **substantially** |
| Tension, perception, or decision moments | **Never compress** to meet artificial limits |

---

## Priority order

1. Narrative clarity and emotional readability  
2. Structural coherence of events  
3. Character-driven pacing  
4. Word count (**non-binding**)

---

## Hard rules

- If a chapter naturally **exceeds** previous length norms → **do not compress**
- If a chapter naturally **resolves early** → **do not pad**

---

## Book One precedent (rewrite, Ch. 1–5)

Actual prose counts (headers excluded) at time of principle adoption:

| Ch | Title | Words | Note |
|----|-------|------:|------|
| 1 | The Empire | ~3,965 | World introduction |
| 2 | A Captain's Dream | ~4,857 | Character establishment |
| 3 | The Ship That Refuses to Die | ~5,335 | Ensemble / ship |
| 4 | Routine Patrol | ~3,163 | Quiet operational; resolves early |
| 5 | Mission's End | ~10,669 | Contact / escalation — **expanded by function** |

**Act I total (Ch. 1–5):** ~27,989 words.

Ch. 5 demonstrates the dynamic model: contact-driven escalation justified length well above the typical band. Ch. 4 demonstrates the lower bound when the chapter's job is setup and unease, not climax.

---

## Generation checklist (replace fixed length targets)

- [ ] Environment and characters grounded before escalation  
- [ ] Primary objective progresses to a clear shift  
- [ ] Chapter ends on perceptual or emotional resolution — not word count  
- [ ] No padding if the shift is complete  
- [ ] No compression of high-stakes beats if the shift is not complete
