# Solmare Chapter Editing Pass

**Trigger phrase:** *Apply editing pass to Chapter X*

**Canonical template for prose polish on locked manuscript chapters.**

**For Act III+ hard checklist (colon tags, *file* density, tea/coffee clocks, Claude 4.6):** see `chapter_final_edit_pass.md` — trigger *Final edit pass on Chapter X*.

---

## Scope

This is **not** a rewrite.

Do **not** change plot, pacing, scene order, reveals, or character arcs.

Only improve prose where it is objectively weaker than the surrounding manuscript.

Match the mature editorial style established in late Act III and locked Act IV chapters.

---

## Core Standard

**Trust the reader.**

Do not explain what the reader already knows.

Show institutions through routine.
Show people through work.
Let observations carry conclusions.

Whenever possible:

**Observation → Comparison → Decision**

Avoid:

**Observation → Explanation → Theme**

---

## Observation Discipline

Maintain strict evidence discipline.

Fleet knows only what Fleet has observed.
Dominion knows only what Dominion has observed.

Neither side should accidentally possess the other's knowledge.

Avoid language that attributes intent where only behavior has been observed.

**Use observation language:**

- observed
- recorded
- compared
- recurring
- repeated
- consistent
- low confidence
- unresolved
- pending classification

**Avoid unless POV can genuinely know:**

- maintenance
- refurbishment
- enemy
- hostile
- holiday (as Dominion intent)
- civilian intent
- Dominion doctrine
- Dominion motivations

## Segment Seven Terminology

**Segment seven is a place, not a ship or an adversary.**

It is a charted frontier segment in the Neutral Zone—a patrol/reconnaissance **volume**, not a hull, contact, or political entity.

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Segment seven** | Geographic segment; mission designation for reconnaissance in that volume |
| **Corridor seven-alpha** | Named corridor inside the segment |
| **The Incident** | Kestran Spiral—cloak failure, discharge, withdrawal (**separate** from the crossing) |
| **Segment seven crossing** | The *Veil*'s passive reconnaissance transit through that volume |

**Incident vs. crossing must stay distinct.** Rowan's line: *Not the Incident. The crossing.*

### Correct usage

- *segment seven reconnaissance* — mission in that volume
- *segment seven crossing* — geographic transit
- *during segment seven* / *during the crossing* — time operating in the volume
- *segment seven volumes* — bound archive of the reconnaissance
- *segment seven close* — end of reconnaissance in the segment
- *occupancy in the segment* — what witnesses observed there

### Wrong usage — segment seven as actor

Do not write as if segment seven can act, emit, or be illuminated like a target:

| Wrong | Prefer |
|-------|--------|
| Segment seven did not shoot back | Passive archive. No return fire. |
| Segment seven showed no upstream emissions | The crossing record showed no upstream emissions |
| illuminate segment seven | illuminate during the crossing |
| Segment seven charts—incomplete | Charts for that segment were incomplete |
| unknown for segment seven occupancy | unknown for occupancy in the segment |
| Segment seven chain intact | Reconnaissance archive chain intact |

When testimony or narration needs a subject, use **the crossing**, **the record**, **the archive**, **the scout registry**, or **occupancy in the segment**—not segment seven itself.

## Record Medium (Ink vs. Custody)

Fleet is spacefaring. **Certified records live in custody chains, checksums, archive bands, and sealed slates**—not ink or paper as final authority.

### Paper and hardcopy are appropriate for

- working markup, redlines, historian pencil
- printed reference strips (harmonics, correlation)
- handwritten strip notes beside a slate (Park, Ortega)
- Historical Office comparison artifacts (charters, maps, petitions, epoch folders)
- Doctrine template drafts, whiteboards, briefing binders in working rooms

### Avoid implying ink/paper is the final or authoritative record

| Weak | Prefer |
|------|--------|
| Fleet trusted ink when history was at stake | the certified record lived in the chain |
| paper records beside slates (as authority) | hardcopy annexes for witness reference; certified record in the chain |
| truth on paper | truth in the record |
| paper was already moving | packets were already routing |
| bound volumes (on archive cart / custody) | custody modules · archive cartridges |
| ink signatures became archive record | authentications logged · seal band closed |
| watch ink dry to be first | watch the seal complete |

**Do not strip** physical texture from Historical Office, Doctrine working rooms, or witness markup. **Do strip** the idea that Fleet certifies history in ink when the institution runs on digital custody.

## Meetings and Dialogue

Avoid the "everyone gets one turn" meeting structure.

Do not introduce every participant at the start of a scene.

Readers already know the core crew and recurring institutional officers.

Let conversations develop naturally. Interruptions and silence are acceptable. Not every character needs dialogue.

**Never use screenplay formatting:**

```
Character:
"Dialogue."
```

Use normal novel dialogue.

---

## Institutional Language

Fleet reports should sound like reports.

Avoid invented bureaucratic jargon that obscures meaning.

Prefer concrete, visualizable terms:

- archive
- packet
- report
- routing
- queue
- custody
- appendix
- file
- review
- validation

Over abstract stacks such as *monitoring layer*, *custody stream* unless already established.

---

## Technical Language

Avoid stacking multiple undefined technical nouns in one sentence.

If a sentence contains overlay, lane, return, packet, drift, and header together, simplify or ensure each term is already established.

Readers should understand what professionals are doing even if they do not understand every technical detail.

---

## Propulsion Continuity

The *Kestrel Veil*:

- operates on **degraded auxiliary propulsion**
- main engines remain offline
- movement is slow and carefully managed
- burns are conservative
- every maneuver has engineering cost

Do not imply normal cruise capability, unrestricted maneuverability, or effortless travel.

Use **correction burn** / **correction thrust** only for small course adjustments unless context already establishes degraded cruise.

## Avoid Repeated Habits

Reduce unnecessary repetition of:

- nodded once
- almost smiled
- quietly
- without looking up
- without ceremony
- not because
- for a moment
- held / holding
- nominal
- within tolerance

Reduce **drift / tolerance brush-offs** in witness testimony and institutional narration—*drift inside tolerance*, *handshake drift inside tolerance*, *within tolerance, not manipulation*—when they reassure without answering the question. Keep drift language only when it names specific work (timestamp mismatch closed, relay drift tolerance on appendix recompute, blind legs from handshake lag).

| Weak | Prefer |
|------|--------|
| Reing 3 handshake lag—drift inside tolerance. Observed. Doesn't explain… | Handshake lag forced blind legs. That doesn't explain routing antiquity. Park's checksums are clean. |
| resolved handshake drift inside tolerance without drama | closed a three-millisecond timestamp mismatch on the chain |

Keep them only where they genuinely strengthen the scene.

Reduce unnecessary em dashes. Prefer periods, commas, colons, or clean sentence breaks where readability improves.

## Plain Language

Avoid clever or compressed phrasing that sounds literary but reads unclear on first pass.

**Watch for:**
- Objects or rooms given odd personification (*a table that had seen worse Tuesdays*)
- Metaphors that require insider knowledge to parse
- Aphorisms dressed as scene detail
- Abstract nouns where a concrete verb would work

**Prefer normal professional words:**

| Weak / unclear | Stronger |
|----------------|----------|
| table that had seen worse Tuesdays | table scarred by years of use |
| before Mercer's coffee cooled | before Ahn opened the fork protocol slide |
| coffee had gone cold while they routed | all three stamps were down before Mercer gathered his notes |
| felt the projection lie back at her | the overlay contradicted the epoch band |
| Performance was how institutions lied | Large rooms turned testimony into theater |

**Avoid beverage temperature as time passing.** Do not use coffee/tea cooling, going cold, or still warm from the printer as the primary way to show elapsed time or institutional fatigue. Characters may drink on scene; do not use drink state as the clock. Prefer: slides opened, stamps applied, printouts stacked, shift change, queue depth, certification steps completed.

**Cultural beverages (series canon):** Dominion institutions and civilian life default to **tea**; Fleet institutions default to **coffee**. Use each in its civilization — see `Universe/cultural_touchstones.md` → § Cultural beverage distinction. The temperature rule does not remove tea or coffee; it removes drink temperature as a narrative clock.

Read each sentence aloud. If it sounds like a quote from the author, reword it.

## Procedural vs. Thematic Lines

**The idea:** A line can be *on-theme* and still feel wrong if it restates the book's thesis where a professional would state the **work**.

Recurring themes—observation before doctrine, custody before conclusion, new words—are assets. They are not the only acceptable way to say what a scene means. When a sentence sounds like a chapter motto instead of something Rowan, Thessaly, or a department officer would actually say, **say it differently**: name what the meeting, certification, or testimony is *for*, in plain institutional language.

**Ask:**

- What is this person doing right now?
- What would they call it in the room—not in the author's outline?
- Has this theme already landed nearby? If yes, the next beat can be factual.

**Common failure modes:**

| Problem | Fix the idea |
|---------|----------------|
| Theme phrase where purpose belongs | State procedural purpose (*preserve the record accurately*, *certify witness testimony*) |
| Clever cap after two facts (*Fleet used sentences.*) | End on fact or action; let dialogue carry the next beat |
| Section/chapter title echoed with chapter numbers (*Chapter Eighteen's new words*) | Echo in-universe (*the new words*, *yesterday's intake*)—never manuscript structure |

**Do not** treat any single replacement line as canonical. The goal is the right *register*: procedural when the scene is procedural, thematic when the scene earns it.

Chapter titles and section headers are for the reader. Characters live inside the institution, not the table of contents.

## Concrete Scene Setup

State what people are **doing**, **where**, and **why** — in normal professional language.

This is the preferred Solmare institutional voice: plain, specific, and logically consistent.

**Requirements:**

1. **Name the work, not an abstract label**
   - Not: *failing a taxonomy*
   - Yes: *striking categories that would not fit segment seven*

2. **Scene logic must hold**
   - If a character is invited to a meeting, they are present or the text explains why not.
   - If they are elsewhere, say where and what they are doing there.
   - Opening beats must match what the chapter shows later.

3. **Prefer facts over clever contrast**
   - Not: *she would arrive when she had times, not opinions*
   - Yes: *she would join when she had facts to state, not labels to borrow*

4. **Use institutional locations readers can picture**
   - intelligence alcove, Classification Annex, validation bay, Map Room Four
   - reading the raw archive, striking failed labels, routing an overlay

**Gold-standard rewrite (Chapter 18 opening):**

| Weak | Preferred |
|------|-----------|
| Sato had been invited and had chosen to read archives first; she would arrive when she had times, not opinions. Mercer was downstairs failing a taxonomy. | Sato was in the intelligence alcove, reading the raw archive. She would join Rowan's synthesis when she had facts to state, not labels to borrow. Mercer was not in the room yet. He was downstairs in the Classification Annex, striking categories that would not fit segment seven. |

When revising institutional openings, ask:
- Can the reader picture the room and the work?
- Does this sentence contradict a later scene?
- Would a professional actually say or do this?

## Reduce Deception / Lying Metaphor Cluster

The manuscript can overuse variations of **lie, lied, lying, pretend, performance, false, deception** as narrator commentary on institutions, data, charts, or history.

**One instance in a scene can work.** A cluster across a chapter starts to sound thematic and repetitive.

**Reduce in narrator prose and repeated author-voice lines.**

**Keep in dialogue when:**
- A professional states a concrete institutional risk (*the briefing would be wrong*)
- The word is tied to a specific action, not a general thesis about Fleet

**Replace narrator/theme clusters with concrete behavior:**

| Overused pattern | Prefer |
|------------------|--------|
| institutions lied when facts were still moving | large rooms turned testimony into theater |
| felt the projection lie back at her | the overlay contradicted the shading |
| hearing words prevented lying | hearing words aloud caught false categories |
| we lie in briefings / lie in archives | the briefing would be wrong / the archive would be incomplete |
| elegant lie | tidy label / comfortable category |

Do not strip all moral precision from the chapter. Remove **repetition**, not **judgment**.

## Global Manuscript Style Pass

Run across chapters when recurring author habits become noticeable. **Not a rewrite.** Preserve story, pacing, characterization, and institutional voice.

### 1. Tuesday References

Search for unintended weekday motifs: *worse Tuesdays*, *ordinary Tuesday*, *Tuesday traffic*, *Tuesday morning*, *Tuesday behavior*, *Tuesday continues*, etc.

**Replace or remove** unless the specific weekday is genuinely important (e.g., a Dominion IT patch window may become *scheduled patch window*).

**Prefer:** ordinary workday · routine shift · normal duty cycle · standard work period · weekday operations · routine traffic · traffic continues

### 2. "Lied" Metaphors (Manuscript-Wide)

Search for *lie / lied / lying / lies* applied to data, history, institutions, reports, rooms, charts, records, numbers, terminology.

**Keep** in dialogue when characters speak plainly (*You're lying*, *Panel's lying again*, *Feed's not lying*).

**Replace narrator/institutional clusters** with specific language:

| Overused | Prefer |
|----------|--------|
| institutions lied | testimony became theater / briefing would be wrong |
| data lied | data overstated / contradicted returns |
| slates lied | slate color scales drifted / slates updated smoothly |
| projection lie back | overlay contradicted the band |
| lie in briefings | brief from old slides would be wrong |

### 3. "Grammar" Metaphor Drift

Search for *grammar*, *obligation grammar*, *witness grammar*, *passive grammar*, *report grammar*, *Fleet grammar*.

Replace with **department-specific** vocabulary:

| Department | Prefer |
|------------|--------|
| Doctrine | classification · template · terminology · category · doctrine language |
| Communications | custody · record · chain · authentication |
| Historical | narrative · edition · archival language |
| Cartography | map language · confidence bands · epoch terminology |
| Witness testimony | witness discipline · band notation · observed/inferred bands |

### 4. "Not Yet Earned" Constructions

Search for *confidence/certainty/authority/vocabulary/labels not yet earned*, *performing confidence it had not yet earned*.

**Prefer:** premature certainty · unsupported confidence · conclusions ahead of evidence · assumptions beyond the data · classifications unsupported by observation

### 5. "[Department] Saw..." Summaries

Search for *Communications saw evidence*, *Cartography saw geography*, *Doctrine saw categories*, *Historical saw history*.

**Replace explanation with action.** Let departments demonstrate philosophy through work.

### 6. Abstract Authorial Aphorisms

Search for universal-truth sentences: *Large rooms invited performance*, *Timestamps were how civilizations...*, *Performance was how institutions...*, *coffee was how executives stayed human*.

**Keep only the strongest.** Replace weaker instances with concrete observation or cut.

### 7. Ink and Paper as Final Record

Search for *trusted ink*, *truth on paper*, *ink signatures became*, *watch ink dry*, *paper records beside slates* (as authority), *bound volumes* on custody carts, *paper was already moving* (routing).

**Keep** paper/ink where it is clearly **working reference**—Historical comparison sheets, Doctrine binders, Park's strip notes, Cartography redlines.

**Replace** language that elevates ink or paper to certified custody. Final record = chain, checksum, archive band, keyed seal.

See **Record Medium (Ink vs. Custody)** section above.

### 8. Procedural vs. Thematic Default

Search for lines that restate a **book theme** where **institutional purpose** would read more naturally—especially openings, witness beats, and officer statements.

**The test:** Would this person say the thesis, or would they say what the work is for? If it sounds like a motto, rewrite in plain procedural terms. Vary phrasing; no single replacement line is canonical.

**Also search** for manuscript meta in prose (*Chapter Eighteen*, *last chapter*). Replace with in-universe time or work references.

See **Procedural vs. Thematic Lines** section above.

### 9. Listen Watch / Civilian Signature Clarity

Search for *named individuals*, *not observed faces*, *not faces*, and unclear civilian-signature testimony.

**Prefer:** traffic acoustics (observed) vs. individual operator patterns (not observed). Civilian life = inference, flagged in appendix.

See **Listen Watch / Civilian Signature Bands** section above.

## Witness Testimony Rhythm

In briefing, debrief, and witness scenes, evidence discipline does not require a report label on every sentence.

**Use Observed / Inferred / Speculation labels only when:**
- Rowan or Thessaly asks for a band
- A line crosses from observation into inference and must be corrected
- The executive summary or ledger format is being established

**Prefer natural professional speech:**

| Mechanical | Natural |
|------------|---------|
| Observed: authentication density increased. Observed: no pursuit signature. | Authentication density increased without search-pattern spikes. On egress, no pursuit signature. |
| Inferred: border-adjacent discipline increasing. | Border-adjacent discipline increasing. Inference. Authentication density increased without search-pattern spikes. |

Let Thessaly or Rowan correct the record when Calder slips category. Trust the reader to know testimony stays in the Observed section until someone moves it.

Avoid checklist dialogue where every sentence opens with the same prefix.

## Listen Watch / Civilian Signature Bands

Passive reconnaissance hears **aggregate corridor behavior**, not identifiable people. This distinction recurs in Calder, Ortega, and Thessaly testimony. State it in plain language readers can follow.

### The idea

| Band | What passive listen watch actually gives |
|------|----------------------------------------|
| **Observed** | **Traffic acoustics** — cluster spacing, density peaks, baseline routing, authentication curves |
| **Inferred** | Civilian life, commute windows, "somewhere people call home," occupancy beyond Fleet survey assumptions |
| **Not observed** | **Individual operator patterns** — no faces, names, or single-ship identities resolved from harmonics |

Fleet did not illuminate. They did not handshake. They logged **how busy the corridor sounded**, not **who** was in it.

### Preferred phrasing

Use **traffic acoustics** for the observed layer. Use **not individual operator patterns** (or *traffic acoustics only* on correction) for the limit of observation.

| Unclear | Clearer |
|---------|---------|
| We did not observe named individuals | Not individual operator patterns |
| Not observed faces | Not individual operator patterns / Traffic acoustics only |
| Listen watch harmonics: recurring cluster spacing… | Traffic acoustics—recurring cluster spacing… |
| Routine traffic by flow, not faces | Routine traffic acoustics by flow—not individual operator patterns |

**Thessaly correction pattern:** *Ordinary civilian life is inference from traffic acoustics—flagged in the appendix. Not individual operator patterns.*

Keep technical detail (cluster spacing, density peaks, same-day baseline) where it belongs. The reader needs the **frame** first: acoustics yes, individuals no.

## Scene Endings

Do not end scenes by explaining their meaning.

End with work continuing:

- file submitted
- watch changed
- report routed
- ship underway
- tool returned
- archive updated
- comparison continuing

The world should continue after the reader leaves the scene.

---

## After Editing — Report

Provide:

1. Continuity issues found.
2. Technical language simplified.
3. Repeated phrasing reduced.
4. Observation-discipline violations corrected.
5. Listen watch / civilian signature bands stated clearly (traffic acoustics, not individual operator patterns).
6. Segment seven terminology checked (place/volume, not actor or ship).
7. Record medium checked (custody chain vs. working hardcopy).
8. Character voice issues corrected.
9. Plain-language fixes (unclear metaphors reworded).
10. Deception/lying metaphor cluster reduced.
11. Concrete scene-setup fixes (who is where, doing what, in plain terms).
12. Procedural-vs-thematic lines checked (work stated plainly when a theme phrase feels like a motto; no chapter-number meta).
13. Global style tics reduced (Tuesday motif, grammar drift, ink-as-final-record, "not yet earned," department-saw summaries, aphorisms).
14. Sections intentionally left unchanged because already stronger than proposed edits.
