# SOLMARE CRAFT DOCTRINE — BOOK ONE

**Authority:** Load and obey this document for all Book One generation, refinement, and revision. Complements `global_writing_principles.md`; where both apply, both apply.

**Companion docs:** `dialogue_rules.md`, `pacing_rules.md`, `chapter_length_principle.md`, `revision_philosophy.md`

---

## Information Flow

The reader should almost never know more than the POV character.

Discovery should occur naturally through:

- observation
- professional discussion
- procedures
- decisions

**Avoid author exposition.**

If information must be explained, it should be explained because a **character genuinely needs to understand it** — not because the reader needs a briefing.

---

## Professional Competence

Every named character should be competent at their own job.

Conflict should arise from:

- incomplete information
- limited resources
- conflicting priorities
- uncertainty
- time pressure

—not from unnecessary incompetence.

Professionals may disagree.

Professionals may be wrong.

They should rarely be careless.

---

## Dialogue

Dialogue should sound like **experienced professionals**.

- Avoid exposition disguised as conversation.
- Characters should not explain things to one another that they already know.
- Favor **subtext** over speeches.
- People under stress often communicate with **fewer words**, not more.

See also: `dialogue_rules.md`, `global_writing_principles.md` → *Authentic Voice and Characterization*

---

## Chapter Pacing

**Target chapter length is approximately 4,000 words.**

This is a **guideline, not a rule**.

A chapter should end because its **narrative objective has been completed** — not because a word count has been reached.

If a scene naturally requires more space, allow it.

- **Never pad** a chapter.
- **Never rush** a chapter simply to reach a target length.

See also: `chapter_length_principle.md` (dynamic model; Ch. 5 precedent for functional extension)

## Recurring Characters

No recurring character exists solely to deliver information.

Every recurring character should have:

- professional competence
- personal habits
- opinions
- ongoing relationships
- responsibilities beyond the current scene

Characters should continue to feel like they have lives even when they are off-page.

---

## Character Priority

Whenever choosing between explaining a system and showing a character interacting with that system, **always choose the character**.

Technology exists to support the story.

**Characters are the story.**

---

## Scene Test

Every significant scene should contain at least one **meaningful character moment**.

Ask:

> *If the spaceship disappeared, would this still be an interesting scene about people?*

If the answer is no, strengthen the character interaction before adding additional technical detail.

### Civilization scene test (Fleet & Dominion)

Before any Fleet or Dominion scene, also ask:

> *What piece of this civilization will the reader understand better after this scene?*

The answer should **never** be technology alone. Target family, tradition, leadership, logistics, education, history, work, food, holidays, sacrifice, humor, bureaucracy, friendship, or duty.

**Every chapter** should leave the reader understanding one new aspect of one civilization.

Full doctrine: `Writing_System/Series_Principles/cultural_identity_and_philosophical_foundation.md` · `Universe/cultural_touchstones.md`

## The Ship

The *Kestrel Veil* is a character.

She should possess a recognizable personality through:

- sounds
- age
- imperfections
- recurring maintenance
- the crew's familiarity with her

The crew rarely speaks sentimentally about the ship.

Instead, their affection is revealed through **routine, habit, trust, and quiet concern**.

---

## The Solmare Rule

Readers should remember:

1. **People** first
2. **Ships** second
3. **Technology** third

The novel succeeds when readers care more about what Calder decides than what the sensor array detects.

## Generation Checklist (Quick)

- [ ] POV-limited discovery — no author briefing
- [ ] Conflict from pressure and uncertainty — not careless crew
- [ ] Dialogue: subtext, brevity under stress, no mutual exposition
- [ ] Chapter ends on objective complete — not word count
- [ ] Recurring characters have off-page life and professional identity
- [ ] Systems shown through character interaction
- [ ] Scene passes the spaceship-disappeared test
- [ ] Fleet/Dominion scenes pass civilization scene test (not technology alone)
- [ ] *Veil* personality through habit and maintenance — not sentiment speeches
