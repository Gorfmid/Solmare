# ACT IV INSTITUTIONAL PROSE — DIRECT VOICE

**Scope:** Chapters 17–24 (Act IV), especially Fleet institutional scenes — Operations, Communications, Historical Office, Doctrine, Cartography, custody routing, strategic reading.

**Authority:** Load for Act IV generation and revision. Complements `prose_style.md`, `fleet_systems_lexicon.md`, and `global_writing_principles.md`.

---

## Core Principle

State action. Use institutional vocabulary. Land judgment once. Stop.

Meaning comes from what gets **filed, routed, refused, or read** — not from narration explaining what the moment meant.

---

## Gold Standard (Chapter 17)

> Rowan did not pace. He read the executive summary to the end: observation-first, restraint documented, departure clean by Fleet metrics. The recommendation refused *enemy* without refusing seriousness.

**Why it works:**
- One physical beat (did not pace; read to the end)
- Report language listed plainly (observation-first, restraint documented…)
- One institutional judgment in Fleet terms (*enemy* refused, seriousness retained)
- No metaphor, aphorism, or undefined jargon

---

## Write This Way

| Do | Example |
|----|---------|
| One action per beat | He filed it. Incident window closed. |
| Vocabulary from the institution | routing, custody, segregated, executive synthesis, classification, recommendation |
| List what a document says | restraint documented, departure clean by Fleet metrics |
| Land one clean judgment | refused *enemy* without refusing seriousness |
| Separate packages by office/department | raw archive to Historical Office; department comments stay on the packet |

---

## Avoid

| Don't | Why | Instead |
|-------|-----|---------|
| Undefined jargon | "summon the room" | Cut, or name the action: *He did not stop the floor.* |
| Technical ornament in wrong POV | "archived in triple redundancy" in Rowan's read | Park's chain already established; omit or say *custody confirmed in attachments* |
| Aphorisms / clever asides | "Quiet satisfaction is allowed" | Cut or replace with procedural action |
| Abstract wrap-up sentences | "without reducing operational significance" | Name the institutional choice: *refused enemy without refusing seriousness* |
| Staccato verification stacks | "Fourteen returns. Same bearing. Same profile." | Establish once; light callbacks only |
| Legacy merge language | combine lane, combined bundle, upstream combine | segregated, department review independent, executive synthesis pending |
| Philosophy in system voice | "void did not change when you looked" | repeated-pass logs, sweep files, routing entries |
| Analogies unrelated to systems | traffic lane metaphors for career moves | capital routing active; fringe relay under sweep |

## Institutional Routing Model (Act IV)

When writing custody, Communications, or ingest scenes:

- Departments receive **separate data packages**
- Custody chains remain **independent**
- Department comments stay **attached to originating departments**
- Fleet delays **executive synthesis** until review completes

The issue is **premature interpretation**, not accidental data merging.

**Preferred terms:** segregated · custody separation · pending departmental review · executive synthesis · routing comments unresolved · raw archive

---

## Revision Filter

Before keeping a sentence in Act IV institutional prose, ask:

1. Can a reader who is not technical follow what **happened**?
2. Is every term either **plain English** or **defined institutional vocabulary**?
3. Does the sentence do **one job** (action, data state, or routing state)?
4. Would removing it lose **plot, custody logic, or character judgment**?

If no to 4 and the sentence sounds wise, clever, or technical for its own sake — cut or simplify.
