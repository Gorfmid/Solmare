# DIALOGUE RULES — BOOK ONE

**Voice and differentiation:** See `global_writing_principles.md` → *Authentic Voice and Characterization*. No two major characters should sound alike; dialogue carries subtext and personality.

**Language:** See `global_writing_principles.md` → *Language and Swearing*. Cap at *hell* / *damn* / *bastard*; prefer invented in-universe oaths where appropriate.

## CORE PRINCIPLE
Dialogue is functional, not theatrical—**within each character's distinct voice.**

## RULES
- concise communication where profession and crisis demand it
- rank-aware speech without erasing individual personality
- emotional expression calibrated to character, not flattened across the cast

## EXAMPLE
"Status?"
"Unstable."
"Define unstable."
"Unknown signal conflict."
