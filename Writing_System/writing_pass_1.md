## 0. Scope

This is **not a rewrite.**

Do **not** change plot, pacing, scene order, reveals, or character arcs unless the author explicitly unlocks that work.

Only improve prose where it is **demonstrably weaker** than the surrounding manuscript.

**Revision philosophy:**
- Subtraction is as valid as addition.
- Do not revise simply because you can.
- Preserve excellent prose, memorable dialogue, and emotionally effective scenes.
- Every paragraph should justify its existence.

**Voice target:** Professional. Restrained. Institutional. Human. Trust the reader.

## 2. How to run

1. Open the chapter file.
2. Trigger: **Writing Pass 1 on Chapter N** (or legacy final/editing pass phrasing).
3. Work **section by section**. Prefer small surgical edits over sweeping rewrites.
4. Before accepting institutional or continuity-sensitive lines, verify against **LOCKED FINAL** prior text and book canon (§15).
5. Deliver the **report** (§17).
6. When the author locks: follow the lock checklist (§18).

## 3. Core standards

### Trust the reader

Do not explain what the reader already knows.

Show institutions through routine.  
Show people through work.  
Let observations carry conclusions.

**Prefer:** Observation → Comparison → Decision  
**Avoid:** Observation → Explanation → Theme

### Priority order (when deciding what belongs)

1. Character development  
2. World development  
3. Story progression  
4. Technical details  

Technical information only when it supports 1–3.

### Information flow

The reader should almost never know more than the POV character. Discovery through observation, professional discussion, procedures, and decisions — not author briefing.

### Professional competence

Conflict from incomplete information, limited resources, conflicting priorities, uncertainty, time pressure — not unnecessary incompetence.

### Craft checks (chapter-level)

- **70/30 (crew first):** Human operational story dominates; anomaly is pressure, not protagonist (~85/15 feel from Act II forward).
- **Chapter length:** No fixed quota. ~4,000 guideline; length follows narrative function. Never pad; never compress a real beat to hit a number.
- **Dialogue:** Experienced professionals; no mutual exposition; fewer words under stress; no screenplay formatting.

## 4. Hard checklist (line polish)

### 4.1 Colon dialogue tags → comma attribution

**Never** screenplay / colon tags:

```
Halden: "Every day widens…"
Reyes, flat: "Every day adds…"
```

**Prefer:**

```
"Every day widens…," Halden said.
"Every day adds…," Reyes said, flat.
```

Content colons inside dialogue (lists/labels) may stay.  
**Search:** `Name:` / `Name, adverb:` immediately before a quote.

---

### 4.2 Beverage temperature / fill as clock

Characters may drink. Do **not** use drink temperature or fill as the narrative clock.

| Weak | Prefer |
|------|--------|
| coffee/tea gone cold while they… | stamps applied / watch turned / packet opened |
| tea too strong / coffee bitter as opener | tea on the warmer; coffee on the table. Then action. |

**Canon:** Dominion → **tea**. Fleet → **coffee**. Do not bleed across POV.  
**Search:** `cold`, `warm`, `too strong`, `bitter`, `cooled`, `gone cold`, `still warm` near tea/coffee.

---

### 4.3 Merge / separate / room geography

- Merge redundant paragraphs that reset the same room or restate the same beat.
- Separate when two jobs are crammed into one paragraph.
- Geography must hold: who is in the room, who left, who enters once. No double entrances.
- When speakers blur, use **full title + role** on key beats.

---

### 4.4 Vary *file / filed / filing*

Do not stamp every institutional action with *file*.

| Overused | Prefer |
|----------|--------|
| File exactly | Record / Log / No adjustments |
| File Standing Observation | Report Standing Observation |
| filed a note | added a note / noted / reported |

*File* as a noun for a literal dossier is fine (`opened the file`). Aim for **few** verb instances per chapter, not zero.  
**Search:** `\bfile\b`, `\bfiled\b`, `\bfiling\b`

---

### 4.5 Em dashes — prefer almost none

**Hard preference:** remove em dashes (`—`) whenever a period, comma, colon, parentheses, or a clean sentence break works.

Em dashes were a major Book One cleanup item. Default posture: **cut**. Do not use them as habitual rhythm punctuation.

| Weak (em dash habit) | Prefer |
|----------------------|--------|
| He looked up— long enough. | He looked up. Long enough. / He looked up long enough. |
| overlays— traffic density, slopes | overlays: traffic density, slopes / overlays with traffic density and slopes |
| suspension— the one logged while… | suspension, the one logged while… |
| No illumination— keep listening. | No illumination. Keep listening. |
| The corridor held— quiet, busy, ordinary. | The corridor held. Quiet. Busy. Ordinary. / The corridor held: quiet, busy, ordinary. |

**Keep an em dash only when:**
- A hard interrupt in dialogue needs it, **or**
- Cutting it clearly worsens clarity

If you are unsure, **cut it**.

**Search:** `—` (em dash). Also catch spaced hybrids like ` - ` used as fake em dashes; convert to period/comma or a real comma splice fix.

**Pass habit:** After other edits, do one dedicated em-dash sweep. Count remaining instances. Most chapters should need **very few**.

### 4.6 Plain English — clearer language, better analogies

Analogies are allowed. **Weak, showy, or foggy ones are not.**

Book One needed many rewrites because drafts reached for comparisons that sounded literary and failed on first read: forced personification, theme-metaphors, occupational poetry, and clever clocks. Writing Pass 1 should keep the good comparisons and replace the bad ones with either a **stronger analogy** or **plain professional language**.

**Rule of thumb:** The beat must be clear *before* the comparison does any stylistic work. If the reader must unpack the metaphor to understand what happened, rewrite.

#### What “better” means

A good analogy:

- Clarifies something concrete (scale, risk, habit, failure mode)
- Fits the POV’s profession and temperament
- Lands in one clean image
- Does not require insider decoding
- Does not restate a book theme as poetry

A bad analogy:

- Exists to sound smart, wise, or literary
- Personifies rooms, maps, institutions, or furniture for mood
- Stacks two comparisons in one beat
- Repeats a motif already used nearby (*lie*, *performance*, *grammar*, drink-as-clock)

#### Cut or rewrite these weak patterns

| Weak pattern | Why it fails | Prefer |
|--------------|--------------|--------|
| Rooms/institutions that “lie,” “perform,” or “invite theater” | Theme metaphor, not scene craft | Concrete institutional failure, **or** one earned comparison that names the failure mode clearly |
| Odd object personification | *a table that had seen worse Tuesdays* | Plain scar/wear detail, **or** a sharper image that still reads instantly |
| History/data as weather or bruised body | Mood without information | Process language (returns stacked, bands revised), **or** a cleaner comparison of mismatch |
| Occupational poetry | *Fleet grammar* · *witness grammar* | Department terms first; analogy only if it still parses without the jargon |
| Clever clock metaphors | coffee cooling as time | Institutional clocks (stamps, watch turn, queue depth) |
| Soft wisdom lines | *Performance was how institutions…* | Fact/action, or an analogy that earns the judgment without lecture |
| Extended simile stacks | two+ comparisons per beat | One strong image, or none |

#### Prefer clear wording (with room for a strong image)

| Weak / foggy | Better |
|--------------|--------|
| felt the projection lie back at her | the overlay contradicted the shading · *or* a sharper visual of mismatch the POV would actually think |
| Large rooms invited performance | Officers spoke for the record · *or* one precise comparison for record-theater if earned |
| timestamps were how civilizations remembered | they logged timestamps into the chain |
| Improved is how restored begins | Name the work: patched systems certified functional, not restored |
| quiet satisfaction is allowed | Cut; show the read ending |

#### Tests before keeping a figurative line

1. **Clarity first:** Does the sentence still make sense if you temporarily remove the analogy? If not, the analogy is carrying plot — rewrite.  
2. **Improve-or-cut:** If the comparison is weak, replace with a **better** analogy or with plain language. Do not keep a mediocre one for texture.  
3. **POV test:** Would this person reach for this image?  
4. **One-image test:** One comparison per beat.  
5. **Neighbor test:** Same analogy family already nearby? Keep the stronger instance only.

**Default when unsure:** plain fact/work/decision. Add analogy only when it sharpens understanding.

Also cut: author winks, theme mottos where procedural speech belongs, and stacked *without drama / without ceremony / without applause / without announcement* as style tics.

**Prefer:** concrete work, concrete orders, concrete observation — plus occasional **strong**, clear comparison when it earns its place.

#### Compressed institutional talk (plain speak)

Do not pack three departments and a thesis into one clever sentence. Break it. Name the work.

| Weak (compressed / staff poetry) | Prefer (plain) |
|----------------------------------|----------------|
| Eighteen months of contact bought Fleet Communications and Dominion Science a small pool of speakers who can… | Eighteen months was long enough for Fleet Communications and Dominion Science to train a few speakers each. Those people can… |
| keep the nouns from killing people | make sure a bad word does not start a fight |
| Who can walk. / walk-out rights | Who can end a session. / either side can still end the session |
| temporary permission wearing permanent bolts | The bolts were permanent. The permission to use them for politics was not. |
| Certainty would have felt like penance | A stronger word would have felt like making up for the mistake |
| silence that functioned as permission | silence that meant they could keep planning |
| mission-critical / not decorative | can kill people if we get it wrong / not optional |
| like the noun / inventing shared terms | like the job / building a word list |

**Test:** Can a reader get the fact on first pass without unpacking? If not, rewrite plainer — even if the idea is good.

### 4.7 Artificially profound prose

Do not make the writing sound literary by inserting poetic metaphors, philosophical observations, or clever turns of phrase that do not arise naturally from the POV character.

Avoid sentences that feel written to impress the reader.

**Combat / crisis especially:** cut thesis openings (*doctrine had already failed*, *arithmetic was the only courage left*, *pride held longer than either*, *treated them as weather*). Prefer what happened: orders, fire, damage, lock, miss, hit.

Meaning should emerge through:

- character actions  
- dialogue  
- observations  
- consequences  
- relationships  

When in doubt, choose **authenticity and clarity** over eloquence.

### 4.8 Sensor / pass jargon

Cut overuse of *second pass*, *every pass*, *survived second pass* as listen filler.  
Keep literal workshop/QC uses.  
Vary with: *would not stay off the board*, *refuses to retract*, *compare harder*, *stack the returns*.

### 4.9 Scan modes: *passive* / *active* (not a habit prefix)

Sensors have **two modes only** (submarine sonar / network listen vs ping):

| Mode | Meaning | Cost |
|------|---------|------|
| **Passive** | Receive / listen only — no illumination, no ping, no paint | Weaker, muddier picture |
| **Active** | Emit to measure — illumination, ranging, challenge, paint | Much clearer picture of **everything in the volume**, for **everyone** listening — including a clear picture of **the ship that went active** |

Going active is not a private upgrade to your own board. It is like an active sonar ping: better data for you, and better data for every other platform in range, with the pinger among the brightest returns.

Do **not** put *passive* in front of every board, plot, archive, threshold, display, array, or watch. That is a bad default tic. If the ship is already listening / dark / not illuminating, the method is already clear.

**Sweep `\bpassive\b`.** Cut the prefix unless mode contrast is the point.

| Weak (habit prefix) | Prefer |
|---------------------|--------|
| passive board / passive plot / passive display | board · plot · display · sensor board |
| passive archive comparison | archive comparison |
| passive threshold / passive sensor edge | threshold · sensor edge |
| passive watch / priority passive | watch · listen watch · priority listen |
| passive return (when already on listen) | return · mass return |

| Keep when mode is the work |
|----------------------------|
| *passive vs active* contrast (*stay dark* / *no illumination* / *do not go active*) |
| formal order that names the mode once (*passive only* · *active scan authorized*) |
| doctrine line where the mode itself is the decision (painting the volume / revealing self) |

**Rule of thumb:** Say *passive* or *active* when choosing or changing mode. Everywhere else, name the tool or the work (*board*, *return*, *listen*, *stay dark*).

Canon detail: `Universe/Technology/Technology_Bible.md` → Passive vs active.

### 4.11 Fleet POV naming limits

- Fleet POV must **not** say **Dominion** until authorized discovery beats. Prefer: traffic, their infrastructure, the sector, returns.
- Dominion POV uses native names (e.g. **K-17 spine**), never Fleet *seven-alpha* as their native label.
- Fleet must **not** “remember” breakaway / shared heritage. They can see *already there* vs empty chart. Not kinship.

Prefer: *standing orders hold* · *keep listening* · explain **why** once (floodlights alert watchers).

### 4.13 Terminology locks

| Prefer | Avoid / note |
|--------|----------------|
| **volume** (Dominion spatial / V## map headers) | Dominion POV saying **sector** for their own map units |
| **sector** (Fleet spatial / chart language) | Fleet POV saying **volume** for regions of space (audio *volume* OK) |
| **fringe / outer / approach bearing** | cardinal directions in open space (*eastern bearing*, *north of*) unless a local named frame is established |
| **work slate** | pager |
| **lattice** = structural pieces/joints | lattice = whole hull |
| **hull** / hull sections | vague “the lattice” for ship |
| Edition / survey edge / frontier provisional | mapping-as-ownership lectures |
| spines, berths, commute windows, traffic, corridor | “lanes” as default for all traffic |
| **unsurveyed frontier sector** / **Fleet's charts** | **epoch** / epoch shading — use plain chart language |
| **Edition … public map** / **marked provisional** | frontier shading / public shading |
| **make contact** / **open contact** | **handshake** — keep in formal doctrine/custody only |
| **report** / **records** | vague **annex** as substitute for report/records |

**Propulsion / Reing lock (do not conflate):**

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Main engines** / main drive | Primary propulsion (often damaged / offline) |
| **Correction thrust** | Low-power transit / helm — not the main drive |
| **Reing 1–5** | Ship **system / power rings** (spires) — distribution under load |
| **Reing 3** | Midship aux bus — flicker, scrubbers/thermal, life-support load. **Not** engines. **Not** correction thrust. |

Wrong: writing as if Reing 3 *is* the engines, or correction-thrust transit = “running on Reing 3.”  
**Search:** `Reing` near `engine` / `thrust` / `propulsion` / `drive`

**Degraded *Veil* continuity:** auxiliary / correction-thrust movement is slow and costly; do not imply normal cruise or unrestricted maneuver.

### 4.14 Semicolon crew rosters

Avoid: `Brenner on helm…; Thessaly aft…; Ortega on listen…`  
Prefer a prose sentence tied to situation.

### 4.15 Inference discipline

Fleet may infer from rhythm, timing, density, throughput, maintenance layering relative to itself.

Do not:

- Decode language/content from harmonics
- Claim absolute calendar age vs Fleet survey
- Treat yards as enemy/hostility without observed intent

Observation → comparison → decision.

---

### 4.16 Dialogue and meetings

- No “everyone gets one turn” wardroom.
- Interruptions and silence OK.
- Do not introduce every participant at scene open.
- Not every character needs dialogue.
- Never screenplay formatting.
- Calder/Thessaly trust beats: earned, brief, not speechified.

---

### 4.17 Scene endings

End on work continuing — packet opened, watch held, archive tagged, departure ordered — **not** a thematic caption.

---

### 4.18 Consistency with prior chapters and book canon

**Check before keeping or inventing:**

- Names, ranks, roles, custody owners
- Orders, mission headers, classification stamps already locked
- Timeline, location, POV knowledge limits
- Ship state, damage, propulsion, repair status
- Terminology already used in locked text

Prefer matching locked phrasing or cutting the beat over inventing a new bureaucratic label.  
When draft and canon conflict: correct draft to match **LOCKED FINAL**. Flag irresolvable conflicts in the report — do not rewrite locked history.

## 5. Observation discipline

Fleet knows only what Fleet has observed.  
Dominion knows only what Dominion has observed.  
Neither side should accidentally possess the other's knowledge.

**Use:** observed · recorded · compared · recurring · repeated · consistent · low confidence · unresolved · pending classification

**Avoid unless POV can genuinely know:** maintenance-as-intent · refurbishment-as-intent · enemy · hostile · holiday-as-Dominion-intent · civilian intent · Dominion doctrine/motivations (in Fleet POV)

## 6. Segment / corridor terminology (place ≠ actor)

**Segment seven (and survey segments) are places / sectors — not ships or adversaries.** *(Fleet chart language; Dominion equivalent: volume.)*

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| Segment seven | Geographic reconnaissance sector |
| Corridor seven-alpha | Named corridor inside that sector |
| The Incident | Cloak failure / discharge / withdrawal — **distinct** from the crossing |
| Segment seven crossing | Passive transit through that sector |

Do not write as if the segment can act, emit, or be illuminated like a target. Prefer: the crossing · the record · the archive · occupancy in the segment.

## 7. Record medium (ink vs. custody)

Certified records live in **custody chains, checksums, archive bands, sealed slates** — not ink/paper as final authority.

**Paper/hardcopy OK for:** markup, strip notes, Historical comparison artifacts, Doctrine working drafts, whiteboards.

**Avoid implying ink/paper is certified authority.** Keep physical texture; strip “Fleet certifies history in ink.”

---

## 8. Institutional language

Fleet reports should sound like reports. Prefer concrete terms: archive · packet · report · routing · queue · custody · review · validation.

Avoid invented bureaucratic fog (*monitoring layer*, *custody stream*) unless already established.

Avoid stacking multiple undefined technical nouns in one sentence.

### Act IV institutional voice (summary)

State action. Use institutional vocabulary. Land judgment once. Stop.

Meaning from what gets **filed, routed, refused, or read** — not narration explaining what the moment meant.

- Separate custody packages by department
- Independent custody chains
- Executive synthesis delayed until review completes
- Cut undefined jargon, verification staccato stacks, and philosophy in system voice

Full gold-standard: `Style_Guide/act_iv_institutional_prose.md`

## 10. Witness testimony rhythm

Use Observed / Inferred / Speculation labels only when asked for a band, when correcting a slip, or when establishing ledger format. Prefer natural professional speech. Avoid checklist dialogue where every sentence opens with the same prefix.

---

## 11. Procedural vs. thematic lines

Themes are assets. They are not the only way to say what a scene means.

When a sentence sounds like a motto instead of what the officer would say, state the **work** in plain institutional language.

| Problem | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Theme phrase where purpose belongs | State procedural purpose |
| Clever cap after two facts | End on fact or action |
| Chapter numbers / manuscript meta in prose | In-universe time or work references |

Chapter titles are for the reader. Characters live inside the institution.

---

## 12. Concrete scene setup

State what people are **doing**, **where**, and **why** in normal professional language.

1. Name the work, not an abstract label.  
2. Scene logic must hold (invited → present or explained elsewhere).  
3. Prefer facts over clever contrast.  
4. Use pictureable institutional locations.

## 13. Reduce habit clusters

### Deception / lying metaphor cluster

Reduce narrator/theme stacks of *lie / lied / lying / pretend / performance / false*. Keep in dialogue when concrete. Prefer specific behavior over institutional thesis.

Treat *lying / lies* like *wrong* and *geometry*: a bad default word. Do **not** make plots, returns, or instruments "lie" (*finished lying about size*, *even if the return lies*). Prefer: *before the mass return settled*, *even if the lock will not hold*, *even if the profile keeps shifting*.

### Repeated habits (reduce unless earned)

nodded once · almost smiled · quietly · without looking up · without ceremony · not because · for a moment · held/holding · nominal · within tolerance · drift-inside-tolerance brush-offs · Tuesday motifs · *grammar* metaphors (*Fleet grammar*) · *not yet earned* constructions · *[Department] saw…* summaries · abstract authorial aphorisms · **gloves on/off** as Dominion character tell or emotional semaphore · **meta grammar talk** (*the noun*, *the verb*, *without liking the verb*, *neither noun was large enough*, *hero noun*, *orphan noun*) · **geometry** as vague spatial filler (*inside their geometry*, *damage on their geometry*) — prefer concrete: *this close*, *return*, *leading edge*, *shape*, *profile*, *corridor*, *watch* · **wrong** as vague evaluative filler (*the return was wrong*, *wrong in the same way*, *less wrong*, *corridor went wrong*, *never learned to love*) — prefer concrete: *will not settle*, *does not match*, *inconsistent*, *unclassed* · **margin name / margin notation** for Peligar label — prefer *designation*, *comparison class*; keep engineering *structural margin* / *fuel margin* · **lag** as default for remote presence / relay / Helion participation — prefer *relay*, *remote*, *from Helion*, *delay*, *pause*

For gloves specifically: Book One already cut most of the choreography. Do not use gloves on/off/removed/put-back as Petrov/Vex/Senka/Kellin/Neve punctuation, as a "difficult numbers" tell, or as scene dressing that only signals Dominion formality. Keep gloves only when they are literal objects the plot needs.

For noun/verb meta talk: say the actual thing (*word*, *name*, *phrase*, *call*) instead of labeling parts of speech. **Never** use *soft nouns*, *soft language*, *soft term*, *clean noun*, *wrong nouns*, *finished/better/incomplete label*, or *invent words* — even in Park/lexicon scenes. Prefer *vague word*, *means two actions*, *bad word*, *category*, *classification*, *finished answer*. Exception only when someone is literally teaching grammar as the on-page subject (*noun* / *verb* as classroom terms). Keep *label* for mug/pedestal print only.

For *wrong*: do not use it as atmosphere or cute judgment. Say what failed (*shape will not settle*, *profile does not match*, *class unavailable*). Keep rare concrete uses only when the error itself is the point (*wrong header*, *wrong bin*).

For *lying / lies* on instruments and plots: same rule. Instruments do not lie; they fail to settle, refuse a lock, or keep shifting. Do **not** use *lying / honest* as capacity metaphor either (*without lying to somebody*). Keep *lie* only for people actually deceiving. (Also Revision Pass 3 §3.2c.)

For *margin* as naming jargon: do not use *margin name*, *margin notation*, *outer-margin analysts* for the Peligar designation. Prefer: *designation*, *comparison class*, *Dominion label*, *frontier analysts*. Keep *margin* only for literal engineering (*structural margin*, *fuel margin*).

For *lag* / *lag voice*: do not use as the default for remote officers, pedestal channels, or “not in the room.” Prefer *relay*, *remote*, *from Helion*, *on the channel*, *came through*. Keep *lag* / *delay* only when the actual time delay is the point of the beat (*the delay was long enough that interruption felt like rudeness*). *Pause* is fine for ordinary hesitation. Full ban detail: Revision Pass 3 §3.2i.

### Tuesday references

Replace unintended weekday motifs unless the weekday is plot-important. Prefer: ordinary workday · routine shift · standard work period.

### Grammar metaphor drift

Replace *witness grammar / Fleet grammar / obligation grammar* with department vocabulary (classification, custody, edition, confidence bands, witness discipline).

## 16. Institutional routing model (Fleet custody scenes)

- Departments receive **separate** data packages  
- Custody chains remain **independent**  
- Comments stay attached to originating departments  
- Executive synthesis waits for review  

Issue = premature interpretation, not accidental merging.  
**Preferred:** separate custody · pending departmental review · executive synthesis · raw archive
