## 0. Scope

Revision Pass makes the chapter **structurally and narratively correct** before line polish.

Priorities:

1. **People first.** Characters — their choices, voices, and relationships — before any policy or procedure.  
   *People create story.*
2. **Story second.** What changes for these people in this scene?  
   *Story carries policy.*
3. **Policy last.** Institutional mechanics, authorization language, and worldbuilding support or complicate choices. Policy is allowed; it fails when it replaces human conflict, decision, humor, fear, competence, or consequence.
4. Clear information (what is happening and why) — in plain speech.
5. Canon / continuity.
6. Cut fog; expand where expansion earns clarity or character.

**Working test:** Could you remove every policy line and still feel who these people are and what they chose? If yes, the policy level is probably doing too much work and the character level too little.

This is **not** Writing Pass 1. Em-dash sweeps, habit-cluster polish, and micro rhythm are Stage 3 unless they block comprehension now.

### 3.3l People-first rewrite playbook (common changes)

#### What the rewrite is

A people-first rewrite is **not** line polish. It rebuilds a chapter that is procedurally correct but emotionally dead.

1. State the chapter job as a **human outcome** (who decides / what changes), not a policy deliverable.
2. Cut or demote paragraphs that only re-teach spent facts or re-justify locked wording.
3. Put people in the gaps: corridor texture, private work agreements, competence noticing competence, someone arriving from real work with a practical fix.
4. One memorable concrete example per institutional idea — then move (Pass 2 — trust the reader).
5. **Plant life; pay off loss later.** Ordinary/wanted now; horror in the payoff chapter — no doom captions.
6. End on the **next useful action**, not a narrator thesis.
7. Plain-speech fail: if the author asks *what does this mean?*, rewrite now (Pass 3 clarity; Pass 4 first-hearing preflight).

**Working test:** Remove every policy line. Can you still feel who these people are and what they chose? If yes, rewrite — policy was doing the story’s job.

#### Common changes (checklist)

| Area | Change |
|------|--------|
| Structure | People first; one scene / one purpose; corridor beats between boards; practical-solver entrance; private channels stay private; side-channels only know what that job would know |
| Character | Competence before personality (never appearance); need ≠ affection; mentor asks, doesn’t rescue; careers advance instead of characters vanishing; protected cast get occasional real voice |
| Language | Cut soft-noun / *label* / invent-words / institutional-*gift* / *polite X*; *shared strip* → *shared lexicon*; no default *fringe*; name board facts (*Peligar advance*, not *pressure*/*contour*); *strategic charts* not *depth maps*; expand staff poetry; no *And*-pile closes; no theme captions after the beat |
| Plants | Quiet ordinary + practical future use; no foreshadow wink; payoff chapter owns the loss |
| Continuity | No re-teach; seed ≠ full greenlight; fix identity drift; **new names: both given name and surname unique** unless characters are directly related (search cast before inventing) |

#### How to run on Chapter N

1. Read once for **who decided what**.  
2. Cut/demote policy-only reinforcement.  
3. Plain-expand every first-hearing fail.  
4. Keep or add one lived human beat if the chapter is all rooms and boards.  
5. End on next action.  
6. Run language bans above in Pass 3 (soft-noun / name-the-thing / plain vocabulary).  
7. Report under the router’s combined report — Pass 1 items.

**Search for regressions:** `polite fiction|shared strip|prettier reason|soft target|as a gift|not a gift|strategic gift|\bfringe\b|depth maps|Shared channel only|freeze the venue|That was as close|Those were not the same`

## 5. Character building (Revision Pass priority)

Every revised scene should leave at least one character clearer than before — by behavior, not by caption.

### Ask per scene

- Whose scene is this, really?  
- What does this person want *right now*?  
- What do they refuse to pretend?  
- What small habit, joke, silence, or competence marks them?  
- Does dialogue sound like them, or like the institution explaining itself?

### Expand character through:

- **Specific resistance** (Calder hates envoy nouns; still pockets the packet)  
- **Competence under stress** (Thessaly turns politics into seating and stop rules)  
- **Dry humor / private objects** (Park and the cup — cryptic first, clear later)  
- **Ship-side honesty** (Brenner: tools, coffee urn, no diplomacy)  
- **Relationship micro-beats** (who talks whom down; who does not elaborate)

### Avoid

- Theme mottos wearing a character’s mouth  
- Everyone sounding like the same briefing officer  
- Explaining a character’s interior after the action already showed it  
- Turning every Dominion beat into a quotation contest (keep earned inherited speech; cut costume)

### Voice quick check

| Character | Revision tell |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Calder** | Better questions than answers; complains about wrong job; trusts specialists |
| **Thessaly** | Converts mess into lists; short corrections; little speechifying |
| **Park** | Dry, precise, custody-minded; humor sideways |
| **Brenner** | Rare; ship/tool reality; snide when politics wastes a hatch |
| **Dominion** | Live inside continuity; do not explain civilization to Fleet |
