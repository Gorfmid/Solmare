# Competence Before Attraction

**Status:** Locked series editorial canon  
**Linked from:** Revision Pass 1 · router report  

---

## Rule

Relationships between professionals should generally begin through **observed competence** rather than physical appearance or instant attraction.

This applies to:

- romantic interest  
- friendship  
- trust  
- rivalry  
- mentorship  
- professional respect  
- reluctant cooperation  

### Preferred progression

1. One character observes the other doing useful work.  
2. They recognize judgment, discipline, courage, precision, restraint, or skill.  
3. They begin seeking or valuing the other person’s input.  
4. Personal curiosity develops.  
5. Attraction or affection may emerge later.  

Do **not** begin important relationships with generic physical appraisal.

Avoid lines such as:

- she was pretty  
- he was handsome  
- something about her eyes  
- he noticed her body  
- she could not explain why she liked him  

unless physical appearance is specifically relevant to the POV, situation, or established character voice.

Use concrete professional observations instead:

- how someone checks a hatch  
- how they respond under pressure  
- whether they listen before speaking  
- whether they waste time  
- how they handle subordinates  
- whether they admit uncertainty  
- how they solve a technical problem  
- whether they protect people without performing heroism  
- whether their answer matches the observer’s own professional judgment  

**Competence may create interest.**  
**Interest is not yet affection.**  
**Need is not affection.**  
**Respect is not trust.**  
**Trust is not romance.**  

Do not rush those stages.

---

## Relationship revision check (every Revision Pass)

Ask:

1. What first made these characters notice one another?  
2. Is the answer competence, behavior, judgment, or shared work?  
3. Has attraction appeared before respect was earned?  
4. Is the narration explaining a relationship the actions already show?  
5. Are need, respect, trust, affection, and romance being treated as distinct stages?  
6. Has a professional interaction become flirtation too quickly?  
7. Is a relationship developing through repeated work rather than author announcement?  

**Report item:** *Relationship progression:* competence-first development preserved; attraction, trust, respect, need, and affection kept distinct; no appearance-first shorthand or premature romance.

## Current series applications (Book Two+)

### Vex and Walsh

Initial interest is **professional recognition** only.

**Vex notices:** Walsh moves directly toward useful work; thinks in security architecture; does not defer merely because of rank; her answers often match his own; she understands how systems fail and how people exploit them.

**Walsh notices:** Vex is operational rather than theatrical; sees security flaws quickly; does not waste time; accepts competent correction; professional instincts align despite national difference.

Do not reduce this to physical attraction. Do not over-caption it. Let repeated work create curiosity.

### Park and Orlen

Grows through language work, precision, correction, shared professional standards, dry humor, tea, and mutual respect for competence.

**Tea is the personal bridge. The lexicon is the foundation.** Do not make tea replace the work.

### Calder and Vex

Professional respect under protest. They recognize traits they would value in an officer from their own navy. They do not forget Alpha Seven.

Need is not forgiveness. Cooperation is not friendship. Respect may grow without either man becoming emotionally demonstrative.

### Calder and Thessaly

Built on long-earned trust, truthful disagreement, command rhythm, Thessaly correcting Calder without humiliating him, Calder trusting her judgment enough to delegate real command decisions, and both understanding the other’s limits.

Do not invent conflict merely to prove they are different. Disagreements should come from real command judgment.

---

## Over-correction guard

Do not make every professional relationship romantic. Competence-first applies to respect and trust even when romance never arrives.
