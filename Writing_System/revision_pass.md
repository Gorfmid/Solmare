# Revision Pass — Stage 2 Editorial Runbook

**Lifecycle role:** **Stage 2 — Revision Pass**  
(See `Writing_System/Pipeline/solmare_writing_lifecycle.md`.)

**Status:** Canonical author/AI revision guide for Book Two and later books  
**Trigger phrases:**  
- *Revision Pass on Chapter X*  
- *Revise Chapter X*  
- *Structural revision on Chapter X*  
- *Apply revision pass to Chapter X*

**Companion (not a substitute):** `Style_Guide/revision_philosophy.md`  
**Does not replace Stage 3:** Do **not** run full Writing Pass 1 polish here. That belongs to Final Candidate.

---

## 0. Scope

Revision Pass makes the chapter **structurally and narratively correct** before line polish.

Priorities:

1. **People first.** Characters — their choices, voices, and relationships — before any policy or procedure.
2. **Story second.** What changes for these people in this scene?
3. **Policy last.** Institutional mechanics, authorization language, and procedure are background. They serve the character decisions; they are not the subject.
4. Clear information (what is happening and why) — in plain speech.
5. Canon / continuity.
6. Cut fog; expand where expansion earns clarity or character.

**Working test:** Could you remove every policy line and still feel who these people are and what they chose? If yes, the policy level is probably doing too much work and the character level too little.

This is **not** Writing Pass 1. Em-dash sweeps, habit-cluster polish, and micro rhythm are Stage 3 unless they block comprehension now.

## 2. How to run

1. Open the chapter.  
2. Trigger: **Revision Pass on Chapter N**.  
3. Work **scene by scene**. Prefer surgical fixes over rewrite.  
4. When a line is foggy: **name the real dispute** or **show the character**, do not caption the theme.  
5. When a beat is thin but important: **expand detail** (see §4–5).  
6. **Mandatory first-hearing preflight before handoff** (§3.3i). Do not call the pass done until this runs.  
7. Deliver a short report (§9).  
8. When structure is stable and author is ready: promote to **Final Candidate**, then Writing Pass 1.

**Author mid-read rule:** If the author asks *what does this mean?* or *this is weird*, treat that line as a **hard fail** — rewrite in plain speech immediately, then check neighbors for the same compression habit. Do not defend the shorthand.

## 3. Hard revision rules (from live Book Two practice)

### 3.1 Cut clever captions after the work is already shown

If action, seating, procedure, or dialogue already carries the beat, cut the narrator wink that explains it.

| Weak | Prefer |
|------|--------|
| People-reading disguised as furniture. | Cut. Keep the chair move. |
| People-judgment as furniture again. Useful furniture. | Cut. Keep the seating note. |
| Soft wisdom after two facts | End on the fact or the next action |

**Test:** If removing the last sentence leaves the scene clearer, remove it.

**Also — do not stack room atmosphere.** One useful concrete detail beats a paragraph of gray walls + bolted tables + bright screws + three smells. If the previous paragraph already said the tables were bolted, do not say it again. Cut resin/crate/anteroom tourism unless a character uses it.

| Weak | Prefer |
|------|--------|
| dull institutional gray of a corridor station built for packet handoff and emergency docking, not for politics + bolted tables + second display + recycled air and machine oil and faint resin of Dominion packing seals | institutional gray; screws still bright on a new display; recycled air and machine oil |

**Test:** Can the reader see the room in one breath? If the paragraph needs a second breath for smell alone, cut.

### 3.1a Cut self-denying analogies

Do not explain a behavior by comparing it to the thing you then claim nobody is admitting.

| Weak | Prefer |
|------|--------|
| Orlen watched him the way a professional watches a subject without admitting the watch | Orlen watched how he handled the unfamiliar seasoning, then wrote one line when he looked away |
| X did Y the way Z does W without admitting it | Show the concrete beat: what they looked at, what they wrote, what they delayed |
| It was not quite accusation / not quite permission / not quite a smile (as caption) | Name the tone once, or show the next action — do not half-take the label back |

**Pattern to kill:** *does [behavior] the way a [role] does [same behavior] without admitting [behavior].*

### 3.1b Ban *soft nouns* / *label* / invent-words meta talk

Do **not** write stylish shorthand about wording, classification, or readiness:

- *soft nouns*, *soft language*, *soft term*, *soft synonym*, *clean noun*, *wrong nouns*
- *invent words*, *invent a phrase*, *tomorrow's words*
- *finished label*, *better label*, *incomplete label*, *working label*, *fix the label*, *earned one* (as classification poetry)
- *the noun*, *the verb*
- *strategic gift*, *as a gift*, *not a gift* (disclosure metaphor)

| Weak | Prefer |
|------|--------|
| finished label / better label / incomplete label | finished answer / better category / incomplete classification — or name the real thing (*distress*, *hold weapons*) |
| when the table has earned one | when we have one / when both sides agree |
| invent words for next week | do not fake next week's readiness |
| soft nouns under pressure | guess under pressure / a word that means two actions |
| not a full strategic gift / not as a gift | not a full strategic brief / under limits / not free access |
| *fringe* as default border noun | *outer corridor* / *border* / *Neutral Zone edge* — keep *fringe* only if a locked map name requires it |

**Keep *label* only** for literal objects (mug label, pedestal labeled for Rowan) or rare technical stamps — never as atmospheric talk about meaning.

**Keep *gift* only** for literal objects (Lisette's mug). Never for maps, archive access, or disclosure.

**Test:** If the sentence is about *labels* or *gifts* instead of the concrete failure or ask, rewrite. Never reintroduce this cluster after cutting it.

### 3.2 Dumb down institutional jargon — then expand the real fight

Do not leave the reader to decode bureaucracy labels.

| Weak | Prefer |
|------|--------|
| Recording rights still unresolved. | Name both sides’ ask: full audio vs written summary; dual consent for raw sound. |
| veto language / stop rules / who can walk (as cute shorthand) | who can end a session / who can leave the table |
| shared nouns / shared terms (as cute shorthand) | word lists / what both sides are allowed to say / operational words |
| stop rules and shared nouns / walk-outs and word lists | who can leave and what words mean — name the work, not the label pair |
| provisional instruments (when first introduced) | Say what they do in one plain sentence, then keep the term if useful |
| *header* as default for any traffic / classification / status message | *status line*, *message*, *classification line*, *shared traffic*, *stamp* — name what the reader needs to see |
| soft term gets into a header | soft term gets into a status line |

**Rule:** First say the **dispute in plain speech**. Labels may follow.

**Also — *header* is staff jargon.** Officers may think in headers; readers should not have to. In dialogue and narration, default to the plain object (*status line*, *message*, *classification*). Keep *header* only when the beat is literally a field name on a form or protocol stack and a specialist is naming that field — and even then, prefer plain first.

**Also:** Do not stack neat institutional shorthand in POV or narration (*stop rules and shared nouns*, *who can walk*, *custody and headers*). If the character hates the assignment, show the hated **work**, not a tidy pair of staff terms.

| Weak (tidy staff talk) | Prefer (hated work) |
|------------------------|---------------------|
| an envoy with stop rules and shared nouns | an envoy arguing over who can leave and what words mean |
| Field judgment. Who can walk. | Field judgment. Who can end a session. What words both sides may use. |

**Plain speak over staff poetry:** Keep the idea. Cut the elevated phrasing. If a sentence needs unpacking to understand what the character believes, rewrite it shorter and plainer.

| Weak (overwritten) | Prefer (plain) |
|--------------------|----------------|
| the right to refuse a clean story when the board would not settle | not claiming more than the instruments showed |
| refusing to invent certainty the instruments had not earned | not claiming more than the instruments showed |
| provisional categories as moral architecture | watching longer than politics preferred |
| bought X a small pool of speakers who can… | was long enough for X to train a few speakers. Those people can… |
| keep the nouns from killing people | make sure a bad word does not start a fight |
| temporary permission wearing permanent bolts | bolts permanent; permission for politics temporary |
| felt like penance / buy penance with an adjective | felt like making up for the mistake |
| silence that functioned as permission | silence that meant they could keep planning |
| mission-critical / not decorative | can kill people if wrong / not optional |

**Test:** Would this officer say it in a ready room, or only in a style guide? Ready-room speech wins. Can the reader get the fact on first pass? If not, break the sentence.

**Do not hammer a plain phrase:** Once you have said *who can end a session* (or any cleared plain term) clearly once, later beats can shorten (*suspension*, *exit rules*, *seating*) instead of repeating the full phrase every paragraph.

### 3.2a *Argue* habit (especially ship / object talk)

*Argue* / *arguing* is fine for **people** in a real dispute (*Rowan did not argue the point*, *morning argument about a term*).

Do **not** make ships, rooms, or gear “argue” as a default joke — especially in Brenner’s mouth — unless the character is literally in a fight with a machine that matters.

| Weak | Prefer |
|------|--------|
| arguing / fighting with the coffee urn (vague personification) | I spend half my watch keeping the coffee urn running (maintenance fact) |
| the ship argues with its systems | name the real fault: will not take / keeps dropping / needs a kick |
| back arguing about false friends (when “argue” is already nearby) | back on false friends / back at the word list |

**Brenner note:** Ship-instinct humor should be **literal maintenance**, not metaphor. The coffee urn lands because he fixes it — not because gear “argues.”

### 3.3 Ban odd default words when a plain one exists

| Avoid as habit | Prefer |
|----------------|--------|
| *lag* for remote people / pedestals / Helion | *relay*, *remote*, *from Helion*, *on the channel* |
| *lag* when delay is not the point | *pause*, *moment*, or cut |
| *holds* for custody | *has … in custody*, *logged*, *sealed* |
| *geometry* as vague filler | *map*, *contour*, *chart*, *board*, *corridor*, *this close* |

Keep *lag* / *delay* only when **time delay itself** is the beat.

### 3.3b Cut soft *wrong* talk and bin metaphors in narration

Do not use *wrong* as atmosphere, mood paint, or a vague emotional noun. Do not say a character “had no bin” unless they are literally talking classification work aloud.

| Weak | Prefer |
|------|--------|
| Park felt the same wrong recognition and had no bin for it | Park saw it too. Same hands. Same eyes. |
| incomplete in a way that still sat wrong with him | provisional and unknown. He still did not have a better category. |
| still wrong against every human emergency format | still matched no human emergency format |
| the stun held / the wrongness landed | name the silence, the stare, or the next action |
| quieter wrongness | cut; say the fact (company / already running / etc.) |

**Test:** If *wrong* can be deleted and the sentence still means the same concrete thing, delete it. If the beat is classification anxiety, say *category*, *label*, or *format* — once — then move on.

Also already in Writing Pass 1 (apply early if they confuse the scene): gloves-as-tell · meta noun/verb talk · instrument “lies”.

### 3.3c Do not ban or name shared-origin before the story earns it

Characters cannot police **Founders' Week**, **shared-origin**, **shared frontier history**, **speculative kinship**, or **contamination** of common ancestry until the chapter (or prior locked chapters) has actually put that dispute in play.

**Do not invent a beat just to police it.** Do not add a Fleet staff note saying *shared frontier history*, then bring Ellis in to strike it, in early Act I contact chapters. That is author scaffolding, not story.

| Premature | Prefer |
|-----------|--------|
| Senka / Ellis forbids Founders' Week / shared-origin / *shared frontier history* in Session One | Cut the whole beat; open on maps, warnings, Peligar name, disclosure limits |
| Orlen fears “contamination” / kinship leak | Soft language travels; political claims travel — keep it institutional |
| *No archive history as shared fact* / *No commemorative language* as early ban list | Operational scope only |
| *Your people / ours* as sibling cultures | *Fleet / Dominion* institutional habits |
| Linguist teases “what is not us” before intercept review | Hesitation / clean answers / the work in front of them |
| Narrator “he knew what she meant” about Peligar before the afternoon open | Cut the wink; land recognition when the recording plays |
| Ellis present in Ch.2 solely to strike shared-history phrasing | Leave Historical Office out until the dispute is live |

**Allowed when earned (later):** Helion Historical Office (Ellis) may sit for narrative hygiene once commemorative / shared-history language is actually contested in the plot. Founders' Week naming belongs with that later fight.

**Test:** Would this character know to name Founders' Week, kinship, or *shared frontier history* *today*? If not, cut the line. Search and kill: `shared frontier history|Founders'|speculative kinship|shared-origin|commemorative language` in early Act I.

| Cut | Prefer |

Ordinary talk already works through interpreters and trained speakers. Do **not** open lexicon scenes on kindergarten nouns (*Ship*). Open on **lethal false friends** (*engage* / *stand down* / *hold weapons*).

#### F. Orphan flashbacks and distress loops

| Cut | Prefer |
|-----|--------|
| Mid-lexicon dump of Ch.1 intercept shame with no trigger | Keep shame/classification only where the recording or Calder exit earns it |
| Hammering *distress* / *rescue* after the room has moved on | Once reclassified, stop looping; operational rules only (*do not engage*) |
| *I refused distress* as opaque shorthand | *I did not flag it as distress* — or drop if already spent |

#### G. Peligar recognition pacing and knowledge limits

- Neither empire “understands” Peligar. Dominion may **recognize the channel shape** without meaning.

- Deeper archive share needs Senka / Directorate — recognition can still go up Fleet channels as priority security.
- **Fleet already has the name:** Dominion does **not** control Fleet communications. Negotiate only the **shared** board (warning maps / strategic charts). Fleet routes classified traffic as Fleet sees fit.

| Wrong | Right |
|-------|-------|

#### I. Geography nouns — name the real line

| Ambiguous | Clear |
|-----------|--------|

#### J. Near-miss / process failure texture

- If the morning table was tested clean, say so — then the overnight SSD patch breaks it.
- Finger-pointing notes are finger-pointing — do not say they “become policy.”
- Do not invent “do not punish” for good questioning unless punishment is established.

#### K. Tech asymmetry (seed, do not lecture)

Dominion: stronger Peligar-facing listening / war. Fleet: native stealth (not cloak), speed, colonization. Characters need not see the whole split yet. Light seed only (e.g. Dominion already used better listening gear on this channel). See `Notes/FLEET_DOMINION_TECH_ASYMMETRY.md`.

#### L. Plain elevated junk that keeps creeping back

| Cut | Prefer |
|-----|--------|
| *temporary politeness* | name the real ask (*one map talk*) |
| *forms happier* / *record look finished* (when fake) | *make the record look finished* only if earned; else cut |
| *corrected scream* | *corrected classification* |
| *header* as default | *status line* / *message* / *classification* |

### 3.3e Successive chapter continuity (build forward)

Chapters are **successive**, not parallel resets.

If Chapter N establishes a fact, name, recommendation, authority lane, or vocabulary change, Chapter N+1 must **build on that spend** — not re-reveal it as new, and not drop it as if it never happened.

| Failure | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Re-open a reveal already locked (Peligar named again as first hearing) | Treat as known; add one new constraint, cost, or use |
| Seed a recommendation (outpost, packet note) then silence | Carry forward lightly: pending / capitals / not on today’s board — or show why it died |
| Lexicon / procedure renamed in N+1 while N still lists the old term | Sync both chapters (*walk out* → *suspend*) |
| Geography noun settled in N (*Peligar contact edge*) then N+1 defaults to vague *fringe* | Keep the settled noun; only upgrade precision |
| Callback that only restates yesterday | Callback that uses yesterday to decide something today |
| Re-teach a spent fact (exonym lecture, *Speech. Meaning none.*, who authorized *Peligar*, why the ugly phrase exists) | Use the spent fact as working equipment; spend the page on today’s decision |

**Do not re-teach spent facts (hard rule).**

If the prior chapter (or earlier in this chapter) already locked a fact, later prose may **use** it. It may not **re-explain** it for the reader.

| Spent (examples) | Illegal re-teach | Legal use |
|------------------|------------------|-----------|
| *Peligar* named; exonym; never translated | Orlen/Vorin re-gloss *exonym / not self-name / meaning incomplete* | Chart titled Peligar-contact-edge; someone says the name in work |
| Speech / meaning none | Park’s corrected classification sermon again | Incomplete categories press a decision now |
| *request for cooperative warning posture* written | Park re-explains *Not help. Not petition.* | Phrase is spoken as the live ask / sealed as the instrument |
| Near-miss / spoken confirmation | Retell the overnight patch drama | “Spoken confirmation stays” / design constraint |
| Authority assignments for Session Two | Full roster roll-call again | “Yesterday’s assignments held” + only name what’s new |

**Test:** Would a reader who finished the prior chapter learn anything new from this paragraph? If no, cut the gloss. Keep only what changes today’s fight.

**Load rule:** Before revising Chapter N, skim Final Candidate / LOCKED prior chapter for spent beats. Ask: *What did yesterday leave unfinished that today must acknowledge or advance?* Also ask: *Am I re-teaching anything the room already knows?*

### 3.3f Plain speech, less hyperbole (Ch.3 live-edit distillate)

Distilled from Book Two Ch.3 author line edits (2026-07-20). Apply on every Revision Pass and when the author is mid-read fixing foggy lines.

**Voice target:** Plainer. Less hyperbole. Less costume metaphor. Blunt where the fight is real. Expand when a clever compression hides the meaning.

| Weak (clever / compressed) | Prefer (plain / expanded) |
|----------------------------|---------------------------|
| *salute our archives* | not asking Fleet to accept our records as finished fact |
| *edge does not hold still for your survey edition* | a line that moves before your charts can finish printing it |
| *enemy with a face* / *fails under longer watch* | Fleet wants a captain to blame; longer watch shows advance and take, not a navy that declares war |
| *give us the longer watch* (ambiguous) | show us the years behind that claim / the watch product that made you sure |
| *yesterday's quiet proof of tomorrow's safety* | quiet is not the same as safe / missing returns Fleet cannot ignore |
| *Fleet prefers a single failure story* | point at the board: three clusters one way, one reverse — don’t force one clean cause |
| *the question Fleet procedure always asked when absences arrived without a recovery brief* | Look at the list. Fleet procedure was simple: if traffic stopped answering, ask whether anyone went looking. |

**Rules:**

1. **Plain first.** If a line needs a second read to decode the metaphor, rewrite it in ordinary professional speech.
2. **Expand fog; do not decorate it.** One clear extra clause beats a stylish compression.
3. **Cut hyperbole and costume.** No saluting archives, enemy-with-a-face poetry, pride-as-tax, funeral rehearsals, weather they cannot command.
4. **Dialogue points at the work.** Prefer dates, clusters, gaps, search ships, strategic charts — not abstract “stories,” “frames,” or “narratives” unless a character is literally fighting a word.
5. **Narrator setups stay short and concrete.** *She looked at the list. Procedure was X. Then the question.* Not a thesis about what institutions always do.
6. **Keep blunt.** Vex/Senka/Calder may be hard; they should not be ornate.
7. **Test:** Could a tired watch officer understand the line on first hearing? If not, simplify and expand.
8. **Name the damn thing.** Do not rotate cute synonyms for the same board fact. If the Peligar advance reaches Segment approaches, say *the Peligar advance*. If systems are going quiet, say *systems going quiet*. Do not hide the referent behind *pressure*, *contour*, *the pattern*, *the edge*, *the line*, or other synonym bun fillers unless a character is literally fighting over what word to use on a status line.

**Search for regressions:** `narrative|salute|with a face|proof of tomorrow|failure story|recovery narrative|conceptual gap|payment completed|pride was expensive|without becoming a speech|That was as close|Liking is not required|Ugly phrases|war words|held the language line|Strike stewardship|smuggles|Not relief|Cost paid|experience, not poetry|Friendship was not on offer|Those were not the same sentence|did not invent significance`

**Hard cut (do not soften — delete):**

- Empty linguist banter that restates rails already locked (*war words*, *ugly phrases keep people honest*, *liking is not required*)
- Ellis / historian decorative word-strikes in early Act I when the dispute is not live (*shared losses*, *stewardship*, kinship policing)
- Caption endings after the beat already landed (*That was as close as…*, *Those were not the same sentence*, *It sounded like experience, not poetry*)
- Ritual-sounding custody shorthand (*spoke the chain aloud*) — say the real check in plain speech
- Clever two-noun traps (*trap shaped like caution* / *weapon shaped like science*) unless a character needs that exact hardness — prefer plain accusation names
- Triadic Council rhetoric (*Fleet Council will hear trap… theater…*) — name the concrete institutional ask or fear once, in plain speech
- Default fog nouns for the Peligar advance (*pressure*, *contour*, *the pattern*, *the line*) when the sentence means *Peligar advance*, *systems going quiet*, *missing returns*, or *advance marks*. Keep ordinary *under pressure* for stress. Keep *contour lines* only as literal chart drawing language, not as the name of the threat.
- Default *buffer* / *buffer posture* for the Fleet ask — prefer *treat warnings as actionable where Fleet ships already travel* / *Segment approaches* / *shared warning work*. Keep *buffer* only if a character is literally fighting that word on a draft.

### 3.3h Name the damn thing (no cute synonym rotation)

Distilled from Book Two Ch.3 author edit (2026-07-21).

**Rule:** When a sentence refers to a concrete board fact, **use the concrete name**. Do not invent a softer synonym and rotate it for variety.

| Fog / cute synonym | Name the thing |
|--------------------|----------------|
| *the pressure* / *contact pressure* | *the Peligar advance* / *we take the first hit* |
| *the contour* (as actor: reaches, advances, waits) | *the Peligar advance* / *systems going quiet* / *missing returns* |
| *the pattern* (when meaning the advance) | *the Peligar advance* or name what is on the board |
| *the line* / *the edge* (when meaning the advance) | *Peligar contact edge* / *advance marks* / *Peligar advance* |

**Allowed exceptions:**

- A character is **literally** fighting a status-line word (*processing* vs *aggression*, *cooperative warning posture*).
- Literal chart craft: *contour lines* drawn on a map product.
- Ordinary stress English: *under pressure*.
- Synonym buns (rare; earned; edible).

**Test:** If you swap the fog noun for the concrete name and the sentence gets clearer, keep the concrete name.

**Search for regressions:** `\bthe pressure\b|\bthe contour\b|moves the contour|contour keeps|contour will not|contour is real|inside the contour|expanding-contour|contact pressure|pressure maps|pressure marks`

### 3.3i First-hearing preflight (stop staff shorthand)

Distilled from Book Two Ch.3 author mid-read (2026-07-21). This is the main guard against another chapter that “works” on draft and fails on first human read.

**Rule:** Institutional dialogue and custody lines must be clear on **first hearing**. If a tired watch officer — or the author — has to ask what a phrase means, the phrase fails.

#### Fail patterns (rewrite; do not keep)

| Fail | Why | Fix |
|------|-----|-----|
| Compressed custody (*Shared channel only*, *freeze the venue*, *Phrase stands*) | Staff poetry; meaning hidden | Expand to the real custody rule in ordinary speech |
| Memo voice as spoken dialogue | Documents are not how officers talk at a live table | Paraphrase in the speaker’s voice, or keep the document private |
| Private politics briefed to the other empire | SSD slate / Council advisory are internal | Character reads privately; states only shared limits / what they can authorize |
| Cute synonym rotation (*pressure*, *contour*, *buffer*, *the pattern*) | Soft stand-in for a named board fact | Name the damn thing (§3.3h) |
| Triadic / double *I am not* rhetoric | Speechifying | One concrete institutional fact, then the decision |
| Narrator explaining what the moment meant | Caption after the work | Cut; end on action or logged product |

#### Mandatory agent preflight (before claiming Revision Pass done)

1. **First-hearing sweep:** Read every institutional / lexicon / Council / SSD dialogue line aloud in your head. Mark any line that needs a second decode. Rewrite those before handoff.  
2. **Author-confusion test:** Would the author ask *what is X?* about any phrase? If yes, expand it now.  
3. **Authority-lane check:** Did Fleet brief Council votes/advisories to Dominion? Did Dominion police Fleet doctrine? Fix per §3.3d H.  
4. **Name-the-thing check:** Search `pressure|contour|buffer|the pattern|the line|Shared channel|freeze the venue|read .* aloud|into the room`. Expand or replace.  
5. **Private note check:** SSD slate and Fleet Council advisories stay off the joint speakers unless a character deliberately chooses to leak — and that leak is a plot choice, not default narration.  
6. **Trust-the-reader check:** One memorable example? Scene already made its point? Cut reinforcement stacks (§3.3j).

#### Compressed custody → plain (examples from Ch.3)

| Compressed | Plain |
|------------|-------|
| *Shared channel only* | *for the shared board only — not Fleet doctrine, not Dominion doctrine* |
| *freeze the venue* | *close this session before we have written instruments* |
| *read it into the room* | paraphrase, or keep private |
| *buffer posture* | *treat warnings as actionable where Fleet ships already travel* |
| *the contour already reaches* | *the Peligar advance already reaches* |

**Report item:** Include “First-hearing preflight: pass / fails fixed” in §9.

### 3.3j Trust the reader (momentum over briefing)

Distilled from Book Two Ch.3 author rewrite (2026-07-21).

**Rule:** Once the reader understands an idea, **move on**. Do not persuade with exhaustive evidence, repeated examples, or multiple versions of the same argument.

| Prefer | Avoid |
|--------|-------|
| One memorable example (one vanished station; one search watch that did not return) | Lists of missing colonies, patrols, and near-identical map passes |
| Character points, asks, decides | Maps/reports as primary storyteller |
| One scene, one purpose — then leave | Same point from three angles |
| Something changes every few pages | Information accumulates; nothing moves |
| Memorable moment six chapters later | “They discussed maps” |

**Cut ruthlessly:** paragraphs that only reinforce a point already established; second conversations that duplicate a goal; example stacks that can be one example.

**Reader memory test:** What will the reader remember six chapters from now? If the answer is a briefing, the scene is too long.

### 3.3k Plain vocabulary: no odd synonyms, weird analogies, or substitute language

Distilled from Book Two Ch.3 author edits (2026-07-21).

When the author has to ask *what does this mean?* the word choice has failed. This usually happens in one of three ways:

**1. Odd synonym rotation** — substituting a different word each time to avoid repetition, even when the first word was correct.

| Odd rotation | Keep the plain word |
|---|---|
| *notices* / *the posture* / *the ask* / *the product* (all meaning warnings) | *warnings* or name the concrete object — an unsafe-approach alert, a pressure map |
| *the instruments* cycling through *the posture*, *the agreement*, *the package* | name the specific thing signed or agreed |
| *the advance* / *the edge* / *the contour* / *the pressure* (all meaning the Peligar advance) | *the Peligar advance* — name it once, keep it |

**2. Weird or extended analogies** — a comparison that needs explaining is not working.

| Fails | Why | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Analogy that takes longer to read than the point it makes | The analogy became the scene | Cut the analogy; state the point |
| Two different analogies for the same thing in one scene | Confusion, not clarity | Pick one or cut both |
| Analogy that invites the next character to ask what you mean | It failed | State the real thing |

**Rule:** If the analogy clarifies in one short clause, keep it. If it needs three sentences to land, it is not an analogy — it is a scene within a scene. Cut it or build it as its own beat.

**Brenner exception:** Brenner may use a literal maintenance story (engines, couplers, feeds) as the setup for a proposal. This works because the story *is the argument*, not a metaphor for the argument. Keep those. Do not extend them into recipe lists or flour-and-yeast asides.

**3. Substitute language** — a word standing in for a concrete thing because the writer did not want to commit to the real name.

| Substitute | Name the thing |
|---|---|
| *the ask* (policy level) | name what was asked: *a warning exchange*, *treaty authorization*, *Calder's proposal* |
| *the work* (vague) | name what the work is: *the lexicon*, *the survey order*, *the station design* |
| *the posture* | *how Fleet responds to Dominion warnings* / *the cooperative warning agreement* |
| *the framework* | name the actual document, instrument, or agreement |

**Search for regressions:** `\bthe ask\b|\bthe posture\b|\bthe framework\b|\bthe product\b|\bthe instrument\b` (when vague, not named) · `odd synonym|substitute|weird analogy`

**Test:** Read the line aloud. If you would pause and wonder what the word refers to, replace it with the concrete name.

### 3.4 Prefer concrete custody / work language

Institutions act through packets, custody, seals, status lines, classification stamps, and named procedures — not soft verbs that sound literary, and not cute shorthand pairs that only staff would love.

| Weak | Prefer |
|------|--------|
| Park holds the recording | Park has the recording in custody |
| Trust had not been purchased | Trust had not arrived / still absent |
| radioactive parts (if foggy) | contested / unresolved / disputed — or keep if the POV earned the metaphor |
| stop rules and shared nouns | walk-outs and word lists / who can suspend and what words may travel |
| who can walk / walk-out rights (shorthand) | who can end a session / who can leave the table / either side can still end the session |
| safe pedestal / watched from a pedestal (metaphor) | safe distance / from Helion / on the relay — keep *pedestal* only as the literal relay stand in the room |
| header / headers (default staff talk) | status line / message / classification / shared traffic — keep *header* only as a named field when specialists are literally editing one |

## 4. Expand details when the beat matters

Subtraction is default (revision philosophy). **Expansion is required** when:

- The reader cannot tell what the fight is about  
- A procedure is named but not shown  
- A motivation is asserted but not felt  
- A location or tool is abstract when one concrete detail would land  
- A character decision needs one more beat of resistance, humor, or cost  

### Expand by showing work, not by adding theme

| Thin | Stronger |
|------|----------|
| Recording still unresolved | Fleet wants whole-session audio; Dominion wants summary only; no raw sound without dual yes |
| They practiced walk-out | Fleet suspends; Dominion acknowledges; reverse; silence is not consent |
| He disliked the assignment | One concrete complaint + one concrete reason he stays |

Do **not** pad with second metaphors, thesis lines, or repeated explanations.

## 5. Character building (Revision Pass priority)

Every revised scene should leave at least one character clearer than before — by behavior, not by caption.

### Ask per scene

- Whose scene is this, really?  
- What does this person want *right now*?  
- What do they refuse to pretend?  
- What small habit, joke, silence, or competence marks them?  
- Does dialogue sound like them, or like the institution explaining itself?

### Expand character through:

- **Specific resistance** (Calder hates envoy nouns; still pockets the packet)  
- **Competence under stress** (Thessaly turns politics into seating and stop rules)  
- **Dry humor / private objects** (Park and the cup — cryptic first, clear later)  
- **Ship-side honesty** (Brenner: tools, coffee urn, no diplomacy)  
- **Relationship micro-beats** (who talks whom down; who does not elaborate)

### Avoid

- Theme mottos wearing a character’s mouth  
- Everyone sounding like the same briefing officer  
- Explaining a character’s interior after the action already showed it  
- Turning every Dominion beat into a quotation contest (keep earned inherited speech; cut costume)

### Voice quick check

| Character | Revision tell |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Calder** | Better questions than answers; complains about wrong job; trusts specialists |
| **Thessaly** | Converts mess into lists; short corrections; little speechifying |
| **Park** | Dry, precise, custody-minded; humor sideways |
| **Brenner** | Rare; ship/tool reality; snide when politics wastes a hatch |
| **Dominion** | Live inside continuity; do not explain civilization to Fleet |
