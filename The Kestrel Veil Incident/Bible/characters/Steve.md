# ENTITY DESIGNATION: STEVE

## CLASSIFICATION
Non-indexed anomalous personnel entity (UFA registry mismatch class)

---

## FUNCTIONAL ROLE

Steve is not assigned a formal rank or permanent position within Fleet records.

He appears intermittently in operational environments without consistent assignment history.

He is classified as:

> “Unresolved Administrative Presence”

---

## CORE PROPERTY

Steve is a **high-stability anomaly in human perception systems**.

This manifests as:

- Inconsistent memory retention across observers
- Incomplete or conflicting interaction records
- Absence from some official logs despite direct witnessed presence
- Normal behavior during direct interaction

---

## PHYSICAL / OPERATIONAL BEHAVIOR

- Appears fully ordinary during observation
- Performs standard procedural tasks competently
- Does not display abnormal behavior under stress
- Does not initiate anomalous events directly

---

## ANOMALY TYPE

Steve is NOT a physical anomaly.

He is a:

> “Continuity persistence failure in observational and record systems”

Meaning:
- reality behaves normally around him
- systems fail to consistently record or retain him

---

## MEMORY EFFECT RULE

Observers of Steve may experience:

- partial recall loss after interaction
- inconsistent recollection of conversation details
- disagreement between multiple observers about Steve’s presence

BUT:
- no direct cognitive impairment occurs
- no hallucination effects are induced
- discrepancies only appear in post-event reconciliation

---

## LIMITATIONS (IMPORTANT FOR ENGINE SAFETY)

Steve MUST NOT:

- alter physical systems directly
- override ship or empire systems
- create physical anomalies in space-time behavior
- possess knowledge outside his observable scope

He is strictly a **recording/retention inconsistency**, not a reality manipulator.

---

## BEHAVIORAL CONSISTENCY RULE

Across all appearances:

- Steve behaves rationally
- Steve is polite but unremarkable
- Steve does not acknowledge his own anomaly status
- Steve does not explain himself
- Steve does not contradict others about his identity

---

## NARRATIVE FUNCTION

Steve exists to create:

- continuity uncertainty
- retrospective contradiction
- “wait… was he here earlier?” effect
- subtle destabilization of record trust systems

---

## STEVE RULE (CORE)

> Steve must always feel normal in the moment and questionable in retrospect.

---

## PRESENCE CONSTRAINT

- Maximum narrative visibility per book: LOW
- Maximum scenes per book: 1–3
- Must never resolve his nature within a single book

---

## ENGINE COMPATIBILITY RULE

Steve must always:

- conform to local physical rules
- obey ship protocols
- be logically explainable at time of interaction
- become harder to confirm after interaction

---

## THEMATIC ROLE

Steve represents:

> The failure of systems to reliably record truth, even when truth is observed directly.
